Exploring the Future of Soft X-ray Polarimetry: the Capabilities of the REDSoX Instrument for XDINS and Magnetar Studies

Time: 2025-03-14

Authors: Ruth M. E. Kelly, Herman L. Marshall, Silvia Zane, Nabil Brice, Swati Ravi, Roberto Turolla, Roberto Taverna, Denis González-Caniulef

Abstract: The REDSoX mission will test X-ray polarimetry instruments for probing neutron star physics, enabling future orbital missions to study XDINSs and magnetars by detecting proton-cyclotron absorption and QED-induced polarization mode conversion at vacuum resonance with ~25,000 counts, as demonstrated in case studies of RX J1856.5-3754 and RX J0720.4-3125.

Neutrinos as a new tool to characterise the Milky Way Centre

Time: 2025-03-14

Authors: Paul C. W. Lai, Beatrice Crudele, Matteo Agostini, Hayden P. H. Ng, Ellis R. Owen, Nishta Varma, Kinwah Wu

Abstract: The Central Molecular Zone's inefficient star formation, hindered by challenges in mapping dense molecular clouds with traditional tracers, can be addressed by future neutrino detection from cosmic-ray interactions (alongside gamma-rays) using advanced telescopes like KM3NeT, Baikal-GVD, and P-ONE, enabling precise gas density measurements to calibrate conventional methods and improve gas analysis in distant galaxies.

Recent Highlights from the VERITAS AGN Discovery Program

Time: 2025-03-14

Authors: Wystan Benbow, Jodi Christiansen, Julia Francescutti, Garrett Kunkler, Will Root, Phoebe Zyla

Abstract: VERITAS, a leading very-high-energy gamma-ray observatory operational since 2007, has dedicated over 8,300 hours (50% of its good-weather data) to discovering and studying active galactic nuclei (AGN), including recent detections like B2 0912+29, using spectral, variability, and broadband observations to investigate jet-powered processes in AGN.

Validating and improving two-fluid simulations of the magnetic field evolution in neutron star cores

Time: 2025-03-14

Authors: F. Castillo, N. A. Moraga, M. E. Gusakov, J. A. Valdivia, A. Reisenegger

Abstract: This study validates the fictitious friction approach for modeling the evolution of axially symmetric magnetic fields in neutron star cores using a two-fluid model, showing that bulk velocity dominates over ambipolar diffusion, leading to faster evolution toward a barotropic Grad-Shafranov equilibrium with timescales proportional to \( B^{-2} \) and shorter for equations of state with smaller proton-neutron density gradients.

Scaling and universality in strange quark stars

Time: 2025-03-14

Authors: G. Lugones, A. G. Grunfeld

Abstract: This study derives scaling laws and universal relations that connect macroscopic properties of strange quark stars to microscopic quark matter characteristics, distinguish them from neutron stars, and use observational mass constraints to bound quark-matter self-binding and repulsive interactions.

Time: 2025-03-14

Authors: Vladimir Dergachev, Maria Alessandra Papa

Abstract: The full release of the continuous gravitational wave atlas expands the frequency range to 20 Hz–1700 Hz and spindowns to ±5e-10 Hz/s, incorporates outlier follow-up, and enables efficient, resource-light searches despite the computationally intensive nature of such investigations.

Cygnus X-3 as semi-hidden PeVatron

Time: 2025-03-14

Authors: M. Kachelriess, E. Lammert

Abstract: This study demonstrates that Cygnus X-3, assuming a black hole compact object, accelerates cosmic rays beyond PeV energies via magnetic reconnection, Fermi, and diffusive shock mechanisms, producing high-energy photons and neutrinos through photo-hadronic interactions and stellar wind scattering, with a low 10⁻³ acceleration efficiency sufficient to explain LHAASO's observed excess and suggesting potential bright phases of significantly increased PeV flux.

Probing fundamental physics using compact astrophysical objects

Time: 2025-03-14

Authors: E. -A. Kolonia, C. J. A. P. Martins, Konstantinos N. Gourgouliatos

Abstract: This study examines how variations in the fine-structure constant α, predicted by alternative theories to the Standard Model, influence the Mass-Radius relations of white dwarfs (via polytropic models) and neutron stars (using realistic equations of state), providing constraints from current data and suggesting future observations to distinguish between such theoretical extensions.

Machine-learning heat flux closure for multi-moment fluid modeling of nonlinear Landau damping

Time: 2025-03-14

Authors: Ziyu Huang, Chuanfei Dong, Liang Wang

Abstract: This study develops a machine learning-assisted fluid model using a Fourier neural operator to integrate kinetic physics from Vlasov simulations into a fluid closure, enabling computationally affordable and accurate modeling of nonlinear Landau damping evolution that matches Vlasov results, surpassing previous fluid models.

Time: 2025-03-14

Authors: Leonardus B. Putra, H. S. Ramadhan
