Proto-neutron star oscillations including accretion flows

Time: 2025-03-20

Authors: Dimitra Tseneklidou, Raimon Luna, Pablo Cerdá-Durán, Alejandro Torres-Forné

Abstract: This study introduces a general relativistic framework for modeling proto-neutron star oscillations in core-collapse supernovae, incorporating accretion flows and a stalled shock to analyze gravitational wave signatures and SASI-related modes through numerical eigenvalue methods validated via code testing.

The First Model-Independent Chromatic Microlensing Search: No Evidence in the Gravitational Wave Catalog of LIGO-Virgo-KAGRA

Time: 2025-03-20

Authors: Aniruddha Chakraborty, Suvodip Mukherjee

Abstract: Using the μ-GLANCE method for a model-independent microlensing search in the wave-optics limit on 80 GW events from GWTC-3, researchers identified one candidate (GW190408_181802) with a marginally elevated residual amplitude but found no conclusive microlensing evidence, confidently ruling out significant signals in the dataset.

Neutrino emission and corona heating induced by high-energy proton interactions in Seyfert galaxies

Time: 2025-03-20

Authors: A. Neronov, O. Kalashev, D. V. Semikoz, D. Savchenko, M. Poleshchuk

Abstract: The detection of high-energy neutrinos from Seyfert AGNs suggests their emission may lack direct electromagnetic counterparts due to proton-driven disk/corona heating, but indirect correlations with hard X-ray variability and contributions to the diffuse TeV neutrino background could emerge depending on proton heating's role in corona energy balance.

Superluminal Dark Photons as a Solution to the GRB 221009A Anomaly

Time: 2025-03-20

Authors: Antonios Valamontes, Emmanuel Markoulakis, Ioannis Adamopoulos

Abstract: The LHAASO Collaboration's detection of 18 TeV γ-photons from GRB 221009A challenges conventional physics, and while photon-ALP oscillations require parameter tuning, this study proposes superluminal dark photons—building on Markoulakis and Valamontes' frameworks—to enable faster-than-light propagation and bypass extragalactic background light attenuation, aligning with cosmological observations and LHC results.

EP240414a: A Gamma-Ray Burst Jet Weakened by an Extended Circumstellar Material

Time: 2025-03-20

Authors: Hamid Hamidani, Yuri Sato, Kazumi Kashiyama, Masaomi Tanaka, Kunihito Ioka, Shigeo S. Kimura

Abstract: The Einstein Probe event EP240414a, exhibiting hybrid features between long and low-luminosity gamma-ray bursts with a thermal-then-red-peak optical transient, is explained by a "barely failed" jet weakened by dense circumstellar material, demonstrating how CSM shapes jet dynamics and the importance of early multi-wavelength observations in understanding diverse jet-driven transients.

Gamma-Ray Burst Jets in Circumstellar Material: Dynamics, Breakout, and Diversity of Transients

Time: 2025-03-20

Authors: Hamid Hamidani, Kunihito Ioka, Kazumi Kashiyama, Masaomi Tanaka

Abstract: This study develops an analytic model to analyze long gamma-ray burst jet dynamics in circumstellar material, deriving universal formulas for jet head velocity and breakout time, establishing a success/failure criterion dependent on CSM radius, categorizing three jet outcomes via energy-weighted proper velocity, and linking large CSM radii to luminous optical transients through cocoon emission, providing a framework to connect LGRB diversity with environmental factors.

Evolution of binary stars

Time: 2025-03-20

Authors: Pablo Marchant

Abstract: Binary stars are crucial for testing stellar evolution theories through precise mass and radius measurements, and their close interactions lead to phenomena like stellar mergers, chemically altered stars, and gravitational wave sources, significantly influencing the evolution of massive stars and their large-scale astrophysical impacts.

Compression fronts from fast radio bursts

Time: 2025-03-20

Authors: Andrei M. Beloborodov

Abstract: This study investigates how fast radio bursts (FRBs) from magnetars interact with their surrounding electron-positron wind plasma, causing stochastic heating and compression at smaller radii and regular heating or quasisteady compression waves at larger radii, with survival and damping avoidance depending on emission radius and luminosity scaling as \( L^{1/3} \).

Hide and seek with a Neutron Star. Hint of Neutron Star activity in SN 2015ap light curve

Time: 2025-03-20

Authors: Fabio Ragosta, Giulia Illiano, Andrea Simongini, Matteo Imbrogno, Silvia Piranomonte, Andrea Melandri

Abstract: The study attributes the 8.4-day periodic modulation in SN 2015ap's multi-wavelength light curves and Hα emission shifts to accretion onto a magnetized neutron star in a close binary system (separation <50 Ro) via the Taylor-Spruit dynamo model, challenging traditional circumstellar interaction explanations.
