Cosmological Parameters Estimate from Persistent Radio Sources of Fast Radio Bursts

Time: 2025-04-17

Authors: Zi-Liang Zhang, Bing Zhang

Abstract: A novel method using the Yang relation (linking persistent radio source luminosity to fast radio burst rotation measures) combined with a two-stage MCMC analysis constrains the Hubble constant to \( H_0 = 75 \pm 30~\mathrm{km\,s^{-1}\,Mpc^{-1}} \), demonstrating potential as a cosmological probe for FRBs with future refinements expected to reduce uncertainties.

Performance of the advanced gamma-ray trigger system for the High Energy Cosmic Radiation Detection (HERD) facility

Time: 2025-04-17

Authors: Keerthana Rajan Lathika

Abstract: The High Energy Cosmic-Radiation Detection (HERD) facility, slated for installation on China's Space Station in 2027, is a decade-long mission designed to detect dark matter indirectly, study cosmic-ray spectra up to the knee region, and observe gamma rays above 100 MeV using a unique large-acceptance telescope with an advanced ultra-low-energy gamma-ray trigger system, validated by 2023 prototype tests at CERN.

A Novel View on the Inner Crusts of Neo-Neutron Stars: exotic light nuclei, diffusional and thermodynamical stability

Time: 2025-04-17

Authors: Mikhail V. Beznogov, Adriana R. Raduta

Abstract: The study reveals that young neutron stars' non-accreted crusts, modeled via extended nuclear equilibrium, feature a deep ¹⁴He layer challenging nuclear pasta theories, demonstrate stability against ion diffusion, and confirm thermodynamic stability of subsaturated matter, ruling out a first-order phase transition.

Hunting for newborn magnetars: a multi-messenger approach

Time: 2025-04-17

Authors: Dafne Guetta, Simone Dall'Osso

Abstract: Numerical simulations reveal that gravitational wave emission from magnetars has minimal effect on shock break-out light-curves but imprints a detectable signature in late-stage (>20-30 days) supernova light-curves, with ULTRASAT and the Einstein Telescope projected to observe such multi-messenger (UV+GW) events at a rate exceeding one biennially, offering critical insights into magnetar formation.

Testing the Star-disk Collision Model for Quasi-periodic Eruptions

Time: 2025-04-17

Authors: Wenyuan Guo, Rong-Feng Shen

Abstract: The star-disk collision model for quasi-periodic eruptions (QPEs) faces challenges as it fails to explain most sources due to implausibly large stellar radii or stellar disruption risks, except for two cases requiring solar-sized stars, and predicts temperatures an order of magnitude lower than observed.

Data-driven extraction, phenomenology and modeling of eccentric harmonics in binary black hole merger waveforms

Time: 2025-04-16

Authors: Tousif Islam, Tejaswi Venumadhav, Ajit Kumar Mehta, Isha Anantpurkar, Digvijay Wadekar, Javier Roulet, Jonathan Mushkin, Barak Zackay, Matias Zaldarriaga

Abstract: This paper introduces a data-driven framework using singular-value decomposition (SVD) and post-Newtonian insights to extract monotonic eccentric harmonics from gravitational waveforms, revealing their phase/frequency structure as \(j\phi_{\lambda} + \phi_{\rm ecc}\) (or \(jf_{\lambda} + f_{\rm ecc}\)), provides analytical fits based on mean anomaly for efficient eccentric merger modeling, and offers a modular tool (gwMiner) adaptable to various waveform models.

Modeling Supernova 2023ixf: Lightcurve Degeneracies and Morphological Differences

Time: 2025-04-16

Authors: Shannon Forde, Jared A. Goldberg

Abstract: The Type IIP supernova SN 2023ixf, the closest of the decade observed in Messier 101, was modeled using MESA+STELLA simulations, revealing its lightcurve is best explained by a high-mass progenitor with significant mass loss and minimal hydrogen-rich ejecta, challenging standard stellar evolution models.

gwharmone: first data-driven surrogate for eccentric harmonics in binary black hole merger waveforms

Time: 2025-04-16

Authors: Tousif Islam, Tejaswi Venumadhav, Ajit Kumar Mehta, Isha Anantpurkar, Digvijay Wadekar, Javier Roulet, Jonathan Mushkin, Barak Zackay, Matias Zaldarriaga

Abstract: The first data-driven surrogate model, *gwharmone*, for eccentric harmonics and full radiation in non-spinning binary black hole mergers—trained on 173 waveforms (mass ratios 1–3.5, eccentricities 0–0.2) using singular value decomposition and Gaussian process regression—achieves rapid evaluation (~0.1s), low errors (~0.001 time-domain, <0.01 frequency-domain mismatch), and enables efficient eccentric merger searches/parameter estimation, with public release via gwModels.
