A Search for Dark Matter Annihilation in Stellar Streams with the Fermi-LAT

Time: 2025-02-21

Authors: Cristina Fernandez-Suarez, Miguel A. Sanchez-Conde

Abstract: This study analyzes 15 years of Fermi-LAT gamma-ray data from 11 stellar streams—potential remnants of dark matter (DM)-rich dwarf galaxies—finding no detectable signals from WIMP annihilation but setting the first constraints on WIMP parameters (∼10× the thermal relic cross-section for \( b\bar{b} \) and \( \tau^+\tau^- \) channels), highlighting both systematic uncertainties in DM modeling and the promising role of streams in future DM searches.

Massive vector field perturbations in the Schwarzschild spacetime from supersymmetric gauge theory

Time: 2025-02-21

Authors: Xian-Hui Ge, Masataka Matsumoto, Kilar Zhang

Abstract: This study employs the Seiberg-Witten/quasinormal modes duality and instanton counting to analytically determine eigenfrequencies of quasinormal modes and quasi-bound states for massive vector fields in Schwarzschild spacetime, linking gauge and gravity theories via Heun-type equations and boundary conditions through connection formulas, with results consistent with numerical research.

Unveiling the origin of fast radio bursts by modeling the stellar mass and star formation distributions of their host galaxies

Time: 2025-02-21

Authors: Nick Loudas, Dongzi Li, Michael A. Strauss, Joel Leja

Abstract: This study demonstrates that fast radio burst (FRB) progenitors predominantly trace star formation rather than stellar mass, based on comparisons of 51 FRB host galaxies with mock samples, while allowing for hybrid models (up to 40-50% stellar mass-tracking FRBs) and providing the publicly available GALFRB code for future host galaxy analyses.

High-redshift quasars at $z \geq 3$ -- III. Parsec-scale jet properties from VLBI observations

Time: 2025-02-21

Authors: Shaoguang Guo, Tao An, Yuanqi Liu, Chuanzeng Liu, Zhijun Xu, Yulia Sotnikova, Timur Mufakharov, Ailing Wang

Abstract: This study's VLBI observations of 86 high-redshift (z ≥ 3) radio-loud quasars reveal predominantly compact morphologies (cores/core-jet), lower brightness temperatures, slower jet motions, and a high fraction of young/confined peak-spectrum sources, suggesting early AGN evolution in dense environments and underscoring the need for multi-wavelength observations to unravel their cosmic history.

Implications of Photon Mass: Vortextrap Magnetization of Black Holes

Time: 2025-02-21

Authors: Gia Dvali, Zaza N. Osmanov, Michael Zantedeschi

Abstract: The photon mass enables vortextrap magnetization (VTM), generating extreme magnetic fields in black holes via numerous Nielsen-Olesen vortex lines collected over cosmic timescales, aligning with supermassive black hole observations and photon mass constraints, and influencing particle acceleration, electromagnetic radiation, and gravitational waves during mergers, as demonstrated by simulations.

Measurement of the Forward Shock Velocities of the Supernova Remnant N132D Based on the Thermal X-ray Emission

Time: 2025-02-21

Authors: Yoshizumi Okada, Yuken Ohshiro, Shunsuke Suzuki, Hiromasa Suzuki, Paul P. Plucinsky, Ryo Yamazaki, Hiroya Yamaguchi

Abstract: The study measures shock velocities in the supernova remnant N132D using Chandra X-ray data and a self-consistent model, revealing velocities of 800–1500 km/s with north rim discrepancies suggesting efficient cosmic ray acceleration, consistent with γ-ray observations.

Multi-messenger detection of black hole binaries in dark matter spikes

Time: 2025-02-21

Authors: Fani Dosopoulou, Joseph Silk

Abstract: The inspiral of a high mass-ratio black hole binary in a galactic nucleus, interacting with a dense dark matter spike around the primary black hole, reduces the spike's self-annihilation-induced γ-ray luminosity by 10–90% as the binary emits gravitational waves detectable by LISA, offering a multi-messenger method to indirectly probe dark matter.

Detection of molecular clouds in the PeVatron candidate source LHAASO J0341$+$5258 by the Nobeyama 45-m radio telescope

Time: 2025-02-21

Authors: Naomi Tsuji, Shunya Takekawa, Kaya Mori, Alison Mitchell, Shuo Zhang, Priyadarshini Bangale, Stephen DiKerby, Tülün Ergin, Jooyun Woo, Samar Safi-Harb, Shinta Kausya

Abstract: A CO survey of the ultra-high-energy gamma-ray source LHAASO J0341+5258 using the NRO 45-m telescope identified five nearby compact molecular clouds that could explain the observed emission via hadronic interactions with TeV-PeV protons from a distant SNR or leptonic processes from a pulsar wind nebula, with one cloud linked to an AGB star ruled out as a PeVatron.

Discovery of a Pulsar Wind Nebula Candidate Associated with the Galactic PeVatron 1LHAASO J0343+5254u

Time: 2025-02-21

Authors: Stephen DiKerby, Shuo Zhang, Tulun Ergin, Naomi Tsuji, Kaya Mori, Fabio Acero, Samar Safi-Harb, Shunya Takekawa, Jooyun Woo

Abstract: Using XMM-Newton observations, researchers discovered a candidate pulsar wind nebula linked to the LHAASO PeVatron J0343+5254u, finding that its multiwavelength emissions are primarily explained by a leptonic model with elevated IR fields, though excess gamma rays may indicate hadronic interactions in nearby molecular clouds, potentially producing detectable astroparticle fluxes.
