Flux tube clustering from magnetic coupling of adjacent type-I and -II superconductors in a neutron star: persistent gravitational radiation

Time: 2025-04-24

Authors: K. H. Thong, A. Melatos

Abstract: This study refines models of adjacent type-I and -II superconducting regions in pulsars by incorporating magnetic repulsion, finding clustered vortices amplify gravitational wave strain via flux trees, potentially reaching current detector sensitivities when sparse flux branching forms large flux trees.

Quantifying jet-interstellar medium interactions in Cyg X-1: Insights from dual-frequency bow shock detection with MeerKAT

Time: 2025-04-24

Authors: P. Atri, S. E. Motta, Jakob van den Eijnden, James H. Matthews, James C. A. Miller-Jones, Rob Fender, David Williams-Baldwin, Ian Heywood, Patrick Woudt

Abstract: Using MeerKAT radio observations, researchers detected and characterized the bow shock formed by Cyg X-1's jets, determining its interstellar medium density (6–7 cm⁻³), jet age (0.04–0.3 Myr), power (2×10³¹–10³⁵ erg/s), velocity (21–364 km/s), and revealing a lower jet energy transport rate than prior estimates through spectral analysis and H-alpha map comparisons.

The stellar corona-chromosphere connection. A comprehensive study of X-ray and Ca II IRT fluxes from eROSITA and Gaia

Time: 2025-04-24

Authors: S. Freund, S. Czesla, B. Fuhrmeister, P. Predehl, J. Robrade, P. C. Schneider, J. H. M. M. Schmitt

Abstract: This study combines X-ray data from eROSITA surveys and Gaia DR3 Ca II infrared triplet (IRT) measurements to analyze stellar activity correlations, revealing stronger X-ray–chromospheric links in late-type stars, saturation of both indicators in fast rotators at similar Rossby numbers, and a decline in X-ray/IRT flux ratios for slower rotators, with no distinct flux branches observed.

Time: 2025-04-24

Authors: Gregory Ashton, Ann-Kristin Malz, Nicolo Colombo

Abstract: The abstract describes a machine learning approach that integrates results from multiple gravitational-wave detection pipelines using conformal prediction to deliver calibrated uncertainty quantification, enhancing detection efficiency in simulations and boosting confidence in real-world multi-pipeline detections like the sub-threshold binary neutron star candidate GW200311_103121 within GWTC-3.

A non-uniform metal distribution in the ram-pressure stripped M86 group of galaxies

Time: 2025-04-24

Authors: Sinancan Kara, François Mernier, Norbert Werner, E. Nihal Ercan

Abstract: Using XMM-Newton observations of the ram-pressure-stripped galaxy M86, researchers found its core's chemical composition aligns more with the Universe's hot gas than its stars, indicating supersonic ram-pressure stripping cannot remove the inner hot atmosphere, and suggest primordial gas stripping requires combined ram-pressure and AGN activity, with the plume likely originating from low-entropy group gas via galaxy collisions rather than core stripping.

Time: 2025-04-24

Detection and Characterization of Quasi-Periodic Oscillations in Seyfert Galaxy NGC 4151

Time: 2025-04-24

Authors: Yan Yongkang, Zhang Peng, Lu Zhou, Bao Tong, Liu Dejian, Liu Gaochao, Liu Qingzhong, Yan Jingzhi, Zeng Xiangyun

Abstract: This study detected quasi-periodic oscillations (QPOs) in NGC 4151's X-ray data via Chandra and XMM-Newton observations, revealing the first independently observed QPOs with matched frequencies (∼5.9×10⁻⁴ Hz) over eight years and a combined 5.2σ confidence, linking them to the central black hole's properties and offering insights into X-ray variability mechanisms and mass estimation.
