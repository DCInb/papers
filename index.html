Supermassive black hole in NGC 4649 (M60) with a dark matter halo: Impact on shadow measurements and thermodynamic properties

Time: 2025-05-06

Authors: Francisco S. N. Lobo, Jorde A. A. Ramos, Manuel E. Rodrigues

Abstract: This study investigates black hole solutions within dark matter halos using observational data from NGC 4649 to construct two spacetime models dependent on parameters \(M\), \(V_c\), and \(a\), demonstrating that dark matter modifies geometric (event horizon, curvature, shadow radius) and thermodynamic properties, validated by observational comparisons and highlighting DM's role in both Schwarzschild-extended frameworks.

Understanding large-scale dynamos in unstratified rotating shear flows

Time: 2025-05-06

Authors: Tushar Mondal, Pallavi Bhat, Fatima Ebrahimi, Eric G. Blackman

Abstract: This study combines simulations with new analyses that overcome previous pitfalls to explicate the growth and saturation mechanisms of nonhelical mean-field dynamos in unstratified, magnetorotationally driven turbulence, revealing shear-driven amplification of magnetic fields, turbulent diffusivity effects from Lorentz and Coriolis forces, and saturation via a third-order correlator in the mean electromotive force.

A Systematic Search for X-ray Eclipse Events in Active Galactic Nuclei Observed by Swift

Time: 2025-05-06

Authors: Tianying Lian, Chichuan Jin, Weimin Yuan

Abstract: Using two decades of Swift X-ray Telescope data from 40 active galactic nuclei (AGNs), researchers identified three high-confidence and eight candidate X-ray eclipse events, linking obscuring cloud properties (column densities ~0.2–31.2×10²² cm⁻², ionization log ξ ~-1.3–2.2) to black hole mass and accretion rates, suggesting accretion-driven outflows, and underscoring the future potential of the Einstein Probe for further discoveries.

Variability of the Faraday Rotation Measure in the Parsec-scale Jet of AGN 1633+382

Time: 2025-05-06

Authors: Kah Chun Lai, Juan Carlos Algaba Marcos, Zamri bin Zainal Abidin

Abstract: This study of flat spectrum radio quasar 1633+382's Faraday rotation measure (RM) variability from 2004–2008 using multi-frequency VLBI data reveals a core RM scaling with ν², uncorrelated core-jet RM sign changes suggesting a limited Faraday screen, core variations linked to a new component, and jet RM changes more consistent with jet kinematics than medium interaction or foreground screens, which are disfavored by uniform spectral indices and extreme density/field fluctuations.

A generalized solution for advection-dominated accretion flow, standard disc, and slim disc

Time: 2025-05-06

Authors: Mingjun Liu, B. F. Liu, Yilong Wang, Huaqing Cheng, Weimin Yuan

Abstract: This study presents a generalized axisymmetric height-averaged model that self-consistently incorporates entropy advection, radiation pressure, and photon trapping to unify four accretion solutions—Shakura-Sunyaev thin disc (SSD), Shapiro-Lightman-Eardley (SLE), slim disc, and advection-dominated accretion flow (ADAF)—reproducing their branches across sub- to super-Eddington accretion rates, demonstrating S-curve dynamics in the $\dot{m}-\Sigma$ plane, SSD-slim disc transitions via photon trapping, hybrid disc structures, ADAF-SSD coexistence below a critical accretion rate, and providing luminosity, radiation efficiency, and spectral data for stellar-mass black holes.

Slowly Rotating Neutron Stars in Aether Scalar-Tensor Theory

Time: 2025-05-06

Authors: Christopher Reyes, Jeremy Sakstein

Abstract: The Aether Scalar-Tensor theory, a relativistic gravity model mimicking cold dark matter cosmologically and MOND astrophysically while aligning with cosmic observations, predicts distinct neutron star moment-of-inertia–compactness relations testable via X-ray and gravitational wave data, differing from general relativity.

The Frequency-dependent Modulation Features of PSR J1948+3540

Time: 2025-05-06

Authors: Kaige Chang, Na Wang, Feifei Kou, Wenming Yan, Jianping Yuan, Shijun Dang, Jumei Yao, Vishal Gajjar, Xia Zhou

Abstract: A multi-wavelength study of pulsar J1948+3540 using GMRT and FAST observations revealed a novel, frequency-dependent shift in the dominant intensity modulation component (first half at low frequencies, second half at high frequencies), accompanied by spectral and polarization variations, presenting an unexplained challenge to current theoretical models.

Decoding the cosmological baryonic fluctuations using localized fast radio bursts

Authors: Tzu-Yin Hsu, Tetsuya Hashimoto, Tsung-Ching Yang, Shotaro Yamasaki, Tomotsugu Goto, John Lo, Po-Ya Wang, Yu-Wei Lin, Simon C. -C. Ho, Bjorn Jasper R. Raquel

Abstract: This study statistically links fluctuations in ionized baryons (≲6 Mpc) to galaxy density variations using fast radio burst dispersion measures and galaxy catalogs, addressing the missing baryons problem by demonstrating cosmological-scale correlations.

The long-term optical flux variations of Compact Symmetric Objects

Time: 2025-05-06

Authors: Subhashree Swain, Vaidehi S. Paliya, D. J. Saikia, C. S. Stalin, Arya Venugopal, A. K. Bhavya, C. D. Ravikumar

Abstract: This study comparing the optical variability of 38 Compact Symmetric Objects (CSOs) and 17 blazars using 5-year Zwicky Transient Facility data reveals that CSOs exhibit lower flux variability than blazars—attributed to relativistic jet orientation and beaming effects—but both display similar bluer-when-brighter color trends linked to jet-related processes.

Axion effects on the non-radial oscillations of neutron stars

Time: 2025-05-06

Authors: Deepak Kumar, Hiranmaya Mishra

Abstract: The study finds that axions stabilize hybrid neutron stars by enhancing their quark cores and hyperons, leading to agreement with astrophysical constraints and increased quadrupolar f-mode oscillations, as shown through analyses of the quark-hadron equation of state using the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model and relativistic mean field theory with Gibbs construction.
