Long-Term Multidimensional Models of Core-Collapse Supernovae: Progress and Challenges

Time: 2025-02-20

Authors: H. -Thomas Janka

Abstract: Recent 3D core-collapse supernova simulations have advanced understanding of the neutrino-driven explosion mechanism, successfully reproducing observed supernova energies, neutron-star kicks, and signal predictions, yet uncertainties persist regarding progenitor outcomes, magnetic fields, neutrino interactions, nuclear equations of state, and neutron star spin-kick correlations.

Super-Eddington Accretion in Quasars

Time: 2025-02-20

Authors: P. Marziani, K. Garnica Luna, A. Floris, A. del Olmo, A. Deconto-Machado, T. M. Buendia-Rios, C. A. Negrete, D. Dultzin

Abstract: This review examines super-Eddington accretion in quasars through observational criteria like optical/UV broad-line ratios and extreme Population A traits (high iron emission, extreme outflows, high metallicity), linking these properties to rapid early-universe black hole growth, theoretical Eddington ratios, their potential as cosmological distance indicators via virialized broad-line correlations, and analogies with stellar-mass black hole accretion states.

Constraints on optical and near-infrared variability in the localisation of the long-period radio transient GLEAM-X J1627-52

Time: 2025-02-20

Authors: J. D. Lyman, V. S. Dhillon, S. Kamann, A. A. Chrimes, A. J. Levan, I. Pelisoli, D. T. H. Steeghs, K. Wiersema

Abstract: The enigmatic Galactic radio transient GLEAM-X J1627-52, despite optical, near-infrared, and spectroscopic searches revealing no conclusive evidence for white dwarf pulsars or close binaries (with weak variability and low orbital inclination constraints), remains consistent with isolated magnetars or compact binaries, though dense Galactic environments challenge definitive identification.

Magnetic Thomson Transport in High Opacity Domains

Time: 2025-02-20

Authors: Matthew G. Baring, Kun Hu, Hoa Dinh Thi

Abstract: This paper introduces a novel analytical approach to modeling polarized magnetic Thomson radiative transfer in high-opacity neutron star environments, deriving simplified equations and empirical approximations that enhance simulation efficiency and radiation pressure characterization for applications such as magnetar atmospheres, pulsars, and accreting X-ray binaries.

How does Radiation Reaction Affect Relativistic Magnetized Shocks Emission

Time: 2025-02-20

Authors: Yu Zhang, Yuanpei Yang, Liangliang Ji

Abstract: This study's Particle-In-Cell simulations reveal that radiation reaction in relativistic magnetized shocks alters particle dynamics and emission via the Synchrotron Maser Instability, producing spectral broadening, peak frequency upshift, and enhanced radiation—consistent with observed FRB characteristics like bimodal energy distribution in FRB 121102 and luminosity-bandwidth correlations—thereby supporting magnetized shocks as viable FRB sources.
