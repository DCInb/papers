Association of 220 PeV Neutrino KM3-230213A with Gamma-Ray Bursts

Time: 2025-03-18

Authors: Ruiqi Wang, Bo-Qiang Ma

Abstract: The KM3NeT Collaboration's detection of a 220 PeV neutrino (KM3-230213A) is analyzed for potential associations with gamma-ray bursts (GRBs) through Lorentz invariance violation (LV) without a fixed LV scale, yielding constraints of \(E_{\rm{LV}} \leq 5.3 \times 10^{18}\) GeV (subluminal) and \(E_{\rm{LV}} \leq 5.6 \times 10^{19}\) GeV (superluminal) when considering angular separations up to 5° under the assumption that the neutrino originated from a GRB.

Origin of holes and rings in the Green Monster of Cassiopeia A: Insights from 3D magnetohydrodynamic simulations

Time: 2025-03-18

Authors: S. Orlando, H. -T. Janka, A. Wongwathanarat, F. Bocchino, I. De Looze, D. Milisavljevic, M. Miceli, T. Temim, J. Rho, S. Nagataki, M. Ono, V. Sapienza, E. Greco

Abstract: High-resolution 3D magnetohydrodynamic simulations reveal that Cassiopeia A's "Green Monster" morphology, observed by JWST, arises from dense ejecta clumps and fingers interacting with a circumstellar shell, with radiative cooling and non-equilibrium ionization enhancing fragmentation into observed holes and rings, contrasting hypotheses involving fast-moving ejecta.

Forming a clumpy circumstellar material in energetic pre-supernova activity

Time: 2025-03-18

Authors: Shlomi Hillel, Ron Schreier, Noam Soker

Abstract: Three-dimensional hydrodynamical simulations reveal that pre-supernova energy deposition from vigorous core activity in red supergiants inflates a Rayleigh-Taylor unstable envelope, forming compact, clumpy circumstellar material (CSM) capable of triggering high accretion rates on potential companions and pre-explosion outbursts.

Neutron portal to ultra-high-energy neutrinos

Time: 2025-03-18

Authors: Gustavo F. S. Alves, Matheus Hostert, Maxim Pospelov

Abstract: The study proposes that ultra-high-energy cosmic rays' heavy composition can coexist with a large diffuse flux of cosmogenic neutrinos if new physics enables neutron oscillation into dark neutrons that decay into high-energy neutrinos, though this mechanism does not resolve the tension between the KM3NeT event and IceCube's null results.

Electromagnetic field solver for QED polarization in super-strong magnetic fields of magnetar and laser plasmas

Time: 2025-03-18

Authors: Mahmoud Alawashra, Jan Benáček, Martin Pohl, Mikhail Medvedev

Abstract: This study introduces the first versatile electromagnetic field solver that models quantum electrodynamic (QED) effects beyond the Schwinger limit in super-strongly magnetized plasmas, accurately replicating birefringence in polarization modes and enabling application across diverse simulations, including those relevant to magnetars and laboratory experiments.

Galaxy scale consequences of tidal disruption events: extended emission line regions, extreme coronal lines and infrared-to-optical light echoes

Time: 2025-03-18

Authors: Andrew Mummery, Muryel Guolo, James Matthews, Megan Newsome, Chris Lintott, William Keel

Abstract: Simulations demonstrate that tidal disruption events (TDEs) generate sufficient ionizing radiation to power extended emission line regions (EELRs) through low-density clouds and transient coronal lines through high-density clouds, explaining their observed prevalence in EELR-host galaxies and suggesting some optically classified active galactic nuclei (AGNs) are actually TDEs.
