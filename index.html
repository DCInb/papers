Radio observations point to a moderately relativistic outflow in the fast X-ray transient EP241021a

Time: 2025-05-13

Authors: Muskan Yadav, Eleonora Troja, Roberto Ricci, Yu-Han Yang, Peter Veres, Mark H. Wieringa, Brendan O'Connor, Yacheng Kang, Rosa L. Becerra, Geoffrey Ryan, Malte Busmann

Abstract: Long-term radio observations of the gamma-ray dark fast X-ray transient EP241021a reveal sustained emission and afterglow modeling suggesting either a moderately relativistic collimated outflow in a low-density medium or an off-axis structured jet, with its X-ray flare likely originating from slower ejecta rather than traditional jet-core processes, consistent with standard explosions lacking bright gamma-rays.

Modeling the Light Curve and Spectra of SN 2023aew

Time: 2025-05-13

Authors: Rachid Ouyed

Abstract: The delayed conversion of a neutron star into a quark or hybrid star, injecting thermal energy into supernova ejecta, explains SN 2023aew's dual-peaked light curve and plateau, linking superluminous supernovae, fast blue transients, magnetar formation, and Quantum Chromodynamics implications.

Modelling the multi-wavelength emission and polarisation signatures of the novel white-dwarf pulsar system AR Sco

Time: 2025-05-13

Authors: Louis du Plessis

Abstract: This study developed a general emission code using the Dormand-Prince 8(7) integrator to model AR Scorpii's emission, constraining its white dwarf magnetic field to \( B_{\rm S} = (2.5 - 3.0) \times 10^{8} \, \rm{G} \) via a magnetic mirror scenario, and demonstrated the critical role of particle dynamics in accurately simulating high-field magnetospheric micro-physics.

Dynamics of recaptures, ejections and mergers of stellar mass binaries over multiple encounters with SgrA*

Time: 2025-05-13

Authors: Biancamaria Sersante, Zephyr Penoyre, Elena Maria Rossi

Abstract: The study demonstrates that stellar binaries interacting with a massive black hole via the Hills mechanism predominantly disrupt, with outcomes (disruption, ejection, merger, or survival) depending on orbital parameters like inclination, encounter depth, and eccentricity, while multiple encounters increase disruptions and finite stellar sizes lead to significant merger rates.

Wormholes in 4D Einstein-Gauss-Bonnet gravity with Bose-Einstein Condensated Dark Matter

Time: 2025-05-13

Authors: Bibhash Das, Bikash Chandra Paul

Abstract: The paper presents a stable, spherically symmetric traversable wormhole in 4D Einstein-Gauss-Bonnet gravity coupled with Bose-Einstein condensate dark matter, showing energy conditions are satisfied at the throat for the Gauss-Bonnet parameter \(\alpha \in [-4, -4.222]\) but violated otherwise, with stability at the throat occurring for \(\alpha = -0.0512471\) under specific parameters.

Estimating the spins of supermassive black holes in distant ultraluminous quasars

Time: 2025-05-13

Authors: M. Yu. Piotrovich, S. D. Buliga, T. M. Natsvlishvili

Abstract: The study of extremely distant quasars (6 < z < 7.5) reveals high average black hole spins (>0.9), consistent with other AGNs, and strong parameter correlations, suggesting their supermassive black hole mass growth occurred via rapid disk accretion, which efficiently increased spin in the early universe.

Time: 2025-05-13

Authors: Yue Hu, Alex Lazarian, G. Brunetti, John A. ZuHone

Abstract: This study employs 3D magnetohydrodynamic simulations of galaxy cluster mergers with varying initial plasma β values to reveal that magnetic fields predominantly regulate anisotropic, scale-dependent velocity and magnetic fluctuations—with weaker fields (β=500) reducing anisotropy—while validating the Synchrotron Intensity Gradient method's reliability across magnetization levels.
