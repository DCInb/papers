Deriving the Energy Function of Non-repeaters from CHIME/FRB Baseband Data

Time: 2025-04-18

Authors: Wenqi Ma, Zhifu Gao, Biaopeng Li, Jumei Yao, Fayin Wang

Abstract: This study analyzes CHIME/FRB non-repeating fast radio bursts using the \( V_\mathrm{max} \) method, revealing a Schechter-like energy function at low redshifts and a flatter slope (\( \gamma \approx -2 \)) at high redshifts, with ambiguous redshift evolution trends underscoring the need for more data to clarify their origins.

SRGAJ230631.0+155633: an extremely X-ray luminous, heavily obscured, radio-loud quasar at z=0.44 discovered by SRG/ART-XC

Time: 2025-04-18

Authors: Grigory Uskov, Sergey Sazonov, Igor Lapshov, Alexander Mikhailov, Ekaterina Filippova, Alexander Lutovinov, Ilya Mereminskiy, Maria Mochalina, Andrey Semena, Alexey Tkachenko

Abstract: This study details SRGAJ230631.0+155633, a luminous, obscured (N_H ∼2×10²³ cm⁻²), radio-loud quasar at z=0.4389, exhibiting significant X-ray variability, FRII radio morphology, a ∼1.4×10⁹ M☉ black hole accreting at ∼30% Eddington, and serving as a rare nearby (z=0.5) testbed for understanding obscured AGN dominant in the early Universe.

A two-step strategy to identify episodic sources of gravitational waves and high energy neutrinos in starburst galaxies

Time: 2025-04-18

Authors: M. L. Allen, P. L. Biermann, L. I. Caramete, A. Chieffi, R. Chini, D. Frekers, L. Á. Gergely, B. Harms, I. Jaroschewski, P. S. Joshi, P. P. Kronberg, E. Kun, A. Meli, E. -S. Seo, T. Stanev

Abstract: The abstract summarizes that supermassive and stellar black hole mergers with spin-flips in starburst and normal galaxies, accelerating particles via precessing jets to produce high-energy neutrinos and gravitational waves, may dominate over AGNs in ultra-high-energy particle fluxes observed on Earth, contributing to cosmic backgrounds and requiring luminosity and flux density-based detection strategies.

Geometric and Thermodynamic Properties of Frolov Black Holes with Topological Defects

Time: 2025-04-17

Authors: Faizuddin Ahmed, Ahmad Al-Badawi, İzzet Sakallı

Abstract: The study examines a modified Frolov black hole model integrating a global monopole and cosmic string, revealing parameter-dependent effects on thermodynamics (entropy deviating from area law), shadow size (reduced by regularization parameters, enlarged by defects), and quasinormal modes, with observational implications for testing modified gravity in strong-field regimes.

Cosmological Evolution of Fast Radio Bursts and The Star Formation Rate

Time: 2025-04-17

Authors: Sujay Champati, Vahé Petrosian

Abstract: This study uses non-parametric methods to correct observational biases and redshift uncertainties in Fast Radio Bursts (FRBs), finding significant luminosity evolution, a broken power-law luminosity function, and a redshift-declining comoving density rate akin to short gamma-ray bursts (linked to compact mergers), supporting magnetars as FRB progenitors.

Revealing the Accretion Flow in M87*: Insights from Faraday Rotation

Time: 2025-04-17

Authors: Constanza Echiburú-Trujillo, Jason Dexter

Abstract: This study models M87's accretion flow as Faraday-thick with high rotation measures (∼10⁶ rad m⁻²) and depolarization via polarization analysis of its counter-jet at 43 GHz, finding a persistent linear polarization-wavelength relationship that, when compared to the forward-jet, enables constraints on electron density, magnetic field strength, and mass accretion rate.

Sensitivity of BEACON to Ultra-High Energy Diffuse and Transient Neutrinos

Time: 2025-04-17

Authors: Andrew Zeolla, Jaime Alvarez-Muñiz, Sergio Cabana-Freire, Washington Carvalho Jr., Austin Cummings, Cosmin Deaconu, Jackson Hinkel, Kaeli Hughes, Ryan Krebs, Youwei Liu, Zachary Martin, Katharine Mulrey, Alisa Nozdrina, Eric Oberla, Steven Prohira, Andrés Romero-Wolf, Abigail G. Vieregg, Stephanie A. Wissel, Enrique Zas

Abstract: The BEACON detector concept employs mountain-based phased radio arrays to detect ultra-high energy neutrinos via radio emissions from tau neutrino-induced air showers, achieving sensitivity to transient sources (short/long duration) and surpassing existing experiments by tenfold in cosmogenic flux sensitivity with 1000 stations, enabling discovery of the highest-energy neutrino sources.

Cosmological Parameters Estimate from Persistent Radio Sources of Fast Radio Bursts

Time: 2025-04-17

Authors: Zi-Liang Zhang, Bing Zhang

Abstract: This study presents a novel cosmological method using fast radio bursts (FRBs) and their persistent radio sources (PRSs) via the Yang relation ($L_{\nu} \propto |\mathrm{RM}|$) to address parameter degeneracies in traditional dispersion measure approaches, employing a two-stage MCMC analysis to derive a preliminary Hubble constant constraint of $H_0 = 75 \pm 30~\mathrm{km\,s^{-1}\,Mpc^{-1}}$ from six observed PRS systems, suggesting the Yang relation's potential as a new FRB cosmological probe with future refinements.

Performance of the advanced gamma-ray trigger system for the High Energy Cosmic Radiation Detection (HERD) facility

Time: 2025-04-17

Authors: Keerthana Rajan Lathika

Abstract: The High Energy Cosmic-Radiation Detection (HERD) facility, scheduled for installation on China's Space Station around 2027, will investigate dark matter, cosmic-ray spectra up to the knee, and all-sky gamma rays above 100 MeV using a large-acceptance design with a 3D imaging calorimeter and multiple subdetectors, including an advanced ultra-low-energy gamma-ray trigger system validated in CERN beam tests in 2023.
