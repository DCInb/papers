Predicting the outcome of collisional neutrino flavor conversion

Time: 2025-05-22

Authors: Julien Froustey

Abstract: The study derives the first analytical expressions for the asymptotic states of collisional flavor instabilities in dense astrophysical environments like neutron star mergers and supernovae, highlighting the role of collision terms in achieving a quantum equilibrium with nonzero coherence and enabling subgrid models to incorporate flavor oscillations into global simulations.

Neutron Star crust informed by nuclear structure data

Time: 2025-05-22

Authors: Pietro Klausner, Marco Antonelli, Francesca Gulminelli

Abstract: This study conducts a Bayesian analysis of neutron star equations of state using Skyrme functionals, incorporating a consistent multidimensional treatment of nuclear matter parameters and correlations (bulk, surface, spin-orbit, effective mass) to identify models satisfying astrophysical constraints, revealing increased neutron star surface thickness and crustal moment of inertia compared to prior work.

A NuSTAR study of quasi-periodic oscillations from the ultraluminous X-ray sources in M82

Time: 2025-05-22

Authors: Hamza El Byad, Matteo Bachetti, Silvia Columbu, Giuseppe Rodriguez, Maura Pilia, Matthew J. Middleton, Dominic J Walton, Murray Brightman, Hannah Earnshaw, Karl Forster, Brian Grefenstette, Felix Fürst, Marianne Heida, Matteo Imbrogno, Eleonora Veronica Lai, Thomas Maccarone

Abstract: The study of M82 X-1's quasi-periodic oscillations (20–300 mHz) using 39 NuSTAR observations from 2014–2024 reveals behavior akin to other accreting compact objects and identifies a frequent broad noise component, advancing insights into accretion physics around potential intermediate-mass black holes in ultraluminous X-ray sources.

Periodic Variability in Space Photometry of 181 New Supermassive Black Hole Binary Candidates

Time: 2025-05-22

Authors: Pablo Huijse, Jordy Davelaar, Joris De Ridder, Nicholas Jannsen, Conny Aerts

Abstract: Researchers discovered 181 new supermassive black hole binary candidates using Gaia data and a Bayesian framework to distinguish periodic signals from stochastic variability, nearly doubling the known population and providing key targets for future gravitational wave and galaxy evolution studies.

Inferring neutron star merger ejecta morphologies with kilonovae

Time: 2025-05-22

Authors: Brendan L. King, Soumi De, Oleg Korobkin, Michael W. Coughlin, Peter T. H. Pang

Ray-tracing GR-MHD-generated Outflows from AGNs Hosting Thin Accretion Disks: An Analysis Approaching Horizon Scales

Time: 2025-05-22

Authors: Bidisha Bandyopadhyay, Christian Fendt, Dominik R. G. Schleicher, Neil M. Nagar, Felipe Agurto-Sepulveda, Javier Pedreros

Abstract: This study employs resistive GR-MHD simulations with thin Keplerian disks to reveal that low-mass supermassive black holes (SMBHs) appear optically thicker at 230 GHz due to synchrotron self-absorption, accentuating outflow emissions near the event horizon—though challenging to resolve with current EHT resolution—while high-mass SMBHs remain optically thinner, better suited for probing general relativistic effects like photon rings.

Sequential simulation-based inference for extreme mass ratio inspirals

Time: 2025-05-22

Authors: Philippa S. Cole, James Alvey, Lorenzo Speri, Christoph Weniger, Uddipta Bhardwaj, Davide Gerosa, Gianfranco Bertone

Abstract: This study demonstrates that sequential simulation-based inference, particularly truncated marginal neural ratio estimation, effectively addresses the challenges of analyzing extreme mass-ratio inspirals for space-based gravitational-wave detectors like LISA by reducing the 11-dimensional parameter search space by a factor of \(10^6-10^7\), providing marginal proposal distributions, and outlining its role within a broader future data analysis strategy despite current limitations.

Fast Low Energy Reconstruction using Convolutional Neural Networks

Time: 2025-05-22

Authors: IceCube Collaboration

Abstract: This paper discusses the development and application of convolutional neural networks (CNNs) by IceCube-DeepCore to reconstruct sub-100 GeV atmospheric neutrino interactions and estimate their properties, enabling precise neutrino oscillation measurements despite challenges posed by sparse detector instrumentation and the glacial ice medium.

High angular resolution near-ultraviolet polarization imaging of the Herbig Ae/Be star LK-Hα-233

Time: 2025-05-22

Authors: F. Marin

Abstract: Using archival Hubble Space Telescope polarimetric data, this study of Herbig Ae/Be star LK-Hα233 revealed a circumstellar disk and hollow, X-shaped bipolar outflows scattering starlight, emphasizing the critical role of high-resolution polarimetry in understanding young stellar environments and advocating for future instruments.
