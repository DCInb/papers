Possibility of Multi-Messenger Observations of Quasi-Periodic Eruptions with X-rays and Gravitational Waves

Time: 2025-05-15

Authors: Tomoya Suzuguchi, Hidetoshi Omiya, Hiroki Takeda

Abstract: The study finds that quasi-periodic eruptions (QPEs), likely caused by stellar-mass objects crossing a supermassive black hole's accretion disk, have minimal overlap in X-ray and gravitational wave detection sensitivity (X-rays effective below ~1 mHz, LISA above), predicting at most one joint detection during LISA's mission, but extending gravitational wave sensitivity to sub-millihertz frequencies could enhance multi-messenger potential by an order of magnitude.

Searching for Accreting Compact Binaries in eROSITA DR1

Time: 2025-05-15

Authors: Hao-Bin Liu, Wei-Min Gu, Yongqi Lu, Teng Liu, Jin-Zhong Liu

Abstract: Researchers identified 22 accreting compact binary candidates, including two known systems, by combining Gaia astrometry, ZTF photometry, and eROSITA DR1 data, utilizing X-ray-to-optical flux ratios, optical colors, and short-period light curves to exclude AGNs, demonstrating a scalable method for future galactic surveys.

A possible periodic RM evolution in the repeating FRB 20220529

Time: 2025-05-15

Authors: Yi-Fang Liang, Ye Li, Zhen-Fan Tang, Xuan Yang, Song-Bo Zhang, Yuan-Pei Yang, Fa-Yin Wang, Bao Wang, Di Xiao, Qing Zhao, Jun-Jie Wei, Jin-Jun Geng, Jia-Rui Niu, Jun-Shuo Zhang, Guo Chen, Min Fang, Xue-Feng Wu, Zi-Gao Dai, Wei-Wei Zhu, Peng Jiang, Bing Zhang

Abstract: A three-year FAST monitoring study of repeating FRB~20220529 revealed a potential ∼200-day period in its rotation measure (RM) evolution (4.2σ and 3.5σ significance via Lomb-Scargle and phase-folding methods), supporting a binary origin hypothesis due to RM variations, while also examining burst rate periodicity.

Generation of magnetic fields around black hole accretion discs due to non conservative radiation fields

Time: 2025-05-15

Authors: Mukesh Kumar Vyas, Asaf Pe'er

Abstract: This study demonstrates that magnetic fields generated by the rotational radiation field's curl in black hole accretion discs grow over astrophysical timescales, remaining weak (~few Gauss) in standard discs but reaching dynamically significant strengths (up to 10⁵ Gauss) with a luminous corona, influencing disc/jet dynamics and observational signatures, with future simulations and high-resolution observations poised to validate these findings.

SMIET: Fast and accurate synthesis of radio pulses from extensive air shower using simulated templates

Time: 2025-05-15

Authors: Mitja Desmet, Keito Watanabe, Tim Huege, Stijn Buitink

Abstract: The study presents a computationally efficient hybrid method called template synthesis, implemented in the Python package SMIET, which rapidly synthesizes radio emission from cosmic-ray air showers by rescaling and adjusting phases of sliced shower simulations, achieving high accuracy (4–6% scatter in amplitudes) and applicability across diverse scenarios, including machine-learning analyses, when benchmarked against Monte-Carlo simulations.

S241125n: Binary Black Hole Merger Produces Short GRB in AGN Disk

Time: 2025-05-15

Authors: Shu-Rui Zhang, Yu Wang, Ye-Fei Yuan, Hiromichi Tagawa, Yun-Feng Wei, Liang Li, Rong-Gen Cai

Abstract: The gravitational wave event S241125n, associated with a short gamma-ray burst and X-ray afterglow, is modeled as a binary black hole merger in an active galactic nucleus disk, where hyper-Eddington accretion generates a jet producing the GRB, with spectral features indicative of AGN environmental interactions and absorption, highlighting the need for host galaxy identification and infrared observations to test the model.

Confronting recent light compact star observations with color-flavor locked quark matter

Time: 2025-05-15

Authors: K. Kourmpetis, P. Laskos-Patkos, Ch. C. Moustakidis

Abstract: This study employs the color-flavor locked (CFL) MIT bag model to reconcile the low masses and differing radii of the HESS J1731-347 remnant and PSR J1231-1411 pulsar, demonstrating that absolute stability of strange quark matter with specific superconducting gap (Δ) and bag parameter (B) values aligns with observations, while hybrid star models require HESS J1731-347 to be a slow stable hybrid star to avoid conflicts with massive star constraints.

Implications of pulsar timing arrays for Gauss-Bonnet Inflation

Time: 2025-05-15

Authors: Reginald Christian Bernardo, Seoktae Koh, Gansukh Tumurtushaa

Abstract: Pulsar timing array observations support Gauss-Bonnet inflationary models by showing compatibility with a blue-tilted gravitational wave spectrum, requiring the inflaton to climb its potential before descending, consistent with current data.

Prediction of radio-quiet gamma-ray pulsar distances using the Fundamental Plane relation

Time: 2025-05-15

Authors: Ekrem Oğuzhan Angüner

Abstract: This study demonstrates that machine learning combined with the fundamental plane relation effectively predicts distances for 62 radio-quiet gamma-ray pulsars lacking traditional measurements, validates the approach through alignment with known data and theoretical models favoring curvature radiation, and establishes a promising framework for future improvements with enhanced datasets and refined spectral parameters.
