Cosmological Parameters Estimate from Persistent Radio Sources of Fast Radio Bursts

Time: 2025-04-17

Authors: Zi-Liang Zhang, Bing Zhang

Abstract: This study introduces a novel method to constrain the Hubble constant (\(H_0 = 75 \pm 30~\mathrm{km\,s^{-1}\,Mpc^{-1}}\)) by combining fast radio bursts (FRBs) and their persistent radio sources via the Yang relation, employing a two-stage MCMC analysis to address degeneracies among \(H_0\), baryon density (\(\Omega_b\)), and intergalactic medium baryon fraction (\(f_{\mathrm{IGM}}\)), while suggesting future refinements to enhance cosmological probing.

Performance of the advanced gamma-ray trigger system for the High Energy Cosmic Radiation Detection (HERD) facility

Time: 2025-04-17

Authors: Keerthana Rajan Lathika

Abstract: The High Energy Cosmic-Radiation Detection (HERD) facility, a large-acceptance telescope with a central 3D imaging calorimeter and complementary subdetectors, will launch on China's Space Station around 2027 to study dark matter, cosmic rays up to the knee, and all-sky gamma rays above 100 MeV, supported by an optimized ultra-low-energy gamma-ray trigger system validated in CERN beam tests in 2023.

A Novel View on the Inner Crusts of Neo-Neutron Stars: exotic light nuclei, diffusional and thermodynamical stability

Time: 2025-04-17

Authors: Mikhail V. Beznogov, Adriana R. Raduta

Abstract: This study investigates the properties of young neutron stars' non-accreted crusts using an extended nuclear statistical equilibrium model, revealing a deep inner crust layer dominated by nearly pure ¹⁴He (challenging nuclear pasta phases), demonstrating crust stability against ion diffusion, and confirming thermodynamic stability of subsaturated matter, which precludes a first-order phase transition between inhomogeneous and homogeneous matter.

Hunting for newborn magnetars: a multi-messenger approach

Time: 2025-04-17

Authors: Dafne Guetta, Simone Dall'Osso

Abstract: This numerical study demonstrates that while gravitational wave emissions from magnetars have a negligible impact on shock break-out light-curves, they distinctly imprint supernova light-curves after 20-30 days, with future ULTRASAT observations and the Einstein Telescope enabling multi-messenger (UV+GW) detections of magnetar formations at a rate exceeding one event biennially.

Testing the Star-disk Collision Model for Quasi-periodic Eruptions

Time: 2025-04-17

Authors: Wenyuan Guo, Rong-Feng Shen

Abstract: The star-disk collision model for Quasi-periodic eruptions (QPEs) is challenged as most sources require implausibly large stellar radii or are incompatible, except two needing solar-sized stars, while predicted temperatures are an order of magnitude lower than observed.

Taming systematics in distance and inclination measurements with gravitational waves: role of the detector network and higher-order modes

Time: 2025-04-16

Authors: Adriano Frattale Mascioli, Francesco Crescimbeni, Costantino Pacilio, Paolo Pani, Francesco Pannarale

Abstract: The Einstein Telescope can effectively mitigate the distance-inclination correlation in gravitational-wave parameter estimation, particularly for asymmetric-mass compact binaries, by leveraging higher-order waveform modes, detector network alignment, and sensitivity, as demonstrated through Bayesian analysis of current and future detector networks.

Data-driven extraction, phenomenology and modeling of eccentric harmonics in binary black hole merger waveforms

Time: 2025-04-16

Authors: Tousif Islam, Tejaswi Venumadhav, Ajit Kumar Mehta, Isha Anantpurkar, Digvijay Wadekar, Javier Roulet, Jonathan Mushkin, Barak Zackay, Matias Zaldarriaga

Abstract: This paper introduces a data-driven framework using singular-value decomposition (SVD) and post-Newtonian insights to extract eccentricity-induced gravitational wave harmonics with monotonic amplitudes and frequencies, establishes their phase/frequency as linear combinations of secular ($j\phi_{\lambda}/f_{\lambda}$) and eccentric ($\phi_{\rm ecc}/f_{\rm ecc}$) components, provides analytical fits based on mean anomaly, and offers the modular **gwMiner** package to enhance efficient modeling of eccentric binary mergers.

Modeling Supernova 2023ixf: Lightcurve Degeneracies and Morphological Differences

Time: 2025-04-16

Authors: Shannon Forde, Jared A. Goldberg

Abstract: Supernova 2023ixf, the closest type IIP supernova observed in a decade in galaxy Messier 101, challenges standard stellar evolution models, with lightcurve analysis via MESA+STELLA simulations favoring a high-mass progenitor with significant pre-explosion mass loss and minimal H-rich ejecta.

gwharmone: first data-driven surrogate for eccentric harmonics in binary black hole merger waveforms

Time: 2025-04-16

Authors: Tousif Islam, Tejaswi Venumadhav, Ajit Kumar Mehta, Isha Anantpurkar, Digvijay Wadekar, Javier Roulet, Jonathan Mushkin, Barak Zackay, Matias Zaldarriaga

Abstract: The first data-driven surrogate model, gwharmone, accurately predicts eccentric harmonics and radiation in non-spinning binary black hole mergers using 173 waveforms, leverages SVD and GPR for efficiency (~0.1s evaluation), achieves low errors (<0.01 mismatch), and enables rapid eccentric merger analysis for gravitational-wave detection, with public release planned via gwModels.
