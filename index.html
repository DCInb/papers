Flux tube clustering from magnetic coupling of adjacent type-I and -II superconductors in a neutron star: persistent gravitational radiation

Time: 2025-04-24

Authors: K. H. Thong, A. Melatos

Abstract: This study refines models of adjacent type-I and -II superconductors in neutron stars by incorporating magnetic repulsion, showing that clustered flux tubes and pinned vortices amplify gravitational wave strain by a factor \((1 + N_{\rm v,t})^{1/2}\), potentially making emissions detectable by current interferometric detectors when flux branching forms few large flux trees.

Quantifying jet-interstellar medium interactions in Cyg X-1: Insights from dual-frequency bow shock detection with MeerKAT

Time: 2025-04-24

Authors: P. Atri, S. E. Motta, Jakob van den Eijnden, James H. Matthews, James C. A. Miller-Jones, Rob Fender, David Williams-Baldwin, Ian Heywood, Patrick Woudt

Abstract: This study utilized MeerKAT radio observations to detect and characterize the bow shock formed by Cyg X-1's jets, determining an interstellar medium density of 6-7 cm⁻³, jet age of 0.04–0.3 Myr, power of 2×10³¹–10³⁵ erg/s, and revealing a lower jet energy transport rate than previous estimates.

The stellar corona-chromosphere connection. A comprehensive study of X-ray and Ca II IRT fluxes from eROSITA and Gaia

Time: 2025-04-24

Authors: S. Freund, S. Czesla, B. Fuhrmeister, P. Predehl, J. Robrade, P. C. Schneider, J. H. M. M. Schmitt

Abstract: This study combines eROSITA X-ray data and Gaia DR3 Ca II infrared triplet indices to analyze over 43,000 stars, revealing that coronal and chromospheric emissions strongly correlate in late-type stars, with both activity indicators saturating at similar Rossby numbers for fast rotators and their flux ratio decreasing in slower rotators, while the relationship between X-ray and IRT fluxes evolves without the distinct branches previously reported.

Enhancing gravitational-wave detection: a machine learning pipeline combination approach with robust uncertainty quantification

Time: 2025-04-24

Authors: Gregory Ashton, Ann-Kristin Malz, Nicolo Colombo

Abstract: This study introduces a machine learning method that integrates results from multiple gravitational-wave detection pipelines using conformal prediction to provide calibrated uncertainties, demonstrating enhanced detection efficiency in simulations and increased confidence in multi-pipeline detections like GW200311_103121 in GWTC-3 data.

A non-uniform metal distribution in the ram-pressure stripped M86 group of galaxies

Time: 2025-04-24

Authors: Sinancan Kara, François Mernier, Norbert Werner, E. Nihal Ercan

Abstract: The XMM-Newton study of M86's hot gas shows that supersonic ram-pressure stripping alone cannot strip the galaxy's inner atmosphere, requiring combined AGN activity, and suggests its plume likely originated from a galaxy collision rather than core stripping, based on elevated Mg/Fe ratios.

Atemporality from Conservation Laws of Physics in Lorentzian-Euclidean Black Hole

Time: 2025-04-24

Authors: Silvia De Bianchi, Salvatore Capozziello, Emmanuele Battista

Abstract: The paper explores the philosophical implications of "atemporality"—a mechanism where transitioning from Lorentzian to Euclidean regions in black holes prevents singularities by rendering time imaginary—and demonstrates its inherent connection to conservation laws.

Authors: Gokul P. Srinivasaragavan, Hamid Hamidani, Genevieve Schroeder, Nikhil Sarin, Anna Y. Q. Ho, Anthony L. Piro, S. Bradley Cenko, Shreya Anand, Jesper Sollerman, Daniel A. Perley, Keiichi Maeda, Brendan O'Connor, Hanindyo Kuncarayakti, M. Coleman Miller, Tomás Ahumada, Jada Vail, Paul Duffell, Ranadeep Ghosh Dastidar, Igor Andreoni, Aleksandra Bochenek, Seán J. Brennan, Jonathan Carney, Ping Chen, James Freeburn, Avishay Gal-Yam, Wynn Jacobson-Galán, Mansi M. Kasliwal, Jiaxuan Li, Maggie L. Li, Niharika Sravan, Daniel E. Warshofsky

Abstract: The study presents multi-wavelength observations of EP250108a/SN 2025kg, a broad-line Type Ic supernova with a double-peaked optical light curve and X-ray/radio properties akin to low-luminosity GRB 060218, favoring circumstellar medium interaction or a choked jet in extended material to explain its emission, underscoring the importance of early monitoring in probing pre-supernova stellar environments.

New insights on supernova remnants and HII regions in M82

Time: 2025-04-24

Authors: D. Williams-Baldwin, G. Lucatelli, T. W. B. Muxlow, R. J. Beswick, S. W. Shungube, R. Lumpkin-Robbins, M. K. Argo, D. M. Fenech, N. Kimani, J. Radcliffe

Abstract: The nearby starburst galaxy M82, a prime laboratory for studying stellar evolution and the interstellar medium due to its numerous supernova remnants, HII regions, and transients, has seen advancements in VLBI techniques over the past decade, enabling deeper imaging, in-band spectral index mapping, and wider-field observations to discover new radio sources while leveraging its proximity (3.6 Mpc) for high-resolution, decadal-scale studies.

Prospective sensitivity of CTA on detection of evaporating primordial black holes

Time: 2025-04-24

Authors: Chen Yang, Xin Zhang

Abstract: The Cherenkov Telescope Array (CTA) could detect gamma-ray bursts from evaporating primordial black holes (PBHs) with initial mass ~5×10¹⁴ g, potentially excluding a local burst rate density above ~36 pc⁻³ yr⁻¹ over 5 years—improving sensitivity tenfold over previous limits—while proposing an optimized observation strategy.

Detection and Characterization of Quasi-Periodic Oscillations in Seyfert Galaxy NGC 4151

Time: 2025-04-24

Authors: Yan Yongkang, Zhang Peng, Lu Zhou, Bao Tong, Liu Dejian, Liu Gaochao, Liu Qingzhong, Yan Jingzhi, Zeng Xiangyun

Abstract: This study detected quasi-periodic oscillations (QPOs) at ~5.9×10⁻⁴ Hz in NGC 4151's X-ray emissions using Chandra (2007) and XMM-Newton (2015) data, with combined 5.2σ confidence, linking them to the central supermassive black hole's properties and offering insights into X-ray variability mechanisms and black hole mass estimation.
