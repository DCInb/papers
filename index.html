Baryon asymmetry constraints on magnetic field from the Electroweak epoch

Time: 2025-04-10

Authors: T. Boyer, A. Neronov

Abstract: The decay of helical hypermagnetic fields during the Electroweak epoch, constrained to strengths as low as 1e-5 of the maximum possible with correlation lengths at least ~1e-6 of the Hubble radius, could generate the observed baryon asymmetry, leave relic intergalactic magnetic fields up to 10-100 pG affecting recombination and the Hubble tension, and avoid overproduction or problematic isocurvature perturbations.

Deflection angle in the strong deflection limit of axisymmetric spacetimes: local curvature, matter fields, and quasinormal modes

Time: 2025-04-10

Authors: Takahisa Igata

Abstract: This study investigates photon deflection in strong gravity near compact objects, showing that the logarithmic divergence of the deflection angle at critical impact parameters is determined by coordinate-invariant curvature (via the Weyl tensor) and matter contributions (via the Einstein tensor), while connecting these results to quasinormal modes in the eikonal limit to establish a unified framework linking lensing observables, spacetime geometry, and matter distribution.

Orbit design for mitigating interstellar scattering effects in Earth-space VLBI observations of Sgr A*

Time: 2025-04-10

Authors: Aditya Tamar, Ben Hudson, Daniel C. M. Palumbo

Abstract: This study examines how interstellar medium turbulence affects the Black Hole Explorer (BHEX) mission's ability to detect Sagittarius A*'s photon ring, proposing orbit migration via chemical or electric propulsion to optimize fuel, time, and resolution trade-offs for isolating spacetime features and analyzing the ring's morphology.

Sculpting the outer edge of accretion disks in pre-circumbinary binary black hole systems

Time: 2025-04-10

Authors: Fabien Casse, Peggy Varniere, Léna Arthur, Fabrice Dodu

Abstract: This study uses 2D hydrodynamical simulations to demonstrate that a secondary black hole in a pre-circumbinary disk binary system gravitationally alters the primary's accretion disk, inducing a reduced outer radius (linked to mass ratio), spiral arms, and an elliptical edge, providing observable signatures to identify pre-CBD binary black holes.

Resummation of Universal Tails in Gravitational Waveforms

Time: 2025-04-10

Authors: Mikhail M. Ivanov, Yue-Zhou Li, Julio Parra-Martinez, Zihan Zhou

Abstract: The authors present a universal formula for the anomalous scaling of multipole moments in classical general relativity, derived via effective field theory methods connecting renormalized angular momentum and gravitational wave scattering phase shifts, applicable to diverse compact objects and enabling improved resummation of gravitational waveforms for enhanced signal modeling.

Horizons, throats and bounces in hybrid metric-Palatini gravity with a non-zero potential

Time: 2025-04-10

Authors: Gabriel I. Róis, José Tarciso S. S. Junior, Francisco S. N. Lobo, Manuel E. Rodrigues

Abstract: This study explores exact electrically charged solutions, including traversable wormholes, black holes, and black bounces, in hybrid metric-Palatini gravity with a non-zero scalar potential, using an inverse problem technique in Jordan and Einstein frames to reveal diverse structures like black universes and multi-horizon black holes, demonstrating HMPG's expanded gravitational solutions beyond General Relativity for modeling extreme astrophysical environments.

The effect of background matter on the spin oscillations of neutrinos scattered by the supermassive black hole

Time: 2025-04-10

Authors: Mridupawan Deka, Maxim Dvornikov

Abstract: This study investigates neutrino spin oscillations in relativistic matter within accretion disks around spinning Kerr black holes, analyzing co- and counter-rotating disk scenarios through numerical simulations of gravitationally scattered neutrinos and discussing the resultant dynamics.

Symmetry energy dependence of the bulk viscosity of nuclear matter

Time: 2025-04-10

Authors: Yumu Yang, Mauricio Hippert, Enrico Speranza, Jorge Noronha

Abstract: The study demonstrates that the weak-interaction-driven bulk viscosity and relaxation time in neutrino-transparent neutron-star matter at saturation density are determined by the nuclear symmetry energy (\(S\)) and its slope (\(L\)), with experimental uncertainties in \(L\) causing orders-of-magnitude variations in viscosity, suggesting gravitational-wave observations of binary neutron star mergers could help constrain \(S\) and \(L\).

How to identify the object with mass range of $(2.2-3)M_\odot$ in the merger of compact star systems

Time: 2025-04-10

Authors: ZhaoWei Du, HouJun Lü, Xiaoxuan Liu, XiLong Fan, EnWei Liang

Abstract: This paper proposes a method to identify whether compact objects in the 2.2-3 solar mass range are low-mass black holes or massive neutron stars by analyzing electromagnetic emission patterns (e.g., luminosity scaling during inspiral and kilonova brightness post-merger) and highlights the role of high-spin black holes in enabling tidal disruption events, though current telescopes may detect such weak precursor signals only if the merger occurs nearby.
