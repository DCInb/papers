Long-Term Multidimensional Models of Core-Collapse Supernovae: Progress and Challenges

Time: 2025-02-20

Authors: H. -Thomas Janka

Abstract: Recent 3D core-collapse supernova simulations have advanced understanding of the neutrino-driven explosion mechanism by reproducing observed features like energies, neutron-star kicks, and isotopes while predicting neutrino and gravitational-wave signals, yet uncertainties persist regarding progenitor models, magnetic fields, neutrino flavor conversion, nuclear equation of state, unresolved observational puzzles, and neutron-star spin-kick correlations.

Super-Eddington Accretion in Quasars

Time: 2025-02-20

Authors: P. Marziani, K. Garnica Luna, A. Floris, A. del Olmo, A. Deconto-Machado, T. M. Buendia-Rios, C. A. Negrete, D. Dultzin

Abstract: This review examines super-Eddington accretion in quasars, highlighting identification via optical/UV criteria and extreme features like high iron emission and outflows, supported by early-universe black hole growth evidence and accretion theory predictions, suggesting their potential as cosmological distance indicators and drawing parallels to stellar-mass black hole accretion processes.

Constraints on optical and near-infrared variability in the localisation of the long-period radio transient GLEAM-X J1627-52

Time: 2025-02-20

Authors: J. D. Lyman, V. S. Dhillon, S. Kamann, A. A. Chrimes, A. J. Levan, I. Pelisoli, D. T. H. Steeghs, K. Wiersema

Abstract: The study of the enigmatic Galactic radio transient GLEAM-X J1627-52 (18-minute period) using optical/near-infrared imaging and spectroscopy found no evidence supporting white dwarf pulsar scenarios but suggests isolated magnetars or compact binaries as viable explanations, despite observational challenges in dense environments.

Magnetic Thomson Transport in High Opacity Domains

Time: 2025-02-20

Authors: Matthew G. Baring, Kun Hu, Hoa Dinh Thi

Abstract: This paper presents a new analytical approach to modeling polarized magnetic Thomson radiative transfer in high-opacity neutron star environments, deriving key parameters through numerical solutions that enhance simulation efficiency and radiation pressure specification, with applications to magnetars, pulsars, and accretion columns in X-ray binaries.

How does Radiation Reaction Affect Relativistic Magnetized Shocks Emission

Time: 2025-02-20

Authors: Yu Zhang, Yuanpei Yang, Liangliang Ji

Abstract: This study demonstrates through Particle-In-Cell simulations that radiation reaction effects in relativistic magnetized shocks alter coherent cyclotron motion via the Synchrotron Maser Instability, producing FRB observational signatures like spectral broadening, peak frequency upshift, and energy correlations, thereby supporting magnetized shocks as a viable FRB source.

MOJAVE -- XXII. Brightness temperature distributions and geometric profiles along parsec-scale AGN jets

Time: 2025-02-20

Authors: E. V. Kravchenko, I. N. Pashchenko, D. C. Homan, Y. Y. Kovalev, M. L. Lister, A. B. Pushkarev, E. Ros, T. Savolainen

Abstract: This study of 447 AGN jets using 15 GHz VLBA observations and 2D Gaussian modeling reveals jet components expand conically (R ∝ r^1.02) with brightness temperatures declining as Tb ∝ r^-2.82, where non-monotonic profiles in half the sources are explained by shocks, helical structures, or magneto-hydrodynamic acceleration, supporting optically thin moving blobs and Tb gradient changes during jet shape transitions.

A possible overall scenario for the outburst evolution of MAXI J1820+070 revealed by Insight-HXMT

Time: 2025-02-20

Authors: J. Q. Peng, S. Zhang, Y. P. Chen, L. D. Kong, P. J. Wang, S. N. Zhang, L. Ji, L. Tao, J. L. Qu, M. Y. Ge, Q. C. Shui, J. Li, Z. Chang, Z. S. Li, Y. X. Xiao

Abstract: The study of MAXI J1820+070's 2018 outburst using Insight-HXMT data reveals a reversal in energy-dependent photon lags between outburst phases, reconfiguring the canonical 'q'-shaped hardness-intensity diagram into a linear form and suggesting evolving corona dynamics through spectral and temporal analyses.

The Thousand-Pulsar-Array programme on MeerKAT XVII: Discovery of beating radio emission variability in PSR J1514-4834

Time: 2025-02-20

Authors: J. A. Hsu, P. Weltevrede, G. Wright, M. J. Keith, L. S. Oswald, X. Song, H. Wang

Abstract: This study analyzes PSR J1514-4834's complex subpulse modulation using TPA and MeerKAT data, revealing coexisting periodicities that form a beat system confirmed by a novel phase-correlation methodology, challenging existing pulsar emission models and suggesting time-delayed magnetospheric interactions, with implications for uncovering similar patterns in other pulsars.

Do Short GRBs Exhibit an Anticorrelation between Their Intrinsic Duration and Redshift?

Time: 2025-02-20

Authors: Ali M. Hasan, Walid J. Azzam

Abstract: This study confirms, using expanded datasets, that long gamma-ray bursts (LGRBs) exhibit an anticorrelation between intrinsic duration and redshift, while short gamma-ray bursts (SGRBs) do not, suggesting evolving metallicity's role in this relationship.

Dragging of inertial frames in the composed Kerr-Newman-orbiting-ring system

Time: 2025-02-20

Authors: Shahar Hod

Abstract: The study shows that in a Kerr-Newman-black-hole-orbiting-ring system, the frame-dragging-induced change in horizon angular velocity, ΔΩ_H, explicitly depends on the central black hole’s angular momentum (J_H), contrasting the previously observed spin-independent relation in Kerr-ring systems.
