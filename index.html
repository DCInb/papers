Multimodality in the search for new physics in pulsar timing data and the case of kination-amplified gravitational-wave background from inflation

Time: 2025-03-24

Authors: Bohua Li, Joel Meyers, Paul R. Shapiro

Abstract: The study investigates the kination-amplified inflationary gravitational-wave background (GWB) as an explanation for pulsar timing array (PTA) signals, identifying two equally probable modes (KA and no-KA) through Bayesian analysis, with the KA mode requiring a less blue primordial spectrum and future multi-messenger gravitational-wave measurements needed to resolve model ambiguity.

Role of the $δ$ Meson in the Equation of State an Direct Urca Cooling of Neutron Stars

Time: 2025-03-24

Authors: Luigi Scurto, Helena Pais, Marco Antonelli, Francesca Gulminelli

Abstract: The study investigates how the inclusion of the δ meson in neutron star equations of state alters proton fractions and the dUrca cooling threshold, explores its interplay with proton superfluidity, and uses Bayesian analysis with observational and nuclear constraints to reveal expanded internal compositions, linking young cold neutron stars to core proton superfluidity insights.

Systematic bias in dark siren statistical methods and its impact on Hubble constant measurement

Time: 2025-03-24

Authors: V. Alfradique, C. R. Bom, T. Castro

Abstract: This study demonstrates that correcting galaxy catalog incompleteness and applying proper weighting schemes (e.g., stellar mass) for dark siren analyses of binary black hole mergers detected by LVK can yield unbiased Hubble constant measurements with ~3% precision using 100 O4/O5 detections, while assuming equal host galaxy probabilities reduces precision to ~6%.

Generalized relativistic second-order dissipative hydrodynamics: coupling different rank tensors

Time: 2025-03-24

Authors: Arus Harutyunyan, Armen Sedrakian

Abstract: This work extends second-order relativistic dissipative hydrodynamics by identifying new terms arising from acceleration-induced couplings of quantum correlators between different-rank tensors, which extend Curie's theorem, and derives associated transport coefficients and Kubo formulas via multi-point correlations for quantum systems with multiple conserved charges.

Superheavy Supersymmetric Dark Matter for the origin of KM3NeT Ultra-High Energy signal

Time: 2025-03-24

Authors: Yongsoo Jho, Seong Chan Park, Chang Sub Shin

Abstract: The authors propose that KM3NeT's unexplained ultra-high-energy neutrino signal arises from a supersymmetric multicomponent dark matter model, where a heavy component decaying into a lighter one at high redshift produces ~100 PeV neutrinos, resulting in an isotropic distribution consistent with an extragalactic origin.

Limiting on ALP-photon Coupling through GRB221009A

Time: 2025-03-24

Authors: Tong Chen, Yu Gao, Shijie Liu, Ying-nan Mao, Kechen Wang

Abstract: This study constrains axion-like particle (ALP) parameters (mass *mₐ* and coupling strength *gₐ*) using photon-ALP oscillation modeling of gamma-ray burst GRB 221009A data, achieving an upper limit of *gₐ* < 0.27×10⁻¹¹ GeV⁻¹ (improving CAST constraints tenfold) and demonstrating high-energy data's superior constraining power, offering a novel framework for probing ALPs via astrophysical phenomena.

The Impact of Accretion on FRB Radiation Mechanisms in Binary Systems: Constraints and Implications

Time: 2025-03-24

Authors: Gong-Yu Yao, Can-Min Deng

Abstract: This study concludes that Fast Radio Bursts (FRBs) originating in binary systems with massive companions likely arise from magnetars (neutron stars with magnetic fields $\gtrsim 10^{13}$ G), as coherent curvature radiation requires narrow parameters while magnetic reconnection operates broadly, both mechanisms necessitating such extreme magnetic fields.

Fast Radio Bursts and Electromagnetic Transition Radiation on Gravitational Shockwaves

Time: 2025-03-24

Authors: D. V. Fursaev, E. A. Davydov, V. A. Tainov

Abstract: The authors propose that gravitational shockwaves interacting with magnetars generate transition radiation via magnetic field perturbations, suggesting this mechanism as a novel explanation for fast radio bursts (FRBs) due to the radiation's unique retention of the shock's spatial profile in Maxwell components, modeled using a magnetic dipole with shockwaves from ultrarelativistic objects or cosmic strings, where pulse duration depends on impact distance and angular radiation flux aligns with FRB characteristics.

Multifrequency simultaneous VLBA view of the radio source 3C 111

Time: 2025-03-24

Authors: V. Bartolini, D. Dallacasa, J. L. Gómez, M. Giroletti, R. Lico, J. D. Livingston

Abstract: This study investigates the magnetic field structure and parsec-scale properties of the relativistic jet in radio galaxy 3C111 through multi-frequency Rotation Measure (RM) analysis and Gaussian component modeling, revealing high RM values linked to a dense torus cloud and a transverse RM gradient indicating a helical magnetic field configuration.

How do recollimation-induced instabilities shape the propagation of hydrodynamic relativistic jets?

Time: 2025-03-24

Authors: A. Costa, G. Bodo, F. Tavecchio, P. Rossi, P. Coppi, A. Sciaccaluga, S. Boula

Abstract: Three-dimensional relativistic hydrodynamic simulations demonstrate that recollimation instabilities in AGN jets, influenced by parameters like Lorentz factor and temperature, cause deceleration, heating, and shifted recollimation points, with narrower, denser, more relativistic, and warmer jets experiencing less disruption, offering critical insights for modeling low-power, weakly magnetized AGN jets.
