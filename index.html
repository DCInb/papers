High Energy Emission from the Intrabinary Shocks in Redback Pulsars

Time: 2025-03-31

Authors: Andrew G. Sullivan, Roger W. Romani

Abstract: Joint soft-hard X-ray analyses of three redback spider pulsars, including new NuSTAR data, revealed synchrotron cooling breaks, estimating intrabinary shock magnetic fields (∼40–100 G) and electron energy limits, thereby constraining pulsar wind magnetization and pair-production mechanisms in millisecond pulsars.

EP240414a: Off-axis View of a Jet-Cocoon System from an Expanded Progenitor Star

Time: 2025-03-31

Authors: Jian-He Zheng, Jin-Ping Zhu, Wenbin Lu, Bing Zhang

Abstract: The fast X-ray transient EP240414a's multi-wavelength observations are explained by an off-axis jet-cocoon system, where distinct emissions (prompt X-ray, optical, radio, and supernova) arise from components like the cooling inner/outer cocoon and jet afterglow, with inferred progenitor characteristics and kinetic energies (~10⁵¹ erg) suggesting a massive, slightly inflated helium star progenitor.

Non-linear saturation of gravito-inertial modes excited by tidal resonances in binary neutron stars

Time: 2025-03-31

Authors: Alexis Reboul-Salze, Aurélie Astoul, Hao-Jui Kuan, Arthur G. Suvorov

Abstract: This study investigates the linear and non-linear saturation of gravito-inertial modes during binary neutron-star mergers using a new formulation and 3D simulations, revealing potential dynamo-driven magnetic field amplification to ≥10¹⁴ G, parametric instabilities, and spin-up effects that extend resonance windows.

Environmental effects in stellar mass gravitational wave sources I: Expected fraction of signals with significant dephasing in the dynamical and AGN channels

Time: 2025-03-31

Authors: Lorenz Zwick, János Takátsy, Pankaj Saini, Kai Hendriks, Johan Samsing, Christopher Tiede, Connar Rowan, Alessandro A. Trani

Abstract: This study predicts that next-generation gravitational wave detectors will detect hundreds of signals exhibiting astrophysical dephasing effects caused by environmental mechanisms like Roemer delays, tidal forces, and hydrodynamical interactions in binary black hole systems, based on models of dynamical and AGN formation channels.

Written in the Stars: How your (pens and) papers decide the fate of the arXiverse

Time: 2025-03-31

Authors: Joanne Tan

Abstract: This study quantifies the expansion rate of the "arXiverse" (arXiv astro-ph submissions from 1992 onward) using a newly defined "arXiverse constant" (\(a_0\)), finding overall positive expansion for astro-ph but variability among its subcategories, with temporal trends in \(a_0\) used to infer the arXiverse's future trajectory.

Populations of evolved massive binary stars in the Small Magellanic Cloud II: Predictions from rapid binary evolution

Time: 2025-03-31

Authors: C. Schürmann, X. -T. Xu, N. Langer, D. Lennon, M. U. Kruckow, J. Antoniadis, F. Haberl, A. Herrero, M. Kramer, A. Schootemeijer, T. Shenar, T. M. Tauris, C. Wang

Abstract: The study reveals that massive binary star evolution in the Small Magellanic Cloud requires a mass-dependent mass-transfer efficiency (high for Be stars and low for Wolf-Rayet binaries) to align simulations with observed populations, while predicting unobserved OB+BH binaries.

Populations of evolved massive binary stars in the Small Magellanic Cloud I: Predictions from detailed evolution models

Time: 2025-03-31

Authors: X. -T. Xu, C. Schürmann, N. Langer, C. Wang, A. Schootemeijer, T. Shenar, A. Ercolino, F. Haberl, B. Hastings, H. Jin, M. Kramer, D. Lennon, P. Marchant, K. Sen, T. M. Tauris, S. E. de Mink

Abstract: The study uses MESA models of massive binary evolution in the Small Magellanic Cloud to show partial agreement with observed populations, suggesting adjustments to merger rates or mass transfer efficiency to resolve discrepancies in OBe stars and Be/X-ray binaries, while uncertainties persist regarding the fate of wide O binaries.

Explodability criteria for the neutrino-driven supernova mechanism

Time: 2025-03-31

Authors: K. Maltsev, F. R. N. Schneider, I. Mandel, B. Müller, A. Heger, F. K. Röpke, E. Laplace

Abstract: This study introduces explodability criteria based on carbon-oxygen core mass and metallicity to predict successful supernovae, failed supernovae, and direct black hole formation, validated by semi-analytic models and 3D simulations, enabling rapid population synthesis studies and addressing the missing red supergiant problem through critical progenitor parameters.

Multispacecraft Observations of the 2024 September 9 Backside Solar Eruption that Resulted in a Sustained Gamma Ray Emission Event

Time: 2025-03-31

Authors: Nat Gopalswamy, Pertti Mäkelä, Sachiko Akiyama, Hong Xie, Seiji Yashiro, Stuart D. Bale, Robert F. Wimmer-Schweingruber, Patrick Kuehl, Säm Krucker

Abstract: The September 9, 2024, sustained gamma-ray emission event observed by Fermi's Large Area Telescope, linked to a backside solar eruption involving an impulsively accelerated CME flux rope (peaking at 2162 km/s) and shock-accelerated protons (supported by radio bursts), aligns with six similar events and reveals an east-west asymmetry in source locations.

Narrow-Line Seyfert 1 Galaxies Beyond the Local X-ray Universe: An X-ray spectral sample

Time: 2025-03-31

Authors: Jiachen Jiang, Dominic J. Walton, Luigi C. Gallo, Andrew C. Fabian, Dirk Grupe, Richard McMahon, Christopher S. Reynolds, Andrew Young, Zhibo Yu, Honghui Liu, Zuobin Zhang

Abstract: This study conducts an X-ray spectral analysis of 14 high-redshift (z≈1) Narrow-line Seyfert 1 AGNs, revealing AGN emission consistency, soft excess indications from steep photon indices (Γ≳2.5), and a necessary X-ray luminosity correction (10-120×) to align Eddington ratios with optical data, challenging assumptions of thin accretion disk models and highlighting the need for deeper X-ray surveys to explore X-ray-weak NLS1 populations.
