Long-Term Multidimensional Models of Core-Collapse Supernovae: Progress and Challenges

Time: 2025-02-20

Authors: H. -Thomas Janka

Abstract: Recent advances in 3D core-collapse supernova simulations validate the neutrino-driven explosion mechanism by reproducing observed energies, neutron-star kicks, and radioactive yields while predicting signals, but uncertainties persist regarding progenitor outcomes (explosion vs. black-hole formation), neutrino flavor conversion, nuclear equation of state, SN 1987A neutrino puzzles, and neutron-star spin-kick correlations.

Super-Eddington Accretion in Quasars

Time: 2025-02-20

Authors: P. Marziani, K. Garnica Luna, A. Floris, A. del Olmo, A. Deconto-Machado, T. M. Buendia-Rios, C. A. Negrete, D. Dultzin

Abstract: This review examines super-Eddington accretion in quasars through observational criteria like optical/UV broad-line ratios, identifying extreme properties (e.g., high iron emission, powerful outflows, metal enrichment), evidence from early cosmic black hole growth requiring radiatively inefficient accretion, their potential as cosmological distance indicators, and analogies to stellar-mass black hole accretion in high/soft states.

Constraints on optical and near-infrared variability in the localisation of the long-period radio transient GLEAM-X J1627-52

Time: 2025-02-20

Authors: J. D. Lyman, V. S. Dhillon, S. Kamann, A. A. Chrimes, A. J. Levan, I. Pelisoli, D. T. H. Steeghs, K. Wiersema

Abstract: The enigmatic Galactic radio transient GLEAM-X J1627-52, exhibiting an ~18-minute period, shows no conclusive optical/near-infrared or spectroscopic evidence supporting white dwarf pulsars or close binaries, with isolated magnetars or compact binaries remaining viable explanations despite observational challenges in dense regions.

Magnetic Thomson Transport in High Opacity Domains

Time: 2025-02-20

Authors: Matthew G. Baring, Kun Hu, Hoa Dinh Thi

Abstract: This paper introduces a novel analytical method for modeling polarized X-ray radiation in high Thomson opacity neutron star environments—such as magnetar atmospheres, pulsars, and accretion columns—by simplifying radiative transfer equations into numerical solutions and empirical approximations, enhancing simulation efficiency and enabling precise radiation pressure characterization across diverse astrophysical settings.

How does Radiation Reaction Affect Relativistic Magnetized Shocks Emission

Time: 2025-02-20

Authors: Yu Zhang, Yuanpei Yang, Liangliang Ji

Abstract: This study demonstrates through Particle-In-Cell simulations that radiation reaction effects in relativistic magnetized shocks driven by the Synchrotron Maser Instability fundamentally alter particle dynamics and emission properties, producing spectral broadening, peak frequency upshift, and enhanced radiation intensity, which explain FRB 121102's bimodal energy distribution and luminosity-bandwidth correlations, supporting magnetized shocks as a viable source of fast radio bursts.

MOJAVE -- XXII. Brightness temperature distributions and geometric profiles along parsec-scale AGN jets

Time: 2025-02-20

Authors: E. V. Kravchenko, I. N. Pashchenko, D. C. Homan, Y. Y. Kovalev, M. L. Lister, A. B. Pushkarev, E. Ros, T. Savolainen

Abstract: This study of 447 AGN jets using 15 GHz VLBA observations (1994–2019) reveals conical expansion (R ∝ r¹.⁰²), brightness temperature gradients (Tb ∝ r⁻².⁸²), and non-monotonic profiles explained by shocks, helical structures, or jet dynamics, supporting optically thin moving blobs and magneto-hydrodynamic acceleration at jet geometry transitions.

A possible overall scenario for the outburst evolution of MAXI J1820+070 revealed by Insight-HXMT

Time: 2025-02-20

Authors: J. Q. Peng, S. Zhang, Y. P. Chen, L. D. Kong, P. J. Wang, S. N. Zhang, L. Ji, L. Tao, J. L. Qu, M. Y. Ge, Q. C. Shui, J. Li, Z. Chang, Z. S. Li, Y. X. Xiao

Abstract: The study of MAXI J1820+070's 2018 outburst using Insight-HXMT data revealed energy-dependent time lags (on a days scale) between low- and high-energy photons during different outburst phases, which reformed the canonical 'q'-shaped hardness-intensity diagram into a linear form, alongside variable high-frequency breaks in hard X-rays, dynamic corona coverage fraction changes, and insights into corona evolution scenarios.

The Thousand-Pulsar-Array programme on MeerKAT XVII: Discovery of beating radio emission variability in PSR J1514-4834

Time: 2025-02-20

Authors: J. A. Hsu, P. Weltevrede, G. Wright, M. J. Keith, L. S. Oswald, X. Song, H. Wang

Abstract: This study of PSR J1514-4834 using TPA and MeerKAT data reveals coexisting periodic subpulse modulations forming a beat system, confirmed through a novel phase correlation methodology, which points to time-delayed magnetospheric interactions rather than a rotating carousel model, providing new insights into pulsar dynamics and methodologies for future research.

Do Short GRBs Exhibit an Anticorrelation between Their Intrinsic Duration and Redshift?

Time: 2025-02-20

Authors: Ali M. Hasan, Walid J. Azzam

Abstract: This study confirms an anticorrelation between intrinsic duration and redshift for long gamma-ray bursts (LGRBs) but not for short gamma-ray bursts (SGRBs), suggesting metallicity evolution with redshift may influence the LGRB trend.

Dragging of inertial frames in the composed Kerr-Newman-orbiting-ring system

Time: 2025-02-20

Authors: Shahar Hod

Abstract: The study demonstrates that in a Kerr-Newman-black-hole-orbiting-ring system, the change in the black hole's horizon angular velocity (ΔΩ_H) due to frame-dragging explicitly depends on the black hole's angular momentum \( J_{\text{H}} \), contrasting the spin-independent relation \( \Delta\Omega_{\text{H}} = J_{\text{R}}/(4M^3) \) observed in the Kerr-ring system.
