Expectation for the MeV Gamma-Ray Emission from Pair-Instability Supernovae

Time: 2025-03-27

Authors: Ryo Sawada

Abstract: A 1D Monte Carlo radiation transport study reveals next-generation MeV gamma-ray telescopes could detect key gamma-ray lines (847 and 1238 keV) from 130M⊙ pair-instability supernovae (PISNe) up to 300-400 Mpc, with an estimated rate of ~0.1 events per year within 300 Mpc, underscoring their rarity but feasibility as future observational targets.

Transitioning to Memory Burden: Detectable Small Primordial Black Holes as Dark Matter

Time: 2025-03-27

Authors: Gia Dvali, Michael Zantedeschi, Sebastian Zell

Abstract: The memory burden effect in small primordial black holes (PBHs) below \(10^{15}\,{\rm g}\) strengthens their viability as dark matter by suppressing decay rates through a transition from semi-classical evaporation, with current astrophysical particle fluxes imposing stringent abundance constraints and high-energy neutrinos providing detectable signatures of transitioning PBHs.

A possible GeV-TeV connection in the direction of the Globular Cluster UKS 1

Time: 2025-03-27

Authors: Jiwon Shin, C. Y. Hui, Sangin Kim, Kwangmin Oh, Ellis R. Owen

Abstract: Using 16 years of Fermi LAT and 3HWC HAWC data, researchers detected GeV emission at the globular cluster UKS 1 and a displaced TeV feature aligned with its motion, supporting a scenario where relativistic particles escaping a bow shock—due to the cluster's supersonic speed—produce very high-energy gamma-rays via inverse Compton scattering, marking the second such TeV detection near a GC.

3D MHD simulations of runaway pulsars in core collapse supernova remnants

Time: 2025-03-27

Authors: D. M. A. Meyer, D. F. Torres, Z. Meliani

Abstract: The evolutionary history of massive progenitor stars shapes the morphology and radio emission of pulsar wind nebulae through 3D magnetohydrodynamic simulations, revealing how pulsars moving through circumstellar mediums and supernova remnants bend jets into arched structures, necessitating progenitor star understanding to interpret nebula dynamics.

Polarized equatorial emission and hot spots around black holes with a dark matter halo

Time: 2025-03-27

Authors: Tsanimir Angelov, Rasim Bekir, Galin Gyulchev, Petya Nedkova, Stoytcho Yazadjiev

Abstract: This study investigates the linear polarization of accretion disks around black holes with dark matter halos, modeled via a Schwarzschild black hole combined with a Hernquist-type distribution, revealing minimal deviations (under 1% for direct images, up to 10% for indirect ones) that make detecting dark matter's impact on polarized emissions extremely challenging for near-future observations.

Study of the origin of the azimuthal variation of synchrotron X-ray spectrum from SNR RX J0852.0-4622

Time: 2025-03-27

Authors: Dai Tateishi, Nobuaki Sasaki, Yukikatsu Terada, Satoru Katsuda, Shiu-Hang Lee, Hidetoshi Sano, Yasuo Fukui, Aya Bamba

Abstract: The azimuthal variations in X-ray flux and photon index of supernova remnant RX J0852.0-4622, observed via Suzaku/XIS, are primarily caused by cloud density variations in the north (correlated with flux) and shock velocity in the south, ruling out galactic magnetic field influence.

Gravitational-wave kicks impact spins of black holes from hierarchical mergers

Time: 2025-03-27

Authors: Angela Borchers, Claire S. Ye, Maya Fishbach

Abstract: The spin distribution of hierarchically merging black holes is skewed from a near-universal distribution by gravitational-wave kick ejections, producing bimodal or broad spin ranges depending on escape velocity and merger history, with observable features that link spin measurements to formation environments.

Are Neutron Stars Rich in H-dibaryons?

Time: 2025-03-27

Authors: Jesper Leong, Anthony W. Thomas, Pierre A. M. Guichon

Abstract: The study explores the impact of a potentially existing H-dibaryon near the Λ-Λ threshold on neutron stars using the quark-meson coupling model and excluded volume formalism, finding that while H-dibaryons are abundant in heavy stars, their presence only slightly reduces the maximum mass.

Heavy Neutron Star Phenomenology with an H-dibaryon

Time: 2025-03-27

Authors: Jesper Leong, Anthony W. Thomas, Pierre A. M. Guichon

Abstract: The study finds that including a doubly-strange H-dibaryon near the ΛΛ threshold in β-equilibrium nuclear matter using the quark-meson coupling model slightly reduces neutron stars' maximum mass, leaves tidal deformability unchanged, and results in abundant H-particles extending up to 6 km from the core in heavy stars.
