Long-term Timing Results of Ecliptic Pulsars Observed with I-LOFAR

Time: 2025-05-14

Authors: S. C. Susarla, O. A. Johnson, D. J. McKenna, E. F. Keane, P. J. McCauley, J. P. W. Verbiest, C. Tiburzi, A. Golden

Abstract: The Irish LOFAR station's multi-year study of seven ecliptic pulsars demonstrates its capability to detect solar wind-induced dispersion measure (DM) variations dependent on ecliptic latitude, profile variability across frequencies, and phenomena like pulse-nulling and DM chromaticity, highlighting its potential for probing the interstellar medium, solar wind, and ionosphere in low-frequency pulsar timing.

A weak Ly$α$ halo for an extremely bright Little Red Dot. Indications of enshrouded SMBH growth

Time: 2025-05-14

Authors: Alberto Torralba, Jorryt Matthee, Gabriele Pezzulli, Tanya Urrutia, Max Gronke, Sara Mascia, Francesco D'Eugenio, Claudia Di Cesare, Anna-Christina Eilers, Jenny E. Greene, Edoardo Iani, Yuzo Ishikawa, Ruari Mackenzie, Rohan P. Naidu, Benjamín Navarrete, Gauri Kotiwale

Abstract: The study of the luminous "Little Red Dot" A2744-45924 demonstrates that its Hα emission stems from an obscured active galactic nucleus (AGN) cloaked in dense gas, while Lyα emission originates from star formation, with no significant AGN radiative impact on its surrounding Lyα halos, supporting models of heavily obscured supermassive black holes.

The construction and use of dephasing prescriptions for environmental effects in gravitational wave astronomy

Time: 2025-05-14

Authors: János Takátsy, Lorenz Zwick, Kai Hendriks, Pankaj Saini, Gaia Fabj, Johan Samsing

Abstract: This study synthesizes two decades of research into a unified framework for incorporating environmental dephasing effects in gravitational wave templates, resolving inconsistencies and providing key methodologies, while also introducing novel insights into dephasing in eccentric binaries, demonstrating their unique potential for advancing gravitational wave astronomy.

A 70 pc-Diameter Nova Super-remnant Surrounding the Recurrent Nova RS Ophiuchi

Time: 2025-05-14

Authors: Michael M. Shara, Kenneth M. Lanzetta, Alexandra Masegian, James T. Garland, Stefan Gromoll, Alexei Kniazev, Lee Townsend, David Zurek, Joanna Mikolajewska, David Valls-Gabaud, Frederick M. Walter, John K. Webb

Abstract: Deep imaging and spectroscopy reveal a ~70-parsec shell around the recurrent nova RS Ophiuchi, supporting the prediction that all such novae are surrounded by vast, low-surface-brightness super-remnants formed by colliding ejecta from successive eruptions.

Supernova in lost common envelope and SN 2006gy

Time: 2025-05-14

Authors: Nikolai Chugai

Abstract: The proposed mechanism suggests that a red supergiant's moderate expansion in its final decade triggers a common envelope phase with a companion, leading to a core-collapse supernova via dense shell formation from intense mass loss, as exemplified by superluminous SN 2006gy.

The asymmetry of white dwarf double detonations and the observed scatter around the Phillips relation

Time: 2025-05-14

Authors: Alexander Holas, Friedrich K. Roepke, Rüdiger Pakmor, Fionntan P. Callan, Josh Pollin, Stuart A. Sim, Christine E. Collins, Luke J. Shingles, Javier Morán-Fraile

Abstract: This study reveals that while off-center carbon-oxygen core ignition in double detonation Type Ia supernova models introduces viewing angle effects, the excessive scatter around the Phillips relation in other simulations likely stems from large helium shell masses, suggesting that reducing these masses could reconcile synthetic observables with observed data.

Simulating jet launching in binary neutron star mergers with no artificial atmosphere limitations

Time: 2025-05-14

Authors: Jay V. Kalinani, Riccardo Ciolfi, Manuela Campanelli, Bruno Giacomazzo, Andrea Pavan, Allen Wen, Yosef Zlochower

Abstract: General relativistic magnetohydrodynamic simulations of binary neutron star mergers, using an unprecedentedly low radial-dependent density floor, demonstrate that relativistic jets compatible with gamma-ray bursts emerge only when a black hole forms post-merger, while non-collapse scenarios produce slower outflows, supporting a black hole origin for such jets.

Quantum signatures in black hole accretion: Pair production in dynamical magnetic fields

Time: 2025-05-14

Authors: Susobhan Mandal, Tausif Parvez, S. Shankaranarayanan

Abstract: This study demonstrates through quantum field theory that the extreme magnetic fields in Magnetically Arrested Disks (MADs) around black holes drive significant Schwinger mechanism pair production, generating tunable synchrotron radiation (1–3000 MHz) with detectable flux (1–100 mJy) by next-generation telescopes like SKA and ngVLA, providing direct observational evidence of quantum effects in relativistic accretion environments.

The X-ray variability of tidal disruption events

Time: 2025-05-14

Authors: Andrew Mummery

Abstract: This study combines theoretical models of long-term accretion disk evolution and short-term turbulence in tidal disruption events (TDEs) to predict asymmetric luminosity dimming, a near-linear flux-variability correlation, energy-dependent variability, and TDEs' utility in probing disk turbulence, with observational validation of the first two predictions and evidence supporting the third in two TDEs.

Formation and Early Evolution of Protoplanetary Disks under Nonuniform Cosmic-Ray Ionization

Time: 2025-05-14

Authors: Erika Nishio, Kengo Tomida, Yuki Kudoh, Shigeo S. Kimura

Abstract: Non-uniform ionization due to cosmic ray transport and attenuation during protoplanetary disk formation alters magnetic field dynamics, suppressing angular momentum transport and leading to more gravitationally unstable disks with prominent spiral arms.
