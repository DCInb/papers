On the progenitor of the type Ia supernova remnant 0509-67.5

Time: 2025-03-06

Authors: Noam Soker

Abstract: The study concludes that the type Ia supernova remnant 0509-67.5 likely originated from a spherical explosion within an old planetary nebula, favoring core-degenerate and double degenerate merger-delay scenarios due to the absence of a surviving star, suggesting a higher prevalence of such supernovae and attributing its calcium structures to Rayleigh-Taylor instabilities during the explosion.

Inferring kilonova ejecta photospheric properties from early blackbody spectra

Time: 2025-03-06

Authors: Gilad Sadeh

Abstract: The study introduces analytic corrections to standard blackbody fitting for kilonovae, reducing relativistic-induced systematic overestimations of photospheric velocities and temperatures from up to 50% to within 10%, as demonstrated by reanalyzing AT2017gfo and GRB211211A observations where traditional methods overestimated values by 10–40%.

Impact of multi-messenger spectral modelling on blazar-neutrino associations

Time: 2025-03-06

Authors: Julian Kuhlmann, Francesca Capel

Abstract: The study demonstrates that using Bayesian analysis with proton-photon interaction spectral models, rather than power-law assumptions, for 10 years of IceCube Northern hemisphere data results in fewer overall neutrino-blazar associations but stronger high-energy event connections (e.g., TXS 0506+056), enhancing interpretability through theoretical predictions.

Searching for Ultralight Dark Matter Solitons with Gravitational Waves

Time: 2025-03-06

Authors: Silvia Gasparotto

Abstract: This study shows that solitonic cores from Ultralight Dark Matter (ULDM) alter gravitational waves (GWs), detectable via next-generation observatories like LISA, enabling probes of ULDM masses \(m \lesssim 10^{-22}\, \mathrm{eV}\), surpassing constraints for \(m \lesssim 10^{-20}\, \mathrm{eV}\) through Galactic neutron star signals, while GW modulation in massive halos reveals extragalactic solitons, underscoring GW methods' potential to uncover ULDM properties.

Introducing APERTURE: A GPU-based General Relativistic Particle-in-Cell Simulation Framework

Time: 2025-03-06

Authors: Alexander Y. Chen, Martin Luepker, Yajie Yuan

Abstract: The authors present Aperture, a GPU-accelerated, modular general relativistic particle-in-cell (GRPIC) code framework developed in C++ with CUDA/HIP for simulating low-luminosity AGN magnetospheres, which resolves conflicting prior findings by demonstrating observer-dependent spark gap dynamics in black hole environments.

Super heavy dark matter origin of the PeV neutrino event: KM3-230213A

Time: 2025-03-06

Authors: Kazunori Kohri, Partha Kumar Paul, Narendra Sahu

Abstract: This study explains the ultra-high-energy neutrino event KM3-230213A observed by KM3NeT as originating from the decay of super-heavy dark matter in a model extending the Standard Model with a Z₂-symmetry-breaking singlet scalar and fermion, leading to dark matter-neutrino mixing and domain walls that could produce gravitational waves, while constraining the dark matter mass to 1.7×10⁸–5.5×10⁹ GeV and lifetime to 6.3×10²⁹–3.6×10²⁹ s using IceCube and Auger data.

Can we disentangle between the emission of an accretion disc around a single black hole and a circumbinary disc ?

Time: 2025-03-06

Authors: Peggy Varniere, Raphaël Mignon-Risse, Fabien Casse

Abstract: This study investigates whether features of circumbinary discs around binary black holes (BBHs), such as periodic modulations, can be distinguished from those around single black holes (SBHs), finding that while SBH systems can mimic many features through parameter adjustments, the presence of two distinct variabilities linked to the BBH’s orbital and "lump" periods serves as a critical identifier to disqualify false candidates.

Magnetars

Time: 2025-03-06

Authors: Nanda Rea, Davide De Grandis

Abstract: This chapter summarizes current experimental and theoretical knowledge on magnetars—neutron stars with extreme magnetic fields discovered via X-ray flares in the 1970s—their multi-band emissions, and their connections to supernovae, gamma-ray bursts, fast radio bursts, and other transient cosmic phenomena.

The Firmamento platform

Time: 2025-03-06

Authors: Paolo Giommi

Abstract: Firmamento is an online astronomical research platform focused on studying blazars and multi-messenger emitters, offering access to diverse data (imaging, spectral, timing), analytical tools, machine learning capabilities, the ERCI for identifying blazar-like counterparts, full-spectrum SED generation, VHE detectability estimates, and support for user-provided data.
