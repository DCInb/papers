Polarization Studies in Different Blazar Types. Part I: Correlation between Optical Brightness and Polarization Degree

Time: 2025-04-25

Authors: E. A. Shkodkina, S. S. Savchenko, D. A. Morozova, S. G. Jorstad, G. A. Borman, T. S. Grishina, E. N. Kopatskaya, E. G. Larionova, A. A. Vasilyev, I. S. Troitskiy, Yu. V. Troitskaya, P. A. Novikova, E. V. Shishkina, A. V. Zhovtan

Abstract: This study analyzes the cross-correlation between optical brightness and polarization degree in 23 blazars (11 FSRQ, 11 BL Lac, 1 radio galaxy) over 10+ years, finding significant correlations in 5 FSRQ and 1 BL Lac, suggesting differences may arise from more frequent simultaneous flux-polarization flares in FSRQ rather than intrinsic class distinctions.

Equation of state and Fermi liquid properties of dense matter based on chiral EFT interactions

Time: 2025-04-25

Authors: Faruk Alp, Yannick Dietz, Kai Hebeler, Achim Schwenk

Abstract: This study employs third-order many-body perturbation theory with chiral nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon interactions to investigate the equations of state for symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter, extracting properties like incompressibility and symmetry energy, assessing theoretical uncertainties from higher-order contributions in both the perturbation theory and chiral effective field theory frameworks, and exploring Fermi liquid theory results such as Landau parameters, effective mass, and speed of sound in pure neutron matter.

Reconstruction of inclined extensive air showers using radio signals: from arrival times and amplitudes to direction and energy

Time: 2025-04-25

Authors: Marion Guelfand, Valentin Decoene, Olivier Martineau-Huynh, Simon Prunet, Matías Tueros, Oscar Macias, Aurélien Benoit-Lévy

Abstract: This article introduces a novel radio-based reconstruction method using the Angular Distribution Function (ADF) to accurately determine the arrival direction (resolution <0.07°) and energy (∼15% precision) of highly inclined extensive air showers (zenith >60°) for ultra-high-energy cosmic ray and neutrino studies, validated via simulations.

Phenomenology of Schwarzschild-like Black Holes with a Generalized Compton Wavelength

Time: 2025-04-25

Authors: Reggie C. Pantig, Ali Ovgun, Gaetano Lambiase

Abstract: This study explores the influence of the generalized Compton wavelength (GCW) from a 3D dynamical quantum vacuum on Schwarzschild-like black holes, deriving constraints on the deformation parameter ε using Event Horizon Telescope data and weak lensing analyses, and demonstrates how GCW-induced quantum corrections modify key properties like shadow size, Hawking temperature, and quasinormal modes, presenting a viable semiclassical model with testable predictions for future gravitational wave and VLBI observations.

Authors: D. Williams-Baldwin, T. W. B. Muxlow, G. Lucatelli, R. J. Beswick

The Role of Population III Star Tidal Disruption Events in Black Hole Growth at the Cosmic Dawn

Time: 2025-04-25

Authors: Zijian Wang, Yiqiu Ma, Yuxuan Li, Zheng Cai, Chanyan Wang, Qingwen Wu

Abstract: This study demonstrates that tidal disruption events (TDEs) of Population III stars significantly accelerate the growth of lighter seed black holes (10⁴–10⁵ solar masses) by tenfold within 10 million years, supporting high-redshift quasar formation via semi-analytical modeling and highlighting future observational validation through facilities like JWST and gravitational wave observatories.

Flux tube clustering from magnetic coupling of adjacent type-I and -II superconductors in a neutron star: persistent gravitational radiation

Time: 2025-04-24

Authors: K. H. Thong, A. Melatos

Abstract: This study reveals that magnetic repulsion in pulsar superconductors causes flux tube clustering and neutron vortex pinning, amplifying gravitational wave strain by a factor of √(1+Nₑ,ₜ), potentially reaching current detector sensitivity when flux branching produces few large flux trees.

Quantifying jet-interstellar medium interactions in Cyg X-1: Insights from dual-frequency bow shock detection with MeerKAT

Time: 2025-04-24

Authors: P. Atri, S. E. Motta, Jakob van den Eijnden, James H. Matthews, James C. A. Miller-Jones, Rob Fender, David Williams-Baldwin, Ian Heywood, Patrick Woudt

Abstract: This study uses MeerKAT radio observations to detect and characterize the bow shock formed by Cyg X-1's jets interacting with the interstellar medium (ISM), determining ISM density (6-7 cm⁻³), temperature (10⁴-3×10⁶ K), shock velocity (21-364 km/s), jet age (0.04-0.3 Myr), and power (2×10³¹-10³⁵ erg/s), revealing higher ambient ISM density and lower jet energy transport than prior estimates.
