H, He-like recombination spectra VI: Quadrupole $l$-changing collisions

Time: 2025-04-04

Authors: E. Deliporanidou, N. R. Badnell, P. J. Storey, G. Del Zanna, G. J. Ferland

Abstract: The authors developed a simple analytic formula for quadrupole \( l \)-changing collisions, validated by numerical and prior studies (with discrepancies in large \( l > n/2 \) transitions), showing quadrupole rates are two orders of magnitude lower than dipolar ones and causing minimal impact (≤1%) in astrophysical plasma models, now providing complete theories for astrophysical applications.

Clustering analysis of Fermi-LAT unidentified point sources

Time: 2025-04-04

Authors: Giovanni Cozzolongo, Alison M. W. Mitchell, Samuel T. Spencer, Dmitry Malyshev, Tim Unbehaun

Abstract: Using the DBSCAN algorithm on Fermi-LAT data from the 4FGL-DR4 catalog, researchers identified 44 clusters (106 sources) containing unidentified sources, with statistical modeling favoring extended emission over multiple point sources in some cases, as exemplified by the HESS J1813-178 region.

Coupling hydrodynamics with comoving frame radiative transfer. III. The wind regime of early-type B hypergiants

Time: 2025-04-04

Authors: M. Bernini-Peron, A. A. C. Sander, F. Najarro, G. N. Sabhahit, D. Pauli, R. R. Lefever, J. S. Vink, V. Ramachandran, L. M. Oskinova, G. González-Torà, E. C. Schösser

Abstract: This study employs hydrodynamically consistent models of the B hypergiant zet1 Sco to demonstrate that, despite higher mass-loss rates than B supergiants, BHGs have optically thin winds primarily driven by Fe III opacity, with their spectral features arising from atmospheric inhomogeneities, suggesting they are distinct from transition-type Of/WNh stars and require proximity to the Eddington limit or increased clumping to resemble luminous blue variables.

Generalized entropic dark energy with spatial curvature

Time: 2025-04-04

Authors: Sergei D. Odintsov, Simone D'Onofrio, Tanmoy Paul

Abstract: The study constructs a dark energy model using a 4-parameter generalized entropy for the apparent horizon in a spatially non-flat universe, showing compatibility with the universe's thermal history (matter and dark energy epochs), resolving the Hubble tension via a higher present Hubble parameter, suggesting positive spatial curvature, and validating the model against observational datasets (CC, BAO, Pantheon+, SH0ES) with parameter values consistent with these outcomes.

X-ray Spectral Variability as Probe of Multimessenger Emission in Blazar 5BZB J0630-24064

Time: 2025-04-04

Authors: Jose Maria Sanchez Zaballa, Sara Buson, Stefano Marchesi, Francesco Tombesi, Thomas Dauser, Joern Wilms, Alessandra Azzollini

Abstract: The study of blazar 5BZB J0630-2406's X-ray spectral variability reveals a transition from jet-dominated emission to multi-component mechanisms (possibly corona or photoionised absorption) during low-flux states, suggesting hadronic processes linked to neutrino production and advancing multimessenger insights into jet-nuclear environment synergies.

Probing Gauged $U(1)$ Sub-GeV Dark Matter via Cosmic Ray Cooling in Active Galactic Nuclei

Time: 2025-04-04

Authors: Arvind Kumar Mishra, Ning Liu, Chih-Ting Lu

Abstract: This study uses cosmic ray cooling effects in active galactic nuclei (e.g., NGC 1068, TXS 0506+056) to impose stringent constraints on sub-GeV dark matter particles charged under U(1) symmetries, showing tighter bounds on DM-electron and DM-proton scattering cross-sections than certain boosted DM mechanisms and current direct detection limits.

Launching of asymmetric outflows from the star-disk magnetosphere

Time: 2025-04-04

Authors: Miljenko Čemeljić, Aleksandra Kotek, Włodek Kluźniak

Abstract: This study uses resistive and viscous magnetohydrodynamic simulations to demonstrate asymmetric axial outflows launched from a star-disk system's innermost magnetosphere, identifying quasi-stationary outflow parameters, quantifying mass load and angular momentum flux, and analyzing their geometry, propagation speed, and rotational dynamics.
