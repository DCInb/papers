How Charged Can Neutrinos Be?

Time: 2025-04-28

Authors: Sudip Jana, Michael Klasen, Vishnu P. K

Abstract: This study demonstrates that neutrinos can acquire small electric charges within the Standard Model through flavor-universal \( U(1)_X \) symmetries (e.g., \( U(1)_{B-L} \) or \( U(1)_L \)), preserving electromagnetic gauge invariance and aligning with neutrino oscillation data, while flavor-dependent \( U(1)_X \) models are incompatible, and highlights the necessity of Dirac neutrinos and framework-specific evaluation of charge constraints rather than generic approaches.

Polarization of light from fast rotating Wolf-Rayet stars: A Monte Carlo simulations compared to analytical formula

Time: 2025-04-28

Authors: Slah Abdellaoui, Jiří Krtička, Brankica Kubátová, Petr Kurfürst

Abstract: This study employs a 3D Monte Carlo model to show that intrinsic polarization in Wolf-Rayet star winds, influenced by rotation velocity and inclination, diverges from single-scattering predictions at high rotation rates, varies linearly with mass-loss rates, and enhances understanding of their role as gamma-ray burst progenitors and stellar wind dynamics.

XMM-Newton Observations of the High Temperature Plasma in the Large Magellanic Cloud Supernova Remnant N132D

Time: 2025-04-28

Authors: Adam R. Foster, Paul P. Plucinsky, Terrance J. Gaetz, Xi Long, Diab Jerius

Abstract: Analysis of XMM-Newton data reveals that Fe K emission in the LMC supernova remnant N132D, concentrated asymmetrically in the southern region and linked to high-temperature plasma heated by the reverse shock, indicates enhanced iron abundance and suggests a progenitor mass between 13–15 solar masses.

Approximating neutron-star radii using gravitational-wave only measurements with symbolic regression

Time: 2025-04-28

Authors: Michał Bejger

Abstract: This study develops a symbolic regression-derived formula using pySR to estimate neutron-star radii from gravitational-wave data (mass and tidal deformability), validated across various equations of state and applied to GW170817, achieving meter-scale accuracy consistent with astrophysical observations.

Machine Learning Identification of Gravimentally Microlensed Gamma-Ray Bursts

Time: 2025-04-28

Authors: Mohammad H. Zhoolideh Haghighi, Zeinab Kalantari, Sohrab Rahvar, Alaa Ibrahim

Abstract: This study developed a machine learning model (Random Forest) that achieved 94% accuracy in detecting gravitational microlensing in simulated gamma-ray burst light curves, demonstrating its potential to aid future investigations of compact dark matter and small-scale cosmic structures.

The Dust Echo Emission of Fast Blue Optical Transients and Application to the Near-Infrared Excess of AT 2018cow

Time: 2025-04-28

Authors: Jing-Yao Li, Yun-Wei Yu, Liang-Duan Liu, Ming-Yan Xiao

Abstract: A study modeling the near-infrared excess in the fast blue optical transient AT 2018cow reveals that its emission rapidly forms a dust-free cavity and a surrounding dust shell with varied grain sizes, with evolutionary dust echo explaining time-varying and widely distributed dust temperatures, and indicates significantly larger dust mass and distance than prior estimates that ignored spectral evolution.

Tracing the ejecta structure of SN 1987A: Insights and diagnostics from 3D MHD simulations

Time: 2025-04-28

Authors: S. Orlando, M. Miceli, M. Ono, S. Nagataki, M. -A. Aloy, F. Bocchino, M. Gabler, B. Giudici, R. Giuffrida, E. Greco, G. La Malfa, S. -H. Lee, M. Obergaulinger, O. Petruk, V. Sapienza, S. Ustamujic, J. Weng

Abstract: This study uses 3D MHD simulations of SN 1987A, aligned with JWST observations and XRISM predictions, to reveal bipolar Fe-rich ejecta structures shaped by asymmetric core-collapse mechanisms, while highlighting discrepancies suggesting stronger explosion asymmetries and evolving X-ray emission dominated by shocked ejecta in future observations.

Accretion and Recovery in Giant Eruptions of Massive Stars

Time: 2025-04-28

Authors: Bhawna Mukhija, Amit Kashi

Abstract: This study uses numerical simulations to show that in binary systems where a companion star accretes mass from a massive star undergoing Giant Eruptions, lower accretion rates (≲0.01 M☉/yr) result in minor luminosity increases without expansion, while higher rates (≳0.01 M☉/yr) trigger dramatic luminosity spikes, inflation, cooling, and eventual evolution as a more massive star post-recovery.

Synchrotron Circular Polarization in Gamma-Ray Burst Prompt Optical Emission: Relativistic Thermal Electron Contribution

Time: 2025-04-28

Authors: Cheng Kangfa, Mao Jirong, Zhao Xiaohong, Liu Hongbang, Chen Zhegeng, Yu Gaojin, Chen Zhifu

Abstract: This study investigates synchrotron circular polarization in gamma-ray burst prompt optical emissions using a hybrid thermal-non-thermal electron model, demonstrating that polarization degrees exceed those of non-thermal-only models (reaching ~1% at ~10¹⁰ K) and decrease with higher optical depths (linear polarization declining faster than circular), emphasizing the need for future instruments to advance understanding of radiation mechanisms and particle acceleration.

Magnetic Moment-Field Interactions, A Universal Mechanism for Particle Energization

Time: 2025-04-28

Authors: Anil Raghav, Ajay Kumar, Mariyam Karari, Shubham Kadam, Kalpesh Ghag, Kishor Kumbhar, Omkar Dhamane

Abstract: The study provides observational evidence from Wind spacecraft data supporting the Raghav effect—where charged particles' magnetic moments interacting with dynamic magnetic fields drive kinetic energy changes—as a universal mechanism explaining plasma energization, overcoming traditional models' limitations in accounting for extreme solar corona heating and supersonic solar wind origins.
