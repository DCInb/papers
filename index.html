Beyond first light: global monitoring for high-energy neutrino astronomy

Time: 2025-03-10

Authors: Lisa Johanna Schumacher, Mauricio Bustamante, Matteo Agostini, Foteini Oikonomou, Elisa Resconi

Abstract: The IceCube Neutrino Telescope's identification of the first high-energy neutrino sources, NGC 1068 and TXS 0506+056, paves the way for the proposed Planetary Neutrino Monitoring network (PLEnuM) to overcome current limitations by combining global neutrino telescopes, increasing detection rates 30-fold, enabling full-sky monitoring, and discovering up to tens of new astrophysical neutrino sources.

Predicted Neutrino Signal Features of Core-Collapse Supernovae

Time: 2025-03-10

Authors: Lyla Choi, Adam Burrows, David Vartanyan

Abstract: This study of 24 3D core-collapse supernova simulations reveals that neutrino luminosity signals correlate with progenitor compactness, exhibit spiral SASI signatures in non-exploding models, display anisotropy increasing with compactness, show detectable trends in detectors like JUNO and DUNE, and link neutrino energy to gravitational-wave f-mode evolution.

Exploring $Δ$-resonance in neutron stars: implications from astrophysical and nuclear observations

Time: 2025-03-10

Authors: Vishal Parmar, Vivek Baruah Thapa, Monika Sinha, Ignazio Bombaci

Abstract: This study uses Bayesian inference within a density-dependent relativistic hadron framework to show that including Δ-resonances in neutron star matter softens the equation of state at low densities while maintaining high-density stiffness to support 2.2M⊙ stars, reconciling neutron star radius and tidal deformability constraints with implications for cooling, Urca processes, and f-mode oscillations.

Cosmic Ray bubbles from nova super remnants and their contribution to local cosmic ray spectra

Time: 2025-03-10

Authors: Rubén López-Coto, David Green, Javier Méndez-Gallego, Emma de Oña Wilhelmi

Abstract: This study concludes that while novae do not significantly contribute to galactic-scale cosmic ray transport, they substantially modify their local interstellar medium by reducing particle densities by over an order of magnitude over parsec-scale distances, as demonstrated through Fermi-LAT observations and cosmic ray propagation modeling.

Analytical description for light propagation with the source inside the black hole photon sphere

Time: 2025-03-10

Authors: Yuan-Xing Gao

Abstract: The study develops high-accuracy analytical formulas to describe photon azimuthal angle changes and travel times near a black hole's photon sphere, revealing that time delays (given by \(2(\pi - \phi_\mathrm{S}) u_\mathrm{m}\)) for photons emitted inside the sphere can produce light echoes in accretion events, aiding future searches for gravitational lensing signatures.

Picking-up Local GRB Candidates Based on Their Host Galaxies

Time: 2025-03-10

Authors: J. Wang, Y. Xu, L. J. Chen, C. Wu, L. P. Xin, E. W. Liang, J. Y. Wei

Abstract: This paper presents a model that rapidly identifies local gamma-ray burst (GRB) candidates by calculating cross-match probabilities with nearby galaxies using host galaxy mass distributions, achieving precision of 0.23–0.33 and recall of 0.75, with a dedicated web service developed for integration into the SVOM observational system.

Properties of low angular momentum general relativistic MHD accretion flows around black holes

Time: 2025-03-09

Authors: Samik Mitra

Abstract: This study introduces a novel GRMHD approach to analyze sub-Keplerian accretion flows around rotating black holes, incorporating radial and toroidal magnetic fields to examine how magnetic flux and BH spin influence flow dynamics and thermal emissions from magnetized disks.

Optical photon-counting observation of the Crab pulsar with Kanata telescope using prototype IMONY

Time: 2025-03-09

Authors: Takeshi Nakamori, Kazuaki Hashiyama, Rin Sato, Masayoshi Shoji, Anju Sato, Eiji Ono, Yuga Ouchi, Tatsuya Nakaoka, Koji S. Kawabata, Toshio Terasawa, Hiroaki Misawa, Fuminori Tsuchiya, Kazuhiro Takefuji, Yasuhiro Murata, Dai Takei, Kazuki Ueno, Hiroshi Akitaya

Abstract: The IMONY optical photon-counting system, featuring a 4×4 Geiger diode array and 100 ns timing resolution, successfully imaged the Crab pulsar on the Kanata telescope, confirming a 304±35 µs optical-radio lag consistent with prior studies.

Core Collapse Supernova Gravitational Wave Sourcing and Characterization based on Three-Dimensional Models

Time: 2025-03-09

Authors: R. Daniel Murphy, Anthony Mezzacappa, Eric J. Lentz, Pedro Marronetti

Abstract: This study combines spatial and modal decomposition in 3D simulations of two progenitor models to demonstrate that high-frequency gravitational wave emission from proto-neutron stars in core-collapse supernovae evolves over time, transitioning from surface accretion-driven ${}^2g$-modes to deeper convective overshoot-driven ${}^2f$-modes, revealing greater complexity than previously assessed.

The Correspondence Between Leaky-Box and Diffusion Models of Cosmic-Ray Propagation

Time: 2025-03-08

Authors: Ramanath Cowsik, Dawson Huth

Abstract: The mid-1960s leaky-box model's path-length distribution, analogous to impulse response functions in dynamical systems, remains valid and extensively used alongside modern diffusion-based computational tools like GALPROP and DRAGON when applied judiciously.
