The soft X-ray transient EP241021a: a cosmic explosion with a complex off-axis jet and cocoon from a massive progenitor

Theoretical modeling of approximate universality of tidally deformed neutron stars

Time: 2025-05-08

Authors: Takuya Katagiri, Gowtham Rishi Mukkamala, Kent Yagi

Abstract: This study demonstrates that neutron stars exhibit quasi-universal relations among tidal deformabilities, such as the I-Love relation, due to low compressibility, validated through a semi-analytic Tolman VII-based model, enabling simplified gravitational-wave modeling and enhanced nuclear matter insights while minimizing equation-of-state dependence.

Significant reflection and absorption effects in the X-ray emission of the Intermediate Polar IGR J17195-4100

Time: 2025-05-08

Authors: Elif Şafak, Şölen Balman, Gloria Sala

Abstract: The study of the Intermediate Polar IGR J17195-4100 using combined NuSTAR and XMM-Newton data (0.3-78.0 keV) reveals that its X-ray spectrum is best modeled with reflection, ionized and neutral absorption, a multi-temperature plasma (∼27 keV), and electron scattering effects, which significantly influence emission characteristics and must be considered for accurate spectral representation.

Late-blooming magnetars: awakening as ultra-long period objects after a dormant cooling epoch

Time: 2025-05-08

Authors: Arthur G. Suvorov, Clara Dehman, José A. Pons

Abstract: Magnetothermal modeling suggests that ultra-long period transients (ULPs) arise from "late-blooming" magnetars, where core electric currents during crust freezing lead to an initial ~0.1 Myr dormant phase, followed by crustal cooling-induced Hall-effect-driven failures that trigger radio pulses and slow rotation, forming a distinct class separate from soft gamma repeaters and anomalous X-ray pulsars.

The Emergence of Little Red Dots from Binary Massive Black Holes

Time: 2025-05-08

Authors: Kohei Inayoshi, Jinyi Shangguan, Xian Chen, Luis C. Ho, Zoltan Haiman

Abstract: The study proposes that Little Red Dots (LRDs), characterized by red optical and blue UV spectra, originate from compact binary black hole systems with mini-disks and a circum-binary disk, where binary torques create spectral gaps resolving luminosity overestimations and aligning with the Soltan argument, while predicting evolutionary links to gravitational wave sources as the binary orbit decays.

Hydrodynamic and Electromagnetic Discrepancies between Neutron Star and Black Hole Spacetimes

Time: 2025-05-08

Authors: Jonathan Gorard, James Juno, Ammar Hakim

Abstract: The study demonstrates that neutron stars' spacetime, influenced by equation-of-state-dependent higher multipole moments, leads to significant discrepancies in accretion dynamics and electromagnetic fields compared to Kerr metric black holes, challenging its use in neutron star simulations and impacting phenomena like gravitational collapse and jet launching.

Gaussian process representation of dispersion measure noise in pulsar wideband datasets

Time: 2025-05-08

Authors: Abhimanyu Susobhanan, Rutger van Haasteren

Abstract: The authors propose a generalized method to incorporate arbitrary dispersion measure (DM) variation models, including Gaussian processes, into pulsar timing and pulsar timing array analyses, enhancing existing wideband approaches that measure DM and arrival times to mitigate interstellar medium-induced delays and data challenges.

Abstract: This review examines neutron star equation of state models—hadronic, hybrid, and quark matter—analyzing their assumptions, predictions, constraints, and implications based on recent theoretical and observational studies, while outlining future research directions.

Orbital phase shifts of Type-I outbursts in EXO 2030+375

Time: 2025-05-08

Authors: Run-Ting Huang, Long Ji, Jing-Zhi Yan

Abstract: This paper reports another orbital phase shift in the high-mass X-ray binary EXO 2030+375, triggered by a 2021 giant outburst that moved Type-I outburst peaks from near periastron to 20 days post-periastron with gradual recovery, followed by missing outbursts recurring every two orbits, interpreted as resulting from precession of an eccentric Be disk.

A broadband spectral-timing study of QPOs in the bright black hole X-ray binary Swift J1727.8-1613

Time: 2025-05-08

Authors: Niek Bollemeijer, Phil Uttley, Bei You

Abstract: In August 2023, the bright black hole X-ray binary Swift J1727.8-1613 exhibited energy-dependent type-C quasi-periodic oscillations (QPOs) with a spectral pivot around 15–20 keV, distinct phase lags at fundamental and harmonic frequencies, and differing variability components identified via bispectrum and cross-spectrum analyses, showing similarities to MAXI J1535-571 and suggesting common QPO properties in some BHXRBs.
