Luminosity Function of collapsar Gamma-Ray Bursts:the Progenitor of Long Gamma-Ray Bursts Is Not Singular

Time: 2025-05-01

Authors: Yan-Kun Qu, Zhong-Xiao Man, Shuang-Xi Yi, Yu-Peng Yang

Abstract: This study analyzes Swift satellite data (2004-2019) to model gamma-ray burst luminosity functions, finding that significant redshift evolution (in luminosity or density) is required to explain observations, and concludes that a substantial fraction of long GRBs at low redshifts (z<2) are not collapsar-related, regardless of the evolutionary scenario.

Spectral-timing analysis of the kilohertz quasi-periodic oscillations and constraints on the mass of the neutron star in $4U 1636-536$ using AstroSat observations

Time: 2025-05-01

Authors: Suchismito Chattopadhyay, Soma Mandal, Ranjeev Misra

Abstract: This study analyzes kHz quasi-periodic oscillations (QPOs) and spectral properties in 4U 1636-536 using AstroSat data, revealing state transitions driven by spectral changes, the presence of low-frequency and twin kHz QPOs in soft states enabling a neutron star mass estimate of \(2.37 \pm 0.02 \, M_\odot\) via the relativistic precession model, and insights into corona size and radiative origins from time-lag and rms analysis.

Joint inference for gravitational wave signals and glitches using a data-informed glitch model

Time: 2025-05-01

Authors: Ann-Kristin Malz, John Veitch

Abstract: This study introduces a machine-learning approach using normalizing flows trained on Gravity Spy glitch data to construct a parameterized glitch model, which is incorporated into Bayesian inference with Bilby for simultaneous estimation of glitch and astrophysical signal parameters, effectively removing glitches, reducing bias, and significantly improving source parameter estimation.

Unified QMF equation of state for neutron star matter: Static and dynamic properties

Time: 2025-05-01

Authors: Zhonghao Tu, Xiangdong Sun, Shuochong Han, Zhiqiang Miao, Ang Li

Abstract: The study constructs quark mean field (QMF) models with varying nuclear symmetry energy slopes (\(L_0\)) to analyze neutron star properties, revealing crustal structural differences from hadronic models, \(L_0\)-dependent cooling behaviors influenced by dUrca processes, observational consistency in X-ray transients, instability window variations with \(L_0\) and mass, and viscosity parameterizations linked to symmetry energy.

Can We Probe Spacetime Non-commutativity Through Tidal Deformability of Compact Objects?

Time: 2025-05-01

Authors: Junting Peng, Yanbo Zhao, Antonino Marciano

Abstract: This study investigates spacetime non-commutativity's impact on tidal deformability of compact objects through de Sitter gauge theory, finding finite corrections at black-hole-compactness limits but concluding that neutron stars and boson stars are unsuitable for detecting such effects via gravitational wave observations due to insufficiently amplified non-commutative signatures.

Magnetized Accretion onto Neutron Stars: from Photon-trapped to Neutrino-cooled Flows

Time: 2025-05-01

Authors: Luciano Combi, Christopher Thompson, Daniel M. Siegel, Alexander Philippov, B. Ripperda

Abstract: This study presents three-dimensional simulations of hypercritical accretion onto neutron stars, demonstrating how radiatively inefficient flows transition to neutrino-cooled regimes mediated by toroidal magnetic fields, achieving steady states when neutrino emission balances accretion power, and develops a one-dimensional model with implications for neutron star spin and mass evolution.

Bayesian Inference of Core Properties of Hybrid Stars from Future High-Precision Measurements of Their Radii

Time: 2025-04-30

Authors: Bao-An Li, Xavier Grundler, Wen-Jie Xie, Nai-Bo Zhang

Abstract: Future high-precision neutron star radius measurements, analyzed via a Bayesian meta-model incorporating a hadron-quark phase transition, reveal that crust-core transition density is largely unaffected by data precision, while constraints on hadron-quark transition density, quark matter fraction, and high-density equation of state parameters depend critically on prior assumptions and improved data precision, aligning with experimental indications from RHIC.
