Possibility of Multi-Messenger Observations of Quasi-Periodic Eruptions with X-rays and Gravitational Waves

Time: 2025-05-15

Authors: Tomoya Suzuguchi, Hidetoshi Omiya, Hiroki Takeda

Abstract: The study finds that quasi-periodic X-ray eruptions (QPEs), likely caused by stellar-mass objects crossing accretion disks around supermassive black holes, have marginal overlap in optimal detection ranges for X-ray and gravitational wave (LISA) observations, predicting at most one joint detection during LISA's mission, but extending gravitational wave sensitivity to sub-millihertz frequencies could increase joint detection prospects tenfold.

Searching for Accreting Compact Binaries in eROSITA DR1

Time: 2025-05-15

Authors: Hao-Bin Liu, Wei-Min Gu, Yongqi Lu, Teng Liu, Jin-Zhong Liu

Abstract: This study identifies 22 accreting compact binary candidates in the eROSITA DR1 survey by combining Gaia astrometry, ZTF photometry, and X-ray-to-optical flux ratios with optical colors, excluding AGNs through short-period light curves, demonstrating an effective and scalable method for future multi-wavelength surveys to improve Galactic compact binary census.

A possible periodic RM evolution in the repeating FRB 20220529

Time: 2025-05-15

Authors: Yi-Fang Liang, Ye Li, Zhen-Fan Tang, Xuan Yang, Song-Bo Zhang, Yuan-Pei Yang, Fa-Yin Wang, Bao Wang, Di Xiao, Qing Zhao, Jun-Jie Wei, Jin-Jun Geng, Jia-Rui Niu, Jun-Shuo Zhang, Guo Chen, Min Fang, Xue-Feng Wu, Zi-Gao Dai, Wei-Wei Zhu, Peng Jiang, Bing Zhang

Abstract: A three-year FAST telescope study of FRB 20220529 detected a possible ∼200-day periodicity in its rotation measure (RM) evolution, aligning with a binary origin hypothesis due to significant RM fluctuations and recovery.

Generation of magnetic fields around black hole accretion discs due to non conservative radiation fields

Time: 2025-05-15

Authors: Mukesh Kumar Vyas, Asaf Pe'er

Abstract: The study demonstrates that rotational radiation fields in black hole accretion discs generate magnetic fields via charge separation, with magnetohydrodynamic simulations showing growth to weak (~Gauss) strengths in standard discs but reaching dynamically significant levels (~10⁵ Gauss) when a luminous inner corona is present, influencing disc dynamics, jet formation, and observational signatures like emission and polarization, potentially confirmable through future high-resolution observations.

SMIET: Fast and accurate synthesis of radio pulses from extensive air shower using simulated templates

Time: 2025-05-15

Authors: Mitja Desmet, Keito Watanabe, Tim Huege, Stijn Buitink

Abstract: The novel template synthesis method rapidly synthesizes radio emissions from cosmic-ray air showers by rescaling sliced simulations and adjusting geometrical phases, achieving high accuracy (4-6% amplitude scatter) when the shower maximum depth difference (ΔXₘₐₓ) is ≤100 g/cm², with a Python implementation (SMIET) validated for diverse conditions and machine-learning applications.

S241125n: Binary Black Hole Merger Produces Short GRB in AGN Disk

Time: 2025-05-15

Authors: Shu-Rui Zhang, Yu Wang, Ye-Fei Yuan, Hiromichi Tagawa, Yun-Feng Wei, Liang Li, Rong-Gen Cai

Abstract: The gravitational wave event S241125n's link to a short gamma-ray burst (GRB) and X-ray afterglow is modeled as a binary black hole merger in an active galactic nucleus (AGN), where hyper-Eddington accretion post-merger drives a jet producing the GRB, with observed spectral features consistent with AGN disk interactions and absorption, highlighting the need for host galaxy identification and infrared follow-up to validate the model.

Confronting recent light compact star observations with color-flavor locked quark matter

Time: 2025-05-15

Authors: K. Kourmpetis, P. Laskos-Patkos, Ch. C. Moustakidis

Abstract: This study reconciles the low masses and differing radii of the HESS J1731-347 remnant and PSR J1231-1411 pulsar using the CFL MIT bag model, showing that color-flavor locked strange quark matter explains both measurements while hybrid models require the HESS object to be a slow stable hybrid star to align with constraints on massive stars.

Implications of pulsar timing arrays for Gauss-Bonnet Inflation

Time: 2025-05-15

Authors: Reginald Christian Bernardo, Seoktae Koh, Gansukh Tumurtushaa

Abstract: Pulsar timing array observations support Gauss-Bonnet inflationary models by naturally accommodating a blue-tilted gravitational wave spectrum, consistent with current data, while imposing specific conditions on the inflaton potential and coupling function—demonstrated through two viable examples requiring the inflaton to climb the potential before inflation ends.

Prediction of radio-quiet gamma-ray pulsar distances using the Fundamental Plane relation

Time: 2025-05-15

Authors: Ekrem Oğuzhan Angüner

Abstract: This study employs machine learning methods (random forest, support vector regression) alongside the fundamental plane relation to predict distances for 62 radio-quiet gamma-ray pulsars without prior measurements, validating the relation’s applicability for pulsars with significant spectral cutoffs and curvature radiation dominance, thereby demonstrating a novel approach to estimate distances directly from gamma-ray emission data.
