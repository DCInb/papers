A Catalogue of Ultracompact Mass-Transferring White Dwarf Binaries

Time: 2025-05-15

Authors: Matthew J. Green, Jan van Roestel, Tin Long Sunny Wong

Abstract: This paper presents a comprehensive, publicly accessible catalogue (hosted on Zenodo with version control) of 123 confirmed and 29 candidate ultracompact binary systems—including AM CVn-type binaries and related objects—featuring white dwarf accretors with orbital periods under 70 minutes, summarizing their observed properties, recent advancements, open questions, and interdisciplinary connections to serve as a central resource for researchers.

Possibility of Multi-Messenger Observations of Quasi-Periodic Eruptions with X-rays and Gravitational Waves

Time: 2025-05-15

Authors: Tomoya Suzuguchi, Hidetoshi Omiya, Hiroki Takeda

Abstract: The study predicts at most one joint X-ray and gravitational wave detection of quasi-periodic eruptions (QPEs)—caused by stellar-mass objects orbiting supermassive black holes—during LISA's mission due to marginal sensitivity overlap, but extending gravitational wave observations to sub-millihertz frequencies could increase multi-messenger detections by an order of magnitude.

Searching for Accreting Compact Binaries in eROSITA DR1

Time: 2025-05-15

Authors: Hao-Bin Liu, Wei-Min Gu, Yongqi Lu, Teng Liu, Jin-Zhong Liu

Abstract: This study identifies 22 promising accreting compact binary candidates in the eROSITA DR1 survey by combining Gaia astrometry, ZTF photometry, and X-ray-to-optical flux ratios to distinguish accretion-driven systems from AGNs, demonstrating an effective, scalable method for future Galactic binary census efforts.

A possible periodic RM evolution in the repeating FRB 20220529

Time: 2025-05-15

Authors: Yi-Fang Liang, Ye Li, Zhen-Fan Tang, Xuan Yang, Song-Bo Zhang, Yuan-Pei Yang, Fa-Yin Wang, Bao Wang, Di Xiao, Qing Zhao, Jun-Jie Wei, Jin-Jun Geng, Jia-Rui Niu, Jun-Shuo Zhang, Guo Chen, Min Fang, Xue-Feng Wu, Zi-Gao Dai, Wei-Wei Zhu, Peng Jiang, Bing Zhang

Abstract: The study reports a possible ∼200-day periodicity in the rotation measure (RM) evolution of FRB~20220529, observed via FAST telescope over three years, with statistical significance (4.2σ and 3.5σ) supporting a binary origin linked to RM changes and burst rate patterns.

Generation of magnetic fields around black hole accretion discs due to non conservative radiation fields

Time: 2025-05-15

Authors: Mukesh Kumar Vyas, Asaf Pe'er

Abstract: The study demonstrates that rotational radiation fields in black hole accretion discs generate magnetic fields through charge separation, yielding weak fields (~Gauss) in standard discs but dynamically significant strengths (up to 10⁵ Gauss) in coronae-influenced regions, impacting disc/jet dynamics, emissions, and observational signatures, with future polarimetric observations poised to validate these predictions.

SMIET: Fast and accurate synthesis of radio pulses from extensive air shower using simulated templates

Time: 2025-05-15

Authors: Mitja Desmet, Keito Watanabe, Tim Huege, Stijn Buitink

Abstract: The researchers developed a hybrid template synthesis method to rapidly generate radio emissions from cosmic ray air showers by rescaling sliced simulations and adjusting phases, addressing the computational limitations of Monte-Carlo approaches, which was validated against CoREAS simulations (≤6% scatter for X_max differences ≤100 g/cm²) and implemented in the SMIET Python package for diverse conditions and machine-learning applications.

S241125n: Binary Black Hole Merger Produces Short GRB in AGN Disk

Time: 2025-05-15

Authors: Shu-Rui Zhang, Yu Wang, Ye-Fei Yuan, Hiromichi Tagawa, Yun-Feng Wei, Liang Li, Rong-Gen Cai

Abstract: The gravitational wave event S241125n, linked to a short gamma-ray burst and X-ray afterglow, is modeled as a binary black hole merger in an active galactic nucleus (AGN) environment, where hyper-Eddington accretion produces a jet consistent with observed spectral features, highlighting the need for host galaxy identification and further infrared observations to test the scenario.

Confronting recent light compact star observations with color-flavor locked quark matter

Time: 2025-05-15

Authors: K. Kourmpetis, P. Laskos-Patkos, Ch. C. Moustakidis

Abstract: This study reconciles the similar low masses and differing radii of the HESS J1731-347 remnant and PSR J1231-1411 pulsar using the CFL MIT bag model, showing compatibility with astrophysical constraints via strange quark matter stability or by considering HESS J1731-347 as a slow stable hybrid star, despite early phase transitions otherwise conflicting with massive star observations.

Implications of pulsar timing arrays for Gauss-Bonnet Inflation

Time: 2025-05-15

Authors: Reginald Christian Bernardo, Seoktae Koh, Gansukh Tumurtushaa

Abstract: The study shows that Gauss-Bonnet inflationary models align with pulsar timing array (PTA) data and cosmological observations, naturally accommodating a blue-tilted gravitational wave spectrum by requiring the inflaton to ascend its potential before descending, as evidenced by two viable models.
