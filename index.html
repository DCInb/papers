The Late-time Afterglow of GW170817 and Implications for Jet Dynamics

Time: 2025-04-16

Authors: Aman Katira, Kunal P. Mooley, Kenta Hotokezaka

Abstract: The reanalysis of GW170817's X-ray afterglow data identifies discrepancies in data processing techniques and, combined with updated panchromatic analysis, finds no evidence for a new afterglow component, reveals a mildly relativistic jet, and a shallower-than-expected light curve decline suggesting late-time energy injection or velocity-dependent microphysics parameters, with implications for jet dynamics.

Suppression of composition g-modes in chemically-equilibrating warm neutron stars

Time: 2025-04-16

Authors: Tianqi Zhao, Peter B. Rau, Alexander Haber, Steven P. Harris, Constantinos Constantinou, Sophia Han

Abstract: This study demonstrates that in warm neutron stars, chemical equilibration-induced bulk viscosity significantly damps composition g-modes at high temperatures through resonant interactions, while f-modes remain unaffected, and introduces the dynamic sound speed as a critical descriptor for oscillation properties under realistic equations of state and thermal conditions.

Exploring GRBs and supernovae connection: does a superluminous hypernova population exist?

Time: 2025-04-16

Authors: Achille Fiore, Ludovica Crosato Menegazzi, Giulia Stratta

Abstract: This review explores the connection between ultra-long gamma-ray bursts (GRBs) like GRB 111209A and superluminous supernovae (SLSNe) such as SN 2011kl, examining observational and theoretical evidence that suggests a magnetar-driven origin as a plausible but inconclusive mechanism, with further research needed to resolve uncertainties in progenitor pathways and explosion models.

Magnetically driven outflows in 3D common-envelope evolution of massive stars

Time: 2025-04-16

Authors: Marco Vetter, Friedrich K. Roepke, Fabian R. N. Schneider, Rüdiger Pakmor, Sebastian Ohlmann, Javier Morán-Fraile, Mike Y. M. Lau, Giovanni Leidi, Damien Gagnier, Robert Andrassy

Abstract: Recent 3D magnetohydrodynamic simulations of a common-envelope interaction between a red supergiant and a black hole reveal that magnetically driven bipolar outflows eject 6.4% of the envelope mass, reduce final orbital separation by 24% compared to non-magnetic scenarios by enhancing angular momentum extraction, and suggest an adjusted α_CE-formalism incorporating outflow mass effects.

Towards asteroseismology of neutron stars with physics-informed neural networks

Time: 2025-04-16

Authors: Dimitra Tseneklidou, Alejandro Torres-Forne, Pablo Cerda-Duran

Abstract: This study introduces a physically informed neural network (PINN) method that accurately solves eigenvalue problems for neutron star oscillations in a simplified scenario, demonstrating potential to reveal interior structure and Equation of State via gravitational waves while offering adaptability for future complex systems.

Pulsar Coherent Radio Emission from Solitons : Average Emission Properties

Time: 2025-04-16

Authors: Rahul Basu, Dipanjan Mitra, George I. Melikidze, Krzysztof Maciesiak

Abstract: A study demonstrates that coherent curvature radiation from charged solitons in pulsar magnetospheres explains key observational features, including radius-to-frequency mapping, power law spectra with core-cone steepening, and polarization geometry, aligning with plasma instability models and emission mechanisms.

Origin of the Moon's Earth-like isotopic composition from giant impact on a differential rotating proto-Earth

Time: 2025-04-16

Authors: Wenshuai Liu

Abstract: The study proposes that a sub-Mars-sized impactor colliding with a differentially rotating proto-Earth (fast-spinning outer layer, slower inner region) could generate a Moon-forming debris disk predominantly from Earth-derived materials, resolving isotopic similarity and angular momentum constraints of the Earth-Moon system.

A Heavy-Metal Scenario of Ultra-High-Energy Cosmic Rays

Time: 2025-04-16

Authors: Jakub Vícha, Alena Bakalová, Ana L. Müller, Olena Tkachenko, Maximilian K. Stadelmaier

Abstract: The study proposes that ultra-high-energy cosmic rays above ~40 EeV could consist predominantly of iron nuclei, reconciling Pierre Auger Observatory data by adjusting hadronic interaction models to predict larger shower maximum depths (Xmax) than currently estimated.

Jets from a stellar-mass black hole are as relativistic as those from supermassive black holes

Time: 2025-04-16

Authors: Xian Zhang, Wenfei Yu, Francesco Carotenuto, Rob Fender, Sara Motta, Arash Bahramian, James C. A. Miller-Jones, Thomas D. Russell, Stephane Corbel, Patrick A. Woudt, Pikky Atri, Christian Knigge, Gregory R. Sivakoff, Andrew K. Hughes, Jakob van den Eijnden, James Matthews, Maria C. Baglio, Payaswini Saikia

Abstract: Radio interferometry observations of the Galactic black hole X-ray binary 4U 1543-47 revealed highly relativistic jets (Lorentz factor ∼8, minimum 4.6 at 95% confidence) during a single outburst, demonstrating that stellar-mass black holes can launch jets as relativistic as those in active galactic nuclei, challenging prior assumptions of their lower relativistic nature.

A Novel Jet Model for the Novikov-Thorne Disk and its Observable Impact

Time: 2025-04-16

Authors: Sen Guo, Pei Wang, Ke-Jian He, Guo-Ping Li, Xiao-Xiong Zeng, Wen-Hao Deng

Abstract: The proposed jet-modified Novikov-Thorne disk model, which integrates jet luminosity and synchrotron radiation, demonstrates a 33.5% increase in total accretion disk luminosity and highlights the necessity of including jet effects across observational inclinations for accurate flux estimation in black hole systems, impacting future astronomical observations.
