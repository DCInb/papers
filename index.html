Inferring Black Hole Spin from Interferometric Measurements of the First Photon Ring: A Geometric Approach

Time: 2025-02-27

Authors: Lennox S. Keeble, Alejandro Cárdenas-Avendaño, Daniel C. M. Palumbo

Abstract: This study presents a geometric method using the asymmetry of the first photon ring’s interferometric shape at low inclinations to infer black hole spin, demonstrating its potential for estimating spin despite challenges from noise, emission models, and astrophysical variability, while highlighting its promise for future space-based VLBI missions like the Black Hole Explorer (BHEX).

Nyquist-resolving gravitational waves via orbital frequency-based refinement

Time: 2025-02-27

Authors: William K. Black, David Neilsen, Eric W. Hirschmann, David F. Van Komen, Milinda Fernando

Abstract: This study introduces ORBIT, a method that dynamically adjusts mesh refinement in numerical relativity simulations using a binary's orbital frequency to meet Nyquist criteria, improving gravitational wave resolution and computational efficiency by reducing waveform noise and costs while enhancing Dendro-GR's performance, aiding preparation for next-generation detectors.

Inferring the Equation of State from Neutron Star Observables via Machine Learning

Time: 2025-02-27

Authors: N. K. Patra, Tuhin Malik, Helena Pais, Kai Zhou, B. K. Agrawal, Constança Providência

Abstract: Using symbolic regression on diverse equations of state, this study identifies strong correlations between neutron star observables—including maximum mass linked to pressure and baryon density at ~800 MeV.fm⁻³ and the EoS expressed via radius and tidal deformability—providing an efficient framework to decode dense matter properties directly from observational data.

Timing and Spectral Evolution of the Magnetar 1E 1841-045 in Outburst

Time: 2025-02-27

Authors: G. Younes, S. K. Lander, M. G. Baring, M. L. Bause, R. Stewart, Z. Arzoumanian, H. Dinh Thi, T. Enoto, K. Gendreau, T. Guver, A. K. Harding, W. C. G. Ho, C. -P. Hu, A. van Kooten, C. Kouveliotou, N. Di Lalla, A. McEwen, M. Negro, Mason Ng, D. M. Palmer, L. G. Spitler, Zorawar Wadiasingh

Abstract: Observations of magnetar 1E 1841-045 following its August 2024 outburst revealed a 1.6-fold X-ray flux increase with a decaying non-thermal component, a spin-up glitch, and a migrating pulse peak—the second such case after SGR 1830-0645—indicating magnetic field untwisting and crustal motion as potential common magnetar behaviors.

On Bactrian glitch-size distributions

Time: 2025-02-27

Authors: Anantharaman Sekharipuram Viswanathan, Dipankar Bhattacharya

Abstract: The study identifies a bimodal glitch-size distribution in PSR J0537-6910 via Bayesian analysis and superfluid vortex simulations, linking it primarily to annular pinning variations and weakly to sectorial triggers, thereby advancing understanding of neutron star vortex dynamics and glitch mechanisms.

Bayesian inferences on covariant density functionals from multimessenger astrophysical data. I. Nucleonic models

Time: 2025-02-27

Authors: Jia-Jie Li, Yu Tian, Armen Sedrakian

Abstract: This Bayesian analysis incorporating extreme astrophysical data shows that covariant density functional models with density-dependent meson-nucleon couplings support compact star masses up to \(2.4\)–\(2.5\,M_{\odot}\) and provide a robust framework for diverse phenomena, yet face challenges in reconciling low-density equation of state softening with ultra-compact objects like HESS J1731-347, yielding tighter parameter constraints and a minimum radius limit of \(\sim 12\) km due to isovector sector limitations.

Radio Detection of ultra-high-energy Cosmic-Ray Air Showers

Time: 2025-02-27

Authors: Frank G. Schröder

Abstract: Radio antennas offer a weather-independent, accurate method for detecting cosmic-ray air showers by analyzing geomagnetic-induced radio signals, complementing traditional techniques and enhancing measurements when integrated with muon detectors, with advancements driving their expanded role in future ultra-high-energy cosmic ray, photon, and neutrino research, as reviewed in this article's discussion of emission physics, observatories, and recent results.

Science Potential and Technical Design of the IceCube-Gen2 Surface Array (UHECR 2024)

Time: 2025-02-27

Authors: Frank G. Schroeder

Abstract: IceCube-Gen2, integrating a surface array (scintillation panels and radio antennas) and an extended optical array, enhances cosmic-ray research at PeV-EeV energies with a 30-fold larger aperture, enabling advanced studies of air shower particle physics, Galactic cosmic-ray astrophysics, and PeV photon detection through improved mass composition and anisotropy measurements.

H I absorption line and anomalous dispersion in the radio pulses of PSR B1937+21

Time: 2025-02-27

Authors: Jinchen Jiang, Shunshun Cao, Kejia Lee, Bojun Wang, Heng Xu, Siyuan Chen, Yanjun Guo, Peng Jiang, Weicong Jing, Jiguang Lu, Jiangwei Xu, Renxin Xu, Zihan Xue

Abstract: Using the Five-hundred-meter Aperture Spherical radio Telescope (FAST), observations of the millisecond pulsar PSR B1937+21 revised its kinematic distance to a nearer 9.4±0.5 kpc in the Perseus Arm, identified potential tiny-scale atomic structures (TSAS) from absorption line variations, and confirmed previously reported anomalous dispersion effects at the HI line.

Application of Machine Learning to Identify Radio Pulses of Air Showers at the South Pole (ARENA 2024)

Time: 2025-02-27

Authors: Frank G. Schroeder, Abdul Rehman

Abstract: Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) trained on simulations and real background data significantly enhance the detection and cleaning of radio signals from cosmic-ray air showers, yielding five times more events than traditional methods while improving purity and maintaining angular resolution, experimentally confirming their efficacy in lowering detection thresholds for hybrid particle-radio detector arrays.
