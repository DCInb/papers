A Neural Operator-Based Emulator for Regional Shallow Water Dynamics

Time: 2025-02-20

Authors: Peter Rivera-Casillas, Sourav Dutta, Shukai Cai, Mark Loveland, Kamaljyoti Nath, Khemraj Shukla, Corey Trahan, Jonghyun Lee, Matthew Farthing, Clint Dawson

Abstract: MITONet, a novel autoregressive neural emulator using dimensionality reduction, efficiently approximates high-dimensional PDE solvers to enable accurate real-time forecasting of coastal hydrodynamic processes (e.g., tide-driven dynamics), demonstrating extrapolation in time and parametric space for climate adaptation and infrastructure planning.

Catching the wisps: Stellar mass-loss limits from low-frequency radio observations

Time: 2025-02-20

Authors: Sanne Bloot, Harish K. Vedantham, Robert D. Kavanagh, Joseph R. Callingham, Benjamin J. S. Pope

Abstract: The study employs low-frequency radio observations from LOFAR and a simple stellar wind model to directly determine upper mass-loss rate limits for 19 M dwarf stars, achieving sensitivity near the solar mass-loss rate and extending constraints to later spectral types (up to M6) and greater distances (50 pc), with potential to scale to ∼1000 stars using future surveys like SKA.

MOJAVE -- XXII. Brightness temperature distributions and geometric profiles along parsec-scale AGN jets

Time: 2025-02-20

Authors: E. V. Kravchenko, I. N. Pashchenko, D. C. Homan, Y. Y. Kovalev, M. L. Lister, A. B. Pushkarev, E. Ros, T. Savolainen

Abstract: This study of 447 AGN jets using 15 GHz VLBA data (1994–2019) reveals conical expansion (jet size scaling as \( R \propto r^{1.02} \)) and brightness temperature gradients (\( T_b \propto r^{-2.82} \)), with non-monotonic profiles attributed to shocks, helical structures, or jet recollimation, supporting jet features as optically thin moving blobs and MHD acceleration at geometry transitions from conical to parabolic.

Introduction to magnetic star-planet interactions

Time: 2025-02-18

Authors: A. Strugarek, E. Shkolnik

Abstract: This chapter reviews magnetic star-planet interactions in close-in systems, exploring observational and theoretical approaches to understand their effects on stellar activity, planetary atmospheres, and orbital dynamics, while summarizing key detections and the physical mechanisms driving these interactions.

Accretion onto supermassive and intermediate mass black holes in cosmological simulations

Time: 2025-02-18

Authors: Rainer Weinberger, Aklant Bhowmick, Laura Blecha, Greg Bryan, Johannes Buchner, Lars Hernquist, Julie Hlavacek-Larrondo, Volker Springel

Abstract: The study introduces a flexible two-parameter gas inflow model with an unresolved accretion disk component to manage uncertainties in cosmological simulations of black hole accretion, finding that while accretion mechanisms are hard to distinguish for supermassive black holes via luminous AGN, significant differences emerge in intermediate mass black holes at high redshifts regarding accretion rates and merger distributions.
