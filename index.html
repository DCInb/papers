Dynamical tides in neutron stars with first-order phase transitions: the role of the discontinuity mode

Time: 2025-04-23

Authors: Jonas P. Pereira, Lucas Tonetto, Michał Bejger, J. Leszek Zdunik, Paweł Haensel

Abstract: This study demonstrates that during binary neutron star inspirals, dynamical tides in hybrid stars can induce significant gravitational wave phase shifts through g-mode excitations from phase transitions, rivaling the f-mode's impact and potentially affecting detector observations and parameter estimation, highlighting their importance in probing neutron star equations of state and detecting dense-matter phase transitions.

Fermi-LAT and FAST observation of the gamma-ray binary HESS J0632+057

Time: 2025-04-23

Authors: Yanlv Yang, Dengke Zhou, Zihao Zhao, Jian Li, Diego F. Torres, Pei Wang

Abstract: A 15-year Fermi-LAT analysis of HESS J0632+057 reveals a GeV power-law spectrum with a turnover at 10-100 GeV, orbital-phase flux enhancement consistent with X-ray/TeV data (suggesting a shared particle origin), and FAST radio observations detecting no pulsations, likely due to its dense stellar wind environment.

A LOFAR-style reconstruction of cosmic-ray air showers with SKA-Low

Time: 2025-04-23

Authors: A. Corstanje, S. Buitink, S. Bouma, M. Desmet, J. R. Hörandel, T. Huege, P. Laub, K. Mulrey, A. Nelles, O. Scholten, K. Terveer, S. Thoudam, K. Watanabe

Abstract: The high-density SKA-Low radio telescope enables precise cosmic-ray composition measurements between \(10^{16}\) and \(10^{18}\) eV by applying LOFAR's reconstruction methods to simulations, with accuracy influenced by dynamic range, beamforming, and statistical/systematic uncertainties.

The FAST Discovery of a Millisecond Pulsar Hidden in the Harmonics of PSR J2129+1210A (M15A)

Time: 2025-04-23

Authors: Yinfeng Dai, Zhichen Pan, Lei Qian, Liyun Zhang, Dejiang Yin, Baoda Li, Yaowei Li, Yuxiao Wu, Yujie Lian

Abstract: Researchers discovered an isolated millisecond pulsar, M15O, in the core-collapsed globular cluster M15, whose spin period's proximity to the 10th harmonic of pulsar M15A led to its previous oversight, prompting a recommendation for enhanced spectral analysis in pulsar searches, with its unique spin derivatives and closest position to the cluster's center suggesting an origin in M15's dense core.

Cyclic Zoom: Multi-scale GRMHD Modeling of Black Hole Accretion and Feedback

Time: 2025-04-23

Authors: Minghao Guo, James M. Stone, Eliot Quataert, Volker Springel

Abstract: The "cyclic zoom" method in GRMHD simulations accelerates black hole accretion studies by dynamically adjusting spatial resolution to capture phenomena across vast scales, enabling 10^5-fold speed improvements and successfully modeling diverse scenarios like Bondi accretion and rotating tori with both steady and secular evolution.

Binarity at LOw Metallicity (BLOeM): Bayesian inference of natal kicks from inert black hole binaries

Time: 2025-04-23

Authors: R. Willcox, P. Marchant, A. Vigna-Gómez, H. Sana, J. Bodensteiner, K. Deshmukh, M. Esseldeurs, M. Fabry, V. Hénault-Brunet, S. Janssens, L. Mahy, L. Patrick, D. Pauli, M. Renzo, A. A. C. Sander, T. Shenar, L. A. C. van Son, M. Stoop

Abstract: This study presents a Bayesian framework to infer natal kicks (<27 km/s) and mass loss (<2.9 Msun) during black hole formation using combined spectroscopic and astrometric data from the BLOeM survey and Gaia, demonstrating its effectiveness with the inert black hole binary VFTS 243.

FAST Observation and Results for Core Collapse Globular Cluster M15 and NGC 6517

Time: 2025-04-23

Authors: Yuxiao Wu, Dejiang Yin, Yu Pan, Liyun Zhang, Zhichen Pan, Lei Qian, Baoda Li, Yinfeng Dai, Yaowei Li, Xingnan Zhang, Minghui Li, Yifeng Li

Abstract: This study uses the FAST telescope to monitor pulsars in M15, confirming all known ones (including the unexpectedly detectable M15C) and discover new isolated pulsars in NGC 6517, increasing its total to 17, far surpassing earlier predictions.

Scattering of stellar-mass black holes and gravitational wave bremsstrahlung radiation in AGN disks

Time: 2025-04-23

Authors: Peter Lott, Christian Faulhaber, Joshua Brandt, Gongjie Li, Hareesh Bhaskar, Laura Cadonati

Abstract: This study analyzes gravitational-wave bremsstrahlung from stellar-mass black holes in AGN disks during hyperbolic encounters, finding higher detection likelihood around lower-mass supermassive black holes (~10⁵–⁶ M☉) and estimating LIGO-detectable rates of 0.08–1194 Gpc⁻³ yr⁻¹ via Monte Carlo simulations (3.2 Gpc⁻³ yr⁻¹ fiducial), while providing corresponding gravitational wave templates.

Detection of X-ray Polarization in the Hard State of IGR J17091-3624: Spectro-Polarimetric Study with IXPE and NuSTAR Data

Time: 2025-04-23

Authors: Dipak Debnath, Subham Srimani, Hsiang-Kuang Chang

Abstract: The joint IXPE and NuSTAR spectro-polarimetric analysis of IGR J17091–3624 revealed significant energy-dependent X-ray polarization (11.3% in 2–8 keV, peaking at 29.9% in 6–8 keV), non-thermal emission from a Compton cloud indicating a hard spectral state, and estimated the black hole’s mass as \(14.8^{+4.7}_{-3.4}~M_\odot\) with spin \(a^* \sim 0.54\).

Accretion Flow Properties of MAXI J1834-021 During Its Double-Outbursts In 2023

Time: 2025-04-23

Authors: Dipak Debnath, Hsiang-Kuang Chang

Abstract: The 2023 faint outburst of MAXI J1834-021, analyzed via NICER data, comprised a primary and mini-outburst with monotonically decreasing QPO frequencies and persistent hard spectral states dominated by nonthermal emissions, classifying it as double 'failed' outbursts due to the absence of softer spectral states.
