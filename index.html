Does the 220 PeV Event at KM3NeT Point to New Physics?

Time: 2025-02-28

Authors: Vedran Brdar, Dibya S. Chattopadhyay

Abstract: The KM3NeT observation of a 100+ PeV neutrino conflicting with IceCube's absence of such high-energy events can be explained by sterile neutrinos converting to active ones via new physics-induced oscillations over ~100 km distances, suggesting neutrino telescopes may be detecting new physics.

Reconstruction of spider system's observables from orbital period modulations via the Applegate mechanism

Time: 2025-02-28

Authors: Vittorio De Falco, Amodio Carleo, Alessandro Ridolfi, Alessandro Corongiu

Abstract: This study develops a time-evolution model based on the Applegate mechanism to explain orbital period variations in redback and black widow pulsars, applying it to sources 47 Tuc W and 47 Tuc O to analyze magnetic field dynamics in companion stars and compare results with existing literature.

Probing the interstellar medium toward GRB 221009A through X-ray dust scattering

Time: 2025-02-28

Authors: B. Vaia, Ž. Bošnjak, A. Bracco, S. Campana, P. Esposito, V. Jelić, A. Sacchi, A. Tiengo

Abstract: The detection of 21 X-ray dust-scattering rings around GRB 221009A enabled high-resolution mapping of the Galactic ISM, revised the GRB's soft X-ray fluence downward by ~35% (still exceeding model predictions), and through spectral variability analysis constrained the host galaxy's hydrogen column density to \( N_{\rm{H,z=0.151}} = (3.7 \pm 0.3) \times 10^{21} \, \rm{cm^{-2}} \), providing critical insights into the host's ISM and implications for afterglow modeling and supernova studies.

Pattern and Origin for the Extreme $γ$-ray Flares of 3C 454.3 and 3C 279: An Astrophysical Critical Damper?

Time: 2025-02-28

Authors: Haiyun Zhang, Dahai Yan, Jianeng Zhou, Li Zhang, Niansheng Tang

Abstract: The study applies Gaussian process methods with SHO, DRW, and Matérn-3/2 kernels to analyze extreme γ-ray flares in 3C 454.3 and 3C 279, finding a preference for over-damped SHO and Matérn-3/2 kernels, revealing abrupt energy release post-disturbance and characteristic damping timescales (2-3 days for 3C 454.3, 3-5 days for 3C 279) that constrain the size of their energy dissipation regions.

Discovery of 15 new pulsars at high Galactic Latitudes with FAST

Time: 2025-02-28

Authors: Xin Xu, Shi Dai, Qijun Zhi, Juntao Bai, Joanna Berteaud, Francesca Calore, Maïca Clavel, Weiwei Zhu, Di Li, Rushuang Zhao, Renxin Xu, Guojun Qiao

Abstract: Using the FAST telescope, 15 pulsars (11 normal, 4 millisecond) were discovered in a high Galactic latitude survey near the Galactic Bulge, including three binary millisecond pulsars with orbital periods of ~3.1, 4.6, and 12.5 days, with timing solutions for three normal pulsars and observed mode-changing/subpulse drifting in four pulsars, suggesting these likely reside in the Galactic disk but demonstrating the survey's potential to enhance understanding of the Bulge's millisecond pulsar population.

The Chinese pulsar timing array data release I. Polarimetry for 56 millisecond pulsars

Time: 2025-02-28

Authors: Jiangwei Xu, Jinchen Jiang, Heng Xu, Bojun Wang, Zihan Xue, Siyuan Chen, Yanjun Guo, R. Nicolas Caballero, Kejia Lee, Jianping Yuan, Yonghua Xu, Jingbo Wang, Longfei Hao, Zhixuan Li, Yuxiang Huang, Zezhong Xu, Jintao Luo, Jinlin Han, Peng Jiang, Zhiqiang Shen, Min Wang, Na Wang, Renxin Xu, Xiangping Wu, Lei Qian, Youling Yue, Xin Guan, Menglin Huang, Chun Sun, Yan Zhu

Abstract: Using the Five-hundred-meter Aperture Spherical radio Telescope (FAST), researchers observed polarization profiles of 56 millisecond pulsars, revealing new features in three previously unreported pulsars, widespread weak components and interpulses, full rotational emission in six, and evidence that magnetospheric wave propagation shapes their polarization characteristics.

Photon-ALP beam propagation from Mrk 501

Time: 2025-02-28

Authors: L. J. Dong, Y. G. Zheng, S. J. Kang, C. Y. Yang

Abstract: The study demonstrates that photon-ALP oscillations with axion-like particles (ALPs) of mass \(m_a \sim 10^{-10}\) eV and coupling \(g_{a\gamma} \sim 0.417 \times 10^{-11} \, \text{GeV}^{-1}\) in astrophysical magnetic fields can reduce TeV \(\gamma\)-ray absorption by extragalactic background light, aligning with observed spectra from Mrk 501.

Numerical studies of (in)stabilities of shocks in perturbed advective flows around black holes

Time: 2025-02-28

Authors: Junxing Zhou, Junxiang Huang, Xin Chang, Toru Okuda, Chandra B. Singh

Abstract: Using two-dimensional hydrodynamic simulations, we investigated the stability of shocked, hot advective accretion flows around black holes under non-axisymmetric perturbations, observing quasi-periodic oscillations (QPOs) in the 0.44–146.67 Hz range that span the low- to high-frequency QPOs detected in black hole X-ray binaries.

Resummed multi-line gamma-ray spectra for Cherenkov Telescopes from heavy spin-1 dark matter

Time: 2025-02-28

Authors: Motoko Fujiwara, Martin Vollmann

Abstract: This study explores a TeV-scale electroweak spin-1 dark matter candidate, demonstrating that next-to-leading-log resummation via SCET yields universal gamma-ray spectral features (including two peaks from $\gamma\gamma/Z\gamma$ and $Z'$ channels) across different dark matter spins, reducing theoretical uncertainties and enhancing prospects for indirect detection in future observations.

On the Extremely X-ray Variable Active Galactic Nuclei in the XMM-LSS Field

Time: 2025-02-27

Authors: Zijian Zhang, Bin Luo, Linhua Jiang, W. N. Brandt, Jian Huang, Qingling Ni

Abstract: A systematic investigation of extremely X-ray variable active galactic nuclei (AGNs) in the XMM-SERVS region attributes their extreme variability to changing accretion states (five AGNs, supported by multiwavelength data and a confirmed changing-look AGN) or obscuration by clumpy accretion-disk winds with variable column densities and covering factors (three AGNs).
