An active repeating fast radio burst in a magnetized eruption environment

Time: 2025-03-06

Authors: Y. Li, S. B. Zhang, Y. P. Yang, C. W. Tsai, X. Yang, C. J. Law, R. Anna-Thomas, X. L. Chen, K. J. Lee, Z. F. Tang, D. Xiao, H. Xu, X. L. Yang, G. Chen, Y. Feng, D. Z. Li, R. Mckinven, J. R. Niu, K. Shin, B. J. Wang, C. F. Zhang, Y. K. Zhang, D. J. Zhou, Y. H. Zhu, Z. G. Dai, C. M. Chang, J. J. Geng, J. L. Han, L. Hu, D. Li, R. Luo, C. H. Niu, D. D. Shi, T. R. Sun, X. F. Wu, W. W. Zhu, P. Jiang, B. Zhang

Abstract: The repeating fast radio burst FRB 20220529, originating from a disk galaxy at redshift 0.1839, exhibited an abrupt Faraday rotation measure (RM) surge from 21 ± 96 rad/m² to 1976.9 rad/m² in December 2023—over twenty times the prior variation—before returning to baseline within two weeks, suggesting a dense magnetized clump (likely from a stellar flare-induced coronal mass ejection) traversed the line of sight, implying the FRB resides in a binary system with a companion star.

On the progenitor of the type Ia supernova remnant 0509-67.5

Time: 2025-03-06

Authors: Noam Soker

Abstract: The study concludes that the Type Ia supernova remnant 0509-67.5 likely originated from a spherical explosion within an old planetary nebula, favoring core-degenerate and double degenerate merger-delay scenarios due to the absence of a surviving companion star, suggesting a higher prevalence of such events and attributing observed calcium structures and fast-moving elements to Rayleigh-Taylor instabilities during the explosion.

Inferring kilonova ejecta photospheric properties from early blackbody spectra

Time: 2025-03-06

Authors: Gilad Sadeh

Abstract: This study introduces analytic corrections to standard blackbody fitting for kilonovae, showing that relativistic effects (Doppler boosting, time delay, temperature evolution) cause overestimations of photospheric velocity and temperature by up to 50%, which are reduced to within 10% with corrections, as demonstrated for AT2017gfo and GRB211211A where prior estimates were 10%-40% too high.

Impact of multi-messenger spectral modelling on blazar-neutrino associations

Time: 2025-03-06

Authors: Julian Kuhlmann, Francesca Capel

Abstract: This study examines how incorporating theoretical proton-photon interaction models into a Bayesian analysis of IceCube neutrino data results in fewer but stronger high-energy neutrino associations with blazars like TXS 0506+056, enhancing the interpretability of source-neutrino connections compared to traditional power-law spectral assumptions.

Searching for Ultralight Dark Matter Solitons with Gravitational Waves

Time: 2025-03-06

Authors: Silvia Gasparotto

Abstract: Our study demonstrates that solitonic cores formed by Ultralight Dark Matter (ULDM) influence gravitational waves (GWs), detectable via next-generation observatories like LISA, Einstein Telescope, and Cosmic Explorer, where continuous GWs from Galactic Center neutron stars probe ULDM masses \(m \lesssim 10^{-22}\, \mathrm{eV}\) and surpass existing constraints for \(m \lesssim 10^{-20}\, \mathrm{eV}\), while GW modulation in massive extragalactic halos reveals solitons, underscoring GW-based methods' potential to uncover ULDM properties.

Introducing APERTURE: A GPU-based General Relativistic Particle-in-Cell Simulation Framework

Time: 2025-03-06

Authors: Alexander Y. Chen, Martin Luepker, Yajie Yuan

Abstract: The authors present Aperture, a GPU-accelerated, modular general relativistic particle-in-cell code framework used to study spark gaps and plasma injection in black hole magnetospheres, demonstrating observer-dependent gap dynamics that reconcile previous discrepancies between 1D and 2D simulations.

Super heavy dark matter origin of the PeV neutrino event: KM3-230213A

Time: 2025-03-06

Authors: Kazunori Kohri, Partha Kumar Paul, Narendra Sahu

Abstract: The KM3NeT observation of the ultra-high-energy neutrino event KM3-230213A is analyzed as a potential decay product of super-heavy dark matter (SHDM) in a minimal type-I seesaw extension, yielding constraints on SHDM mass (1.7×10⁸–5.5×10⁹ GeV) and lifetime (6.3×10²⁹–3.6×10²⁹ s) while linking the event to domain wall-induced gravitational waves.

Can we disentangle between the emission of an accretion disc around a single black hole and a circumbinary disc ?

Time: 2025-03-06

Authors: Peggy Varniere, Raphaël Mignon-Risse, Fabien Casse

Abstract: This study investigates whether features of circumbinary discs around binary black holes (BBHs), such as periodic modulations from a "lump," can reliably distinguish BBHs from single black hole (SBH) systems, finding that while SBH models can mimic many features, the presence of dual variabilities (from the lump and binary periods) serves as a critical indicator for BBHs in certain parameter ranges.

Magnetars

Time: 2025-03-06

Authors: Nanda Rea, Davide De Grandis

Abstract: Magnetars, the most magnetic neutron stars discovered through X-ray flares in the late 1970s, exhibit multi-band emissions and serve as unique laboratories for extreme physics, with current research exploring their connections to supernovae, gamma-ray bursts, fast radio bursts, and transient cosmic phenomena.

The Firmamento platform

Time: 2025-03-06

Authors: Paolo Giommi

Abstract: Firmamento is an online astronomical research platform designed for studying blazars and multi-messenger emitters, offering access to diverse data (imaging, spectral, timing), analytical and machine learning tools, the ERCI for identifying high-energy source counterparts, SED generation, VHE detectability estimation, and integration of user-provided data.
