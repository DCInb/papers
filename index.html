The middle-aged pulsar PSR J1741-2054 and its bow-shock nebula in far ultraviolet

Time: 2025-03-13

Authors: Vadim Abramkin, George G. Pavlov, Yuriy Shibanov, B. Posselt, Oleg Kargaltsev

Abstract: The Hubble Space Telescope's far ultraviolet observations of the middle-aged pulsar J1741-2054 and its bow-shock nebula revealed a spectrum combining thermal (cold and possible hot) and nonthermal components with distinct optical/X-ray photon indices, and detected the first far ultraviolet bow-shock nebula around a middle-aged pulsar, exhibiting higher flux and surface brightness than previously known counterparts.

Energy Reconstruction of Non-fiducial Electron-Positron Events in the DAMPE Experiment Using Convolutional Neural Networks

Time: 2025-03-13

Authors: Enzo Putti-Garcia, Andrii Tykhonov, Andrii Kotenko, Hugo Boutin, Manbing Li, Paul Coppin, Andrea Serpolla, Jennifer Maria Frieden, Chiara Perrina, Xin Wu

Abstract: The Dark Matter Particle Explorer (DAMPE) utilizes Convolutional Neural Networks to accurately reconstruct the energy of high-energy cosmic-ray electrons from non-fiducial events, addressing low detection rates and complex topologies at multi-TeV energies.

Irradiated Pulsar Planets and Companions as 511 keV Positron Annihilation Line Sources

Time: 2025-03-13

Authors: Zachary Metzler, Zorawar Wadiasingh

Abstract: Simulations using MEGAlib demonstrate that companions in compact millisecond pulsar binaries emit detectable 511 keV annihilation radiation due to pulsar-induced particle showers, with flux modulation enabling coherent searches to identify hidden pulsars, potentially explaining the galactic bulge 511 keV excess, and highlighting prospects for joint LISA and MeV gamma-ray studies to characterize these systems.

The stochastic gravitational wave background from cosmic superstrings

Time: 2025-03-13

Authors: Anastasios Avgoustidis, Edmund J. Copeland, Adam Moss, Juhan Raidal

Abstract: This study demonstrates that incorporating higher-mass cosmic superstring species is crucial for accurately modeling the stochastic gravitational wave background, with NANOGrav 15-year data fits providing fundamental string tension estimates (log₁₀(Gμ₁) ranging from -11.6 to -9.7 depending on loop characteristics), a string coupling upper bound (g_s < 0.65), and constraints on extra dimensions, while showing comparable likelihood to supermassive black hole models and implications for future gravitational wave experiments.

Simulating the shaping of point-symmetric structures in the jittering jets explosion mechanism

Time: 2025-03-13

Authors: Jessica Braudo, Amir Michaelis, Muhammad Akashi, Noam Soker

Abstract: Three-dimensional hydrodynamical simulations reveal that the jittering jets explosion mechanism (JJEM), involving multiple stochastically directed jet pairs, produces observed point-symmetric morphologies in core-collapse supernova remnants through interactions and instabilities, strengthening its role as the primary CCSN explosion mechanism.

Distinguishing Tidal Disruption Events and Changing-look Active Galactic Nuclei via Variation of Mid-infrared Color

Time: 2025-03-13

Authors: Yujun Yao, Jingjing Ye, Luming Sun, Ning Jiang, Megan Masterson, Xinwen Shu

Abstract: This study proposes using mid-infrared (MIR) color variation rates to differentiate tidal disruption events (TDEs) and ambiguous nuclear transients (ANTs) from changing-look active galactic nuclei (CLAGNs), finding that TDEs/ANTs redden faster during outbursts, estimating infrared TDE rates (~1.5–2.8×10⁻⁵ galaxy⁻¹ yr⁻¹) comparable to optical TDEs, and predicting future high-quality infrared surveys will uncover more dust-obscured TDEs with this method.

Temporal variability and obscuration effects in the X-ray emission of classical nova V339 Delphini (Nova Delphini 2013)

Time: 2025-03-13

Authors: Songpeng Pei, Nataly Ospina, Xiaowan Zhang, Qiang Li, Ziwei Ou, Taozhi Yang, Yongzhi Cai

Abstract: This study's analysis of *Chandra* and *XMM-Newton* data from Nova Del 2013's supersoft X-ray phase reveals blue-shifted absorption features (∼1200 km s⁻¹) and a transient, drifting ∼54-second flux modulation with variable amplitude and non-sinusoidal profiles, attributed to temporary obscuration events affecting the central source.

A demarcation criterion for hydrogen burning of millinovae

Time: 2025-03-13

Authors: Izumi Hachisu, Mariko Kato

Abstract: The study establishes that millinovae with hydrogen-burning white dwarfs, identified by the criterion \(I_{\rm q} - I_{\rm max} > 2.2\), are those with detected supersoft X-rays, supporting their origin from WD photospheres rather than accretion belts in dwarf novae.

R-JET: A postprocessing code for radiative transport in relativistic jets

Time: 2025-03-13

Authors: Kouichi Hirotani, Hsien Shang, Ruben Krasnopolsky, Kenichi Nishikawa

Abstract: This study presents a post-processing radiative transport code for modeling spectra, coreshift, and surface-brightness distributions in special relativistic jets with mixed electron-positron and electron-proton plasmas, incorporating thermal and nonthermal components, validating Konigl jet coreshift dependencies, and demonstrating limb-brightened jets and face-on ring-like brightness structures when energized by black hole rotation.

Korteweg-de Vries Integrals for Modified Black Hole Potentials: Instabilities and other Questions

Time: 2025-03-13

Authors: Michele Lenzi, Arnau Montava Agudo, Carlos F. Sopuerta

Abstract: This study demonstrates that Korteweg-de Vries integrals, derived from hidden symmetries in black hole dynamics, serve as a novel tool for detecting instabilities in quasi-normal modes and greybody factors under modifications of black hole potentials.
