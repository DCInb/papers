Baryon asymmetry constraints on magnetic field from the Electroweak epoch

Time: 2025-04-10

Authors: T. Boyer, A. Neronov

Abstract: Revised constraints on helical hypermagnetic fields from the Electroweak epoch indicate their decay (contributing to baryon asymmetry) requires a minimum correlation length relative to field strength, potentially leaving detectable intergalactic remnants (10-100 pG) that could influence cosmological recombination and the Hubble tension, with no constraints from baryon isocurvature perturbations.

Deflection angle in the strong deflection limit of axisymmetric spacetimes: local curvature, matter fields, and quasinormal modes

Time: 2025-04-10

Authors: Takahisa Igata

Abstract: This study establishes a coordinate-invariant framework connecting photon deflection in the strong-field limit and eikonal quasinormal modes of compact objects to local spacetime geometry (via the Weyl tensor) and matter distribution (via the Einstein tensor), characterizing logarithmic divergence in deflection angles and deriving quasinormal frequencies using curvature invariants expressed through Newman-Penrose scalars.

Orbit design for mitigating interstellar scattering effects in Earth-space VLBI observations of Sgr A*

Time: 2025-04-10

Authors: Aditya Tamar, Ben Hudson, Daniel C. M. Palumbo

Abstract: The study examines how interstellar medium turbulence parameters impact the Black Hole Explorer (BHEX) mission's ability to observe Sagittarius A*'s photon ring at 320 GHz, finding the BHEX-ALMA baseline sufficiently sensitive across most ISM conditions, proposing orbit migration to shorter baselines (≈13.5 Gλ) via propulsion methods with fuel-time trade-offs (chemical for speed, electric for efficiency), and showing higher orbits enhance spacetime feature isolation and photon ring morphology access despite increased scattering and reduced temporal resolution.

Sculpting the outer edge of accretion disks in pre-circumbinary binary black hole systems

Time: 2025-04-10

Authors: Fabien Casse, Peggy Varniere, Léna Arthur, Fabrice Dodu

Abstract: This study employs 2D hydrodynamical simulations to reveal that a secondary black hole in a pre-circumbinary disk (CBD) binary system gravitationally alters the primary's accretion disk by reducing its outer radius, generating spiral arms, and inducing an elliptical edge—features dependent on the binary's mass ratio and separation, which could aid in identifying pre-CBD binary black holes.

Resummation of Universal Tails in Gravitational Waveforms

Time: 2025-04-10

Authors: Mikhail M. Ivanov, Yue-Zhou Li, Julio Parra-Martinez, Zihan Zhou

Abstract: The authors derive a universal formula for the anomalous scaling of multipole moments in gravitating systems using effective field theory, linking it to black hole perturbation theory's renormalized angular momentum and gravitational wave scattering phase shifts, applicable to diverse objects (e.g., black holes, neutron stars) and enabling improved modeling of gravitational wave signals through resummation of universal short-distance effects.

Horizons, throats and bounces in hybrid metric-Palatini gravity with a non-zero potential

Time: 2025-04-10

Authors: Gabriel I. Róis, José Tarciso S. S. Junior, Francisco S. N. Lobo, Manuel E. Rodrigues

Abstract: This study explores exact electrically charged solutions, such as traversable wormholes, black holes, and black bounces, in the scalar-tensor representation of hybrid metric-Palatini gravity (HMPG) with a non-zero scalar potential, using an inverse problem approach in both Jordan and Einstein frames to reveal diverse geometric configurations—particularly in the phantom sector—that surpass General Relativity in complexity, demonstrating HMPG's potential for modeling extreme astrophysical phenomena.

The effect of background matter on the spin oscillations of neutrinos scattered by the supermassive black hole

Time: 2025-04-10

Authors: Mridupawan Deka, Maxim Dvornikov

Abstract: This study investigates neutrino spin oscillations in relativistic accretion disks around Kerr black holes through numerical simulations of gravitationally scattered test neutrinos, analyzing the effects of co-rotating and counter-rotating disk dynamics relative to the black hole's spin and presenting key findings.

Symmetry energy dependence of the bulk viscosity of nuclear matter

Time: 2025-04-10

Authors: Yumu Yang, Mauricio Hippert, Enrico Speranza, Jorge Noronha

Abstract: The weak-interaction-driven bulk viscosity and relaxation time in neutron star matter depend critically on the nuclear symmetry energy parameters \( S \) and \( L \), with experimental uncertainties in \( L \) causing orders-of-magnitude variations in viscosity, suggesting gravitational-wave signatures from binary neutron star mergers could constrain these nuclear properties.

How to identify the object with mass range of $(2.2-3)M_\odot$ in the merger of compact star systems

Time: 2025-04-10

Authors: ZhaoWei Du, HouJun Lü, Xiaoxuan Liu, XiLong Fan, EnWei Liang

Abstract: This study proposes identifying whether a mystery compact object (2.2–3 solar masses) in a binary system is a low-mass black hole or massive neutron star by analyzing electromagnetic emissions (e.g., luminosity trends during inspiral, kilonova brightness post-merger dependent on ejecta mass ratios, or tidal disruption requiring high-spin black holes), though current telescopes likely cannot detect these weak signals unless the event is nearby.
