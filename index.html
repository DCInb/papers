A Neural Operator-Based Emulator for Regional Shallow Water Dynamics

Time: 2025-02-20

Authors: Peter Rivera-Casillas, Sourav Dutta, Shukai Cai, Mark Loveland, Kamaljyoti Nath, Khemraj Shukla, Corey Trahan, Jonghyun Lee, Matthew Farthing, Clint Dawson

Abstract: The study introduces MITONet, a novel autoregressive neural emulator that employs dimensionality reduction to efficiently approximate high-dimensional numerical solvers for accurately forecasting coastal hydrodynamic processes, such as tide-driven dynamics governed by parameterized shallow-water equations, demonstrating robust extrapolation capabilities in both time and parametric space for real-world climate adaptation applications.

Catching the wisps: Stellar mass-loss limits from low-frequency radio observations

Time: 2025-02-20

Authors: Sanne Bloot, Harish K. Vedantham, Robert D. Kavanagh, Joseph R. Callingham, Benjamin J. S. Pope

Abstract: This study introduces a direct method combining low-frequency radio emission observations, absence of free-free absorption, and a simple wind model to establish upper mass-loss rate limits for 19 M dwarf stars, achieving sensitivity near the solar rate and extending constraints to later spectral types (M6) and greater distances (50 pc) than previous indirect methods, with potential application to ∼1000 stars using upcoming surveys like LOFAR and the Square Kilometre Array.

MOJAVE -- XXII. Brightness temperature distributions and geometric profiles along parsec-scale AGN jets

Time: 2025-02-20

Authors: E. V. Kravchenko, I. N. Pashchenko, D. C. Homan, Y. Y. Kovalev, M. L. Lister, A. B. Pushkarev, E. Ros, T. Savolainen

Abstract: This study of 447 AGN jets using 15 GHz VLBA observations reveals brightness temperature gradients (Tb ∝ r⁻²·⁸²) and component size expansion (R ∝ r¹·⁰²) consistent with conical geometry, with deviations attributed to shocks, helical structures, and MHD acceleration in regions transitioning to parabolic shapes.

Introduction to magnetic star-planet interactions

Time: 2025-02-18

Authors: A. Strugarek, E. Shkolnik

Abstract: This chapter reviews magnetic star-planet interactions (SPI) in close-in exoplanetary systems, where combined observational and magnetohydrodynamic simulation studies reveal how these interactions influence stellar activity, planetary atmospheric erosion, and orbital dynamics, extending insights from hot Jupiters to habitable-zone planets around M dwarfs.

Accretion onto supermassive and intermediate mass black holes in cosmological simulations

Time: 2025-02-18

Authors: Rainer Weinberger, Aklant Bhowmick, Laura Blecha, Greg Bryan, Johannes Buchner, Lars Hernquist, Julie Hlavacek-Larrondo, Volker Springel

Abstract: The study introduces a flexible parametrization for black hole gas inflow in cosmological simulations to address modeling uncertainties, revealing moderate impacts on supermassive black hole populations but significant differences in accretion rates and merger distributions for intermediate mass black holes at high redshifts.
