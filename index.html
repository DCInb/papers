Revisiting X-ray polarization of the shell of Cassiopeia A using spectropolarimetric analysis

Time: 2025-04-09

Authors: Alessandra Mercuri, Emanuele Greco, Jacco Vink, Riccardo Ferrazzoli, Silvia Perri

Abstract: Combining spatially resolved spectropolarimetric data from IXPE and Chandra on Cassiopeia A's nonthermal X-ray regions, this study reveals local polarization degrees of 10-26% with significant regional variations, a predominantly radial magnetic field, higher polarization values from refined thermal modeling, and estimates of magnetic turbulence based on photon index and polarization measurements.

Probable evidence for a transient mega-electron volt emission line in the GRB 221023A

Time: 2025-04-09

Authors: Lu-Yao Jiang, Yun Wang, Yu-Jia Wei, Da-Ming Wei, Xiang Li, Hao-Ning He, Jia Ren, Zhao-Qiang Shen, Zhi-Ping Jin

Abstract: This study reports the probable detection of a narrow 2.1 MeV emission line in GRB 221023A with a statistical significance >4.20σ, likely originating from de-excitation of excited electrons in relativistic hydrogen-like ions within the GRB jet, advancing insights into GRB jet composition and conditions.

A post-Newtonian approach to neutron star oscillations

Time: 2025-04-09

Authors: Shanshan Yin, Nils Andersson, Fabian Gittins

Abstract: This paper develops a post-Newtonian framework to model neutron star oscillations for gravitational-wave studies of extreme-density matter, demonstrating accuracy for low-mass stars (≲0.8M☉) and adjusted solutions for higher masses that match relativistic Cowling approximation precision at 1.4M☉ while outperforming it for lighter stars.

Universal profile for cosmic birefringence tomography with radio galaxies

Time: 2025-04-09

Authors: Fumihiro Naokawa

Abstract: The proposed method to tomographically probe cosmic birefringence using radio galaxies reveals a universal redshift-dependent profile (up to \(z \lesssim 10\)) for dark energy-linked pseudoscalar fields, contrasting with negligible low-redshift effects from pseudoscalar dark matter, motivating large-scale radio surveys (ASKAP/SKA) requiring \(\sim 10^5-10^6\) samples to distinguish these origins.

Evolution of the Comptonizing medium of the black-hole candidate Swift J1727.8$-$1613 along the hard to hard-intermediate state transition using NICER

Time: 2025-04-09

Authors: Divya Rawat, Mariano Méndez, Federico García, Pierre Maggi

Abstract: The analysis of Swift J1727.8-1613's 2023 outburst using NICER data and the vkompth model shows that increasing QPO frequency correlates with a shrinking disk truncation radius (from ~30-40 Rg to ~10 Rg), initial corona contraction followed by expansion, and a phase lag reversal from hard to soft, linked to jet interactions through radio observations.

Spectroscopic diagnostics of high-temperature plasma in stellar corona using Fe XXIV--XXVI K-shell lines with XRISM

Time: 2025-04-09

Authors: Miki Kurihara, Masahiro Tsujimoto, Marc Audard, Ehud Behar, Liyi Gu, Kenji Hamaguchi, Natalie Hell, Caroline A. Kilbourne, Yoshitomo Maeda, Frederick S. Porter, Haruka Sugai, Yohko Tsuboi

Abstract: The Resolve X-ray microcalorimeter on XRISM observed the RS CVn binary GT Mus during quiescence, resolving Fe K-shell lines for the first time in stellar sources and revealing a two-temperature plasma (1.7 and 4.3 keV) consistent with collisional equilibrium and thermal broadening, while disfavoring alternative models like non-Maxwellian distributions, demonstrating the utility of Fe line ratio diagnostics with microcalorimeters.

Evidence for repeating fast radio bursts association with fast super-twisted magnetars

Time: 2025-04-09

Authors: Guillaume Voisin, Théo Francez

Abstract: A study modeling two repeating FRBs (20180814A and 20180916B) from the CHIME/FRB catalog suggests their burst patterns originate from young magnetars with strong toroidal magnetic fields and spin periods of ~2.3 s and ~0.8 s, indicating super-twisted magnetospheres.

Dielectronic recombination studies of ions relevant to kilonovae and non-LTE plasma

Time: 2025-04-09

Authors: Suvam Singh, Zoltán Harman, Christoph H. Keitel

Abstract: This study calculates dielectronic recombination (DR) rate coefficients, resonance strengths, and cross sections for Y⁺, Sr⁺, Te²⁺, and Ce²⁺ using relativistic methods, revealing the critical role of low-lying resonances in kilonovae (~10⁴ K) and non-LTE plasmas, the necessity of including high-n Rydberg states for accuracy in complex ions, discrepancies with existing data underscoring computational refinements, and providing essential insights for interpreting neutron star merger observations (e.g., via JWST), refining nucleosynthesis models, and enhancing plasma simulations in astrophysical and laboratory settings.

Search for Type IL Gamma-ray Bursts: Criterion, Results, Verification and Physical Implication

Time: 2025-04-09

Authors: Wen-Jun Tan, Chen-Wei Wang, Peng Zhang, Shao-Lin Xiong, Bo-bing Wu, Jia-Cong Liu, Yue Wang, Sheng-Lun Xie, Wang-Chen Xue, Zheng-Hang Yu, Jin-Peng Zhang, Wen-Long Zhang, Yan-Qiu Zhang, Chao Zheng

Abstract: This study establishes criteria to identify Type IL gamma-ray bursts (GRBs)—characterized by long-duration emissions from compact binary mergers—and, through a systematic analysis of Fermi/GBM data from 2014–2024, identifies five such events (GRB 230307A, 211211A, 200914A, 200311A, 170228A), finding consistent temporal/spectral properties, a nearly constant energy ratio (~0.7) between extended and main emissions, and implications for their progenitor and emission mechanisms.

Radio emission across the entire rotation phases of pulsars

Time: 2025-04-09

Authors: P. F. Wang, J. L. Han

Abstract: The model explains FAST's detection of continuous weak radio emission across pulsars' rotation phases, showing that polarized emission from various magnetospheric heights in pulsars with short periods and abundant relativistic particles produces varying polarization angles depending on the observer's line of sight and persistent emission, naturally accounting for emission throughout all phases.
