Proto-neutron star oscillations including accretion flows

Time: 2025-03-20

Authors: Dimitra Tseneklidou, Raimon Luna, Pablo Cerdá-Durán, Alejandro Torres-Forné

Abstract: This study presents a novel general relativistic framework incorporating accretion flow and a stalled shock to model proto-neutron star oscillations, advancing asteroseismology for understanding gravitational wave signals from core-collapse supernovae.

The First Model-Independent Chromatic Microlensing Search: No Evidence in the Gravitational Wave Catalog of LIGO-Virgo-KAGRA

Time: 2025-03-20

Authors: Aniruddha Chakraborty, Suvodip Mukherjee

Abstract: This study conducted the first model-independent microlensing search in the wave-optics limit using µ-GLANCE on 80 GWTC-3 events, identifying one candidate (GW190408\_181802) with a marginal residual amplitude but finding no conclusive evidence of microlensing, thereby ruling out statistically significant signals in the dataset.

Neutrino emission and corona heating induced by high-energy proton interactions in Seyfert galaxies

Time: 2025-03-20

Authors: A. Neronov, O. Kalashev, D. V. Semikoz, D. Savchenko, M. Poleshchuk

Abstract: The detection of high-energy neutrinos from Seyfert active galactic nuclei (AGN) suggests that proton acceleration near accretion disks may obscure electromagnetic counterparts by heating the disk and corona, potentially linking neutrino flux to X-ray variability and contributing to the astrophysical neutrino background in the TeV range.

Superluminal Dark Photons as a Solution to the GRB 221009A Anomaly

Time: 2025-03-20

Authors: Antonios Valamontes, Emmanuel Markoulakis, Ioannis Adamopoulos

Abstract: The detection of exceptionally high-energy gamma photons from GRB 221009A by the LHAASO Collaboration is explained through superluminal dark photons, which enable faster-than-light propagation to circumvent extragalactic background light attenuation, offering a robust alternative to photon-ALP oscillations while aligning with cosmological observations and experimental data.

EP240414a: A Gamma-Ray Burst Jet Weakened by an Extended Circumstellar Material

Time: 2025-03-20

Authors: Hamid Hamidani, Yuri Sato, Kazumi Kashiyama, Masaomi Tanaka, Kunihito Ioka, Shigeo S. Kimura

Abstract: The Einstein Probe event EP240414a's intermediate characteristics between long and low-luminosity gamma-ray bursts are explained by a dense circumstellar material (0.03 M☉, extending to 3×10¹³ cm) weakening a conventional jet into a "barely failed" state, producing thermal cocoon cooling emission and a red non-thermal afterglow, highlighting the role of CSM in jet shaping and the importance of early multi-wavelength observations.

Gamma-Ray Burst Jets in Circumstellar Material: Dynamics, Breakout, and Diversity of Transients

Time: 2025-03-20

Authors: Hamid Hamidani, Kunihito Ioka, Kazumi Kashiyama, Masaomi Tanaka

Abstract: This study develops an analytic model for long gamma-ray burst jet dynamics in circumstellar material, deriving criteria for jet success/failure based on CSM radius, categorizing three outcomes via energy-weighted proper velocity, and linking these to luminous optical transients and diverse GRB-environment relationships.

Time: 2025-03-20

Authors: Pablo Marchant

Abstract: Binary stars, through their gravitational interactions and evolutionary pathways, provide critical insights into stellar structure and evolution, lead to diverse outcomes such as mergers, chemically altered stars, and gravitational waves, and are essential for understanding the evolution of massive stars and their large-scale impacts on stellar populations.

Compression fronts from fast radio bursts

Time: 2025-03-20

Authors: Andrei M. Beloborodov

Abstract: This study demonstrates that fast radio bursts (FRBs) from magnetars interact with their surrounding electron-positron wind plasma, inducing stochastic heating and compression at radii scaling with luminosity¹/³, and survive damping if emitted beyond ~10¹¹ cm.

Hide and seek with a Neutron Star. Hint of Neutron Star activity in SN 2015ap light curve

Time: 2025-03-20

Authors: Fabio Ragosta, Giulia Illiano, Andrea Simongini, Matteo Imbrogno, Silvia Piranomonte, Andrea Melandri

Abstract: The study attributes SN 2015ap's 8.4-day periodic modulation in multi-wavelength light curves and Hα emission lines to accretion onto a magnetized neutron star within a close binary system (separation <50 R☉) via the Taylor-Spruit dynamo model, ruling out circumstellar interactions as the cause.
