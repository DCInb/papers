The dependence of Polarization Degree of Blazars on particle energy distributions

Time: 2025-02-26

Authors: Ranjeev Misra, Hritwik Bora, Rupjyoti Gogoi

Abstract: The authors present a formalism to predict synchrotron polarization degree (PD) in ordered and random magnetic fields, applying it to Mrk 501's data to show that while a broken power law particle distribution requires higher-energy particles in stronger ordered fields to explain PD, a log parabola distribution achieves observed PD with uniform fields, suggesting future observations could distinguish these models.

Probing a low-mass $Z^{\prime}$ gauge boson at IceCube and prospects for IceCube-Gen2

Time: 2025-02-26

Authors: Reinaldo Francener, Victor P. Goncalves, Diego R. Gratieri

Abstract: The study investigates how the $L_\mu - L_\tau$ model's massive gauge boson $Z'$ disrupts the astrophysical neutrino flux power law via interactions with the cosmic neutrino background, using 12 years of IceCube HESE data to demonstrate sensitivity to MeV-scale $Z'$ masses and small couplings, with IceCube-Gen2 expected to significantly expand parameter space coverage.

Anisotropy signal of UHECRs from a structured magnetized Universe

Time: 2025-02-26

Authors: Simone Rossoni, Günter Sigl

Abstract: Simulations of ultra-high-energy cosmic ray propagation in structured magnetic fields and varying source distributions demonstrate that source characteristics primarily drive anisotropy more than magnetic field models, but consistently overpredict quadrupole strength, conflicting with observed dipole and quadrupole values above 8 EeV.

A strike of luck: could the KM3-230213A event be caused by an evaporating primordial black hole?

Time: 2025-02-26

Authors: Andrea Boccia, Fabio Iocco

Abstract: The study investigates whether the ultra-high-energy neutrino detected by KM3NeT could originate from evaporating primordial black holes (PBHs) with masses below 10⁷ grams, where quantum "memory burden" effects prolong their lifetimes, identifies viable PBH dark matter scenarios consistent with current constraints, and predicts KM3NeT could test this explanation within a few years.

Deciphering the Electron Spectral Hardening in AMS-02

Time: 2025-02-26

Authors: Carmelo Evoli

Abstract: The study confirms a 7σ-significant intrinsic spectral hardening at 42 GeV in primary cosmic-ray electrons from AMS-02 data, proposes a refined parametric fit for future analyses, and suggests interstellar transport or acceleration effects as the likely origin once systematic uncertainties are better constrained.

Fermi-detection of gamma-ray Emissions from the Hot Coronae of Radio-quiet Active Galactic Nuclei

Time: 2025-02-26

Authors: The Fermi-LAT Collaboration, Jian-Min Wang, Jian Li, Jun-Rong Liu

Abstract: Using 15-year Fermi-LAT data and a stacking technique, researchers detected significant gamma-ray emission (5.2σ) from 37 nearby Seyfert galaxies, revealing that ≥GeV photons originate from an extended corona (~2.7×10⁶ Rg) while 1–several GeV emissions arise from a compact X-ray corona (~10 Rg), with the extended structure potentially explained by a fireball model similar to gamma-ray bursts.

Effect of primary scalar hair on black hole's strong lensing in Beyond Horndeski Gravity

Time: 2025-02-26

Authors: Mohsen Fathi

Abstract: This study analyzes strong gravitational lensing in a scalar-haired black hole under Beyond Horndeski gravity, employing Event Horizon Telescope data to constrain the scalar hair by interpreting the observed shadow as the secondary image of an accretion disk, while dismissing higher-order images due to observational challenges and uncovering local instabilities in compliant models.

Bias-corrected Fast Radio Bursts Population and Spectra Using CHIME Injection Data

Time: 2025-02-26

Authors: Xianghan Cui, Clancy James, Di Li, Chengmin Zhang

Abstract: This study uses an improved statistical model incorporating property correlations from the CHIME/FRB catalog to reveal a low-frequency-favored FRB population spectrum (best-fit power-law α = -2.29 ± 0.29) and suggests distinct progenitors or mechanisms for non-repeaters (α = -2.50 ± 0.43) and repeaters (α = -1.91 ± 0.20).

Exploring the Origin of Solar Energetic Electrons II: Investigating Turbulent Coronal Acceleration

Time: 2025-02-26

Authors: Ross Pallister, Natasha L. S. Jeffrey, Morgan Stores

Abstract: The study investigates turbulent acceleration parameters in the solar corona, revealing spectral index differences between in-situ electrons and simulated X-ray fluxes, and suggests extended, non-uniform regions may be needed to reconcile observed δ≈γ relationships.

Modulation of the galactic cosmic ray spectrum in an anisotropic diffusion approach

Time: 2025-02-26

Authors: V. D. Borisov, V. O. Yurovsky, I. A. Kudryashov

Abstract: The study presents a new anisotropic diffusion model for cosmic rays within a realistic large-scale Galactic magnetic field, demonstrating that spectral modulation around the CR knee (1–30 PV) arises from spatial variations in leakage mechanisms influenced by both the anisotropic diffusion tensor and turbulence-aligned magnetic field dynamics.
