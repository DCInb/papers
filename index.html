Polarization Properties of Energetic Pulsars at Meterwavelengths

Time: 2025-05-19

Authors: Dipanjan Mitra, Rahul Basu, George I. Melikidze

Abstract: This study of high spin-down energy pulsars (E >5×10³³ erg/s) at 300-750 MHz identifies two polarization behaviors—high linear polarization with stable tracks and lower polarization with scattered patterns—attributed to incoherent mixing of orthogonally polarized X and O modes from coherent curvature radiation, where O-mode damping and X-mode interaction explain the observed variations.

The CHIME/FRB Discovery of the Extremely Active Fast Radio Burst Source FRB 20240114A

Time: 2025-05-19

Authors: Kaitlyn Shin, Alice Curtin, Maxwell Fine, Ayush Pandhi, Shion Andrew, Mohit Bhardwaj, Shami Chatterjee, Amanda M. Cook, Emmanuel Fonseca, B. M. Gaensler, Jason Hessels, Naman Jain, Victoria M. Kaspi, Bikash Kharel, Adam E. Lanman, Mattias Lazda, Calvin Leung, Robert Main, Kiyoshi W. Masui, Daniele Michilli, Mason Ng, Kenzie Nimmo, Aaron B. Pearlman, Ue-Li Pen, Ziggy Pleunis, Masoud Rafiei-Ravandi, Mawson Sammons, Ketan R. Sand, Paul Scholz, Kendrick Smith, Ingrid Stairs

Abstract: The highly active repeater FRB 20240114A, discovered by CHIME/FRB and followed extensively by observatories, exhibits a burst rate 49 times higher than non-repeaters, stable dispersion measure, and no significant scattering, offering insights into potential fundamental differences between repeating and non-repeating FRBs.

Inflight calibration of SRG/ART-XC point spread function at large off-axis angles

Time: 2025-05-19

Authors: R. Krivonos, R. Burenin, E. Filippova, I. Lapshov, A. Tkachenko, A. Semena, I. Mereminskiy, V. Arefiev, A. Lutovinov, B. D. Ramsey, J. J. Kolodziejczak, D. A. Swartz, C. -T. Chen, S. R. Ehlert, A. Vikhlinin

Abstract: This study calibrates the far off-axis point spread function (PSF) of the ART-XC telescope using in-flight data from Sco X-1 and the Crab Nebula, confirming consistency with ground calibrations, enabling accurate modeling for all-sky surveys, characterizing extended structures up to ~150", and providing an energy-dependent analytic parametrization of the slewing PSF.

ALMA observation of evolving magnetized corona in the radio-quiet changing-state AGN NGC 1566

Time: 2025-05-19

Authors: Arghajit Jana, Claudio Ricci, Sophie M. Venselaar, Chin-Shin Chang, Mai Liao, Yoshiyuki Inoue, Taiki Kawamuro, Franz E. Bauer, Elena Shablovinskaya, Benny Trakhtenbrot, Jacob S. Elford, Michael J. Koss

Abstract: The study of NGC 1566's millimeter variability using ALMA data revealed a correlation between millimeter and X-ray fluxes, supporting the origin as compact, optically thick synchrotron emission from the magnetized X-ray corona, confirming millimeter emission as a reliable tracer of AGN activity in radio-quiet AGNs.

A rapid black hole spin or emission from the plunging region?

Time: 2025-05-19

Authors: Andrew Mummery, Jiachen Jiang, Adam Ingram, Andrew Fabian, Jake Rule

Abstract: The study shows that including emission from within the ISCO in black hole accretion disks allows low-spin black holes to mimic high-spin X-ray spectra, suggesting current spin estimates from continuum fitting may be overestimated, as demonstrated with M33 X-7 and other binaries.

Linear analysis of I-C-Love universal relations for neutron stars

Time: 2025-05-19

Authors: Zexin Hu, Yong Gao, Lijing Shao

Abstract: This study introduces a new framework for analyzing neutron star universal relations (I-C and I-Love) by decomposing deviations into equation-of-state (EOS) perturbations and background structural components, demonstrating that the minimal contribution of the latter underpins their EOS-insensitive universality, offering insights into gravitational interactions and neutron star structure.

RGE effects on new physics searches via gravitational waves

Time: 2025-05-19

Authors: Katsuya Hashino, Daiki Ueda

Abstract: Gravitational wave observations can still effectively probe dimension-six Standard Model Effective Field Theory (SMEFT) effects related to electroweak phase transitions despite renormalization scale uncertainties, provided future colliders precisely measure the SMEFT $(H^{\dagger}H)^3$ operator.

Lecture Notes: WISPs in gamma-ray astrophysics

Time: 2025-05-19

Authors: Francesca Calore, Christopher Eckner

Abstract: These lecture notes examine the observational signatures of axions, axion-like particles (ALPs), and weakly interacting slim particles (WISPs) in high-energy astrophysical environments—such as active galactic nuclei and galaxy clusters—through processes like ALP-photon conversion and decay, exploring their implications for beyond-Standard Model physics, dark matter scenarios, and high-energy observations, supplemented by tutorials on axion electrodynamics and gamma-ray data analysis from NGC 1275.

A magnetic tertiary in the most massive compact triple-star system

Time: 2025-05-19

Authors: S. Hubrig, A. Vigna-Gómez, S. P. Järvinen, M. Schöller, I. Ilyin

Abstract: The massive compact triple-star system TIC 470710327, containing a 1.10-day eclipsing binary and a 52-day magnetic tertiary, supports a merger-driven formation scenario and highlights the role of dynamical studies in understanding massive stellar evolution and magnetic field generation.

The Composite Spectrum of the Little Red Dots from an Inner Standard Disk and an Outer Gravitationally Unstable Disk

Time: 2025-05-19

Authors: Chenxuan Zhang, Qingwen Wu, Xiao Fan, Luis C. Ho, Jiancheng Wu, Huanian Zhang, Bing Lyu, Xinwu Cao, Jianmin Wang

Abstract: The study proposes that JWST-observed "little red dots" (LRDs) with V-shaped spectral energy distributions (SEDs) are explained by a two-component AGN accretion disk model, naturally accounting for their sub-Eddington states and weak X-ray emissions without invoking dust extinction or unusual stellar processes.
