Luminosity Function of collapsar Gamma-Ray Bursts:the Progenitor of Long Gamma-Ray Bursts Is Not Singular

Time: 2025-05-01

Authors: Yan-Kun Qu, Zhong-Xiao Man, Shuang-Xi Yi, Yu-Peng Yang

Abstract: The study finds that significant redshift evolution in luminosity (δ=1.54) or density (δ=2.09) is required to explain collapsar gamma-ray bursts (GRBs), ruling out non-evolution models and showing that a substantial fraction of long GRBs at z<2 are not collapsars, regardless of the evolutionary scenario.

Spectral-timing analysis of the kilohertz quasi-periodic oscillations and constraints on the mass of the neutron star in $4U 1636-536$ using AstroSat observations

Time: 2025-05-01

Authors: Suchismito Chattopadhyay, Soma Mandal, Ranjeev Misra

Abstract: This study uses AstroSat data to analyze kHz quasi-periodic oscillations (QPOs) in neutron star 4U 1636-536, linking spectral state transitions to changes in corona and disk properties, estimating a neutron star mass of \(2.37 \pm 0.02 \, M_\odot\) via the relativistic precession model, and probing QPO origins through timing and radiative characteristics.

Joint inference for gravitational wave signals and glitches using a data-informed glitch model

Time: 2025-05-01

Authors: Ann-Kristin Malz, John Veitch

Abstract: This study introduces a machine-learning approach using normalizing flows trained on Gravity Spy glitches to build a parameterized model, which is integrated into Bayesian inference via Bilby, effectively removing glitches and significantly improving gravitational wave source parameter estimation by reducing bias.

Unified QMF equation of state for neutron star matter: Static and dynamic properties

Time: 2025-05-01

Authors: Zhonghao Tu, Xiangdong Sun, Shuochong Han, Zhiqiang Miao, Ang Li

Abstract: This study develops quark mean field (QMF) models with varying nuclear symmetry energy slopes (\(L_0\)) to analyze neutron star properties, finding that QMF predicts distinct crust structures and longer thermal relaxation with high \(L_0\), broader r-mode instability windows, and provides viscosity parameterizations, while both QMF and hadronic models align with observed cooling data despite slight parameter variations.

Can We Probe Spacetime Non-commutativity Through Tidal Deformability of Compact Objects?

Time: 2025-05-01

Authors: Junting Peng, Yanbo Zhao, Antonino Marciano

Abstract: This study investigates the impact of spacetime non-commutativity on tidal deformability in compact objects using de Sitter gauge theory, showing that non-commutative corrections converge to finite values at black-hole-compactness limits, and despite leading-order effects dominating near these limits, concludes that neutron stars and boson stars are not viable candidates for detecting such effects through gravitational wave observations.

Magnetized Accretion onto Neutron Stars: from Photon-trapped to Neutrino-cooled Flows

Time: 2025-05-01

Authors: Luciano Combi, Christopher Thompson, Daniel M. Siegel, Alexander Philippov, B. Ripperda

Abstract: Three-dimensional general-relativistic magnetohydrodynamic simulations of hypercritical neutron star accretion reveal that radiatively inefficient flows transition to neutrino-cooled regimes as toroidal magnetic fields mediate energy and angular momentum transport, achieving steady states when neutrino emission balances accretion power, with implications for neutron star spin and mass evolution supported by a one-dimensional analytical model.

Bayesian Inference of Core Properties of Hybrid Stars from Future High-Precision Measurements of Their Radii

Time: 2025-04-30

Authors: Bao-An Li, Xavier Grundler, Wen-Jie Xie, Nai-Bo Zhang

Abstract: Future high-precision neutron star radius measurements will enhance constraints on the equation of state of supradense matter, with Bayesian analysis revealing that while crust-core transition density remains largely unaffected by data precision, hadron-quark transition parameters and high-density EOS constraints depend critically on prior assumptions and improve significantly with tighter data, aligning with indications from RHIC experiments.
