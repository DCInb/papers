Accretion inside astrophysical objects : Effects of rotation and viscosity

Time: 2025-04-30

Authors: H. A. Adarsha, Chandrachur Chakraborty, Sudip Bhattacharyya

Abstract: The study investigates how dark matter-induced endoparasitic black holes, potentially explaining sub-solar and near-solar mass black holes observed in gravitational wave events like GW190425 and GW190814, accrete host matter under rotational and viscous effects, finding accretion stalls in some white dwarfs—creating polar openings—but not in neutron stars.

On the difficulty of capturing the distribution function of neutrinos in neutron star merger simulations

Time: 2025-04-30

Authors: Francois Foucart

Abstract: The abstract highlights challenges in modeling neutrinos' fermionic behavior in neutron star merger simulations, where current methods either inadequately handle quantum exclusion principles or produce unrealistic neutrino density estimates via Monte Carlo techniques, necessitating improved weighting schemes and smoothed distributions to address these limitations for accurate nuclear physics insights.

Time: 2025-04-30

Authors: Adriana R. Raduta, Mikhail V. Beznogov

Abstract: Researchers developed a new set of general-purpose equation of state tables compliant with current nuclear physics constraints and astrophysical observations, using advanced Skyrme interactions and Bayesian inference to explore inhomogeneous nuclear matter, revealing exotic isotopes and transition mechanisms, with the tables to be made publicly available in the Compose database.

Broadband study of the SMC pulsar RX J0032.9-7348 during its X-ray brightening in 2024

Time: 2025-04-30

Authors: Birendra Chhotaray, Gaurava K. Jaisawal, Sachindra Naik, Arghajit Jana

Abstract: This study presents timing and spectral analysis of the SMC pulsar RX J0032.9-7348 using NuSTAR and NICER data, revealing a 7.02 s pulsation, double-peaked energy-dependent pulse profiles, absorbed power-law spectra with a high-energy cutoff, no detected iron or cyclotron lines, 0.5-50 keV luminosity variations (∼8×10³⁶–4×10³⁷ erg s⁻¹), and phase-resolved spectral parameter dependencies.

Compact stellar systems hosting an intermediate mass black hole: magnetohydrodynamic study of inflow-outflow dynamics

Time: 2025-04-30

Authors: Matúš Labaj, Sean M. Ressler, Michal Zajaček, Tomáš Plšek, Bart Ripperda, Florian Peißker

Abstract: 3D magnetohydrodynamic simulations demonstrate that in compact Wolf-Rayet clusters hosting intermediate-mass black holes (IMBHs), high-velocity stellar winds and metallicity-enhanced cooling suppress accretion rates by five orders of magnitude, drive turbulent outflows, and produce dominant X-ray emissions from colliding winds that mask the IMBH's signature, explaining their low detectability and informing future X-ray and infrared observational strategies.

Rapid Spectral Evolution of SGR 1935+2154 During its 2022 Outburst

Time: 2025-04-30

Authors: Chin-Ping Hu, Zorawar Wadiasingh, Wynn C. G. Ho, Matthew G. Baring, George A. Younes, Teruaki Enoto, Sebastien Guillot, Tolga Guver, Marlon L. Bause, Rachael Stewart, Alex Van Kooten, Chryssa Kouveliotou

Abstract: High-cadence X-ray observations of magnetar SGR 1935+2154 revealed a pre-FRB intermediate flare that caused spectral softening, reduced burst rates, and magnetospheric evolution, linking magnetar X-ray activity to FRBs in the local Universe, though post-flare bursts lacked radio emission, indicating FRBs require conditions beyond peculiar X-ray bursts.

Plausible Indication of Gamma-Ray Absorption by Dark Matter in NGC 1068

Time: 2025-04-30

Authors: Gonzalo Herrera

Abstract: The observed deficit in gamma-ray flux from NGC 1068 compared to its high-energy neutrino emission can be explained by dark matter surrounding the central black hole absorbing gamma rays via a photon scattering cross-section of $\sigma_{\rm DM-\gamma}/m_{\rm DM} \simeq 10^{-28}-10^{-30}$ cm$^2$/GeV, reconciling IceCube and Fermi-LAT data while respecting particle physics constraints.

Physics-Informed Priors Improve Gravitational-Wave Constraints on Neutron-Star Matter

Time: 2025-04-30

Authors: Spencer J. Magnall, Christian Ecker, Luciano Rezzolla, Paul D. Lasky, Simon R. Goode

Abstract: The study introduces physics-informed priors combining nuclear theory, QCD calculations, and astrophysical data, significantly improving neutron star radius and tidal deformability constraints for GW170817 (e.g., \( R_{1.4} = 12.11^{+0.91}_{-1.11} \, \text{km} \)), offering marginal support for GW190425 as a binary neutron star merger (\( \mathcal{BF}=1.33 \)), and advocates adopting these priors as standard with open-source code provided.

Collisions with tidal disruption event disks: implications for quasi-periodic X-ray eruptions

Time: 2025-04-30

Authors: Andrew Mummery

Abstract: The study demonstrates that tidal disruption event (TDE) disk properties, which conserve angular momentum and mass, invalidate existing quasi-periodic X-ray eruption (QPE) models involving collisions with EMRI black holes or stellar surfaces but support a scenario involving a puffed-up star with a trailing debris stream, altering QPE observable scaling and requiring further investigation.
