Inferring Black Hole Spin from Interferometric Measurements of the First Photon Ring: A Geometric Approach

Time: 2025-02-27

Authors: Lennox S. Keeble, Alejandro Cárdenas-Avendaño, Daniel C. M. Palumbo

Abstract: This study presents a geometric method for estimating black hole spin using the interferometric shape asymmetry of the first photon ring, demonstrating effectiveness at low inclinations and moderate-to-high spins despite noise and astrophysical variability skewing results or limiting constraints to lower bounds, thereby highlighting its potential for future space-based VLBI missions like the Black Hole Explorer.

Nyquist-resolving gravitational waves via orbital frequency-based refinement

Time: 2025-02-27

Authors: William K. Black, David Neilsen, Eric W. Hirschmann, David F. Van Komen, Milinda Fernando

Abstract: The ORBIT method dynamically optimizes adaptive mesh refinement in numerical relativity by using a binary's orbital frequency to meet gravitational wave resolution requirements, significantly reducing computational costs and waveform noise while enhancing accuracy, thereby advancing preparations for next-generation gravitational wave detectors.

Inferring the Equation of State from Neutron Star Observables via Machine Learning

Time: 2025-02-27

Authors: N. K. Patra, Tuhin Malik, Helena Pais, Kai Zhou, B. K. Agrawal, Constança Providência

Abstract: Using symbolic regression on diverse neutron star equations of state, the study reveals that maximum mass strongly correlates with pressure and baryon density at ~800 MeV.fm⁻³, demonstrates the EoS can be expressed via radius and tidal deformability for 1-2 solar masses, and provides an efficient framework to decode dense matter physics from neutron star observables.

Timing and Spectral Evolution of the Magnetar 1E 1841-045 in Outburst

Time: 2025-02-27

Authors: G. Younes, S. K. Lander, M. G. Baring, M. L. Bause, R. Stewart, Z. Arzoumanian, H. Dinh Thi, T. Enoto, K. Gendreau, T. Guver, A. K. Harding, W. C. G. Ho, C. -P. Hu, A. van Kooten, C. Kouveliotou, N. Di Lalla, A. McEwen, M. Negro, Mason Ng, D. M. Palmer, L. G. Spitler, Zorawar Wadiasingh

Abstract: Following its August 2024 outburst, magnetar 1E 1841-045 exhibited a 1.6-fold X-ray flux increase driven by a new non-thermal component (Γ=1.5) with subsequent 20% decay, a spin-up glitch, a migrating narrow pulse peak (phase shift rate similar to SGR 1830−0645), and radio non-detections, suggesting magnetic field untwisting and crustal dynamics.

On Bactrian glitch-size distributions

Time: 2025-02-27

Authors: Anantharaman Sekharipuram Viswanathan, Dipankar Bhattacharya

Abstract: This study reveals a bimodal glitch-size distribution in pulsar J0537-6910 through Bayesian analysis and simulations, showing that annular variations in superfluid vortex pinning strength and sectorial triggers induce bimodality, offering new insights into neutron star vortex dynamics and glitch mechanisms.

Bayesian inferences on covariant density functionals from multimessenger astrophysical data. I. Nucleonic models

Time: 2025-02-27

Authors: Jia-Jie Li, Yu Tian, Armen Sedrakian

Abstract: This Bayesian analysis incorporating extreme astrophysical data (including the high-mass PSR J0952-0607 and low-mass HESS J1731-347) demonstrates that covariant density functional models with density-dependent couplings support compact stars up to 2.4–2.5 solar masses but face challenges in reconciling low-density equation of state softening for ultra-compact objects, while predicting a minimum radius of ~12 km for low-mass stars due to isovector sector constraints.

Radio Detection of ultra-high-energy Cosmic-Ray Air Showers

Time: 2025-02-27

Authors: Frank G. Schröder

Abstract: This article introduces the physics of radio emission from cosmic-ray air showers, reviews the advancements in radio antenna-based observatories for their all-weather capability and competitive accuracy, discusses their integration with particle and muon detectors to enhance cosmic-ray measurements, and highlights recent results and future prospects, including stand-alone experiments for ultra-high-energy particles.

Science Potential and Technical Design of the IceCube-Gen2 Surface Array (UHECR 2024)

Time: 2025-02-27

Authors: Frank G. Schroeder

Abstract: IceCube-Gen2, the next-generation extension of the IceCube Neutrino Observatory, employs a surface array (scintillation panels and radio antennas) and an extended optical array to study cosmic-ray physics at PeV-EeV energies by simultaneously measuring electromagnetic components and TeV muons in air showers, achieving a 30-fold aperture increase for enhanced sensitivity to mass composition, anisotropy, and PeV photon detection in Galactic cosmic rays, as outlined in its Technical Design Report.

H I absorption line and anomalous dispersion in the radio pulses of PSR B1937+21

Time: 2025-02-27

Authors: Jinchen Jiang, Shunshun Cao, Kejia Lee, Bojun Wang, Heng Xu, Siyuan Chen, Yanjun Guo, Peng Jiang, Weicong Jing, Jiguang Lu, Jiangwei Xu, Renxin Xu, Zihan Xue

Abstract: Using the Five-hundred-meter Aperture Spherical radio Telescope (FAST), observations of millisecond pulsar PSR B1937+21's neutral hydrogen (HI) emission/absorption lines revised its kinematic distance to 9.4±0.5 kpc in the Perseus Arm, detected potential tiny-scale atomic structures (TSAS) of a few AU, and confirmed anomalous faster-than-light dispersion signals previously reported.

Application of Machine Learning to Identify Radio Pulses of Air Showers at the South Pole (ARENA 2024)

Time: 2025-02-27

Authors: Frank G. Schroeder, Abdul Rehman

Abstract: Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) trained on CoREAS simulations and prototype station data at the South Pole effectively identify and clean cosmic-ray air shower radio signals, increasing detected events fivefold compared to traditional methods while maintaining purity and angular resolution, experimentally validating their use in lowering detection thresholds for hybrid particle-radio detector arrays.
