Proto-neutron star oscillations including accretion flows

Time: 2025-03-20

Authors: Dimitra Tseneklidou, Raimon Luna, Pablo Cerdá-Durán, Alejandro Torres-Forné

Abstract: This study introduces a general relativistic framework incorporating accretion flow and a stalled shock to model proto-neutron star oscillations in core-collapse supernovae, using Chebyshev spectral methods to solve eigenvalue problems and assess their implications for SASI-related gravitational wave signatures and asteroseismology.

The First Model-Independent Chromatic Microlensing Search: No Evidence in the Gravitational Wave Catalog of LIGO-Virgo-KAGRA

Time: 2025-03-20

Authors: Aniruddha Chakraborty, Suvodip Mukherjee

Abstract: The first model-independent microlensing search using $\mu$-$\texttt{GLANCE}$ on 80 GWTC-3 events found one tentative candidate (GW190408\_181802) but ruled out statistically significant microlensing signals in the dataset, highlighting challenges in detecting lensing effects due to unknown mass distributions.

Neutrino emission and corona heating induced by high-energy proton interactions in Seyfert galaxies

Time: 2025-03-20

Authors: A. Neronov, O. Kalashev, D. V. Semikoz, D. Savchenko, M. Poleshchuk

Abstract: The recent detection of high-energy neutrinos from Seyfert active galactic nuclei (AGN) suggests that protons accelerated near accretion disks may lack identifiable electromagnetic counterparts due to disk/corona heating, potentially correlating neutrino flux with X-ray variability and contributing significantly to the diffuse GeV-TeV astrophysical neutrino background.

Superluminal Dark Photons as a Solution to the GRB 221009A Anomaly

Time: 2025-03-20

Authors: Antonios Valamontes, Emmanuel Markoulakis, Ioannis Adamopoulos

Abstract: The detection of high-energy gamma photons (up to 18 TeV) from GRB 221009A challenges conventional physics, leading to a proposed explanation using superluminal dark photons that enable faster-than-light propagation to bypass extragalactic background light attenuation, offering an alternative to parameter-dependent photon-axion-like particle oscillations and aligning with cosmological and experimental evidence.

EP240414a: A Gamma-Ray Burst Jet Weakened by an Extended Circumstellar Material

Time: 2025-03-20

Authors: Hamid Hamidani, Yuri Sato, Kazumi Kashiyama, Masaomi Tanaka, Kunihito Ioka, Shigeo S. Kimura

Abstract: The unusual features of the Einstein Probe event EP240414a, including its intermediate position between long and low-luminosity gamma-ray bursts and the evolving thermal-to-red-peak optical transient (AT2024gsa), are explained by a "barely failed" jet weakened by a dense circumstellar material, with thermal cocoon cooling and non-thermal afterglow emissions, highlighting the role of extended CSM in shaping jet-driven transients and the importance of early multi-wavelength observations.

Gamma-Ray Burst Jets in Circumstellar Material: Dynamics, Breakout, and Diversity of Transients

Time: 2025-03-20

Authors: Hamid Hamidani, Kunihito Ioka, Kazumi Kashiyama, Masaomi Tanaka

Abstract: This study develops an analytic model for long gamma-ray burst jet dynamics in circumstellar environments, deriving jet success/failure criteria dependent on CSM radius and introducing an energy-weighted proper velocity that classifies three jet outcomes (successful, barely failed, completely failed), which can power luminous optical transients and link LGRB diversity to environmental factors.

Evolution of binary stars

Time: 2025-03-20

Authors: Pablo Marchant

Abstract: Binary stars, as gravitationally bound systems, enable precise measurements for testing stellar evolution theories and, through interactions at close distances, drive diverse outcomes such as mergers, chemically altered stars, and gravitational wave sources, critically shaping the evolution of massive stars and influencing stellar populations on large scales.

Compression fronts from fast radio bursts

Time: 2025-03-20

Authors: Andrei M. Beloborodov

Abstract: This study finds that fast radio bursts (FRBs) from magnetars interact with their surrounding plasma, inducing stochastic heating/compression at smaller radii and regular heating/compression waves at larger radii, both scaling with luminosity as \( L^{1/3} \), with FRB survival contingent on emission beyond a critical damping radius.

Hide and seek with a Neutron Star. Hint of Neutron Star activity in SN 2015ap light curve

Time: 2025-03-20

Authors: Fabio Ragosta, Giulia Illiano, Andrea Simongini, Matteo Imbrogno, Silvia Piranomonte, Andrea Melandri

Abstract: The study of Type Ib supernova SN 2015ap identifies an 8.4-day periodic modulation in its multi-wavelength light curves and Hα emission lines, attributed to accretion onto a magnetized neutron star interacting with a close binary companion, challenging traditional circumstellar models and supporting a magnetosphere-driven mechanism.
