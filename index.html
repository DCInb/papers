No Flavor Anisotropy in the High-Energy Neutrino Sky Upholds Lorentz Invariance

Time: 2025-03-19

Authors: Bernanda Telalovic, Mauricio Bustamante

Abstract: By analyzing 7.5 years of IceCube High-Energy Starting Events data for directional flavor anisotropies in astrophysical neutrinos, this study found no evidence of Lorentz-invariance violation (LIV)-induced compass asymmetries, placing significantly improved constraints on hundreds of LIV parameters (dimensions 2-8) and establishing first-time bounds for many.

Spectroscopy of Supernova Remnants and Candidates in M31

Time: 2025-03-19

Authors: Nelson Caldwell, John C. Raymond, Knox S. Long, Myung Gyoon Lee

Abstract: This study uses optical spectroscopy from MMT/Hectospec to analyze 152 SNR candidates in M31, confirming 72 as supernova remnants through elevated [SII]/Hα ratios (>0.4) and high Hα velocity widths (>50 km/s), identifies other likely SNRs, and discovers new candidates via archival data and multi-wavelength observations (X-ray, radio, UV).

Orbital eccentricity in a neutron star - black hole binary

Time: 2025-03-19

Authors: Gonzalo Morras, Geraint Pratten, Patricia Schmidt

Abstract: The first detection of orbital eccentricity in a neutron star-black hole binary (GW200105) was achieved using a waveform model that incorporates both precession and eccentricity, revealing significant eccentricity (median ~0.145 at 0.1s orbital period) and suggesting dynamical formation mechanisms, while precession evidence remained inconclusive.

Connecting a Magnetized Disk to a Convective Low-mass Protostar: A Global Three-dimensional Model of Boundary Layer Accretion

Time: 2025-03-19

Authors: Shinsuke Takasao, Takashi Hosokawa, Kengo Tomida, Kazunari Iwasaki

Abstract: This study uses a three-dimensional magnetohydrodynamic model to reveal that boundary layer accretion around a magnetized protostar involves transitioning angular momentum transport mechanisms (e.g., MRI, spiral shocks, magnetic reconnection) across distinct disk regions, driving phenomena like accretion shocks, explosive reconnection events, and midplane decretion flows crucial for material transport in early star formation.

A constraint on superheavy elements of the GRB-kilonova AT 2023vfi

Time: 2025-03-19

Authors: Zhengyan Liu, Ji-an Jiang, Wen Zhao

Abstract: The study's end-to-end simulation of the kilonova AT 2023vfi, linked to gamma-ray burst GRB 230307A and observed by the James Webb Space Telescope, reveals temporal similarities to AT 2017gfo, confirms a binary neutron star merger origin through lanthanide-rich ejecta analysis, and constrains superheavy element abundances (e.g., \(^{222}\)Rn, \(^{223}\)Ra), advancing understanding of r-process nucleosynthesis in such mergers.

Discrete treatment of inverse Compton scattering: implications on parameter estimation in gamma-ray astronomy

Time: 2025-03-19

Authors: Junji Xia, Xingjian Lv, Kun Fang, Xiaojun Bi, Siming Liu

Abstract: The discrete treatment of inverse Compton scattering (ICS) reveals that high-energy electron spectra with cutoffs evolve to higher cutoff energies than predicted by the continuous approximation, significantly impacting interpretations of astrophysical sources like the Geminga pulsar halo and PeV gamma-ray sources by lowering inferred electron acceleration limits.

Time: 2025-03-19

Authors: Akhil Jaini, Adam T. Deller, Yuanming Wang, Emil Lenc, Marcin Glowacki

Abstract: This study corrects systematic positional errors in the Rapid ASKAP Continuum Survey (RACS) using the Wide-field Infrared Survey Explorer (WISE) catalogue, improving the astrometric precision of Australian Square Kilometre Array Pathfinder (ASKAP)-detected Fast Radio Bursts (FRBs) to 0.3''–0.4'' accuracy, thereby enhancing their utility for cosmological studies.
