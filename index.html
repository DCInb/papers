Hints of Primordial Magnetic Fields at Recombination and Implications for the Hubble Tension

Time: 2025-03-12

Authors: Karsten Jedamzik, Levon Pogosian, Tom Abel

Abstract: This study employs high-precision magnetohydrodynamic simulations and Lyman-alpha radiative transfer to demonstrate that non-helical primordial magnetic fields (PMFs) with present-day strengths of 5-10 picoGauss, which could explain cluster magnetic fields without requiring dynamo amplification, alleviate the Hubble tension by yielding a higher Hubble constant than ΛCDM while fitting observational data (CMB, BAO, SN), with future CMB measurements needed for confirmation.

Evolution of the Three Spectral Components in the Prompt Emission of GRB 240825A

Time: 2025-03-12

Authors: Chen-Wei Wang, Wen-Jun Tan, Shao-Lin Xiong, Rahim Moradi, Yan-Qiu Zhang, Chao Zheng, Bing Li, Xiao-Bo Li, Cheng-Kui Li, Jia-Cong Liu, Yue Wang, Bo-Bing Wu, Sheng-Lun Xie, Wang-Chen Xue, Shu-Xu Yi, Zheng-Hang Yu, Peng Zhang, Shuang-Nan Zhang, Wen-Long Zhang, Zhen Zhang

Abstract: The joint analysis of GRB 240825A's three spectral components—non-thermal, quasi-thermal, and GeV-range hard non-thermal—supports jet penetration, suggests a two-jet structure via light-curve features, and explains spectral lag transitions through independent component evolution affecting cross-correlation.

Observation of Fermi acceleration with cold atoms

Time: 2025-03-12

Authors: G. Barontini, V. Naniyil, J. P. Stinton, D. Reid, J. M. F. Gunn, H. M. Price, A. B. Deb, D. Caprioli, V. Guarrera

Abstract: Researchers have created the first fully controllable Fermi accelerator using ultracold atoms colliding with engineered movable potential barriers, demonstrating high-velocity atomic jets and experimentally validating Bell's energy spectrum theory, thereby enabling astrophysics studies of cosmic ray acceleration and offering a compact (100 µm), simple, and competitive quantum technology platform with virtually no performance limits.

Investigating the correlation between ZTF TDEs and IceCube high-energy neutrinos

Time: 2025-03-12

Authors: Ming-Xuan Lu, Yun-Feng Liang, Xiang-Gao Wang, Xue-Rui Ouyang

Abstract: This study investigates the correlation between Tidal Disruption Events (TDEs)—particularly a subsample of seven with infrared observations—and IceCube neutrinos using Monte Carlo simulations on 143 neutrino alerts and 61 ZTF-BTS TDEs, finding a significant 2.43σ correlation (enhancing to 2.54σ with time delay consideration), suggesting TDEs as potential high-energy neutrino sources.

Secondary black hole-induced magnetic reconnection in OJ 287: Implications for X-ray and radio emission

Time: 2025-03-12

Authors: S. Boula, A. Nathanail

Abstract: This study demonstrates that OJ 287's quasi-periodic outbursts and anomalous emissions arise from magnetic reconnection-induced plasmoid chains triggered by interactions in a supermassive binary black hole system, supported by numerical simulations that challenge single-blob emission models.

Strong Field QED, Astrophysics, and Laboratory Astrophysics

Time: 2025-03-12

Authors: Sang Pyo Kim

Abstract: Astrophysical compact objects with electromagnetic fields exceeding the Schwinger limit enable the study of fundamental strong-field quantum electrodynamics phenomena, such as electron-positron pair production and vacuum refraction, which can be simulated using ultra-intense lasers and high-energy particles.

Abstract: This study introduces a neural network-based method employing Monte Carlo Dropout to efficiently and accurately recover X-ray spectral parameters and quantify uncertainties, overcoming frequentist local minima issues and Bayesian computational bottlenecks while achieving comparable accuracy, improved speed, and robustness for future large-scale astronomical data analysis.

The polarization of the synchrotron radiation from a recollimated jet: application to high-energy BL Lacs

Time: 2025-03-12

Authors: Alberto Sciaccaluga, Agnese Costa, Fabrizio Tavecchio, Gianluigi Bodo, Paolo Coppi, Stella Boula

Abstract: This study employs relativistic magnetohydrodynamic simulations and a Lagrangian macroparticle code to show that turbulence in superluminal recollimation shocks in blazar jets explains observed polarization chromaticity (increasing with frequency) and stable polarization angles across X-ray/optical bands, supporting the energy-stratified shock model for IXPE findings.
