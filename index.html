Variability study of classical supergiant X-ray binary 4U 1907+09 using NuSTAR

Time: 2025-04-03

Authors: Raj Kumara, Sayantan Bhattacharya, Sudip Bhattacharyya, Subir Bhattacharyyaa

Abstract: The 2024 NuSTAR observation of supergiant X-ray binary 4U 1907+09 revealed significant flux variability with dips and flares, confirmed the pulsar's continued spin-down (443.99 s period), detected persistent cyclotron absorption features at ~17.6 keV and ~38 keV unchanged across flux states, and linked its on-state luminosity (2.85 × 10³⁵ erg/s) to a collisionless shock "pencil" beam pattern, advancing understanding of accretion dynamics and magnetic field geometry.

Monte Carlo evaluations of gamma-ray and radio pulsar populations

Time: 2025-04-03

Authors: Shawaiz Tabassum, Duncan Lorimer

Abstract: This study evaluates seven γ-ray luminosity models using Galactic neutron star populations and Fermi-LAT data, finding four models consistent with observed unidentified pulsar-like sources while others over-predict them, revealing discrepancies in canonical pulsar observations and advocating targeted radio searches to resolve these issues.

Unveiling the reflection spectrum in the ultracompact LMXB 4U 1820-30

Time: 2025-04-03

Authors: A. Anitra, A. Gnarini, T. Di Salvo, R. Iaria, A. Sanna, L. Burderi, A. Marino, M. Del Santo, G. Matt, F. Ursini, S. Bianchi, F. Capitanio, S. Fabiani, A. Tarana, A. Di Marco

Abstract: Using simultaneous NICER, NuSTAR, and IXPE observations, the study of ultracompact X-ray binary 4U 1820-30 revealed a strong reflection component indicating a low system inclination (~31°), subsolar iron abundances linked to its metal-poor globular cluster environment, and energy-dependent polarization rising to 8% at higher energies, challenging current models despite the low inclination.

GRB 170519A: Thermal Radiation in an X-ray Flare and Decaying Magnetic Fields for the Early-Time Afterglow

Time: 2025-04-03

Authors: Zi-Min Zhou, Liang-Jun Chen, Rui-Quan Li, Xiang-Gao Wang, Xing-Ling Li, En-Wei Liang, WeiKang Zheng, Alexei V. Filippenko

Abstract: GRB 170519A's multiwavelength afterglow, observed by *Swift* and ground-based telescopes, reveals a structured evolution including X-ray flares with a thermal component (photospheric radius ~10¹¹ cm, time-decreasing temperature and Lorentz factor), an optical onset, decay phases, and a possible jet break, successfully modeled by an external shock with time-dependent ε_B decay and parameter relations \(L_{\rm BB} \propto kT^{2.49}\) and \(\Gamma_{\rm BB} \propto L_{\rm BB}^{0.27}\).

Search for Fast Radio Bursts and radio pulsars from pulsing Ultraluminous X-ray Sources

Time: 2025-04-03

Authors: Juntao Bai, Na Wang, Rui Luo, Wei-Yang Wang, Shi Dai, Songbo Zhang, Shiqian Zhao, Shuangqiang Wang

Abstract: A study using FAST and Parkes telescopes detected no FRBs or pulsars from eight pulsing ultraluminous X-ray sources (PULXs), suggesting that accretion processes, dense stellar winds, FRB beaming effects, and relativistic outflows likely suppress or hinder radio emission, constraining models associating FRBs with magnetized neutron stars in binary systems.

Time: 2025-04-02

Authors: Vo Hong Minh Phan, Pierre Cristofari, Enrico Peretti, Vincent Tatischeff, Andrea Ciardi

Abstract: The study proposes that the observed 2-day delay between GeV and TeV gamma-ray emission peaks in the 2021 RS Ophiuchi nova arises from gamma-ray absorption via interactions with optical photons, underscoring the critical role of multi-wavelength analysis in understanding particle acceleration mechanisms in nova systems.

Probing axion-like particles with multimessenger observations of neutron star mergers

Time: 2025-04-02

Authors: Francesca Lecce, Alessandro Lella, Giuseppe Lucente, Vimal Vijayan, Andreas Bauswein, Maurizio Giannotti, Alessandro Mirizzi

Abstract: Axion-like particles (ALPs) produced in binary neutron star mergers via nucleon-nucleon bremsstrahlung with sizable couplings could generate short MeV gamma-ray signals through magnetic field-induced photon conversions, detectable by proposed gamma-ray telescopes with sensitivities down to \( g_{a\gamma} \gtrsim \text{few} \times 10^{-13} \, \text{GeV}^{-1} \) for ultralight ALPs (mass \( m_a \lesssim 10^{-9} \, \text{eV} \)), rivaling SN 1987A constraints, enabling multi-messenger detection alongside gravitational waves.
