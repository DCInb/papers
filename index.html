Universal fluctuation spectrum of Vlasov-Poisson turbulence

Time: 2025-03-21

Authors: Michael L. Nastac, Robert J. Ewart, James Juno, Michael Barnes, Alexander A. Schekochihin

Abstract: This study derives the universal electric field fluctuation spectrum in turbulent Vlasov-Poisson plasmas at Debye and sub-Debye scales through a critically balanced cascade of the quadratic Casimir invariant \( C_2 \) via linear and nonlinear phase mixing, supported by 1D-1V simulations, predicting termination at thermal noise crossover and necessitating new collision operators due to the invalidation of standard collisional theory.

On the origin of radio polarization in pulsar polar caps

Time: 2025-03-21

Authors: Jan Benáček, Axel Jessner, Martin Pohl, Tatiana Rievajová, Lucy S. Oswald

Abstract: This study uses first-principle kinetic simulations of pulsar polar cap pair cascades with a 60° magnetic inclination to explain observed radio emission properties—including pulse profiles, polarization features (strong linear, weak circular), and spectral behaviors—challenging the rotating vector model's validity for magnetic field geometry and emphasizing pair discharges' role in shaping observable characteristics.

High energy time lags of Gamma Ray Bursts

Time: 2025-03-21

Authors: C. Maraventano, G. Ghirlanda, L. Nava, T. Di Salvo, W. Leone, R. Iaria, L. Burderi, A. Tsvetkova

Abstract: The analysis of spectral lags in Gamma-Ray Bursts using Fermi GBM and LAT data reveals both positive lags (indicating hard-to-soft spectral evolution) and negative lags (suggesting a delayed high-energy component), emphasizing the importance of combining temporal and spectral analyses to understand emission mechanisms.

Dark Matter (S)pins the Planet

Time: 2025-03-21

Authors: Haihao Shi, Junda Zhou, Zhenyang Huang, Guoliang Lv, Xuefei Chen

Abstract: Dark matter heating in planets, influenced by planetary properties and external factors, leads to smaller-than-previously-estimated temperature increases (e.g., 0.015 K for Earth) and angular velocity changes (e.g., \(10^{-8}\) rad/s over 100 years), with high dark matter density causing earlier rotational stabilization dominated by dark matter effects.

Exploring the secondary peaks of autocorrelation in black hole flare movies

Time: 2025-03-21

Authors: Zhenyu Zhang, Yehui Hou, Minyong Guo, Yosuke Mizuno, Bin Chen

Abstract: This study demonstrates that autocorrelation analysis of black hole movies can detect gravitational lensing signatures through persistent secondary peaks in time-lag data, enabling direct measurement of black hole parameters and probing spacetime geometry by disentangling lensing effects from orbital dynamics.

Dipole Polarizability of Finite Nuclei as a Probe of Neutron Stars

Time: 2025-03-21

Authors: P. S. Koliogiannis, E. Yuksel, T. Ghosh, N. Paar

Abstract: This study demonstrates that the electric dipole polarizability in neutron-rich nuclei constrains the symmetry energy's density dependence through relativistic models, bridging nuclear properties to neutron star tidal deformability and radii, thereby reducing mass-radius uncertainties and aligning with multimessenger astrophysical observations.

Searching for continuous gravitational waves from slowly spinning neutron stars with DECIGO, Big Bang Observer, Einstein Telescope and Cosmic Explorer

Time: 2025-03-21

Authors: Gianluca Pagliaro, Maria Alessandra Papa, Jing Ming, Martina Muratore

Abstract: Next-generation gravitational wave detectors, including ground-based facilities like Einstein Telescope and Cosmic Explorer and space-based observatories like DECIGO and Big Bang Observer, could detect continuous gravitational waves below 20 Hz from highly magnetized neutron stars, glitching pulsars, and post-glitch magnetars, with most pulsars' spin-down limits exceeding detectable thresholds and specific cases like PSR J1852+0040 being viable targets under certain emission conditions.

Collapse of Rotating White Dwarfs and Multimessenger Signals

Time: 2025-03-21

Authors: Takami Kuroda, Kyohei Kawaguchi, Masaru Shibata

Abstract: This study's 3D numerical simulations of rotating, magnetized white dwarf collapses reveal prompt bipolar explosions with spiral waves enhancing energy and ejecta mass (albeit still low for typical supernovae), generate detectable gravitational waves up to ∼10 Mpc, and suggest such events could explain fast blue optical transients via magnetar-driven bright cases or ejecta interactions for fainter ones.

Prompt Stellar and Binary Black Hole Mergers from Chemically Homogeneous Evolution in Triples

Time: 2025-03-21

Authors: Alejandro Vigna-Gómez, Evgeni Grishin, Jakob Stegmann, Aleksandra Olejak, Silvia A. Popa, Bin Liu, Abinaya S. Rajamuthukumar, Lieke A. C. van Son, Alexey Bobrick, Andris Dorozsmai

Abstract: The study demonstrates that tertiary companions in tight triple systems (with outer orbital periods ≲100 days) can induce rapid binary black hole mergers via the von Zeipel-Lidov-Kozai mechanism, moderated by tidal damping and relativistic precession, leading to potential electromagnetic counterparts and gravitational-wave signals influenced by the tertiary, with these dynamics applicable even beyond chemically homogeneous evolution binaries.

Investigation of X-ray emission from the unidentified TeV gamma-ray source HESS J1832-085 with Suzaku

Time: 2025-03-21

Authors: Ebru Aktekin

Abstract: The Suzaku X-ray analysis of HESS J1832-085 reveals a highly absorbed power-law spectrum (Γ ∼1.5) with a gamma-ray flux 66 times higher than the X-ray flux, supporting a possible pulsar wind nebula origin while noting unresolved issues.
