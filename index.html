Mind the gap: addressing data gaps and assessing noise mismodeling in LISA

Time: 2025-02-24

Authors: Ollie Burke, Sylvain Marsat, Jonathan R. Gair, Michael L. Katz

Abstract: This study evaluates the impact of data gaps in the LISA mission on massive black hole binary signal analysis, testing the Whittle-likelihood's appropriateness, deriving corrected methods (including tapering windows), analyzing noise modeling effects and segment independence assumptions, and providing practical solutions for integration into data processing pipelines.

Mapping Parameter Correlations in Spinning Binary Black Hole Mergers

Time: 2025-02-24

Authors: Karen Kang, Simona J. Miller, Katerina Chatziioannou, Deborah Ferguson

Abstract: This study employs a neural network-based waveform analysis to identify and quantify correlations between black hole spins and mass ratios in gravitational wave signals, revealing known inspiral-phase correlations in low-mass systems and altered, weaker correlations in high-mass, merger-dominated systems, aiding the development of effective spin parameters where analytic models are lacking.

Accounting for the Known Unkown: A Parametric Framework to Incorporate Systematic Waveform Errors in Gravitational-Wave Parameter Estimation

Time: 2025-02-24

Authors: Sumit Kumar, Max Melching, Frank Ohme

Abstract: This study presents a Bayesian framework incorporating parametrized waveform phase errors (relative or absolute) to address systematic uncertainties in gravitational wave parameter estimation, demonstrating through simulations that modeling these errors corrects parameter biases and integrates with existing tools via a provided Python package.

Double progenitor origin of the S-star cluster

Time: 2025-02-24

Authors: Sill Verberne, Elena Maria Rossi, Sergey E. Koposov, Zephyr Penoyre, Manuel Cavieres, Konrad Kuijken

Abstract: The study demonstrates that combining binary stars from both the nuclear star cluster and the clockwise disk via the Hills mechanism successfully explains the S-star cluster's properties, hypervelocity star counts, and reveals distinct origins: main-sequence S-stars from the disk, evolved ones from the cluster, and most hypervelocity stars from the disk.

The origin of the very-high-energy radiation along the jet of Centaurus A

Time: 2025-02-24

Authors: Cainã de Oliveira, James H. Matthews, Vitor de Souza

Abstract: This study combines relativistic hydrodynamic simulations, shock acceleration theory, and observational data to propose that stationary knots in Centaurus A's jets, formed by interactions with stellar winds, are responsible for accelerating ultra-relativistic electrons that produce the detected very-high-energy gamma-ray spectrum.

Accretion bursts in young intermediate-mass stars make planet formation challenging

Time: 2025-02-24

Authors: Indrani Das, Eduard Vorobyov, Shantanu Basu

Abstract: This study's numerical simulations reveal that in protoplanetary disks around young intermediate-mass stars, magnetorotational instability (MRI) bursts triggered by thermal ionization disrupt dust rings crucial for planetesimal formation via streaming instability (SI), compounded later by increased dust drift and expanded dead zones, explaining the scarcity of planets around stars exceeding 3 solar masses compared to low-mass counterparts.

Observe Gamma-Rays and Neutrinos Associated with Ultra-High Energy Cosmic Rays

Time: 2025-02-24

Authors: Qinyuan Zhang, Xishui Tian, Zhuo Li

Abstract: The IceCube neutrino flux's alignment with the Waxman-Bahcall bound suggests a shared origin with ultra-high-energy cosmic rays (UHECRs), prompting proposed gamma-ray/neutrino observations toward UHECR arrival directions that predict a ∼10% detection probability per event under specific sensitivities and source densities, encouraging future projects like LHAASO-WCDA, CTA, and IceCube/Gen2 to identify sources or constrain their density.

High Energy Jet Emission from GRS 1758-258 & 1E 1740.7-2942 with INTEGRAL?

Time: 2025-02-24

Authors: James Rodi, Angela Bazzano, Pietro Ubertini

Abstract: A 19-year INTEGRAL/IBIS study of Galactic Center black hole binaries GRS 1758-258 and 1E 1740.7-2942 revealed persistent hard X-ray tails above ~200 keV, with correlation analyses favoring synchrotron jet emission over hybrid plasma models, particularly for GRS 1758-258, while 1E 1740.7-2942's 300-600 keV emissions remained constant.

Overall Ultra-high Energy Neutrino Emission from GRBs during Jet Expansion

Time: 2025-02-24

Authors: Qinyuan Zhang, Zhuo Li

Abstract: This study calculates the time-integrated neutrino spectrum from expanding gamma-ray burst jets by numerically solving continuity equations for nucleons, pions, and muons, finding that neutrino production at large radii dominates high-energy fluence and alters flavor fractions, suggesting future observations by GRAND and IceCube-Gen2 could test GRBs as ultra-high-energy cosmic ray sources.

Resolving spatial and temporal shock structures using LOFAR observations of type II radio bursts

Time: 2025-02-24

Authors: D. E. Morosan, I. C. Jebaraj, P. Zhang, P. Zucca, B. Dabrowski, P. T. Gallagher, A. Krankowski, C. Vocks, R. Vainio

Abstract: Using high-resolution LOFAR radio imaging and EUV observations of a 3 October 2023 multi-lane type II solar radio burst, researchers identified multiple accelerating sources linked to a coronal mass ejection-driven shock, revealing nearly self-similar shock expansion with hotspots from corrugations or ambient plasma structures at ~10⁵ km scales, underscoring the critical role of spatial resolution in understanding shock dynamics and electron acceleration mechanisms.
