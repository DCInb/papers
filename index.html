An X-ray and Optical Study of the Dwarf Nova Candidate OGLE-BLG-DN-0064

Time: 2025-04-07

Authors: A. B. Sibgatullin, V. I. Dodon, I. I. Galiullin, A. I. Kolbin, V. V. Shimansky, A. S. Vinokurov

Abstract: The system OGLE-BLG-DN-0064 is classified as an SU UMa-type dwarf nova, supported by its X-ray luminosity (~1.6×10³² erg s⁻¹), high X-ray-to-optical flux ratio (~1.5), double-peaked emission lines, superoutbursts with a ~400-day supercycle, and an estimated orbital period of 1.5–3.5 hours.

Distortions in Periodicity Analysis of Blazars: The Impact of Flares

Time: 2025-04-07

Authors: Pablo Peñil, Nuria Torres-Albà, Alba Rico, Marco Ajello, Sara Buson, Sagar Adhikari

Abstract: This study demonstrates that stochastic flares in blazars can severely distort intrinsic periodic emission patterns, causing deviations exceeding 100% in significance and periods or even erasing periodicity, and proposes Singular Spectrum Analysis and sigma clipping as robust mitigation strategies for such astrophysical sources.

X-ray variability of VHE detected FSRQs: A comparative study

Time: 2025-04-07

Authors: Lekshmi Dathan, Pankaj Kushwaha

Abstract: This study's analysis of nine very high energy (VHE)-detected Flat Spectrum Radio Quasars (FSRQs) using Swift XRT data reveals no correlation between VHE activity and X-ray flux or spectral states, identifies an anti-correlation between X-ray flux and spectral index, and suggests that softer or flat X-ray spectra during low-flux states (indicating potential synchrotron contributions) may extend gamma-ray spectra into the VHE range via inverse Compton scattering, implying these FSRQs could be candidates for persistent VHE activity.

Time-resolved spectro-polarimetric analysis of extremely bright GRB 230307A: Evidence of evolution from photospheric to synchrotron dominated emission

Time: 2025-04-07

Authors: Soumya Gupta, Rahul Gupta, Tanmoy Chattopadhayay, Sunder Sahayanathan, D. Frederiks, D. Svinkin, Dipankar Bhattacharya, Judith Racusin, Santosh Vadawale, Varun Bhalerao, A. Lysenko, A. Ridnaia, A. Tsvetkova, M. Ulanov

Abstract: Spectro-polarimetric analysis of the exceptionally bright, Kilonova-associated GRB 230307A reveals a transition from initially unpolarized thermal photospheric emission to highly polarized non-thermal synchrotron radiation in later stages, driven by evolving magnetic field influences on jet dynamics.

Quasi-periodic sub-structure of RRAT J1913+1330

Time: 2025-04-07

Authors: Zhenfan Tang, Songbo Zhang, Jieshuang Wang, Xuan Yang, Xuefeng Wu

Abstract: Using the FAST telescope, researchers detected 12 quasi-periodic sub-structures in RRAT J1913+1330, the second such RRAT observed with this feature, reinforcing the link between quasi-periodicity and rotational periods, while polarization analysis showed high linear polarization in 11 pulses, circular polarization in 3, and no correlation between sub-structure periods and pulse characteristics despite extreme variability.

Modeling the circumstellar interaction around SN 2004gq

Time: 2025-04-07

Authors: A. P. Nagy, B. H. Pál, T. Szalai

Abstract: The study of SN 2004gq's radio and bolometric light curves, combining models of radioactive decay, magnetar energy, and circumstellar interaction, demonstrates that hidden interaction with circumstellar matter provides the necessary energy to explain its luminosity and supports estimating the progenitor's mass-loss rate for Type Ib/c supernovae.

The GRINTA hard X-ray mission: an Explorer of the Transient Sky

Time: 2025-04-07

Authors: James Rodi, Lorenzo Natalucci

Abstract: The GRINTA mission concept aims to fill the anticipated 2030s gap in multi-messenger astrophysics by combining a large field-of-view detector (20 keV–10 MeV) for detecting impulsive events like gravitational wave counterparts and gamma-ray bursts with a coded mask telescope (5–200 keV) for localization and follow-up, while broadly exploring the hard X-ray/soft gamma-ray sky.

Binary neutron star merger offsets from their host galaxies II. Short-duration gamma-ray bursts

Time: 2025-04-07

Authors: N. Gaspari, A. J. Levan, A. A. Chrimes, A. E. Nugent

Abstract: This study demonstrates that the observed offsets of short gamma-ray bursts (SGRBs) from their host galaxies are consistent with binary neutron star (BNS) mergers—primarily influenced by supernova kicks and host galaxy types—being the dominant progenitors, while neutron star-black hole (NSBH) mergers provide a poorer fit, and suggests revising host association criteria for late-type galaxies and addressing discrepancies in early-types through improved galaxy evolution models or reassessing spurious host associations.

Low-Count X-ray Polarimetry using the Bayesian Approach Reveals Fast Polarization Angle Variations

Time: 2025-04-07

Authors: Hong Li, Qing-Chang Zhao, Hua Feng, Lian Tao, Sergey S. Tsygankov

Abstract: The Bayesian analysis of IXPE data below the minimum detectable polarization threshold revealed that polarization angle variations in GX 13+1 and Sco X-1 involve discrete swings between two emitting components (e.g., boundary and spreading layers) correlated with accretion rates, challenging previous linear rotation interpretations and aligning with historical measurements.
