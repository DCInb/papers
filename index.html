Nonparametric extensions of nuclear equations of state: probing the breakdown scale of relativistic mean-field theory

Time: 2025-05-12

Authors: Isaac Legred, Liam Brodie, Alexander Haber, Reed Essick, Katerina Chatziioannou

Abstract: The study develops a hybrid equation of state model for neutron stars, combining relativistic mean-field theory at low densities with a flexible nonparametric approach at higher pressures to address theoretical uncertainties, finding no current evidence of breakdown but demonstrating that future high-precision observations could detect potential phase transitions.

Rapid formation of a very massive star >50000 $M_\odot$ and subsequently an IMBH from runaway collisions. Direct N-body and Monte Carlo simulations of dense star clusters

Time: 2025-05-12

Authors: Marcelo C. Vergara, Abbas Askar, Albrecht W. H. Kamlah, Rainer Spurzem, Francesco Flammini Dotti, Dominik R. G. Schleicher, Manuel Arca Sedda, Arkadiusz Hypki, Mirek Giersz, Jarrod Hurley, Peter Berczik, Andres Escala, Nils Hoyer, Nadine Neumayer, Xiaoying Pang, Ataru Tanikawa, Renyue Cen, Thorsten Naab

Abstract: Simulations using \textsc{Nbody6++GPU} and \textsc{MOCCA} with updated stellar evolution prescriptions demonstrate that runaway collisions in a dense, massive young star cluster produce a very massive star (>5×10⁴ M☉) within 5 Myr, collapsing into an intermediate-mass black hole, suggesting dense environments as potential nurseries for early black hole seeds and providing theoretical context for JWST observations of high-redshift clusters.

Thermal and Kinematic Properties of Ejecta in SN1987A revealed by XRISM

Time: 2025-05-12

Authors: XRISM collaboration

Abstract: The XRISM Resolve instrument's analysis of SN 1987A's spectra reveals a single-component shock plasma model (2.84 keV temperature, 2.64×10¹¹ s/cm³ ionization) with LMC-like metal abundances, indicating X-rays originate from non-metal-rich ejecta where the reverse shock has not yet reached metal-rich regions, supported by Doppler broadening (1500–1700 km/s velocities) and aligned with previous ⁴⁴Ti mass constraints.

Traces of wobbling accretion disk in X-ray pulsar Her X-1 from observations of the ART-XC telescope of the SRG observatory

Time: 2025-05-12

Authors: V. M. Revnivtsev, K. A. Postnov, S. V. Molkov, N. I. Shakura, A. Lutovinov, I. Yu. Lapshov, D. A. Kolesnikov, A. Yu. Tkachenko

Abstract: The ART-XC telescope's two-day observation of Her X-1 revealed the orbital phase of its X-ray turn-on, provided the first direct evidence of a 0.87-day nutation period in the accretion disk, detected pre-turn-on X-ray pulsations, and identified scattering corona effects during eclipse and post-turn-on flux increase.

Time: 2025-05-12

Authors: K. Perger, S. Frey, K. É. Gabányi, E. Kun

Abstract: New VLBA observations confirm the radio quasar NVSS J171822+423948 as a blazar with highly variable, Doppler-boosted emission, compact multi-scale structures, a slightly inverted spectrum, and a jet inclination ≤5°, supporting its potential link to the IceCube neutrino event IC-201221A.

Iterative Relaxation Method to Obtain Global Transonic Flows around Compact Objects

Time: 2025-05-12

Authors: Shilpa Sarkar, I. M. Kulikov

Abstract: The authors propose a generic methodology (IRM-SP and IRM-SHOCK) to unify global transonic accretion and wind solutions around compact objects by incorporating viscosity, radiative cooling, heating, and the interplay of gravitational and centrifugal forces, which generate multiple sonic points and shocks, extending prior iterative relaxation techniques like Becker and Le (2003).

Tracing the evolution of eccentric precessing binary black holes: a hybrid approach

Time: 2025-05-12

Authors: Amitesh Singh, Nathan K. Johnson-McDaniel, Anuradha Gupta, Khun Sang Phukon

Abstract: This study generalizes the \texttt{tilts\_at\_infinity} code to compute spin tilts at formation for eccentric, spin-precessing binary black holes using hybrid post-Newtonian evolution, demonstrating that eccentricity significantly impacts tilts and identifying scenarios where tilts at infinity effectively approximate those at formation.

Discovery of Main-sequence Radio Pulse emitters from widefield sky surveys

Time: 2025-05-12

Authors: Barnali Das, Matt E. Shultz, Joshua Pritchard, Kovi Rose, Laura N. Driessen, Yuanming Wang, Andrew Zic, Tara Murphy, Gregory Sivakoff

Abstract: This study introduces a novel, polarization-independent strategy using ASKAP's multi-epoch variability data to identify three new Main-sequence Radio Pulse emitters (MRPs) via follow-up ATCA observations, revealing that ECME luminosity in magnetic A and late-B stars is influenced by temperature and magnetic field strength with a degeneracy between them, while the current sample remains insufficient to assess stellar rotation's role in radio emission.

Estimating the Lensing Probability for Binary Black Hole Mergers in AGN disk by Using Mismatch Threshold

Time: 2025-05-11

Authors: Wen-Long Xu, Yu-Zhe Li, Yi-Gu Chen, Hui Li, Wei-Hua Lei

Abstract: This study finds that the probability of gravitational wave events from stellar-mass binary black holes merging in AGN disks being lensed by supermassive black holes is several times higher than prior estimates when accounting for waveform mismatch and signal-to-noise ratio, offering implications for detecting lensed events or constraining BBH formation channels.
