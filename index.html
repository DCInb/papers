Long-Term Multidimensional Models of Core-Collapse Supernovae: Progress and Challenges

Time: 2025-02-20

Authors: H. -Thomas Janka

Abstract: Recent 3D core-collapse supernova simulations have advanced understanding of neutrino-driven explosions, reproducing observed energies, neutron-star properties, and predicting signals, but uncertainties persist regarding progenitor outcomes, black-hole formation scenarios, neutrino physics, nuclear equations of state, and unresolved puzzles like SN 1987A anomalies and NS spin-kick correlations.

Super-Eddington Accretion in Quasars

Time: 2025-02-20

Authors: P. Marziani, K. Garnica Luna, A. Floris, A. del Olmo, A. Deconto-Machado, T. M. Buendia-Rios, C. A. Negrete, D. Dultzin

Abstract: This review explores super-Eddington accretion in quasars by analyzing optical/UV selection criteria, extreme spectral features (e.g., high iron emission, outflows, metal enrichment), and evidence from early-universe black hole growth rates, proposing their potential as stable cosmological distance indicators and drawing parallels with stellar-mass black hole accretion dynamics.

Constraints on optical and near-infrared variability in the localisation of the long-period radio transient GLEAM-X J1627-52

Time: 2025-02-20

Authors: J. D. Lyman, V. S. Dhillon, S. Kamann, A. A. Chrimes, A. J. Levan, I. Pelisoli, D. T. H. Steeghs, K. Wiersema

Abstract: The study of the enigmatic, periodically pulsing (~18 min) radio transient GLEAM-X J1627-52 found no conclusive evidence supporting a white dwarf pulsar origin through optical, near-infrared, and spectroscopic analyses, leaving isolated magnetars or compact binaries as viable models while highlighting the challenges of such searches in dense Galactic regions.

Magnetic Thomson Transport in High Opacity Domains

Time: 2025-02-20

Authors: Matthew G. Baring, Kun Hu, Hoa Dinh Thi

Abstract: This paper presents a new analysis of polarized magnetic Thomson radiative transfer in high-opacity neutron star environments, reducing complex integro-differential equations to a compact pair solved numerically to provide empirical approximations that accelerate simulations, enable radiation pressure specification, and apply to magnetars, pulsars, and X-ray binaries.

How does Radiation Reaction Affect Relativistic Magnetized Shocks Emission

Time: 2025-02-20

Authors: Yu Zhang, Yuanpei Yang, Liangliang Ji

Abstract: This study demonstrates through Particle-In-Cell simulations that radiation reaction effects in relativistic magnetized shocks driven by the Synchrotron Maser Instability modify particle dynamics and emission properties, producing observational signatures such as spectral broadening and peak frequency upshift, which explain key FRB characteristics like bimodal energy distribution and luminosity-bandwidth correlations, supporting magnetized shocks as viable FRB sources.

MOJAVE -- XXII. Brightness temperature distributions and geometric profiles along parsec-scale AGN jets

Time: 2025-02-20

Authors: E. V. Kravchenko, I. N. Pashchenko, D. C. Homan, Y. Y. Kovalev, M. L. Lister, A. B. Pushkarev, E. Ros, T. Savolainen

Abstract: This study of 447 AGN jets using 15 GHz VLBA observations (1994–2019) reveals conical expansion (jet size ∝ r¹.⁰²) and brightness temperature gradients (Tb ∝ r⁻².⁸²), with half the sources showing non-monotonic profiles explained by shocks, helical structures, or jet dynamics, supporting models of optically thin moving blobs and magneto-hydrodynamic acceleration during jet shape transitions.

A possible overall scenario for the outburst evolution of MAXI J1820+070 revealed by Insight-HXMT

Time: 2025-02-20

Authors: J. Q. Peng, S. Zhang, Y. P. Chen, L. D. Kong, P. J. Wang, S. N. Zhang, L. Ji, L. Tao, J. L. Qu, M. Y. Ge, Q. C. Shui, J. Li, Z. Chang, Z. S. Li, Y. X. Xiao

Abstract: The study of MAXI J1820+070's 2018 outburst using Insight-HXMT data reveals energy-dependent time lags (days-scale) between low and high-energy photons, reshaping the canonical hysteresis 'q' diagram into a linear form, with spectral and temporal analyses indicating variable corona coverage and evolution during state transitions linked to changes in high-frequency breaks and coverage fractions.

The Thousand-Pulsar-Array programme on MeerKAT XVII: Discovery of beating radio emission variability in PSR J1514-4834

Time: 2025-02-20

Authors: J. A. Hsu, P. Weltevrede, G. Wright, M. J. Keith, L. S. Oswald, X. Song, H. Wang

Abstract: The study of PSR J1514-4834's complex subpulse modulation using a novel phase-correlation methodology reveals coexisting periodicities forming a beat system from magnetospheric time-delayed interactions, challenging the carousel model and offering a new approach to explore pulsar magnetosphere dynamics.

Do Short GRBs Exhibit an Anticorrelation between Their Intrinsic Duration and Redshift?

Time: 2025-02-20

Authors: Ali M. Hasan, Walid J. Azzam

Abstract: This study confirms an anticorrelation between intrinsic duration and redshift for long gamma-ray bursts (LGRBs) using an expanded dataset, finds no such correlation for short GRBs (SGRBs), and discusses implications for metallicity evolution with redshift in shaping this duration-redshift relationship.

Dragging of inertial frames in the composed Kerr-Newman-orbiting-ring system

Time: 2025-02-20

Authors: Shahar Hod

Abstract: The study reveals that in a Kerr-Newman-black-hole-ring system, the frame-dragging-induced change in the horizon's angular velocity (ΔΩ_H) explicitly depends on the central black hole's angular momentum (J_H), diverging from the spin-independent relation ΔΩ_H = J_R/(4M³) previously observed in Kerr systems.
