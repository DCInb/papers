Baryon asymmetry constraints on magnetic field from the Electroweak epoch

Time: 2025-04-10

Authors: T. Boyer, A. Neronov

Abstract: The study revises constraints on helical hypermagnetic fields from the Electroweak epoch, demonstrating their decay could generate the observed baryon asymmetry without overproduction, with field strengths as low as 1e-5 of the maximum requiring correlation lengths ≥1e-6 of the Hubble radius (relaxing proportionally for weaker fields), potentially leaving detectable intergalactic magnetic fields (up to 10-100 pG) that may influence cosmological recombination and the Hubble tension, while baryon isocurvature perturbations pose no observational constraints.

Deflection angle in the strong deflection limit of axisymmetric spacetimes: local curvature, matter fields, and quasinormal modes

Time: 2025-04-10

Authors: Takahisa Igata

Abstract: This study investigates photon deflection in strong-field regimes of static, axisymmetric spacetimes, characterizing the logarithmic divergence of deflection angles near critical impact parameters via coordinate-invariant curvature and matter contributions, links these to quasinormal modes in the eikonal limit using curvature scalars, and provides a unified framework connecting gravitational lensing observables and quasinormal modes to the local geometry and matter distribution near compact objects.

Orbit design for mitigating interstellar scattering effects in Earth-space VLBI observations of Sgr A*

Time: 2025-04-10

Authors: Aditya Tamar, Ben Hudson, Daniel C. M. Palumbo

Abstract: The study investigates how interstellar medium turbulence affects the Black Hole Explorer (BHEX) mission's ability to observe Sagittarius A*'s photon ring, identifying the BHEX-ALMA baseline's broad sensitivity, the need for shorter baselines via orbit migration (using chemical or electric propulsion), and orbit-dependent trade-offs between spacetime feature resolution and photon ring morphology capture.

Sculpting the outer edge of accretion disks in pre-circumbinary binary black hole systems

Time: 2025-04-10

Authors: Fabien Casse, Peggy Varniere, Léna Arthur, Fabrice Dodu

Abstract: This study uses 2D hydrodynamical simulations to demonstrate that a secondary black hole in a pre-circumbinary disk binary system alters the primary's accretion disk by reducing its outer radius (dependent on mass ratio), generating spiral arms, and creating an elliptical edge—features that could aid in identifying such early-stage binary black holes.

Resummation of Universal Tails in Gravitational Waveforms

Time: 2025-04-10

Authors: Mikhail M. Ivanov, Yue-Zhou Li, Julio Parra-Martinez, Zihan Zhou

Abstract: This study presents a universal formula for the anomalous scaling of multipole moments in gravitating systems, derived via effective field theory through black hole absorption and gravitational wave scattering phase shifts, revealing renormalized angular momentum's role and enabling a novel resummation of gravitational waveform logarithms to enhance signal modeling for experiments.

Horizons, throats and bounces in hybrid metric-Palatini gravity with a non-zero potential

Time: 2025-04-10

Authors: Gabriel I. Róis, José Tarciso S. S. Junior, Francisco S. N. Lobo, Manuel E. Rodrigues

Abstract: This study explores exact electrically charged solutions—including traversable wormholes, black holes, and black bounces—in hybrid metric-Palatini gravity (HMPG) with a non-zero scalar potential, revealing through inverse problem techniques in Jordan and Einstein frames a richer array of geometries (e.g., black universes, multi-horizon black holes) than General Relativity, thereby positioning HMPG as a robust framework for modeling extreme astrophysical environments with novel curvature and causal structures.

The effect of background matter on the spin oscillations of neutrinos scattered by the supermassive black hole

Time: 2025-04-10

Authors: Mridupawan Deka, Maxim Dvornikov

Abstract: This study explores neutrino spin oscillations in relativistic matter within accretion disks around spinning Kerr black holes, employing numerical simulations to analyze gravitational scattering effects in co- and counter-rotating disk scenarios and presenting key findings from extensive test neutrino data.

Symmetry energy dependence of the bulk viscosity of nuclear matter

Time: 2025-04-10

Authors: Yumu Yang, Mauricio Hippert, Enrico Speranza, Jorge Noronha

Abstract: The bulk viscosity and relaxation time in neutron-transparent neutron star matter depend on the nuclear symmetry energy (\(S\)) and its slope (\(L\)), with \(L\)'s experimental uncertainties causing orders-of-magnitude variations in viscosity, suggesting gravitational-wave signatures from neutron star mergers could constrain these nuclear properties.

How to identify the object with mass range of $(2.2-3)M_\odot$ in the merger of compact star systems

Time: 2025-04-10

Authors: ZhaoWei Du, HouJun Lü, Xiaoxuan Liu, XiLong Fan, EnWei Liang

Abstract: This paper proposes identifying the nature of 2.2-3 solar mass compact objects in gravitational wave events through multi-band electromagnetic emissions during inspiral (with luminosity scaling \(L \propto (-t)^{7/4}\) or \(L \propto (-t)^{-5/4}\)) and post-merger phases (bright/dark kilonova depending on ejecta mass ratios), considering scenarios involving low-mass black holes, massive neutron stars, or high-spin black holes, though current telescopes may detect these signals only at close distances.
