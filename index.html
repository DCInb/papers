Variability study of classical supergiant X-ray binary 4U 1907+09 using NuSTAR

Time: 2025-04-03

Authors: Raj Kumara, Sayantan Bhattacharya, Sudip Bhattacharyya, Subir Bhattacharyyaa

Abstract: The 2024 NuSTAR observation of supergiant X-ray binary 4U 1907+09 revealed significant flux variability with dips and flares, a 443.99 s pulsar spin period showing continued spin-down, persistent ∼17.6 keV and ∼38 keV cyclotron features unaffected by 25-fold flux changes, and spectral-phase relationships indicating a "pencil" beam accretion model, offering insights into magnetic field geometry and accretion dynamics.

Monte Carlo evaluations of gamma-ray and radio pulsar populations

Time: 2025-04-03

Authors: Shawaiz Tabassum, Duncan Lorimer

Abstract: This study evaluates seven γ-ray luminosity models using neutron star populations and Fermi-LAT data, finding four models consistent with observed unidentified pulsar-like sources in 4FGL-DR4, while three over-predict them, suggesting revised beaming fractions or luminosity scaling, highlights unresolved discrepancies for γ-ray canonical pulsars, and encourages targeted radio searches to address these issues.

Unveiling the reflection spectrum in the ultracompact LMXB 4U 1820-30

Time: 2025-04-03

Authors: A. Anitra, A. Gnarini, T. Di Salvo, R. Iaria, A. Sanna, L. Burderi, A. Marino, M. Del Santo, G. Matt, F. Ursini, S. Bianchi, F. Capitanio, S. Fabiani, A. Tarana, A. Di Marco

Abstract: This study of the ultracompact X-ray binary 4U 1820-30, combining NICER, NuSTAR, and IXPE data, detected a strong reflection component enabling the first system inclination measurement (~31°), revealed subsolar iron abundances likely tied to its metal-poor globular cluster environment, and observed energy-dependent polarization increasing to ~8% at higher energies, challenging current models despite the low inclination angle.

GRB 170519A: Thermal Radiation in an X-ray Flare and Decaying Magnetic Fields for the Early-Time Afterglow

Time: 2025-04-03

Authors: Zi-Min Zhou, Liang-Jun Chen, Rui-Quan Li, Xiang-Gao Wang, Xing-Ling Li, En-Wei Liang, WeiKang Zheng, Alexei V. Filippenko

Abstract: The multiwavelength analysis of GRB 170519A's afterglow reveals X-ray flares, an optical onset bump, and a possible jet break, characterized by a thermal component in the X-ray flare with evolving temperature and Lorentz factor, empirical luminosity-temperature-Γ relations, and is well-modeled by an external shock with a rapidly decaying magnetic field parameter (ε_B) in early phases.

Search for Fast Radio Bursts and radio pulsars from pulsing Ultraluminous X-ray Sources

Time: 2025-04-03

Authors: Juntao Bai, Na Wang, Rui Luo, Wei-Yang Wang, Shi Dai, Songbo Zhang, Shiqian Zhao, Shuangqiang Wang

Abstract: Researchers conducted radio searches using FAST and Parkes telescopes on eight pulsing ultraluminous X-ray sources (PULXs), detecting no significant FRB or pulsar signals, implying that PULXs' accretion processes, stellar winds, and emission beaming likely suppress coherent radio emissions, thereby constraining models linking FRBs to magnetized neutron stars in binaries.

Transient gamma rays from the 2021 outburst of the recurrent nova RS Ophiuchi: the effect of gamma-ray absorption

Time: 2025-04-02

Authors: Vo Hong Minh Phan, Pierre Cristofari, Enrico Peretti, Vincent Tatischeff, Andrea Ciardi

Abstract: The study demonstrates that the observed two-day delay between GeV and TeV gamma-ray peaks in the 2021 RS Ophiuchi nova outburst arises from gamma-ray absorption caused by interactions with optical photons emitted during the eruption, underscoring the critical role of multi-wavelength observations in understanding particle acceleration mechanisms in such systems.

Probing axion-like particles with multimessenger observations of neutron star mergers

Time: 2025-04-02

Authors: Francesca Lecce, Alessandro Lella, Giuseppe Lucente, Vimal Vijayan, Andreas Bauswein, Maurizio Giannotti, Alessandro Mirizzi

Abstract: The study finds that axion-like particles (ALPs) produced in binary neutron star mergers via nucleon bremsstrahlung could convert into detectable short gamma-ray bursts through ALP-photon interactions in magnetic fields, observable alongside gravitational waves, with current and future MeV gamma-ray experiments achieving sensitivities down to \( g_{a\gamma} \gtrsim \textrm{few} \times 10^{-13}\,\text{GeV}^{-1} \) for \( m_a \lesssim 10^{-9}\,\mathrm{eV} \), rivaling constraints from SN 1987A.
