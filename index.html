The Rapidly--Changing Period of the QPE Source 1ES~1927+654

Time: 2025-05-05

Authors: Andrew King

Abstract: The observed drastic reduction in quasiperiodic eruption intervals in the galaxy nucleus 1ES 1927+654 is attributed to von Zeipel-Lidov-Kozai cycles induced by a distant star perturbing a white dwarf-black hole binary, causing orbital plane oscillations, luminosity variations, and rapid evolution that warrants continued monitoring.

A coherent radio burst from an X-ray neutron star in the Carina Nebula

Time: 2025-05-05

Authors: K. M. Rajwade, J. Tian, G. Younes, B. Posselt, B. Stappers, Z. Wadiasingh, E. D. Barr, M. C. Bezuidenhout, M. Caleb, F. Jankowski, M. Kramer, I. Pastor-Marazuela, M. Surnis

Abstract: The detection of a magnetar-like radio burst from the radio-quiet neutron star 2XMM J104608.7−594306 bridges young, energetic neutron stars and evolved radio-emitting populations, suggesting a common emission mechanism across magnetic field strengths and ages, and highlights the need to monitor similar sources for sporadic radio emissions missed in prior surveys.

Can Transformers help us perform parameter estimation of overlapping signals in gravitational wave detectors?

Time: 2025-05-05

Authors: Lucia Papalini, Federico De Santi, Massimiliano Razzano, Ik Siong Heng, Elena Cuoco

Abstract: This study introduces a machine learning model combining Transformers (KENN) and Normalizing Flows (HYPERION) to rapidly and accurately estimate parameters of overlapping binary black hole mergers in the Einstein Telescope, achieving <10-20% accuracy in chirp mass and coalescence time estimates regardless of data correlations, demonstrating promise for future gravitational wave detectors.

Searching for supermassive black holes binaries within SRG/eROSITA-De I: Properties of the X-ray selected candidates

Time: 2025-05-05

Authors: D. Tubín-Arenas, M. Krumpe, D. Homan, A. Markowitz, M. Powell, G. Lamer, T. Urrutia, A. Schwope, H. Winkler, S. Bahic, J. Buchner, C. Andonie, M. Salvato, A. Merloni, J. Kurpas, S. Ciroi, F. di Mille, A. Chaturvedi, A. Rau, Z. Igo, I. Grotova, Z. Liu, K. Nandra

Abstract: This study identifies 16 supermassive black hole binary (SMBHB) candidates through quasi-periodic X-ray variability detected in SRG/eROSITA data, supported by Swift-XRT, NICER, and optical follow-up observations—with the most promising candidate, eRASSt J0530-4125, showing ~1-year periodicity—estimating an optimistic upper limit of ~0.05 SMBHBs per galaxy while emphasizing the need for further confirmation.

Constraining the neutron star equation of state by including the isoscalar-vector and isovector-vector coupling using the Bayesian analysis

Time: 2025-05-05

Authors: Deepak Kumar, Pradip Kumar Sahu

Abstract: Using Bayesian analysis within a relativistic mean field model, incorporating isoscalar-vector and isovector-vector couplings and nuclear saturation properties, we generated 20,000 viable equations of state from 60 million samples, finding that non-zero coupling parameters and negative sigma meson self-coupling stiffen the equation of state, enabling neutron stars over 2.5 M$_{\odot}$ consistent with GW190419 observations.

Thermonuclear Heating of Accreting Neutron Stars

Time: 2025-05-05

Authors: Martin Nava-Callejas, Dany Page, Yuri Cavecchi

Abstract: This study introduces a method to integrate thermonuclear heating in accreting neutron star envelopes into long-term thermal evolution simulations, deriving boundary conditions from stationary models validated with MESA to explore heat exchange dependencies on accretion rates, outburst characteristics, and crust/core properties like impurities, heating, and neutrino cooling.

The Secular Periodic Evolution of X-ray Quasi-periodic Eruptions Driven by Star-disc Collisions

Time: 2025-05-05

Authors: Jiajun Xian, Fupeng Zhang, Liming Dou, Zhining Chen

Abstract: This study uses numerical simulations of star-disc collisions to show that the observed periodic decay rates of quasi-periodic eruptions in GSN069 and eRO-QPE2 are inconsistent with stellar mass black holes or (for GSN069) degenerate cores as orbiters, but could involve solar-like main-sequence stars ablating mass over ≲12 years under specific disc surface density conditions, with future observations needed to confirm orbiter types.

A simple optimisation for the MeerKAT Pulsar Timing Array

Time: 2025-05-05

Authors: Hannah Middleton, Ryan M. Shannon, Matthew Bailes, Andrew D. Cameron, Alessandro Corongiu, Marisa Geyer, Max Jones, Michael Kramer, Matthew T. Miles, Aditya Parthasarathy, Andrea Possenti, Daniel J. Reardon

Abstract: A greedy algorithm optimizing the MeerKAT pulsar timing array's observation schedule by reallocating integration time among pulsars without altering total observation time could enhance the gravitational wave detection signal-to-noise ratio by ≈20% over a decade, under simplified noise assumptions and a stochastic background from massive black hole binaries.

Supernova remnants in super bubbles as cosmic ray accelerators

Time: 2025-05-05

Authors: Iurii Sushch, Pasquale Blasi, Robert Brose

Abstract: This study finds that supernova remnants (SNRs) evolving in the collective wind of compact star clusters can theoretically accelerate particles to PeV energies under extreme conditions, but such scenarios likely apply only to a rare subset of SNRs, mirroring the limitations seen in isolated SNR environments.

Early evolution of super-Eddington accretion flow in tidal disruption events

Time: 2025-05-05

Authors: Erlin Qiao, Yongxin Wu, Yiyang Lin, Meng Guo, Jifeng Liu, Chenlei Guo, Chichuan Jin, Ning Jiang

Abstract: This study demonstrates through radiation hydrodynamic simulations and Monte Carlo radiative transfer that viewing-angle-dependent outflows from super-Eddington accretion flows around black holes can explain the observed X-ray and optical luminosities, temperatures, emission radii, and their evolution in tidal disruption events (TDEs).
