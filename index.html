Luminosity Function of collapsar Gamma-Ray Bursts:the Progenitor of Long Gamma-Ray Bursts Is Not Singular

Time: 2025-05-01

Authors: Yan-Kun Qu, Zhong-Xiao Man, Shuang-Xi Yi, Yu-Peng Yang

Abstract: The study of high-redshift gamma-ray bursts (GRBs) using Swift satellite data (2004–2019) reveals that significant redshift evolution in luminosity (δ = 1.54) or density (δ = 2.09) is required to explain observations, ruling out a no-evolution model and indicating a substantial portion of long GRBs are not collapsar-related.

Spectral-timing analysis of the kilohertz quasi-periodic oscillations and constraints on the mass of the neutron star in $4U 1636-536$ using AstroSat observations

Time: 2025-05-01

Authors: Suchismito Chattopadhyay, Soma Mandal, Ranjeev Misra

Abstract: This study analyzes AstroSat data of neutron star binary 4U 1636-536, linking soft-state low-frequency QPOs (~30 Hz) and twin kilohertz QPOs to spectral transitions driven by thermal corona reflection and disk dynamics, estimating a neutron star mass of 2.37 ± 0.02 M☉ via the relativistic precession model, while time-lag and rms analyses probe corona size and radiative origins of these oscillations.

Joint inference for gravitational wave signals and glitches using a data-informed glitch model

Time: 2025-05-01

Authors: Ann-Kristin Malz, John Veitch

Abstract: This study introduces a machine-learning-based approach using normalizing flows trained on Gravity Spy glitch data to create a glitch model, which, when integrated into Bayesian inference with Bilby, simultaneously estimates glitch and signal parameters, effectively removing glitches and improving gravitational wave source parameter estimation while reducing bias.

Unified QMF equation of state for neutron star matter: Static and dynamic properties

Time: 2025-05-01

Authors: Zhonghao Tu, Xiangdong Sun, Shuochong Han, Zhiqiang Miao, Ang Li

Abstract: The study develops unified quark mean field (QMF) models with varying nuclear symmetry energy slopes (\(L_0\)) to analyze neutron star properties, revealing that higher \(L_0\) values prolong cooling via the direct Urca process, widen r-mode instability windows, and alter crust structure (heavier clusters, larger cells) compared to hadronic models, while providing viscosity parameterizations dependent on symmetry energy.

Can We Probe Spacetime Non-commutativity Through Tidal Deformability of Compact Objects?

Time: 2025-05-01

Authors: Junting Peng, Yanbo Zhao, Antonino Marciano

Abstract: This study examines the effects of spacetime non-commutativity on tidal deformability in compact objects like neutron stars and boson stars, finding that while non-commutative corrections converge to finite values at black-hole compactness limits and dominate near those regimes, such objects are unsuitable for constraining spacetime non-commutativity via gravitational wave observations.

Magnetized Accretion onto Neutron Stars: from Photon-trapped to Neutrino-cooled Flows

Time: 2025-05-01

Authors: Luciano Combi, Christopher Thompson, Daniel M. Siegel, Alexander Philippov, B. Ripperda

Abstract: Three-dimensional GRMHD simulations of hypercritical accretion onto neutron stars reveal that toroidal magnetic fields mediate energy and angular momentum through a hydrostatic atmosphere transitioning to an outer radiatively inefficient flow, achieving equilibrium via neutrino emission balancing accretion power, with implications for neutron star spin and mass evolution.

Bayesian Inference of Core Properties of Hybrid Stars from Future High-Precision Measurements of Their Radii

Time: 2025-04-30

Authors: Bao-An Li, Xavier Grundler, Wen-Jie Xie, Nai-Bo Zhang

Abstract: Future high-precision neutron star radius measurements, analyzed through a Bayesian meta-model incorporating a hadron-quark phase transition, could elucidate crust-core and phase transition densities, quark matter properties, and high-density equation-of-state parameters, with results depending on prior assumptions and improved data tightening constraints.
