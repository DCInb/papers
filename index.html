Flux tube clustering from magnetic coupling of adjacent type-I and -II superconductors in a neutron star: persistent gravitational radiation

Time: 2025-04-24

Authors: K. H. Thong, A. Melatos

Abstract: This study refines the model of coupled type-I and -II proton superconductors in pulsars by incorporating magnetic repulsion, revealing that clustered flux tubes and pinned vortices amplify gravitational wave emissions, potentially detectable by current interferometers when flux branching forms few large flux trees.

Quantifying jet-interstellar medium interactions in Cyg X-1: Insights from dual-frequency bow shock detection with MeerKAT

Time: 2025-04-24

Authors: P. Atri, S. E. Motta, Jakob van den Eijnden, James H. Matthews, James C. A. Miller-Jones, Rob Fender, David Williams-Baldwin, Ian Heywood, Patrick Woudt

Abstract: Using MeerKAT radio observations, researchers detected and characterized the bow shock formed by Cyg X-1's jets interacting with the ISM, determining an interstellar medium density of 6-7 cm⁻³, constraining the jet's power (2×10³¹–10³⁵ erg/s) and age (0.04–0.3 Myr), and revealing a lower energy transport rate than previous estimates.

The stellar corona-chromosphere connection. A comprehensive study of X-ray and Ca II IRT fluxes from eROSITA and Gaia

Time: 2025-04-24

Authors: S. Freund, S. Czesla, B. Fuhrmeister, P. Predehl, J. Robrade, P. C. Schneider, J. H. M. M. Schmitt

Abstract: This study investigates the correlation between X-ray and Ca II infrared triplet emissions in stars using eROSITA and Gaia DR3 data, revealing stronger correlations in late-type stars, saturation in fast rotators at similar Rossby numbers, and a changing flux relationship without distinct branches, with the X-ray to IRT flux ratio remaining constant in saturation and decreasing for slower rotators.

Enhancing gravitational-wave detection: a machine learning pipeline combination approach with robust uncertainty quantification

Time: 2025-04-24

Authors: Gregory Ashton, Ann-Kristin Malz, Nicolo Colombo

Abstract: The authors present a machine learning method that integrates results from multiple gravitational-wave detection pipelines with conformal prediction for calibrated uncertainty quantification, demonstrating improved efficiency and enhanced confidence in multi-pipeline detections through simulations and application to GWTC-3 events like GW200311_103121.

A non-uniform metal distribution in the ram-pressure stripped M86 group of galaxies

Time: 2025-04-24

Authors: Sinancan Kara, François Mernier, Norbert Werner, E. Nihal Ercan

Abstract: The XMM-Newton study of M86's ram-pressure-stripped hot gas reveals that its core's chemical composition aligns more with the broader Universe's hot gas than its stellar population, indicating supersonic ram-pressure alone cannot strip a galaxy's inner atmosphere, while the plume's elevated Mg/Fe ratio suggests it originated from group gas via galaxy collisions rather than core stripping, highlighting the combined role of ram-pressure and AGN activity in primordial atmosphere removal.

Atemporality from Conservation Laws of Physics in Lorentzian-Euclidean Black Hole

Time: 2025-04-24

Authors: Silvia De Bianchi, Salvatore Capozziello, Emmanuele Battista

Abstract: The paper philosophically examines "atemporality," a mechanism in Lorentzian-Euclidean black holes where transitioning to an Euclidean region with imaginary time prevents singularities and inherently upholds conservation laws.

Prospective sensitivity of CTA on detection of evaporating primordial black holes

Time: 2025-04-24

Authors: Chen Yang, Xin Zhang

Abstract: The Cherenkov Telescope Array (CTA) could improve sensitivity to detect gamma-ray bursts from primordial black holes (PBHs) of ~5×10¹⁴ g evaporating now via Hawking radiation, potentially excluding local burst rates above ~36 pc⁻³ yr⁻¹ over five years—ten times better than current limits—while proposing an optimized observation strategy.

Detection and Characterization of Quasi-Periodic Oscillations in Seyfert Galaxy NGC 4151

Time: 2025-04-24

Authors: Yan Yongkang, Zhang Peng, Lu Zhou, Bao Tong, Liu Dejian, Liu Gaochao, Liu Qingzhong, Yan Jingzhi, Zeng Xiangyun

Abstract: This study detects and characterizes quasi-periodic oscillations (QPOs) in NGC 4151 using Chandra (2007) and XMM-Newton (2015) data analyzed via Weighted Wavelet Z-transform and Lomb-Scargle periodogram methods, revealing signals at ~5.9×10⁻⁴ Hz with 3.7σ and 3.3σ confidence levels—marking the first independent detection by two telescopes over eight years—and achieving a combined 5.2σ significance, which correlates the QPOs to the central supermassive black hole's properties and advances understanding of X-ray variability mechanisms and black hole mass estimation.
