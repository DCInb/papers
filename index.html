Linking the KM3-230213A Neutrino Event to Dark Matter Decay and Gravitational Waves Signals

Time: 2025-04-22

Authors: Sarif Khan, Jongkuk Kim, Pyungwon Ko

Abstract: The KM3NeT collaboration's detection of the ultra-high-energy neutrino event KM3-230213A is proposed to originate from the decay of a 440 PeV Dirac fermion dark matter via a right-handed neutrino portal in a Type-I seesaw model, with UV freeze-in production reconciling relic density and a large dark Higgs vacuum expectation value enabling potential gravitational wave signatures detectable by future experiments.

Modeling Tidal Disruptions with Dynamical Tides

Time: 2025-04-22

Authors: Zihan Zhou, Giovanni Maria Tomaselli, Irvin Martínez-Rodríguez, Jingping Li

Abstract: This paper presents a fast, two-stage model for tidal disruption events (TDEs)—combining linear stellar perturbation theory and free-particle dynamics, calibrated with hydrodynamical simulations—to efficiently compute energy distributions and fallback rates for MESA-generated stars, with code available on GitHub.

Cosmic Clues from Amaterasu: Blazar-Driven Ultrahigh-Energy Cosmic Rays?

Time: 2025-04-22

Authors: Saikat Das, Srijita Hazra, Nayantara Gupta

Abstract: The study links the 244 EeV Amaterasu cosmic ray to the blazar PKS 1717+177 through a multi-messenger model combining UHECRs, gamma rays, and neutrinos, predicting a subdominant neutrino flux compared to TXS 0506+056, suggesting Lorentz invariance violation enables long-distance proton propagation with minimal deflection, implying strong extragalactic magnetic fields, and motivating targeted searches by neutrino telescopes during the blazar's high-activity periods.

SN 2023vbg: A Type IIn Supernova Resembling SN 2009ip, with a Long-Duration Precursor and Early-Time Bump

Time: 2025-04-22

Authors: Sota Goto, Masayuki Yamanaka, Takahiro Nagayama, Keiichi Maeda, Miho Kawabata, D. K. Sahu, Avinash Singh, Anjasha Gangopadhyay, Naveen Dkuniya, Kuntal Misra, Monalisa Dubey, Bhuvya Ailawadhi

Abstract: SN 2023vbg, a 09ip-like Type IIn supernova, exhibits unique features including a long precursor, a pre-maximum bright bump, a smooth decline post-peak, and a two-component Hα profile (lacking the typical broad component), attributed to a denser inner and smoother extended circumstellar material structure, reflecting diverse pre-supernova mass-loss histories and progenitor evolutionary pathways.

Unveiling the electrodynamic nature of spacetime collisions

Time: 2025-04-22

Authors: Siddharth Boyeneni, Jiaxi Wu, Elias R. Most

Abstract: By exactly recasting Einstein's equations into coupled nonlinear Maxwell equations resembling classical electrodynamics, researchers visualized the dynamics of gravitational electric and magnetic fields during binary black hole inspiral, merger, and ring-down, advancing the study of strong-field gravity.

A Novel Relationship Between Gamma Ray Burst Duration And Photospheric Radius

Time: 2025-04-22

Authors: Ranadeep Ghosh Dastidar, Paul Duffell

Abstract: This study revises the Collapsar model's hypothesis on long Gamma-Ray Burst (lGRB) duration, showing that *t_90* either reflects central engine duration (*t_eng*) for longer *t_eng* or depends on photospheric radius (*R_ph*) for shorter *t_eng*, proposing a new relation (*t_90 = t_eng + 0.03(R_ph/c)*) and a lower bound on *t_90* for successful lGRBs, particularly relevant for low-luminous cases where *t_eng* nears jet breakout time.

Infrared and X-ray emission of a supernova remnant in a clumpy medium

Time: 2025-04-22

Authors: S. Yu. Dedikov, E. O. Vasiliev

Abstract: This study uses 3D gas and dust dynamics to show that the infrared-to-X-ray luminosity ratio (IRX) in supernova remnants (SNRs) varies with age, impact distance, and medium inhomogeneity, with large inhomogeneities sustaining higher IRX via dust replenishment, offering insights for analyzing SNRs older than 10 kyr in the Galaxy and Large Magellanic Cloud under varying metallicity and density conditions.

Leveraging differentiable programming in the inverse problem of neutron stars

Time: 2025-04-22

Authors: Thibeau Wouters, Peter T. H. Pang, Hauke Koehn, Henrik Rose, Rahul Somasundaram, Ingo Tews, Tim Dietrich, Chris Van Den Broeck

Abstract: This study employs differential programming to enable efficient, scalable Bayesian inference and a novel optimization method for determining the nuclear equation of state from neutron star observations, addressing computational challenges and revealing consistencies or tensions between nuclear physics and astrophysics to enhance future theoretical and data analysis efforts.

Tracing the imprints of large-scale magnetized structure on $γ$ rays from GRB 221009A

Time: 2025-04-22

Authors: Saikat Das, Soebur Razzaque, Nestor Mirabal, Nicola Omodei, Kohta Murase, Israel Martinez-Castellanos

Abstract: Researchers analyzed Fermi LAT data post-GRB 221009A to explore GeV-TeV gamma-ray imprints of ultrahigh-energy cosmic rays (UHECRs), identifying two significant emission peaks—one with pre-existing gamma-ray activity and another linked to a high-energy photon—but found that alternative scenarios unrelated to UHECRs remain plausible, leaving the evidence for UHECR acceleration inconclusive.

Eccentric or circular? A reanalysis of binary black hole gravitational wave events for orbital eccentricity signatures

Time: 2025-04-22

Authors: Maria de Lluc Planas, Antoni Ramos-Buades, Cecilio García-Quirós, Héctor Estellés, Sascha Husa, Maria Haney

Abstract: The reanalysis of 17 gravitational-wave events using the eccentric model IMRPhenomTEHM found significant evidence of eccentricity in GW200129 and GW200208_22, potential eccentric features in GW190701 and GW190929, and underscored the necessity of incorporating eccentric waveform models in future analyses to mitigate parameter estimation biases as binary black hole populations grow more diverse.
