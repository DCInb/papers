A Neural Operator-Based Emulator for Regional Shallow Water Dynamics

Time: 2025-02-20

Authors: Peter Rivera-Casillas, Sourav Dutta, Shukai Cai, Mark Loveland, Kamaljyoti Nath, Khemraj Shukla, Corey Trahan, Jonghyun Lee, Matthew Farthing, Clint Dawson

Abstract: MITONet, an autoregressive neural emulator using dimensionality reduction, efficiently approximates high-dimensional solvers for parameterized PDEs to enable accurate real-time forecasting of coastal hydrodynamic processes, demonstrating extrapolation capabilities in time and parametric space for tide-driven dynamics governed by shallow-water equations.

Catching the wisps: Stellar mass-loss limits from low-frequency radio observations

Time: 2025-02-20

Authors: Sanne Bloot, Harish K. Vedantham, Robert D. Kavanagh, Joseph R. Callingham, Benjamin J. S. Pope

Abstract: This study employs low-frequency radio observations from LOFAR, combined with the absence of free-free absorption and a simple wind model, to directly determine upper mass-loss rate limits for 19 M dwarf stars, achieving sensitivity near the solar mass-loss rate and extending constraints to later spectral types (M6) and greater distances (50 pc), with future surveys like the Square Kilometre Array potentially applying this method to ~1000 stars.

MOJAVE -- XXII. Brightness temperature distributions and geometric profiles along parsec-scale AGN jets

Time: 2025-02-20

Authors: E. V. Kravchenko, I. N. Pashchenko, D. C. Homan, Y. Y. Kovalev, M. L. Lister, A. B. Pushkarev, E. Ros, T. Savolainen

Abstract: This study of 447 AGN jets using 15 GHz VLBA observations (1994–2019) reveals conical expansion (R ∝ r^1.02) and declining brightness temperatures (Tb ∝ r^-2.82), with half showing non-monotonic profiles due to shocks, helical structures, or recollimation, and Tb gradient changes at jet shape transitions supporting magneto-hydrodynamic acceleration models.

Introduction to magnetic star-planet interactions

Time: 2025-02-18

Authors: A. Strugarek, E. Shkolnik

Abstract: This chapter reviews magnetic star-planet interactions in close-in systems, where multi-wavelength observations and magnetohydrodynamic simulations reveal their sensitivity to stellar and planetary magnetic fields, driving effects on stellar activity, planetary atmospheric erosion, and orbital dynamics, while phase-resolved studies expand insights to compact systems like M dwarf habitable zones.

Accretion onto supermassive and intermediate mass black holes in cosmological simulations

Time: 2025-02-18

Authors: Rainer Weinberger, Aklant Bhowmick, Laura Blecha, Greg Bryan, Johannes Buchner, Lars Hernquist, Julie Hlavacek-Larrondo, Volker Springel

Abstract: The study introduces a flexible two-parameter gas inflow model for cosmological simulations to address uncertainties in black hole accretion, revealing that while accretion mechanisms have minimal impact on supermassive black hole observations at low redshifts, they significantly affect intermediate mass black hole activity and mergers at high redshifts.
