Magnetosphere Evolution and Precursor-Driven Electromagnetic Signals in Merging Binary Neutron Stars

Time: 2025-03-25

Authors: Dimitrios Skiathas, Constantinos Kalapotharakos, Zorawar Wadiasingh, Demosthenes Kazanas, Alice K. Harding, Paul T. Kolbeck

Abstract: This study employs force-free simulations of binary neutron star mergers to reveal configuration-dependent magnetospheric dynamics, anisotropic Poynting flux pulsations at twice the orbital frequency, EM torques capable of altering orbital dynamics or breaking crusts, TeV–PeV photon production (though hindered by pair production), and potential precursor radio signals and delayed afterglow echoes as observational signatures.

The cross-correlation between soft X-rays and galaxies A new benchmark for galaxy evolution models

Time: 2025-03-25

Authors: Johan Comparat, Andrea Merloni, Gabriele Ponti, Soumya Shreeram, Yi Zhang, Thomas H. Reiprich, Ang Liu, Riccardo Seppi, Xiaoyuan Zhang, Nicolas Clerc, Andrina Nicola, Kirpal Nandra, Mara Salvato, Nicola Malavasi

Abstract: This study constructs volume-limited galaxy samples using the Legacy Survey, measures their cross-correlation with SRG/eROSITA X-ray photons with sub-percent statistical precision, and employs an extended halo model to reveal dominant contributions from X-ray point sources at small scales (<80 kpc), satellite galaxies at intermediate scales (80 kpc–2 Mpc), and the 2-halo term beyond, with circumgalactic medium (CGM) contributing ≥10% at 20–200 kpc (increasing with stellar mass thresholds), while constraining the \( M_{500c}-L_X \) scaling relation slope to \( 1.629^{+0.091}_{-0.089} \) at 5% accuracy.

A depolarisation census of ASKAP fast radio bursts

Time: 2025-03-25

Authors: Pavan A. Uttarkar, Ryan M. Shannon, Kelly Gourdji, Adam T. Deller, Tyson Dial, Marcin Glowacki, Apurba Bera, Alexa C. Gordon, Stuart D. Ryder, Nicolas Tejos, Shivani Bhandari, Yuanming Wang

Abstract: This study reports the detection of depolarization in a non-repeating FRB (20230526A) using ASKAP, links magneto-ionic environment complexity (via σ’_RM) and PRS luminosity constraints, and supports a shared origin where non-repeating FRBs represent an older population compared to repeaters.

Stellar-wind feedback and magnetic fields around young compact star clusters: 3D MHD simulations

Time: 2025-03-25

Authors: Lucia Härer, Thibault Vieu, Brian Reville

Abstract: This study employs 3D MHD simulations of compact, massive young star clusters with magnetic stars to demonstrate that their winds create complex, non-uniform magnetic fields and shocks, naturally producing TeV-range particle spectra aligned with gamma-ray observations while ruling out PeV particle acceleration at cluster-wind termination shocks.

Abstract: The updated ALeRCE classifier integrates tidal disruption events (TDEs) as a new subclass by adding 24 features and expanding labeled data to 219,792 sources, improving performance across classes with 91% TDE recall and identifying numerous TDE candidates in ZTF alerts through features capturing their galactic position, decay patterns, and color stability.

Strongly Interacting Dark Matter admixed Neutron Stars

Time: 2025-03-25

Authors: Yannick Dengler, Suchita Kulkarni, Axel Maas, Kevin Radl

Abstract: This study explores how strongly-interacting dark matter (modeled via a QCD-like G$_2$ gauge theory with lattice-derived equations of state) accumulates in neutron stars, finding that dark matter masses between hundreds of MeV to several GeV align with observed neutron star properties.

A comparison between best-fit eccentricity definitions and the standardized definition of eccentricity

Time: 2025-03-25

Authors: Nicolas Chartier, Md Arif Shaikh, Hyung Mok Lee, JeongCho Kim

Abstract: This study demonstrates that while minimizing waveform mismatch effectively aligns eccentricity measurements ($e_{\mathrm{gw}}$) for moderate values in numerical relativity simulations, it can inaccurately favor quasi-circular models for small eccentricities, prompting a proposed convex loss function combining mismatch and $e_{\mathrm{gw}}$ difference to improve accuracy.

Ultra-High-Energy Neutrinos from Primordial Black Holes

Time: 2025-03-25

Authors: Alexandra P. Klipfel, David I. Kaiser

Abstract: The KM3NeT Collaboration's detection of a 220 PeV neutrino, challenging conventional cosmic ray models, aligns with predictions of high-energy neutrinos from evaporating primordial black holes (PBHs) with an initial mass of \(5.34 \times 10^{14} \, \rm{g}\), suggesting PBHs obeying a mass distribution consistent with dark matter could explain recent IceCube and KM3NeT observations.

Spectropolarimetry of A Nuclear Transient AT2023clx: Revealing The Geometrical Alignment between The Transient Outflow and The Nuclear Dusty Region

Time: 2025-03-24

Authors: Kohki Uno, Keiichi Maeda, Takashi Nagao, Giorgos Leloudas, Panos Charalampopoulos, Seppo Mattila, Kentaro Aoki, Kenta Taguchi, Miho Kawabata, Javier Moldon, Miguel Pérez-Torres, Miika Pursiainen, Thomas Reynolds

Abstract: The nearby tidal disruption event AT2023clx in the LINER galaxy NGC3799 exhibited unique spectropolarimetric features—a constant ∼1% polarization from an aspherical outflow and a transient blue-excess polarization (up to ∼2%) from nuclear dust via light echoes, with a 90-degree polarization angle flip indicating perpendicular alignment between outflow and dust—suggesting mechanisms linked to AGN-related structures or stellar disruption in a dusty environment.
