A magnetar powers the luminous supernova 2023pel, associated with a long gamma-ray burst

Time: 2025-04-15

Authors: L. M. Roman Aguilar, M. M. Saez, K. Ertini, M. C. Bersten

Abstract: The study concludes that SN 2023pel, linked to GRB 230812B, is best explained by a millisecond magnetar (with spin period 3.2 ms and magnetic field 28×10¹⁴ G) supplemented by radioactive decay, as its high luminosity and low expansion velocities are incompatible with a purely nickel-powered model, which requires unrealistically high nickel mass (>0.8 solar masses) and low ejecta mass.

The morphology of supernova remnant G0.9+0.1 implies explosion by jittering-jets

Time: 2025-04-15

Authors: Noam Soker

Abstract: The study identifies the point-symmetric morphology of supernova remnant G0.9+0.1 as shaped by multiple jet pairs, supported by comparisons with planetary nebulae and other remnants, strengthening the jittering jets explosion mechanism as the primary process in core-collapse supernovae.

Role of Matter Inhomogeneity on Fast Flavor Conversion of Supernova Neutrinos

Time: 2025-04-15

Authors: Soumya Bhattacharyya, Meng-Ru Wu, Zewei Xiong

Abstract: The study reveals that spatially varying matter potential λ from neutrino-electron interactions can suppress fast neutrino flavor conversions (FFC) in supernovae by stabilizing systems beyond a critical variation rate, particularly during the accretion phase, emphasizing the necessity of incorporating matter inhomogeneity in supernova models to account for neutrino flavor effects.

Discovery of a bimodal luminosity distribution in persistent Be/X-ray pulsar 2RXP J130159.6-635806

Time: 2025-04-15

Authors: Alexander Salganik, Sergey S. Tsygankov, Maria Chernyakova, Denys Malyshev, Juri Poutanen

Abstract: This study presents a comprehensive analysis of the Be/X-ray pulsar 2RXP J130159.6-635806, revealing its transition to spin equilibrium after 20 years of spin-up, the discovery of a new low-luminosity accretion regime with a bimodal distribution and distinct spectral features, ongoing accretion evidenced by pulsations and hard spectra, an estimated magnetic field strength of ∼10¹³ G, and a potential ∼90-day orbital period.

Interstellar scintillation of sources B0821+394 and B1812+412 as observed by the LPA LPI radio telescope

Time: 2025-04-15

Authors: S. A. Tyul'bashev, I. V. Chashei, I. A. Grishanova, G. E. Tyul'basheva, I. A. Subaev

Abstract: A 10-year study using the LPA LPI radio telescope at 111 MHz revealed that the observed 1.5–2.5-year variability in compact radio sources B0821+394 and B1812+412 arises from interstellar scintillation by turbulent plasma screens within 300–400 pc, not intrinsic source changes.

Dynamical Measurement of Supermassive Black Hole Masses: QPE Timing Method

Time: 2025-04-15

Authors: Cong Zhou, Zhen Pan, Ning Jiang

Abstract: This study proposes a Bayesian framework using timing data from quasi-periodic eruptions (QPEs)—caused by collisions between stellar-mass objects and supermassive black hole (SMBH) accretion disks—to precisely measure SMBH masses, particularly effective for lower-mass SMBHs (≲10⁷ M☉) and enhanced by future X-ray telescopes like CATCH.

Statistical complexity as a probe of mass and phase structure in compact objects

Time: 2025-04-15

Authors: Ch. D. Giannios, P. S. Koliogiannis, Ch. C. Moustakidis

Abstract: This study investigates statistical complexity in compact stars, revealing gravitational mass's dominant role and distinct complexity signatures from phase transitions, linking information theory to astrophysics by proposing complexity as a diagnostic tool for stellar properties and equation-of-state characteristics.

Analytical coordinate time at the second post-Newtonian order

Time: 2025-04-15

Authors: Vittorio De Falco, Marco Gallo

Abstract: This study derives the analytical expression of coordinate time \( t \) in terms of eccentric anomaly \( u \) at the second post-Newtonian order for compact binary systems on eccentric orbits, minimizing discontinuous trigonometric functions via parametrization and presenting a compact form for an infinite sum, thereby enabling efficient truncation analysis based on parameter values and desired accuracy.

X-ray polarization of accreting black holes: Cyg X-1 and Swift J1727.8-1613

Time: 2025-04-15

Authors: M. Brigitte, J. Svoboda

Abstract: The Imaging X-ray Polarimetry Explorer observed Cygnus X-1 and Swift J1727.8-1613, finding higher X-ray polarization in hard states with energy-dependent increases, consistent polarization angles aligned with radio jets, and evidence that accretion processes are independent of companion star type.

Investigating the light curve variation of magnetic white dwarfs induced by the axion-photon conversion

Time: 2025-04-15

Authors: Hao-Chen Tian, Zhao-Yang Wang, Yun-Feng Liang, Zhao-Wei Du

Abstract: This study investigates axion-photon oscillations modulated by the rotating magnetic fields of white dwarfs, using light curve data from PG1015+014 to constrain the axion-photon coupling to \( g_{a\gamma\gamma} < 8.1 \times 10^{-12} \, \mathrm{GeV^{-1}} \) at 95% credibility for axion masses \( \lesssim10^{-8}\,{\rm eV} \).
