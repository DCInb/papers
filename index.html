A Neural Operator-Based Emulator for Regional Shallow Water Dynamics

Time: 2025-02-20

Authors: Peter Rivera-Casillas, Sourav Dutta, Shukai Cai, Mark Loveland, Kamaljyoti Nath, Khemraj Shukla, Corey Trahan, Jonghyun Lee, Matthew Farthing, Clint Dawson

Abstract: MITONet, a novel autoregressive neural emulator employing dimensionality reduction, accurately forecasts coastal tide-driven hydrodynamic processes governed by parameterized PDEs, enabling real-time predictions extrapolated in time and parametric space for climate adaptation.

Catching the wisps: Stellar mass-loss limits from low-frequency radio observations

Time: 2025-02-20

Authors: Sanne Bloot, Harish K. Vedantham, Robert D. Kavanagh, Joseph R. Callingham, Benjamin J. S. Pope

Abstract: This study employs a direct method combining low-frequency coherent radio emission observations, absence of free-free absorption, and a simple wind model to establish upper mass-loss rate limits for 19 M dwarf stars detected with LOFAR, achieving sensitivity near the solar rate and extending constraints to later spectral types (M6) and greater distances (50 pc), with potential application to ∼1000 stars in future surveys using LOFAR and the Square Kilometre Array.

MOJAVE -- XXII. Brightness temperature distributions and geometric profiles along parsec-scale AGN jets

Time: 2025-02-20

Authors: E. V. Kravchenko, I. N. Pashchenko, D. C. Homan, Y. Y. Kovalev, M. L. Lister, A. B. Pushkarev, E. Ros, T. Savolainen

Abstract: This study of 447 Active Galactic Nuclei jets using 15 GHz VLBA observations (1994–2019) finds that their brightness temperature gradients (Tb ∝ r⁻².⁸²) and conical expansion (jet component size R ∝ r¹.⁰²) align with optically thin moving blobs and magneto-hydrodynamic acceleration models, with non-monotonic profiles explained by shocks, helical structures, or jet recollimation.
