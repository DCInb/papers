Observing radio transients with Phased ALMA: Pulses from the Galactic Centre magnetar

Time: 2025-04-08

Authors: J. Vera-Casanova, M. Cruces, K. Liu, J. Wongphechauxsorn, C. A. Braga, M. Kramer, P. Torne, P. Limaye, M. C. Espinoza-Dupouy, L. Rodriguez

Abstract: Using ALMA's phased mm-wave observations to bypass propagation effects in dense environments, researchers detected eight pulses from the Galactic Center magnetar PSR J1745-2900, revealing a power-law energy distribution (γ = -2.4) and demonstrating ALMA's potential to detect over 160 pulsars and study FRBs, establishing its pioneering role in mm-wave transient research with polarization capabilities.

Interface modes in inspiralling neutron stars: A gravitational-wave probe of first-order phase transitions

Time: 2025-04-08

Authors: A. R. Counsell, F. Gittins, N. Andersson, I. Tews

Abstract: The detection of resonant tidal excitations of interface modes in neutron stars through gravitational waves could serve as a direct signature of a first-order phase transition to quark matter, potentially observable with next-generation interferometers or LIGO A+ for sufficiently loud events.

Cosmic ray transport and acceleration with magnetic mirroring

Time: 2025-04-08

Authors: A. R. Bell, J. H. Matthews, A. M. Taylor, G. Giacinti

Abstract: The study demonstrates that minor deviations from uniform magnetic fields can restrict cosmic ray transport through mirroring effects, mimicking Bohm diffusion and suggesting non-relativistic shocks in young supernova remnants may accelerate cosmic rays to PeV energies and beyond without requiring magnetic field amplification on Larmor scales.

Black hole quasinormal mode resonances

Time: 2025-04-08

Authors: Yiqiu Yang, Emanuele Berti, Nicola Franchini

Abstract: The study examines resonant behaviors (avoided crossings and exceptional points) in black hole quasinormal modes, revealing that near exceptional points, modes are distinct Riemann surface covers of a single complex function, drawing parallels to resonant harmonic oscillators in time-domain signals, and demonstrating through a toy model that accurate frequency determination necessitates accounting for these resonances.

Sleeping Giants Arise: Monitoring the Return of Three Changing-Look Quasars to Their High States

Time: 2025-04-08

Authors: Laura Duffy, Michael Eracleous, John J. Ruan, Qian Yang, Jessie C. Runnoe

Abstract: Changing-look quasars' observed transitions between high and low states, supported by spectral energy distributions aligning with X-ray binary outburst models and similar transition timescales in two cases, suggest their extreme variability is driven by accretion rate changes altering the accretion flow structure.

Optical and X-ray timing analysis of the 2018-2020 outburst and rebrightening of the black-hole transient MAXI J1820+070

Time: 2025-04-08

Authors: M. Fiori, L. Zampieri, A. Burtovoi, G. Naletto, P. Ochner, U. Munari, F. Manzini, A. Vagnozzi, E. A. Barsukova, M. A. Burlak, V. P. Goranski, N. P. Ikonnikova, N. A. Katysheva, E. G. Sheyanov, S. Yu. Shugarov, A. V. Zharova, A. M. Zubareva, S. E. Motta

Abstract: The 2018-2020 outburst of MAXI J1820+070 exhibited multiwavelength and multitimescale variability, including super-orbital optical/X-ray modulations and linked quasi-periodic oscillations (QPOs) suggesting a slowly spinning black hole (spin ≲0.15) via Lense-Thirring precession models, with post-QPO modulations potentially arising from outer disc self-irradiation.

Comprehensive UV and Optical spectral analysis of Cygnus X-1: Stellar and wind parameters, abundances, and evolutionary implications

Time: 2025-04-08

Authors: V. Ramachandran, A. A. C. Sander, L. M. Oskinova, E. C. Schoesser, D. Pauli, W. -R. Hamann, L. Mahy, M. Bernini-Peron, M. Brigitte, B. Kubátová

Abstract: This comprehensive analysis of Cyg X-1, combining UV/optical/X-ray data and atmospheric models, reveals lower masses for the donor star (29$\,M_\odot$) and black hole (12.7–17.8$\,M_\odot$), super-solar Fe/Si/Mg abundances, wind parameter variations between X-ray states, a predicted future binary black hole merger (~5 Gyr), and a complex formation history linked to high initial metallicity.

The Ultraviolet Spectra of 2003fg-like Type Ia Supernovae

Time: 2025-04-08

Authors: Snehasish Bhattacharjee, Yen-Chen Pan, Hao-Yu Miao, Charles D. Kilpatrick, Willem B. Hoogendam, Katie Auchettl, Aaron Do

Abstract: The study reveals that 2003fg-like Type Ia supernovae exhibit a distinctive UV excess, unexplained by their low-mass, metal-poor host environments, but instead attributable to interaction with circumstellar material via a hot blackbody component, suggesting they explode in environments rich in such material.

Searching for quasi periodic oscillations in optical and $γ$-ray emissions $\&$ black hole mass estimation of blazar ON 246

Time: 2025-04-08

Authors: Ajay Sharma, Raj Prince, Debanjan Bose

Abstract: The detection of a ∼2-year quasi-periodic signal in the γ-ray and optical emissions of blazar ON 246 over 11.5 years of Fermi-LAT and ASAS-SN observations, confirmed with >3σ significance and 99% confidence via multiple analyses, suggests a supermassive binary black hole system or jet-induced orbital motion as potential mechanisms, with an estimated black hole mass of ∼(0.142–8.22)×10⁹ M☉.

Rainbow gravity effects on Klein-Gordon particles in a quantized nonuniform external magnetic field in the Bonnor-Melvin domain walls background

Time: 2025-04-08

Authors: Omar Mustafa, Abdullah Guvendi

Abstract: This study examines how rainbow gravity influences Klein-Gordon bosons in a magnetized Bonnor-Melvin spacetime with a cosmological constant, revealing domain walls that confine particles/antiparticles, conditional exact solutions via Heun functions, energy constraints from specific rainbow functions, and the formation of magnetized rotating vortices.
