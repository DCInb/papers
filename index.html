A Novel Method of Modeling Extended Emission of Compact Jets: Application to Swift J1727.8-1613

Time: 2025-04-29

Authors: Andrzej A. Zdziarski, Callan M. Wood, Francesco Carotenuto

Abstract: This study presents a novel analytical method for analyzing jet structures in black hole systems, applied to the X-ray binary Swift J1727.8–1613, revealing intrinsic jet asymmetry due to light travel effects, slower velocities (~0.3–0.4c), time-increasing magnetic fields, and lower magnetic flux compared to magnetically arrested disks, with implications for broader use in studying stellar-mass and supermassive black hole jets.

Relativistic ejecta from stellar mass black holes: insights from simulations and synthetic radio images

Time: 2025-04-29

Authors: Katie Savard, James H. Matthews, Rob Fender, Ian Heywood

Abstract: Numerical simulations informed by observations of the black hole X-ray binary MAXI J1820+070 reveal that relativistic ejecta must be denser than a low-density interstellar medium and launched with extreme power to propagate parsec-scale distances, forming cavities, instabilities, and particle acceleration, with synthetic radio images validating results against real telescope data.

Chaos Around the Kerr Black Hole: its Effects on Entropy and the Shadow

Time: 2025-04-29

Authors: Aydin Tavlayan, Bayram Tekin

Abstract: Chaotic motion of perturbed massless and massive particles in unstable Kerr black hole orbits produces information, enhancing black hole parameter measurements via photon subring resolution with non-equatorial Lyapunov exponents and linking plunging particles' Kolmogorov-Sinai entropy to thermodynamics while obeying Bekenstein's entropy bound.

Possible evidence for the 478 keV emission line from $^7$Be decay during the outburst phases of V1369 Cen

Time: 2025-04-29

Authors: L. Izzo, T. Siegert, P. Jean, P. Molaro, P. Bonifacio, M. Della Valle, T. Parsotan

Abstract: The first detection of the $^7$Be gamma-ray line at 478 keV from the nova V1369 Cen using INTEGRAL satellite data, yielding a lithium abundance consistent with optical studies, confirms novae as significant Galactic lithium sources and demonstrates gamma-ray astronomy's potential for precise lithium measurements.

Numerical Relativity Simulations of Dark Matter Admixed Binary Neutron Stars

Time: 2025-04-29

Authors: Edoardo Giangrandi, Hannes Rueter, Nina Kunert, Mattia Emma, Adrian Abac, Ananya Adhikari, Tim Dietrich, Violetta Sagun, Wolfgang Tichy, Constanca Providencia

Abstract: This study presents the first numerical-relativity simulations of binary neutron star mergers incorporating dark matter as a non-interacting fermionic gas, revealing that dark matter's morphology (dense core or diluted halo) significantly impacts merger dynamics, post-merger outcomes, and gravitational wave signals, with halo configurations deviating notably from standard models due to tidal deformability in a two-fluid framework, highlighting the need for refined models to guide future observations and multi-messenger astrophysics.

Multiwavelength correlation studies in the era of CTAO

Time: 2025-04-29

Authors: Michael Zacharias, Elina Lindfors, Patrizia Romano, Daniela Dorner, Stefano Vercellone, Matteo Cerruti, Jonathan Biteau

Abstract: Using the Cherenkov Telescope Array Observatory's (CTAO) detailed very-high-energy gamma-ray data and OneHaLe modeling of blazars Mrk 421 and PKS 1510-089, this study reveals that relativistic protons influence multiwavelength correlations by prolonging high-energy flares through pair cascades, while both leptonic and hadronic models only partially replicate observed data.

Timing analysis of the black-hole candidate Swift J1727.8-1613: detection of a dip-like feature in the high-energy cross spectrum

Time: 2025-04-29

Authors: Pei Jin, Mariano Méndez, Federico García, Diego Altamirano, Guobao Zhang, Sandeep K. Rout

Abstract: The study presents the first detection of a ~3-15 Hz dip in the cross spectrum of Swift J1727.8-1613's X-ray emissions, revealing phase lags and accretion flow dynamics consistent with time-dependent Comptonization models, while multi-Lorentzian fitting estimates corona sizes exceeding 10,000 km and identifies energy-dependent spectral trends around 15 keV.

Modeling of the time-resolved spectral energy distribution of blazar OJ 287 from 2008 to 2023: a comprehensive multi-epoch study

Time: 2025-04-29

Authors: G. Harutyunyan, N. Sahakyan, D. Bégué

Abstract: This 15-year multi-wavelength study of blazar OJ 287 utilized a neural network-based leptonic model to analyze 739 time-resolved spectral energy distributions, revealing distinct emission states linked to magnetic field strength, Doppler factor, and electron acceleration processes, thereby advancing understanding of jet physics and particle dynamics in active galactic nuclei.

How to turn a Supernova into a PeVatron

Time: 2025-04-29

Authors: Robert Brose, Iurii Sushch, Jonathan Mackey

Abstract: Simulations reveal that supernova remnants interacting with dense circumstellar shells within the first five months post-explosion can accelerate cosmic rays to over 1 PeV, identifying them as potential PeVatrons through enhanced particle escape and reacceleration in pre-amplified magnetic fields.
