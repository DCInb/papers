Effects of polar structure and moving ejecta on the dynamics of SGRB jets

Time: 2025-02-25

Authors: Leonardo Garcia-Garcia, Diego Lopez-Camara, Davide Lazzati

Abstract: This study uses 2D relativistic hydrodynamic simulations to demonstrate that the collimation, speed, and electromagnetic signatures of short gamma-ray burst jets from neutron star mergers are critically shaped by the density structure of funnel-like ejecta and their expansion dynamics, with narrow or expanding ejecta enhancing jet collimation and influencing post-breakout behavior.

The cosmic-ray sea explains the diffuse Galactic gamma-ray and neutrino emission from GeV to PeV

Time: 2025-02-25

Authors: Pedro De La Torre Luque, Daniele Gaggero, Dario Grasso, Antonio Marinelli, Manuel Rocamora

Abstract: The LHAASO collaboration's gamma-ray data (1 TeV–1 PeV) align with Galactic cosmic ray models matching CALET, DAMPE, and KASCADE spectra, requiring no extra components, with both conventional and γ-optimized transport setups compatible, though the latter is preferred when integrating Fermi-LAT and neutrino data, making it a recommended template for future Galactic neutrino analyses.

Constraints on Lorentz invariance violation in neutrino sector from the ultra-high-energy event KM3-230213A

Time: 2025-02-25

Authors: Yu-Ming Yang, Xing-Jian Lv, Xiao-Jun Bi, Peng-Fei Yin

Abstract: Using the highest-energy neutrino ever detected (KM3-230213A, ~220 PeV), this study establishes a stringent lower bound on second-order Lorentz invariance violation at \(\Lambda_2 > 5.0 \times 10^{19}\) GeV (90% confidence level), reinforcing the principle that no particle exceeds the speed of light.

Non-thermal filaments and AGN recurrent activity in the galaxy group Nest200047: a LOFAR, uGMRT, MeerKAT, VLA radio spectral analysis

Time: 2025-02-25

Authors: M. Brienza, K. Rajpurohit, E. Churazov, I. Heywood, M. Brüggen, M. Hoeft, F. Vazza, A. Bonafede, A. Botteon, G. Brunetti, F. Gastaldello, I. Khabibullin, N. Lyskova, A. Majumder, H. J. A. Röttgering, T. W. Shimwell, A. Simionescu, R. J. van Weeren

Abstract: This study combines high-resolution radio data from uGMRT, MeerKAT, VLA, and LOFAR to analyze Nest200047, a galaxy group hosting AGN-driven radio bubbles and non-thermal filaments, revealing new filamentary structures with steep spectra, continuous feedback via recurrent jet activity over 50–100 Myr, and particle acceleration mechanisms, highlighting implications for understanding AGN dynamics and future SKA research.

Towards robust gravitational wave detections from individual supermassive black hole binaries

Time: 2025-02-25

Authors: Bence Bécsy, Neil J. Cornish, Polina Petrov, Xavier Siemens, Stephen R. Taylor, Sarah J. Vigeland, Caitlin A. Witt

Abstract: This study presents two coherence-based methods—direct signal coherence testing and model scrambling—to validate individual supermassive black hole binary detections in pulsar timing array data, demonstrating their effectiveness in simulations but highlighting that confirming signal coherence is more challenging than initial detection and depends on signal-to-noise ratio, pulsar count, and frequency evolution.

Identification and characterisation of the gamma-ray counterpart of the transitional pulsar candidate CXOU J110926.4-650224

Time: 2025-02-25

Authors: A. Manca, F. Coti Zelati, J. Li, D. F. Torres, J. Ballet, A. Marino, A. Sanna, N. Rea, T. Di Salvo, A. Riggio, L. Burderi, R. Iaria

Abstract: The study confirms the spatial association between the transitional millisecond pulsar candidate CXOU J110926.4-650224 and the gamma-ray source 4FGL J1110.3-6501 using Fermi-LAT data (2008–2023), revealing a power-law spectrum (photon index 2.5 ± 0.1, flux 3.7±0.9 × 10⁻¹² erg cm⁻² s⁻¹) and supporting its classification as a tMSP.

Fastly spinning dark matter-admixed neutron stars

Time: 2025-02-25

Authors: Lorenzo Cipriani, Edoardo Giangrandi, Violetta Sagun, Daniela D. Doneva, Stoytcho S. Yazadjiev

Abstract: This study models uniformly rotating neutron stars with independently rotating dark matter to analyze co- and counter-rotating scenarios, revealing how dark matter's particle mass, fraction, and distribution (core/halo) impact mass-radius relations, Keplerian limits, and moments of inertia, advancing understanding of dark matter's astrophysical effects in neutron stars and self-interacting dark matter implications.

Hybrid stars with large quark cores within the parity doublet model and modified NJL model

Time: 2025-02-25

Authors: Wen-Li Yuan, Bikai Gao, Yan Yan, Renxin Xu

Abstract: This study employs the parity doublet model for hadronic matter and a modified NJL model for quark matter with Maxwell construction, finding stable neutron stars (up to ~2.2 M☉) with two- or three-flavor quark cores, where typical 1.4 M☉ stars may contain quark matter, supported by GW170817 observations indicating a 600 MeV chiral invariant mass for three-flavor cases and two-flavor scenarios allowing large cores (>1 M☉) and early deconfinement before 2ρ₀.

Relationship between the $γ-$ray variability and the pc-scale jet in the blazar 3C 454.3

Time: 2025-02-24

Authors: Eva Palafox, Víctor Manuel Patiño-Álvarez, Vahram Chavushyan, Sergio A. Dzib, Andrei Lobanov, J. Anton Zensus

Abstract: This study analyzes twelve years of γ-ray data and multi-frequency VLBA observations of the blazar 3C 454.3, revealing that its γ-ray variability arises from multiple jet regions (stationary and moving components at 15/43 GHz) through synchrotron self-Compton processes, with significant core correlations and evidence of non-stationary emission zones over time.
