The middle-aged pulsar PSR J1741-2054 and its bow-shock nebula in far ultraviolet

Time: 2025-03-13

Authors: Vadim Abramkin, George G. Pavlov, Yuriy Shibanov, B. Posselt, Oleg Kargaltsev

Abstract: The Hubble Space Telescope's far-ultraviolet (FUV) observations of middle-aged pulsar J1741-2054 and its bow-shock nebula revealed a thermal spectrum with cold (40–50 eV) and hot (~80 eV) components, a nonthermal spectrum showing a hard optical (Γ ≈ 1.0–1.2) and soft X-ray (Γ ≈ 2.6–2.7) emission, and the first FUV-detected bow-shock nebula in such pulsars, exhibiting a flux four times higher than Hα and greater surface brightness than previously observed counterparts.

Energy Reconstruction of Non-fiducial Electron-Positron Events in the DAMPE Experiment Using Convolutional Neural Networks

Time: 2025-03-13

Authors: Enzo Putti-Garcia, Andrii Tykhonov, Andrii Kotenko, Hugo Boutin, Manbing Li, Paul Coppin, Andrea Serpolla, Jennifer Maria Frieden, Chiara Perrina, Xin Wu

Abstract: The Dark Matter Particle Explorer (DAMPE) uses Convolutional Neural Networks to accurately reconstruct the energy of non-fiducial cosmic-ray electron events, enhancing detection capabilities up to 10 TeV despite their low rates and complex topologies.

Irradiated Pulsar Planets and Companions as 511 keV Positron Annihilation Line Sources

Time: 2025-03-13

Authors: Zachary Metzler, Zorawar Wadiasingh

Abstract: Millisecond pulsars in compact binaries emit 511 keV annihilation photons via pulsar-induced particle showers, with orbital modulation enabling detection, contributing to the galactic bulge's 511 keV excess, and necessitating future joint LISA and MeV gamma-ray studies.

The stochastic gravitational wave background from cosmic superstrings

Time: 2025-03-13

Authors: Anastasios Avgoustidis, Edmund J. Copeland, Adam Moss, Juhan Raidal

Abstract: This study demonstrates that including higher-mass cosmic superstring species is essential for accurately modeling the stochastic gravitational wave spectrum, with NANOGrav 15-year data fits yielding likelihoods comparable to supermassive black hole models and providing constraints on fundamental string tension, string coupling, and compact extra dimensions, relevant for future gravitational wave experiments.

Simulating the shaping of point-symmetric structures in the jittering jets explosion mechanism

Time: 2025-03-13

Authors: Jessica Braudo, Amir Michaelis, Muhammad Akashi, Noam Soker

Abstract: Three-dimensional hydrodynamical simulations reveal that the jittering jets explosion mechanism (JJEM), driven by stochastically varying pairs of jets interacting with a collapsing stellar core, produces point-symmetric morphologies through Rayleigh-Taylor instabilities and vortex formation, strongly supporting JJEM as the primary mechanism behind core-collapse supernova explosions.

Distinguishing Tidal Disruption Events and Changing-look Active Galactic Nuclei via Variation of Mid-infrared Color

Time: 2025-03-13

Authors: Yujun Yao, Jingjing Ye, Luming Sun, Ning Jiang, Megan Masterson, Xinwen Shu

Abstract: The study proposes that analyzing the rate of mid-infrared (MIR) color variation (K-corrected W1-W2) can effectively distinguish tidal disruption events (TDEs) from changing-look active galactic nuclei (CLAGNs), revealing that TDEs redden faster during outbursts, estimating comparable infrared TDE rates to optical ones (~1.5–2.8×10⁻⁵ galaxy⁻¹ yr⁻¹), and predicting future surveys will uncover more dust-obscured TDEs using this method.

Temporal variability and obscuration effects in the X-ray emission of classical nova V339 Delphini (Nova Delphini 2013)

Time: 2025-03-13

Authors: Songpeng Pei, Nataly Ospina, Xiaowan Zhang, Qiang Li, Ziwei Ou, Taozhi Yang, Yongzhi Cai

Abstract: This study analyzes archival Chandra and XMM-Newton X-ray data of classical nova V339 Delphini's 2013 outburst, revealing blue-shifted absorption features (~1200 km/s), a transient ~54-second flux modulation with period drift, and temporary obscuration events causing amplitude variations and non-sinusoidal pulse profiles.

A demarcation criterion for hydrogen burning of millinovae

Time: 2025-03-13

Authors: Izumi Hachisu, Mariko Kato

Abstract: Millinovae, a new class of transient supersoft X-ray sources without mass ejection, are proposed to require the criterion \( I_{\rm q} - I_{\rm max} > 2.2 \) to confirm hydrogen-burning white dwarfs, with only two detected soft X-ray candidates meeting this threshold, supporting models where X-rays originate from the WD photosphere and optical light from an irradiated disk.

R-JET: A postprocessing code for radiative transport in relativistic jets

Time: 2025-03-13

Authors: Kouichi Hirotani, Hsien Shang, Ruben Krasnopolsky, Kenichi Nishikawa

Abstract: This paper presents a radiative transport code for analyzing spectra, coreshift, and surface-brightness distributions of special relativistic jets with varying optical thickness, incorporating mixed electron-positron/proton plasmas and synchrotron processes, confirming Konigl model coreshift behavior, and demonstrating limb-brightened and ring-like jet structures when energized by black hole rotation and observed face-on.

Korteweg-de Vries Integrals for Modified Black Hole Potentials: Instabilities and other Questions

Time: 2025-03-13

Authors: Michele Lenzi, Arnau Montava Agudo, Carlos F. Sopuerta

Abstract: This study demonstrates that Korteweg-de Vries (KdV) integrals, derived from hidden symmetries in black hole dynamics, effectively detect instabilities in quasi-normal modes and greybody factors when the black hole potential is modified.
