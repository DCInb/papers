NVSS J151002+570243: accretion and spin of a z > 4 Fermi detected blazar

Time: 2025-05-16

Authors: Gabriele Alzati, Tullia Sbarrato, Gabriele Ghisellini

Abstract: This study models the distant blazar NVSS J151002+570243's accretion disk, finding a low Eddington ratio despite its high mass, and proposes continuous super-critical accretion from a small seed black hole to explain rapid growth and jet formation, reconciling observations of massive high-redshift black holes with formation theories.

Polarization Signatures of Quasi-Periodic Oscillations in Simulated Tilted, Truncated Disks

Time: 2025-05-16

Authors: P. Chris Fragile, Deepika A. Bollimpalli, Jeremy D. Schnittman, Cesare Harvey

Abstract: Using the \pan Monte-Carlo radiation transport code on general relativistic magnetohydrodynamic simulations of tilted, truncated black hole accretion disks, this study investigates how truncation, tilt-induced inclination/azimuth dependencies, and corona precession/oscillations shape spectral, polarization, and timing signatures, evaluating their detectability with current and future X-ray polarimeters to advance insights into accretion disk geometry, quasi-periodic oscillations, and state transitions.

Acceleration Potential and Density Profile of Secondary Plasma in the Magnetosphere of Orthogonal Pulsars

Time: 2025-05-16

Authors: A. Yu. Istomin, F. A. Kniazev, V. S. Beskin

Abstract: This study proposes a self-consistent vacuum gap model to determine the accelerating potential above radio pulsars' polar caps for arbitrary magnetic axis inclinations, enabling calculation of secondary plasma density profiles and demonstrating negligible inverse Compton scattering effects.

The systemic recoil velocity distribution and the scale height of isolated millisecond pulsar systems: Implications on neutron star retention fractions in star clusters

Time: 2025-05-16

Authors: Hao Ding

Abstract: The study of 65 isolated millisecond pulsars (MSPs) reveals their systemic recoil velocity distribution as a combination of three Maxwellian components, estimating ~13% retention in globular clusters and suggesting shared formation channels between isolated and cluster MSPs.

Intriguing nature of AM Her type candidate CXOU J204734.8+300105

Time: 2025-05-16

Authors: Rahul Sharma, Chetana Jain, Biswajit Paul, Anjan Dutta, Vikram Rana

Abstract: The study of CXOU J204734.8+300105, a proposed eclipsing polar-type cataclysmic variable, reveals conflicting X-ray periodicities (∼6000s with an eclipse in Chandra and ∼2000s without in XMM-Newton), a ∼6000s optical period, multi-component X-ray spectra with Fe emission lines, and counterparts in other wavelengths, suggesting a dynamic and evolving accretion environment.

Unveiling the origin of the optical and UV emission during the 2017 giant outburst of the Galactic ULX pulsar Swift J0243.6+6124 (Corrigendum)

Time: 2025-05-16

Authors: J. Alfonso-Garzón, J. van den Eijnden, N. P. M. Kuin, F. Fürst, A. Rouco-Escorial, J. Fabregat, P. Reig, J. M. Mas-Hesse, P. A. Jenke, C. Malacaria, C. Wilson-Hodge

Abstract: A coding error in modeling the Galactic ULX pulsar Swift J0243.6+6124's 2017 outburst led to overestimating the X-ray-heated Be star contribution, but incorporating X-ray irradiation of a misaligned Be disk resolved the discrepancy, yielding a well-fitting revised model.

Relativistic regularized kappa distributions

Time: 2025-05-16

Authors: Linh Han Than, Klaus Scherer, Horst Fichtner

Abstract: The study compares special relativistic isotropic regularized and standard kappa distributions, showing stricter kappa limitations for the standard in relativity (unlike the regularized, which allows all positive kappa values) and examines their non-relativistic limits and pressure moments.

Astrophysical Gamma-ray and Neutrino Production Following from the Physics of Photon-Nucleon Interactions

Time: 2025-05-15

Authors: Floyd W. Stecker

Abstract: This study finds that gamma-He interactions produce about 10% as many pions as gamma-p interactions under cosmological helium abundance, with non-pion mesons contributing negligibly to astrophysical gamma-rays and neutrinos, dominated primarily by Delta(1232) resonance decay.

X-ray Study on Propagation of Non-thermal Particles in Microquasar SS 433/W 50 Extended Jets

Time: 2025-05-15

Authors: Kazuho Kayama, Takaaki Tanaka, Hiroyuki Uchida, Takeshi Go Tsuru, Yoshiyuki Inoue, Dmitry Khangulyan, Naomi Tsuji, Hiroaki Yamamoto

Abstract: This study analyzes XMM-Newton observations of SS 433's eastern jet lobe, revealing gradual spectral steepening away from the binary system without the abrupt softening seen in the western lobe, and highlights differences in brightening and spectral variations that suggest additional physical processes like particle re-acceleration are at play in the eastern jet.

On the effects of radiation on mass transfer in binary stars

Time: 2025-05-15

Authors: Jakub Cehula, Ondřej Pejcha

Abstract: This study reveals that radiative pressure in binary systems shifts the critical mass transfer point from the L1 Lagrange point, exponentially increasing transfer rates near the Eddington limit and enabling super-Eddington flows even pre-Roche-lobe overflow, with implications for binary evolution and Luminous Blue Variable outbursts.
