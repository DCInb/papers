The Trinity of black hole correspondences: Shadows-quasinormal modes-graybody factors and cautionary remarks

Time: 2025-04-02

Authors: Davide Pedrotti, Marco Calzà

Abstract: This study investigates the quasi-normal modes-graybody factors correspondence in Hawking spectra for static and rotating black holes (notably in the superradiant regime), revealing its failure due to divergences, establishes a new shadow-graybody factors link for static, spherically symmetric spacetimes, and discusses limitations to emphasize caution in applying these correspondences.

Synchro-curvature description of γ-ray light curves and spectra of pulsars: concurrent fitting

Time: 2025-04-02

Authors: Daniel Íñiguez-Pascual, Diego F. Torres, Daniele Viganò

Abstract: This study concurrently fits spectra and light curves of gamma-ray pulsars using a synchro-curvature model, successfully matching observational data from the Third Fermi Pulsar Catalog with spectra and many light curves through time (chi-squared) and frequency (Euclidean distance) domain methods, finding similar performance but varied individual fits, insensitivity to timing/spectral parameters, and no strong geometrical correlations in parameter trends.

Investigating the Variable Continuum Lags in PG 2130+099

Time: 2025-04-02

Authors: Jake A. Miller, Edward M. Cackett, Mike Goad, Kirk T. Korista

Abstract: High-cadence, multi-wavelength photometric reverberation mapping of PG 2130+099 reveals its continuum-emitting region is larger than predicted by standard accretion disk models, with flux-flux analysis suggesting a variable disk spectrum and a constant Hα-emitting component, alongside tentative evidence of luminosity-dependent lags.

Is Lorentz invariance violation found?

Time: 2025-04-02

Authors: Giorgio Galanti, Marco Roncadelli, Fabrizio Tavecchio

Abstract: The detection of a 300 TeV photon from gamma-ray burst GRB 221009A by the LHAASO and Carpet collaborations provides the first evidence for Lorentz invariance violation (LIV), challenging conventional physics and suggesting a scenario where LIV coexists with axion-like particles (ALPs), potentially marking a breakthrough in quantum gravity phenomenology.

Energy Density Functional of Confined Quarks: an Improved Ansatz

Time: 2025-04-02

Authors: Udita Shukla, Pok Man Lo

Abstract: This study challenges the common Density Functional Theory (DFT) ansatz that energy functionals scale as \( U \propto n^{\frac{2}{3}} \) for confinement, demonstrating through a non-local approach that the true scaling transitions from \( n^2 \) at low densities to \( n \) at high densities due to an infrared cutoff, necessitating revised models for reliable equation of state construction.

Detector Response to Gravitational Wave Polarizations in Gravitational Quantum Field Theory

Time: 2025-04-02

Authors: Cong Xu, Hong-Bo Jin, Yue-Liang Wu

Abstract: This study analyzes gravitational wave polarization modes (two tensor, two vector, one scalar) within Gravitational Quantum Field Theory (GQFT), identifying three novel observational signatures—interference patterns, null-point discrimination, and sky-dependent detection windows—in space-based interferometers (e.g., LISA, Taiji), offering sky-mapped insights and tools to test gravity theories through polarization fingerprints for future multi-messenger astronomy.

A discussion on the origin of the sub-PeV Galactic gamma-ray emission

Time: 2025-04-02

Authors: S. Kato, M. Anzorena, R. Alves Batista, D. Chen, K. Fujita, R. Garcia, J. Huang, G. Imaizumi, T. Kawashima, K. Kawata, A. Mizuno, M. Ohnishi, C. Prévotat, T. Sako, T. K. Sako, F. Sugimoto, M. Takita, Y. Yokoe

Abstract: The Tibet ASγ and LHAASO experiments found consistent sub-PeV Galactic gamma-ray fluxes in the inner Galactic Plane, indicating diffuse emission dominance, though LHAASO sources may contribute up to ≈60% (a conservative upper limit due to spectral cutoffs), necessitating further spectral studies and diffuse emission contamination assessments for precise origin determination.

Quasinormal Modes and Stability Analysis of the JMN-1 Naked Singularity

Time: 2025-04-02

Authors: Akshat Pathrikar, Parth Bambhaniya, Pankaj S. Joshi, Elisabete M. de Gouveia Dal Pino

Abstract: This study analyzes quasinormal modes in the JMN-1 naked singularity through scalar, electromagnetic, and gravitational perturbations, employing the WKB method to determine stability via frequency calculations, concluding that the spacetime remains dynamically stable under axial perturbations with appropriate boundary conditions.

Searching for kilonova with the SiTian prototype telescope

Time: 2025-04-02

Authors: Zhirui Li, Hongrui Gu, Yongkang Sun, Yang Huang, Youjun Lu, Jifeng Liu

Abstract: The study simulates using the SiTian prototype telescope (limiting magnitude 22.0) triggered by LIGO/Virgo during the O4 run, finding optimistically 0.17–0.25 detectable kilonovae over two years but none otherwise, and suggests extending exposure time to improve detection despite rapid kilonova fading.

An advanced pulse-avalanche stochastic model of long gamma-ray burst light curves

Time: 2025-04-02

Authors: Manuele Maistrello, Lisa Ferro, Lorenzo Bazzanini, Romain Maccary, Cristiano Guidorzi

Abstract: An advanced stochastic avalanche model optimized with genetic algorithms across Fermi/GBM, Swift/BAT, and CGRO/BATSE datasets demonstrates that long GRB prompt emission originates from near-critical dissipation processes, with parameter variations explained by instrument differences, establishing the model as a reliable tool for future GRB light curve simulations.
