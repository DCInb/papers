Expectation for the MeV Gamma-Ray Emission from Pair-Instability Supernovae

Time: 2025-03-27

Authors: Ryo Sawada

Abstract: This study demonstrates that next-generation MeV gamma-ray telescopes could detect key gamma-ray lines (847 and 1238 keV) from the decay of $\mathrm{^{56}Co}$ in 130$M_\odot$ pair-instability supernovae (PISNe) up to 300-400 Mpc, with an estimated event rate of ~0.1 per year within 300 Mpc, underscoring their rarity but feasibility as targets for future observations over the next decade.

Transitioning to Memory Burden: Detectable Small Primordial Black Holes as Dark Matter

Time: 2025-03-27

Authors: Gia Dvali, Michael Zantedeschi, Sebastian Zell

Abstract: The memory burden effect, which suppresses black hole decay over time, allows small primordial black holes (PBHs <10¹⁵g) to serve as dark matter candidates, with observational constraints revised by their transition from semi-classical evaporation and detectable via high-energy neutrinos and current astrophysical particle fluxes.

A possible GeV-TeV connection in the direction of the Globular Cluster UKS 1

Time: 2025-03-27

Authors: Jiwon Shin, C. Y. Hui, Sangin Kim, Kwangmin Oh, Ellis R. Owen

Abstract: This study reports significant GeV emission and a potential second TeV detection near globular cluster UKS 1 using Fermi LAT and HAWC data, with the TeV feature displaced in the direction of the cluster's motion, supporting an inverse Compton scattering origin driven by its supersonic speed.

3D MHD simulations of runaway pulsars in core collapse supernova remnants

Time: 2025-03-27

Authors: D. M. A. Meyer, D. F. Torres, Z. Meliani

Abstract: The study uses 3D magnetohydrodynamic simulations to demonstrate that the circumstellar medium and supernova remnants from a progenitor star's evolution critically shape pulsar wind nebulae by bending jets and altering morphology and radio emission, as observed in PSR J1509−5850 and Geminga, underscoring the importance of the progenitor's history.

Polarized equatorial emission and hot spots around black holes with a dark matter halo

Time: 2025-03-27

Authors: Tsanimir Angelov, Rasim Bekir, Galin Gyulchev, Petya Nedkova, Stoytcho Yazadjiev

Abstract: This study explores the effect of a dark matter halo (modeled by combining a Schwarzschild black hole with a Hernquist-type distribution) on the linear polarization of accretion disks, finding deviations under 1% for direct images and up to 10% for indirect images in polarization properties, indicating that detecting dark matter's impact via polarized emissions in targets like M87* and Sgr A* remains highly challenging in the near future.

Study of the origin of the azimuthal variation of synchrotron X-ray spectrum from SNR RX J0852.0-4622

Time: 2025-03-27

Authors: Dai Tateishi, Nobuaki Sasaki, Yukikatsu Terada, Satoru Katsuda, Shiu-Hang Lee, Hidetoshi Sano, Yasuo Fukui, Aya Bamba

Abstract: The azimuthal variations in X-ray flux and photon index of supernova remnant RX J0852.0−4622, observed via Suzaku/XIS, are primarily caused by cloud density differences in the northern region and shock velocity in the south, with galactic magnetic field influence ruled out through polarization analysis.

Gravitational-wave kicks impact spins of black holes from hierarchical mergers

Time: 2025-03-27

Authors: Angela Borchers, Claire S. Ye, Maya Fishbach

Abstract: The spin distribution of hierarchically merging black holes deviates from the near-universal 0.7 value due to gravitational-wave kick-induced retention biases, producing bimodal distributions in low-escape-velocity environments, variable higher-generation spins (0.4–1), and observable features linked to formation environments, with aligned-spin binaries creating additional peaks.

Are Neutron Stars Rich in H-dibaryons?

Time: 2025-03-27

Authors: Jesper Leong, Anthony W. Thomas, Pierre A. M. Guichon

Abstract: The quark-meson coupling model and excluded volume formalism suggest that while H-dibaryons near the Λ-Λ threshold are abundant in heavy neutron stars, their presence only marginally reduces the maximum stellar mass.

Heavy Neutron Star Phenomenology with an H-dibaryon

Time: 2025-03-27

Authors: Jesper Leong, Anthony W. Thomas, Pierre A. M. Guichon

Abstract: Incorporating a near-ΛΛ threshold mass H-dibaryon in β-equilibrium dense nuclear matter using the quark-meson coupling model (without new parameters) slightly reduces neutron star maximum mass, leaves tidal deformability unchanged, and results in abundant H-particles extending up to 6 km from the core in heavy stars.
