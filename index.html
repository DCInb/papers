On the progenitor of the type Ia supernova remnant 0509-67.5

Time: 2025-03-06

Authors: Noam Soker

Abstract: The study concludes that SNR 0509-67.5's elliptical morphology and lack of a surviving star suggest it resulted from a spherical Type Ia supernova within an old planetary nebula, favoring the core-degenerate and double degenerate merger-delay scenarios, with Rayleigh-Taylor instabilities explaining observed calcium structures and potential fast-moving elements, implying a higher fraction of such supernovae in planetary nebulae than previously estimated.

Inferring kilonova ejecta photospheric properties from early blackbody spectra

Time: 2025-03-06

Authors: Gilad Sadeh

Abstract: This study introduces analytic corrections to account for relativistic effects in kilonova blackbody fitting, showing that standard methods overestimate photospheric velocities and temperatures by 10%–50%, while applying these corrections reduces deviations to within 10%, as demonstrated with observations of AT2017gfo and GRB211211A.

Impact of multi-messenger spectral modelling on blazar-neutrino associations

Time: 2025-03-06

Authors: Julian Kuhlmann, Francesca Capel

Abstract: The study demonstrates that incorporating theoretical p-gamma interaction models for blazar neutrino spectra—which peak at high energies—into Bayesian analyses of IceCube data results in fewer overall associated events but retains or strengthens high-energy neutrino associations, improving interpretability compared to standard power-law spectral assumptions.

Searching for Ultralight Dark Matter Solitons with Gravitational Waves

Time: 2025-03-06

Authors: Silvia Gasparotto

Abstract: The study demonstrates that next-generation gravitational wave observatories (LISA, Einstein Telescope, Cosmic Explorer) can detect solitonic cores induced by ultralight dark matter (ULDM) through continuous waves from Galactic Center neutron stars (probing masses \(m \lesssim 10^{-22}\, \mathrm{eV}\)) and modulation in extragalactic systems, surpassing current ULDM coupling constraints for \(m \lesssim 10^{-20}\, \mathrm{eV}\) and highlighting GW methods' potential to reveal ULDM properties.

Introducing APERTURE: A GPU-based General Relativistic Particle-in-Cell Simulation Framework

Time: 2025-03-06

Authors: Alexander Y. Chen, Martin Luepker, Yajie Yuan

Abstract: The researchers present Aperture, a GPU-accelerated, modular general relativistic particle-in-cell code framework designed to study collisionless magnetized accretion flows and black hole magnetospheres, demonstrating that observer-dependent spark gap dynamics reconcile conflicting results from prior 1D and 2D simulations.

Super heavy dark matter origin of the PeV neutrino event: KM3-230213A

Time: 2025-03-06

Authors: Kazunori Kohri, Partha Kumar Paul, Narendra Sahu

Abstract: The KM3NeT experiment's observation of the ultra-high-energy neutrino event KM3-230213A is explained as originating from super-heavy dark matter decay in a minimal Z2-symmetry extended type-I seesaw model, with derived dark matter mass constraints (1.7×10^8–5.5×10^9 GeV) and lifetime (6.3×10^29–3.6×10^29 s) aligning with IceCube and Auger data, while domain wall disappearance in this framework may produce stochastic gravitational waves.

Can we disentangle between the emission of an accretion disc around a single black hole and a circumbinary disc ?

Time: 2025-03-06

Authors: Peggy Varniere, Raphaël Mignon-Risse, Fabien Casse

Abstract: This study investigates whether circumbinary disc features, like periodic modulations from a "lump," can reliably distinguish pre-merger binary black holes (BBHs) from single black hole (SBH) systems, finding that while SBH models can mimic many features, the presence of dual variabilities (linked to the lump and binary orbital/beat periods) serves as a critical identifier for BBHs across a wide parameter range.

Time: 2025-03-06

Authors: Nanda Rea, Davide De Grandis

Abstract: Magnetars, highly magnetic neutron stars discovered via X-ray flares in the 1970s, exhibit multi-band emissions, serve as laboratories for extreme physics, and are linked to phenomena like supernovae, gamma-ray bursts, and fast radio bursts.

The Firmamento platform

Time: 2025-03-06

Authors: Paolo Giommi

Abstract: Firmamento is a new online astronomical research platform focused on studying blazars and multi-messenger emitters, providing access to imaging, spectral, and timing data, tools like the Error Region Counterpart Identifier (ERCI) for identifying blazar-like counterparts to high-energy sources, SED generation, VHE detectability estimation, and support for user-provided data integration.
