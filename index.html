Magnetar field dynamics shaped by chiral anomalies and helicity

Time: 2025-05-09

Authors: Clara Dehman, José A. Pons

Abstract: The chiral magnetic effect drives magnetar magnetic field evolution by converting magnetic helicity into chiral asymmetry, redistributing energy to amplify large-scale components to $10^{14}$ G within a century, providing a microphysical alternative to hydrodynamic dynamo models.

Constraints to Lorentz violation and ultrahigh-energy electrons in D-foamy space-times

Time: 2025-05-09

Authors: Chengyi Li, Bo-Qiang Ma

Abstract: This study demonstrates that while the absence of vacuum Cherenkov radiation from ultrahigh-energy electrons in LHAASO observations constrains Lorentz-violating models through threshold analysis, such constraints are naturally evaded in specific string theory-inspired space-time foam scenarios involving D-brane defects, where electrons do not radiate despite moving faster than photons due to subluminous refractive indices.

A Study of the Spectral Properties of Gamma-Ray Bursts with the Main and Second Bursts

Time: 2025-05-09

Authors: Ze-Lian Du, Zhao-Yang Peng, Jia-Ming Chen, Yue Yin, Ting Li

Abstract: A spectral analysis of 18 gamma-ray bursts (GRBs) with main and second bursts, using Bayesian time-resolved methods, reveals shared thermal components, similar evolutionary patterns in spectral indices and peak energies, consistent correlations, and adherence to the same Amati and Yonetoku relations, strongly indicating that the second burst is a seamless continuation of the main burst with a common physical origin.

Authors: Jiangwei Xu, Heng Xu, Yanjun Guo, Jinchen Jiang, Bojun Wang, Zihan Xue, Yunpeng Men, Kejia Lee, Bing Zhang, Weiwei Zhu, Jinlin Han

Abstract: The detection of a 26.24-day periodic variation in the Faraday rotation measure of repeating fast radio burst FRB 20201124A, observed via the FAST telescope over a year, provides strong evidence for its binary system origin and suggests that such binary environments may be common among actively repeating FRBs.

Galactic double neutron stars as dual-line gravitational-wave sources: Prospects with LISA and Cosmic Explorer

Time: 2025-05-09

Authors: Wen-Fan Feng, Lijing Shao

Abstract: The study investigates dual-line gravitational-wave emissions from double neutron star systems, utilizing enhanced waveform models and LISA-based population simulations to predict that Cosmic Explorer could detect 6-22 spinning neutron stars with a signal-to-noise ratio of 20-30, enabling ~8% precision in measuring neutron star moments of inertia and providing novel insights into binary orbital dynamics and internal structure.

Screening Mechanisms on White Dwarfs: Symmetron & Dilaton

Time: 2025-05-09

Authors: Joan Bachs-Esteban, Ilídio Lopes, Javier Rubio

Abstract: This study compares symmetron and dilaton fields in white dwarfs, revealing that both reduce mass, radius, and luminosity in low-density stars via enhanced pressure drops, exhibit suppressed effects in high-density regimes (symmetrons decouple, dilatons weaken), share similarities with chameleon models due to runaway potentials, and show density-dependent screening differences, motivating asteroseismology to constrain their parameters.
