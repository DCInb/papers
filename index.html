Peaky Finders: Characterizing Double-Peaked Type IIb Supernovae in Large-Scale Live-Stream Photometric Surveys

Time: 2025-03-05

Authors: Adrian Crawford, Tyler A. Pritchard, Maryam Modjaz, Craig Pellegrino, Sahana Kumar, Raphael Baer-Way

Abstract: This study presents the first photometric population analysis of 14 double-peaked Type IIb supernovae (SNe IIb), using a piecewise linear model to reveal rapid shock-cooling emission (SCE) rises (2.07±1.0 and 1.1±0.8 mags/day in g/r-bands) comparable to fast blue optical transients, and proposes an alert filter for detecting such events in future surveys like the Rubin LSST.

High-Energy Neutrinos by Hydrogen-rich Supernovae interacting with low-massive Circumstellar Medium: The Case of SN 2023ixf

Time: 2025-03-05

Authors: S. P. Cosentino, M. L. Pumo, S. Cherubini

Abstract: This study models high-energy neutrino production in hydrogen-rich supernovae interacting with low-mass circumstellar media (e.g., SN 2023ixf), showing that while current detectors like IceCube cannot detect the predicted flux (∼0.04 events in 50 days), future telescopes such as IceCube-Gen2 and KM3NeT could observe these neutrinos, with emission influenced by shock velocity, CSM mass, and spatial density profiles.

Revisiting the observation-theory confrontation in high-frequency QPOs:a new QPO in NGC 5506, intermediate mass black holes, and the crucial role of accretion state

Time: 2025-03-05

Authors: Haoyang Zhang, Lingwei Meng, Li Zhang, Benzhong Dai

Abstract: A study extends the scale invariance of accretion processes (SIAP) across black hole masses by identifying supermassive black holes (Sgr A*, NGC 1365, NGC 5506) with specific accretion states supporting existing high-frequency quasi-periodic oscillation (HFQPO) models, reconciling observation-theory gaps in active galactic nuclei and suggesting accretion-state differences between stellar and supermassive black holes, while proposing new models or distinct physical processes for unresolved cases.

Identifying multiplets of IceCube alert events

Time: 2025-03-05

Authors: Martina Karl, Paolo Padovani, Paolo Giommi

Abstract: The study analyzed 13 years of IceCube Neutrino Observatory alert events for spatial and temporal clusters to identify potential astrophysical sources but found no statistically significant clustering, likely due to atmospheric background dominance or insufficient data to detect source-related multiplets.

Evolution of human cognition required Einstein's gravitational waves

Time: 2025-03-05

Authors: Bernard F. Schutz, Tsvi Piran, Patrick J. Sutton

Abstract: The emergence of human cognition on Earth is tied to gravitational waves, which enabled neutron star mergers that produced radioactive isotopes whose decay heat maintained plate tectonics and a molten core (generating a protective magnetic field), while subsequent tectonic-driven climate changes spurred brain evolution, ultimately allowing humans to study gravity—a process contingent on gravity's own laws.

A Linear Decomposition Method to Analyze and Study Pulsar Mode Changes

Time: 2025-03-05

Authors: Longfei Hao, Zhixuan Li, Faxin Shen, Yonghua Xu, Yuxiang Huang, Kejia Lee, Qingzheng Yu, Hongguang Wang

Abstract: The paper introduces the Linear Decomposition Method (LDM), a PCA-like technique using Gaussian noise-based likelihood to detect pulsar profile variations and mode changes, validated through simulations and Kunming 40-m telescope data, successfully identifying mode changes in PSR B0329+54, continuous profile evolution in PSR B0355+54, and improving timing precision for the former.

A helical magnetic field in quasar NRAO150 revealed by Faraday rotation

Time: 2025-03-05

Authors: J. D. Livingston, A. S. Nikonov, S. A. Dzib, L. C. Debbrecht, Y. Y. Kovalev, M. M. Lisakov, N. R. MacDonald, G. F. Paraschos, J. Röder, M. Wielgus

Abstract: Multi-frequency polarimetric VLBI observations of quasar NRAO150 using GPCAL revealed Faraday rotation gradients and circular EVPA0 patterns aligned with the jet's curvature, indicating a helical/toroidal magnetic field in the innermost jet, with polarization peaking at 24GHz and increasing fractions at higher frequencies.

Constraints on asteroid-mass primordial black holes in dwarf galaxies using Hubble Space Telescope photometry

Time: 2025-03-05

Authors: Nicolas Esser, Carrie Filion, Sven De Rijcke, Nitya Kallivayalil, Hannah Richstein, Peter Tinyakov, Rosemary F. G. Wyse

Abstract: The study uses Hubble Space Telescope observations of stellar mass functions in ultra-faint dwarf galaxies, particularly Triangulum II, to constrain primordial black holes (PBHs) of ~10¹⁹g as dark matter, excluding them from constituting more than ~55% (78%) of dark matter at 2σ (3σ) and entirely as dark matter at 3.7σ.

Perturbation theory for post-Newtonian neutron stars

Time: 2025-03-05

Authors: Fabian Gittins, Nils Andersson, Shanshan Yin

Abstract: This study develops a post-Newtonian framework for modeling dynamical tides in non-rotating neutron-star binaries during late inspiral, establishing Hermitian perturbation equations derived from a Lagrangian, conserved symplectic product and canonical energy, orthogonality conditions for oscillation modes enabling a mode-sum representation of tides, and demonstrating the formulation's uniqueness.

The relation between black hole spin, star formation rate, and black hole mass for supermassive black holes

Time: 2025-03-05

Authors: Yongyun Chen, Qiusheng Gu, Junhui Fan, Xiaotong Guo, Xiaoling Yu, Nan Ding, Dingrong Xiong

Abstract: This study of 48 low-redshift supermassive black holes reveals that spin increases with black hole mass and star formation rate at lower values but decreases at higher values, suggesting a transition from gas-accretion-driven growth to merger-dominated growth, while also confirming the known stellar mass-black hole mass relationship.
