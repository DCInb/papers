The middle-aged pulsar PSR J1741-2054 and its bow-shock nebula in far ultraviolet

Time: 2025-03-13

Authors: Vadim Abramkin, George G. Pavlov, Yuriy Shibanov, B. Posselt, Oleg Kargaltsev

Abstract: The Hubble Space Telescope's far ultraviolet (FUV) observations of the middle-aged pulsar J1741-2054 and its bow-shock nebula (BSN) revealed a thermal (cold component: ~40-50 eV, 8-15 km radius; possible hot component: ~80 eV, 1 km radius) and nonthermal spectrum (hard optical, Γ≈1.0-1.2; softer X-ray, Γ≈2.6-2.7), along with the first FUV-detected BSN in a middle-aged pulsar, showing a closed-shell morphology and flux four times higher than Hα.

Energy Reconstruction of Non-fiducial Electron-Positron Events in the DAMPE Experiment Using Convolutional Neural Networks

Time: 2025-03-13

Authors: Enzo Putti-Garcia, Andrii Tykhonov, Andrii Kotenko, Hugo Boutin, Manbing Li, Paul Coppin, Andrea Serpolla, Jennifer Maria Frieden, Chiara Perrina, Xin Wu

Abstract: The Dark Matter Particle Explorer (DAMPE) utilizes Convolutional Neural Networks to improve energy reconstruction accuracy for non-fiducial cosmic-ray electrons beyond 10 TeV, addressing low detection rates and complex event topologies to enhance observational acceptance.

Irradiated Pulsar Planets and Companions as 511 keV Positron Annihilation Line Sources

Time: 2025-03-13

Authors: Zachary Metzler, Zorawar Wadiasingh

Abstract: The study demonstrates through MEGAlib simulations that companions in compact millisecond pulsar binaries emit 511 keV annihilation photons modulated by their orbital motion, enabling coherent searches to characterize these systems, detect Earth-unseen pulsars, potentially explain the galactic bulge 511 keV excess, and link to future joint LISA and MeV γ-ray observations.

The stochastic gravitational wave background from cosmic superstrings

Time: 2025-03-13

Authors: Anastasios Avgoustidis, Edmund J. Copeland, Adam Moss, Juhan Raidal

Abstract: This study demonstrates that incorporating higher-mass cosmic superstring species is essential for accurately modeling the stochastic gravitational wave background across nanohertz to kilohertz frequencies, with NANOGrav 15-year data analysis yielding parameter estimates (e.g., fundamental string tension $\log_{10}(G\mu_1)\approx-11.5$ to $-9.7$, string coupling $g_s<0.65$) and showing comparable likelihoods to supermassive black hole models as explanations for the observed signal.

Simulating the shaping of point-symmetric structures in the jittering jets explosion mechanism

Time: 2025-03-13

Authors: Jessica Braudo, Amir Michaelis, Muhammad Akashi, Noam Soker

Abstract: Three-dimensional hydrodynamical simulations reveal that the jittering jets explosion mechanism (JJEM), where a neutron star launches multiple stochastically directed jet pairs, forms point-symmetric morphologies matching observed core-collapse supernova remnants, reinforcing JJEM as the primary CCSN explosion mechanism.

Distinguishing Tidal Disruption Events and Changing-look Active Galactic Nuclei via Variation of Mid-infrared Color

Time: 2025-03-13

Authors: Yujun Yao, Jingjing Ye, Luming Sun, Ning Jiang, Megan Masterson, Xinwen Shu

Abstract: The study proposes using the time variation of mid-infrared (MIR) color to distinguish tidal disruption events (TDEs) and ambiguous nuclear transients (ANTs) from changing-look active galactic nuclei (CLAGNs), revealing that ∼50%–80% of MIR outbursts in non-Seyfert galaxies are likely TDEs with rates comparable to optical TDEs, while the rest are CLAGNs indicating weak undetected AGNs, and predicts future high-quality infrared surveys will uncover more dust-obscured TDEs using this method.

Temporal variability and obscuration effects in the X-ray emission of classical nova V339 Delphini (Nova Delphini 2013)

Time: 2025-03-13

Authors: Songpeng Pei, Nataly Ospina, Xiaowan Zhang, Qiang Li, Ziwei Ou, Taozhi Yang, Yongzhi Cai

Abstract: This study of Nova Del 2013's supersoft X-ray phase using *Chandra* and *XMM-Newton* data revealed blue-shifted absorption features (∼724–1474 km/s) and a transient ∼54-second X-ray flux modulation with period drift, attributed to temporary obscuration events causing amplitude/profile variations and an anti-correlation with count rates.

A demarcation criterion for hydrogen burning of millinovae

Time: 2025-03-13

Authors: Izumi Hachisu, Mariko Kato

Abstract: Millinovae, a new class of transient supersoft X-ray sources with faint, triangular light curves, are proposed to involve hydrogen-burning white dwarfs when the quiescent-to-maximum I-band magnitude difference exceeds 2.2, a criterion met by two X-ray-detected candidates, supporting the nova model over dwarf nova interpretations for these cases.

R-JET: A postprocessing code for radiative transport in relativistic jets

Time: 2025-03-13

Authors: Kouichi Hirotani, Hsien Shang, Ruben Krasnopolsky, Kenichi Nishikawa

Abstract: This study introduces a radiative transport code for modeling spectra, coreshift, and surface-brightness distributions in special relativistic jets with arbitrary optical thickness, incorporating electron-positron and electron-proton plasmas with thermal/nonthermal components, validated by confirming the Konigl model's frequency-dependent coreshift for nonthermal plasmas and demonstrating limb-brightened jets energized by black hole rotation, which exhibit ring-like brightness maps when viewed face-on.

Korteweg-de Vries Integrals for Modified Black Hole Potentials: Instabilities and other Questions

Time: 2025-03-13

Authors: Michele Lenzi, Arnau Montava Agudo, Carlos F. Sopuerta

Abstract: This study proposes that the Korteweg-de Vries (KdV) integrals, associated with hidden symmetries in black hole dynamics, serve as effective tools for detecting instabilities in quasi-normal modes and greybody factors when modifications to the black hole potential are introduced.
