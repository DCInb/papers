Neutrinophilic Super-Resonant Dark Matter

Time: 2025-05-20

Authors: Murat Abdughani, Shao-Song Tang, Kadirya Tursun, Bin Zhu

Abstract: This study investigates how enhanced dark matter annihilation into neutrinos—considering kinetic decoupling effects from resonance and Sommerfeld mechanisms—impacts relic density, using coupled Boltzmann equations to constrain parameters with current data and forecast future experimental sensitivities while evading cosmic microwave background constraints on Standard Model particle channels.

Counterpart-Induced Millisecond-Scale Truncation Mechanism of Fast Radio Bursts

Time: 2025-05-20

Authors: Yutong He, Christoph H. Keitel, Matteo Tamburini

Abstract: This study demonstrates that electron-positron pair cascades driven by Compton scattering and the Breit-Wheeler process truncate fast radio burst (FRB) durations to milliseconds during their propagation, establishing a universal millisecond upper limit independent of FRB origins and relaxing the constraint that their production mechanisms must inherently generate such brief bursts.

Cen A hotspot in Ultra-High-Energy Cosmic Rays illuminated by M83 or Cen A?

Time: 2025-05-20

Authors: K. Dolgikh, A. Korochkin, G. Rubtsov, D. Semikoz, I. Tkachev

Abstract: The study demonstrates that when ultra-high-energy cosmic ray (UHECR) emitting regions are smaller than the intergalactic magnetic field (IGMF) correlation length, a focusing regime emerges, producing smaller, systematically shifted images that deviate from the standard random walk model, complicating source identification and suggesting the observed Cen A excess could arise from M83 due to Galactic magnetic field (GMF) deflection with minor IGMF effects or from Cen A itself if specific IGMF configurations counteract GMF deflections.

No Evidence for Second-Scale Periodicity in FRB 20201124A from FAST Observations

Time: 2025-05-20

Authors: Dotan Gazith, Barak Zackay

Abstract: This study develops a robust method to search for second-scale periodicity in Fast Radio Bursts (FRBs) as potential evidence of magnetar origins, finding no significant periodic signals in FRB 20201124A observed by FAST, contrasting with a prior claim of a ~1.7s periodicity by C. Du et al. (2025).

Accretion of AGN Stars under Influence of Disk Geometry

Time: 2025-05-20

Authors: Yi-Xian Chen, Yan-Fei Jiang, Jeremy Goodman

Abstract: 3D radiation hydrodynamic simulations reveal that massive stars in AGN disks exhibit isotropic accretion in hot, thick disks (sub-thermal), transitioning to anisotropic (super-thermal) accretion in colder disks, driving polar outflows and midplane accretion capped at ~0.02M⊙/year, influenced by spiral arms and gap-opening effects requiring further study.

Multi-dimensional Models of Mixed H/He Flames in X-ray Bursts

Time: 2025-05-20

Authors: Eric T. Johnson, Michael Zingale

Abstract: "In 2D hydrodynamic simulations of mixed hydrogen/helium flames in X-ray bursts, higher initial hydrogen abundance yields slower, less energetic flames, with outcomes sensitive to initial conditions and nuclear networks, and a composition-dependent secondary flame emerging later."

The dynamics of the parsec-scale jet in the neutrino blazar PKS 0735+178

Time: 2025-05-20

Authors: Yu-Sik Kim, Jae-Young Kim

Abstract: A VLBI study of blazar PKS 0735+178, linked to the high-energy neutrino event IC211208A, reveals enhanced jet activity and a rapidly moving component whose interaction with a slower feature near the core coincided spatiotemporally with the neutrino detection, suggesting this collision site as the neutrino's origin.

Pulsar Cyclic Spectroscopy in the Partial-Deconvolution Regime: Benefits & Limitations

Time: 2025-05-20

Authors: Jacob E. Turner, Timothy Dolch, Paul B. Demorest, Ryan S. Lynch, Daniel R. Stinebring, Cody Jessup, Nathaniel Jones, Christopher Scheithauer

Abstract: This study investigates the advantages of cyclic spectroscopy for pulsar observations when full deconvolution is impractical, evaluating cyclic merits and deconvolution regimes for NANOGrav pulsars observed with the Green Bank Telescope and DSA-2000, demonstrating partial phase retrieval benefits in scintillation data, advocating for synergistic methods, and proposing an updated "cyclic merit 2.0" metric using scintillation bandwidth.

TransFit: An Efficient Framework for Transient Light-Curve Fitting with Time-Dependent Radiative Diffusion

Time: 2025-05-20

Authors: Liang-Duan Liu, Yu-Hao Zhang, Yun-Wei Yu, Ze-Xin Du, Jing-Yao Li, Guang-Lei Wu, Zi-Gao Dai

Abstract: This paper introduces TransFit, a novel computational framework that efficiently models transient light curves by solving a generalized energy conservation equation with time-dependent radiative diffusion, heating dynamics, and ejecta expansion, bridging the gap between oversimplified semi-analytical models and computationally intensive simulations to enable accurate, rapid analysis of large astronomical datasets.
