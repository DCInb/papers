Current constraints on the minimally extended varying speed of light model through the cosmic distance duality relation

Time: 2025-05-21

Authors: Jaiane Santos, Carlos Bengaly, Rodrigo S. Gonçalves

Abstract: This study tests the minimally extended varying speed of light (meVSL) model by analyzing the cosmic distance duality relation (CDDR) using Gaussian Process-reconstructed luminosity distances from Pantheon+ supernovae and angular diameter distances from 2D/3D baryon acoustic oscillations (BAO) data, finding no significant evidence for meVSL (within 1-4σ confidence levels depending on datasets) and attributing observed deviations to potential systematic tensions between SN and BAO datasets, particularly at low redshifts.

AstroSat/UVIT far and near UV deep field around IC 4329A

Time: 2025-05-21

Authors: Piyali Ganguly, Priyanka Rani, Gulab C. Dewangan

Abstract: The study presents deep near- and far-ultraviolet imaging of IC 4329A's field using AstroSat's UVIT, detecting 4437 NUV and 456 FUV sources (many previously unknown), identifying optical/X-ray counterparts, variable objects, correcting white dwarf classifications, and revealing galaxies with atypical morphologies, necessitating further multi-wavelength analysis.

Designing a Potential NASA Fermi Orbit Change

Time: 2025-05-21

Authors: Wayne Yu, Trevor Williams, Russell Carpenter

Abstract: The Fermi Gamma-ray Space Telescope, operational since 2008, faces collision risks from proliferating satellite constellations, prompting an analysis of orbit-raising maneuvers, fuel requirements, and Monte Carlo uncertainty modeling to ensure sufficient fuel for end-of-life operations while continuing its study of cosmic phenomena.

Implementation of CR Energy SPectrum (CRESP) algorithm in PIERNIK MHD code. II. Propagation of Primary and Secondary nuclei in a magneto-hydrodynamical environment

Time: 2025-05-21

Authors: Antoine Baldacchino-Jordan, Michal Hanasz, Mateusz Ogrodnik, Dominik Wóltański, Artur Gawryszczak, Andrew W. Strong, Philipp Girichidis

Abstract: The development of a cosmic ray nuclei model within the PIERNIK MHD framework, integrating primary (C, N, O) and secondary (Li, Be, B) species production via supernova remnants and spallation with momentum-dependent Fokker-Planck-MHD coupling, demonstrates that the Secondary-to-Primary flux ratio (B/C) increases with higher diffusion coefficients due to CR buoyancy weakening the magnetic field, while higher supernova rates reduce B/C through enhanced winds and shorter CR residence times in dense disk regions.

Evolution of broad emission lines from double-peaked to single-peaked to support a central tidal disruption event

Time: 2025-05-21

Authors: Zhang XueGuang

Abstract: The study reveals that in tidal disruption events (TDEs), the expansion of disk-like broad emission line regions (BLRs) from evolving fallback debris can transition emission line profiles from double-peaked to single-peaked—observed in candidate AT 2018hyz—with a 3.95% probability, aligning with theoretical models and serving as a diagnostic indicator for TDEs.

Numerical modeling the mass feeding rates onto accretion-modified stars embedded within AGN disks

Time: 2025-05-21

Authors: Yang Luo, Jian-Min Wang

Abstract: This study investigates through 3D simulations how disk shearing and angular momentum in active galactic nuclei (AGN) accretion disks reduce mass feeding rates onto stars formed within them, proposing a formula linking feeding rates to the stars' thermal mass (\(q_{\rm th}\)) and gas angular momentum, while also exploring feedback effects of rapid accretion on AGN disks and stellar evolution.

An Overview of the MUSES Calculation Engine and How It Can Be Used to Describe Neutron Stars

Time: 2025-05-20

Authors: Mateus Pelicer, Veronica Dexheimer, Joaquin Grefa

Abstract: The study investigates how different interpolation functions (hyperbolic tangent, Gaussian, bump, smoothstep) between the Crust Density Functional Theory and Chiral Mean Field models in the MUSES workflow affect neutron star structure calculations via the QLIMR module, highlighting uncertainties in dense matter equations of state.

The Long Road to Alignment: Measuring Black Hole Spin Orientation with Expanding Gravitational-Wave Datasets

Time: 2025-05-20

Authors: Salvatore Vitale, Matthew Mould

Abstract: Simulating up to 1500 binary black hole systems, this study reveals challenges in pinpointing their astrophysical origins via spin tilt distributions—due to spurious peaks and inconclusive mass ratio-tilt correlations—while advocating for model-independent metrics (e.g., tilt angle fractions) as more reliable than model parameters.

Comprehensive Laboratory Benchmark of K-shell Dielectronic Satellites of Fe XXV-XXI Ions

Time: 2025-05-20

Authors: Chintan Shah, Pedro Amaro, Filipe Grilo, Ming Feng Gu, Liyi Gu, José Paulo Santos, F. Scott Porter, Thomas Pfeifer, Maurice A. Leutenegger, José R. Crespo López-Urrutia

Abstract: High-resolution laboratory experiments using an electron beam ion trap resolved KLn satellites up to n=11 in Fe XXV-XXI ions' K-shell dielectronic recombination, crucial for astrophysical X-ray spectra, with cross-section uncertainties below 15%, confirming the accuracy of Flexible Atomic Code calculations for modeling astrophysical and fusion plasmas.

Model-Independent Determination of the Tidal Deformability of a 1.4 $M_{\odot}$ Neutron Star from Gravitational-Wave Measurements

Time: 2025-05-20

Authors: Chun Huang

Abstract: This study presents a novel, equation-of-state-independent framework combining gravitational-wave (GW170817) and X-ray data to constrain the tidal deformability of a 1.4 \(M_\odot\) neutron star (\( \Lambda_{1.4} = 265.18_{-104.38}^{+237.88} \)), offering a robust baseline for future multimessenger analyses of neutron-star interiors without relying on specific nuclear matter models.
