First Observations of Solar Halo Gamma Rays Over a Full Solar Cycle

Time: 2025-05-07

Authors: Tim Linden, Jung-Tsung Li, Bei Zhou, Isabelle John, Milena Crnogorčević, Annika H. G. Peter, John F. Beacom

Abstract: A 15-year analysis of Fermi-LAT data using a novel method for moving sources robustly detects the Sun's inverse-Compton emission (solar halo) between 31.6 MeV and 100 GeV, extending up to 45° from the Sun, revealing time-dependent solar modulation variations and azimuthal asymmetries while offering new insights into galactic cosmic-ray fluxes and enabling probes of astrophysical and beyond-standard-model processes near the solar surface.

Early and Extensive Ultraviolet Through Near Infrared Observations of the Intermediate-Luminosity Type Iax Supernovae 2024pxl

Time: 2025-05-07

Authors: W. B. Hoogendam, C. Ashall, D. O. Jones, B. J. Shappee, M. A. Tucker, M. E. Huber, K. Auchettl, D. D. Desai, J. T. Hinkle, M. Y. Kong, S. Romagnoli, J. Shi, A. Syncatto, C. D. Kilpatrick

Abstract: SN 2024pxl, the third Type Iax supernova in NGC 6384, exhibits intermediate luminosity, a featureless early NIR spectrum (~9,000 K blackbody), mixed ejecta velocities, homogeneous J-H colors distinct from normal SNe Ia, a tentative correlation between H-band Co II velocity and luminosity, and a light curve indicating no significant companion interaction, supporting a CO white dwarf deflagration origin.

I-Love-Q Relations of Fermion-Boson Stars

Time: 2025-05-07

Authors: Kelvin Ka-Ho Lam, Lap-Ming Lin

Abstract: The study investigates how dark matter parameters in fermion-boson stars—neutron stars with bosonic dark matter—affect deviations from I-Love-Q universal relations, revealing up to 5% deviations for boson masses near \(10^{-10}\) eV with moderate dark matter content and setting an upper mass limit of \(26.8 \times 10^{-10}\) eV beyond which deviations stay below 1%, making detection impractical.

Scintillometry of Fast Radio Bursts: Resolution effects in two-screen models

Time: 2025-05-07

Authors: Sachin Pradeep E. T, Tim Sprenger, Olaf Wucknitz, Robert A. Main, Laura G. Spitler

Abstract: This theoretical and simulation-based study on Fast Radio Bursts (FRBs) reveals that resolving plasma screens in two-screen systems gradually quenches broad-scale scintillation, introduces spectral autocorrelation product terms that elevate modulation indices to √3 in unresolved regimes, and provides a new host-screen distance formula applicable only to two-dimensional screens.

Magnetic precession as a model for QPOs in PULXs: flat-top noise ULXs are different

Time: 2025-05-07

Authors: Miljenko Čemeljić, Włodek Kluźniak, Sukalpa Kundu

Abstract: The study applies Dong Lai's magnetic precession model to pulsating ultraluminous X-ray sources (PULXs), finding that observed quasi-periodic oscillations (QPOs) inversely scale with neutron star periods, aligning with theoretical predictions, and suggests QPOs in ULXs and PULXs may originate from distinct mechanisms.

Hot Holographic 2-flavor Quark Star

Time: 2025-05-07

Authors: Le-Feng Chen, Jing-Yi Wu, Hao Feng, Tian-Shun Chen, Kilar Zhang

Abstract: Using a holographic 2-flavor Einstein-Maxwell-dilaton model calibrated with lattice QCD, this study analyzes the equation of state of quark-gluon plasma near the critical point (T=182 MeV), constructs quark-hadron hybrid stars (2–17 solar masses, compactness ∼0.15) as black hole mimickers, examines I-Love-Q-C relations akin to neutron stars under smooth quark-hadron transitions, and maps thermodynamic parameters and thermal conductivity dynamics.

Swift/XRT monitoring of the orbital and superorbital modulations in 4U 1909+07

Time: 2025-05-07

Authors: P. Romano, H. I. Cohen, E. Bozzo, N. Islam, A. Lange, R. H. D. Corbet, J. B. Coley, K. Pottschmidt

Abstract: The Swift/XRT observational campaign on 4U 1909+07 confirmed orbital modulation linked to wind accretion from its companion but found no significant superorbital variability, likely due to weakened modulation during observations, aligning with the corotating interaction region model for supergiant X-ray binaries.

Slow and steady: long-term evolution of the 76-second pulsar J0901$-$4046

Time: 2025-05-07

Authors: M. C. Bezuidenhout, N. D. R. Bhat, M. Caleb, L. N. Driessen, F. Jankowski, M. Kramer, V. Morello, I. Pastor-Marazuela, K. Rajwade, J. Roy, B. W. Stappers, M. Surnis, J. Tian

Abstract: PSR J0901-4046, a radio-loud neutron star with a 75.88-second period, challenges neutron star emission models due to its stable timing over three years, quasi-periodic oscillations (73 ms and 21 ms) in single pulses, evolving pulse morphologies suggesting magnetospheric changes, constant pulse width across frequencies (contradicting radius-to-frequency mapping), and absence of magnetar-like outbursts or glitches, complicating its classification as a magnetar.

Non-Stationary Discs and Instabilities

Time: 2025-05-07

Authors: Omer Blaes, Yan-Fei Jiang, Jean-Pierre Lasota, Galina Lipunova

Abstract: This paper reviews thermal and viscous instabilities in accretion discs around compact objects, examining classical viscosity-based models, ionization-driven instabilities in dwarf novae and X-ray binaries, insights from MHD simulations on outburst mechanisms, and discrepancies between predicted inner-disc instabilities and the observed stability of black hole X-ray binaries' high/soft states.

Relativistic structure of a supermassive black hole embedded in the dark matter halo of NGC 4649 (M60)

Time: 2025-05-07

Authors: Francisco S. N. Lobo, Jorde A. A. Ramos, Manuel E. Rodrigues

Abstract: This study constructs a static, spherically symmetric black hole solution within a dark matter halo modeled using observational data from galaxy NGC 4649, extending the Schwarzschild metric with parameters \(M\), \(V_c\), and \(a\), and demonstrates the halo's influence on spacetime geometry (event horizon, photon sphere, shadow size), curvature invariants (notably enhanced Kretschmann scalar sensitivity near the horizon), and thermodynamics (non-trivial extremal limit with finite tangential pressure), underscoring the importance of incorporating astrophysical environments into black hole models for probing strong-field gravity.
