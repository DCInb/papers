Cosmological Parameters Estimate from Persistent Radio Sources of Fast Radio Bursts

Time: 2025-04-17

Authors: Zi-Liang Zhang, Bing Zhang

Abstract: This study introduces a novel method using fast radio bursts (FRBs) and their persistent radio sources (PRSs) through the Yang relation to constrain the Hubble constant (H₀), addressing degeneracies in traditional approaches by combining dispersion and rotation measures, achieving a preliminary estimate of H₀ = 75 ± 30 km/s/Mpc and highlighting the method's potential as a new cosmological probe with future refinements.

Performance of the advanced gamma-ray trigger system for the High Energy Cosmic Radiation Detection (HERD) facility

Time: 2025-04-17

Authors: Keerthana Rajan Lathika

Abstract: The HERD facility, scheduled for installation on China's Space Station around 2027, is a multi-detector system designed to detect dark matter, study cosmic-ray spectra up to the knee, observe gamma rays above 100 MeV with an advanced trigger system, and validate performance through 2023 prototype beam tests.

A Novel View on the Inner Crusts of Neo-Neutron Stars: exotic light nuclei, diffusional and thermodynamical stability

Time: 2025-04-17

Authors: Mikhail V. Beznogov, Adriana R. Raduta

Abstract: This study investigates non-accreted crusts of young, warm neo-neutron stars using an extended nuclear statistical equilibrium model, revealing three key findings: a deep inner crust layer of nearly pure \(^{14}\)He challenging nuclear pasta phases, crust stability against ion diffusion contradicting other models, and subsaturated matter's thermodynamic stability ruling out a first-order phase transition between inhomogeneous and homogeneous phases.

Hunting for newborn magnetars: a multi-messenger approach

Time: 2025-04-17

Authors: Dafne Guetta, Simone Dall'Osso

Abstract: This study finds that while gravitational wave emission from magnetars minimally affects shock break-out light-curves, it leaves detectable signatures in late-time supernova light-curves (>20-30 days), with ULTRASAT and Einstein Telescope collaborations expected to enable multi-messenger (UV+GW) detections of newly formed magnetars at a rate exceeding once every two years, offering critical insights into magnetar formation and enhancing gravitational wave searches.

Testing the Star-disk Collision Model for Quasi-periodic Eruptions

Time: 2025-04-17

Authors: Wenyuan Guo, Rong-Feng Shen

Abstract: The star-disk collision model for Quasi-periodic eruptions (QPEs) is challenged by a study demonstrating that most sources either require implausibly large stellar radii (except two cases needing solar-sized stars) or are observationally inconsistent, while also predicting temperatures an order of magnitude lower than observed.

Abstract: Gravitational-wave observations' potential in astrophysics and cosmology is hindered by the correlation between luminosity distance and inclination in parameter estimation, but third-generation detectors like the Einstein Telescope can mitigate this error for asymmetric-mass compact binaries through enhanced sensitivity, network alignment, and higher-order waveform modes.

Data-driven extraction, phenomenology and modeling of eccentric harmonics in binary black hole merger waveforms

Time: 2025-04-16

Authors: Tousif Islam, Tejaswi Venumadhav, Ajit Kumar Mehta, Isha Anantpurkar, Digvijay Wadekar, Javier Roulet, Jonathan Mushkin, Barak Zackay, Matias Zaldarriaga

Abstract: This paper introduces a data-driven framework using singular-value decomposition and post-Newtonian insights to extract eccentric harmonics with monotonic amplitudes and frequencies from gravitational waveforms, demonstrating their phase/frequency structure as \(j\phi_{\lambda} + \phi_{\rm ecc}\) (\(jf_{\lambda} + f_{\rm ecc}\)) and providing analytical fits based on mean anomaly to aid efficient eccentric merger modeling, implemented modularly in the \texttt{gwMiner} package.

Modeling Supernova 2023ixf: Lightcurve Degeneracies and Morphological Differences

Time: 2025-04-16

Authors: Shannon Forde, Jared A. Goldberg

Abstract: The Type IIP supernova SN 2023ixf, observed in Messier 101 in May 2023 as the closest such event in a decade, was analyzed using MESA+STELLA simulations to model its lightcurve, revealing that its progenitor likely had a high initial mass with significant mass loss and minimal H-rich ejecta, challenging standard stellar evolution models.

gwharmone: first data-driven surrogate for eccentric harmonics in binary black hole merger waveforms

Time: 2025-04-16

Authors: Tousif Islam, Tejaswi Venumadhav, Ajit Kumar Mehta, Isha Anantpurkar, Digvijay Wadekar, Javier Roulet, Jonathan Mushkin, Barak Zackay, Matias Zaldarriaga

Abstract: The authors present gwharmone, the first data-driven surrogate model for eccentric harmonics in non-spinning binary black hole mergers, trained on 173 waveforms across mass ratios 1-3.5 and eccentricities 0-0.2 using singular value decomposition and Gaussian process regression, enabling fast (~0.1s), accurate predictions (time-domain error ~0.001, frequency mismatches <0.01) for efficient detection and parameter estimation, with public availability in the gwModels package.
