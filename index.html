Cosmogenic Neutrino Point Source and KM3-230213A

Time: 2025-04-14

Authors: Qinyuan Zhang, Tian-Qi Huang, Zhuo Li

Abstract: The study examines how transient ultra-high energy cosmic ray sources like gamma-ray bursts generate cosmogenic neutrinos with time-varying spectra and sub-degree angular profiles influenced by intergalactic magnetic fields, proposing that nearby point sources detectable under low IGMF conditions (e.g., the KM3-230213A event) could help identify cosmic ray origins and surpass diffuse fluxes early on.

On the universality of the split monopole black hole magnetosphere

Time: 2025-04-14

Authors: S. Selvi, O. Porth, B. Ripperda, L. Sironi

Abstract: This study uses axisymmetric general relativistic magnetohydrodynamic simulations to demonstrate that black hole magnetospheres evolve into split monopole configurations through a two-phase process—rapid Alfvénic pressure equilibration followed by slower resistive flux decay dependent on black hole spin (higher spin slows decay)—with implications for reconnection-powered flare timescales and multimessenger counterparts to gravitational wave events.

The Pierre Auger Observatory: Results and Prospects

Time: 2025-04-14

Authors: Qader Dorosti Hasankiadeh

Abstract: The Pierre Auger Observatory advances ultra-high-energy cosmic ray research through hybrid detection, presenting refined spectra, anisotropy, composition insights, and particle physics implications at extreme energies, while its AugerPrime upgrade enhances particle identification and photon/neutrino sensitivity to explore cosmic-ray origins and enable future discoveries.

Search for Kink Events in Variable Fermi-LAT Blazars

Time: 2025-04-14

Authors: P. Peñil, H. Zhang, J. Otero-Santos, M. Ajello, S. Buson, S. Adhikari, A. Rico, J. Escudero Pedrosa, I. Agudo, D. Morcuende, A. Sota, V. Casanova, F. J. Aceituno

Abstract: This study explores detecting Quasi-Periodic Oscillations (QPOs) in 9 blazars via γ-ray and polarized light observations to identify kink events in their jets, finding a segment where QPOs coincided in both data types, offering preliminary evidence for the kink origin despite limited polarized data.

Magneto-Hydrodynamic Simulations of Eccentric Binary Neutron Star Mergers

Time: 2025-04-14

Authors: Anna Neuweiler, Tim Dietrich, Bernd Brügmann

Abstract: This study's general relativistic magneto-hydrodynamic simulations of highly eccentric binary neutron star mergers show that magnetic fields have minimal impact on pre-merger dynamics or post-merger field amplification compared to quasi-circular mergers, but higher eccentricity enhances mass ejection without significantly altering magnetically driven processes.

Searching for quasi-periodicities in short transients: the curious case of GRB 230307A

Time: 2025-04-14

Authors: Daniela Huppenkothen, Matthew G. Baring, Mete Uzuner, Ersin Gögüs, Yuki Kaneko, Chryssa Kouveliotou, Oliver J. Roberts, Lin Lin

Abstract: The second-brightest gamma-ray burst GRB 230307A exhibited quasi-periodic oscillations (QPOs) at ~1.2 Hz and ~2.9 Hz during its peak emission, detected via Fourier analysis, wavelets, and Gaussian Processes, likely tracing plasma circulation in a spinning jet during the final stages of a neutron star merger.

A census of galactic spider binary millisecond pulsars with the Nançay Radio Telescope

Time: 2025-04-14

Authors: Clara Blanchard, Lucas Guillemot, Guillaume Voisin, Ismaël Cognard, Gilles Theureau

Abstract: This study analyzes eclipses in spider pulsars using pulsar timing and phenomenological modeling, revealing correlations between eclipse duration and mass function, categorizing eclipsers by eclipse shape, presenting new polarimetric data, and identifying challenges due to scarce and conflicting orbital inclination measurements.

The Thousand-Pulsar-Array programme on MeerKAT -- XVI. Mapping the Galactic magnetic field with pulsar observations

Time: 2025-04-13

Authors: L. S. Oswald, P. Weltevrede, B. Posselt, S. Johnston, A. Karastergiou, M. E. Lower

Abstract: This study uses MeerKAT pulsar rotation measures to map the Milky Way's magnetic field, revealing a bisymmetric structure aligned with spiral arms and antisymmetric parity relative to the Galactic plane, with variations between the upper and lower halves and a shifted parity change location, indicating a complex matter-magnetic field relationship and guiding future research.

Investigating the emission mechanism in the spatially-resolved jet of two z $\approx$ 3 radio-loud quasars

Time: 2025-04-13

Authors: Jaya Maithil, Daniel A. Schwartz, Aneta Siemiginowska, Preeti Kharb, Diana M. Worrall, John F. C. Wardle, Giulia Migliori, Chi C. Cheung, Bradford Snios, Doug B. Gobeille, Herman L. Marshall, Mark Birkinshaw

Abstract: This study identifies extended kpc-scale X-ray jets in two high-redshift (z > 3) quasars using Chandra data and radio follow-up, testing the inverse Compton scattering of cosmic microwave background photons as the emission mechanism and employing a novel Bayesian method to resolve degeneracies in relativistic jet parameters by estimating the jet angle.

Spatially resolved polarization variation of the Crab Nebula

Time: 2025-04-13

Authors: Chao Zuo, Fei Xie, Mingyu Ge, Wei Deng, Kuan Liu, Fabio La Monaca, Alessandro Di Marco, Wenhao Wei, Wei Chen

Abstract: The study, using two years of Imaging X-ray Polarimetry Explorer data, reveals significant temporal polarization degree variations (up to >5σ) in three Crab Nebula regions, with notable PD decreases in the southwest, no polarization angle changes, and flux shifts likely tied to magnetized turbulence, offering insights into magnetic field dynamics.
