An SS433 flare caught by TESS. Fast rise times and evidence for a 13 hours quasi-period

Time: 2025-03-04

Authors: Idel Waisberg

Abstract: A 10-day flare in the Galactic microquasar SS433, observed by TESS, exhibited a threefold flux increase with rapid 4-7 hour fluctuations and a 13.3-hour quasi-periodicity, attributed to accretion rate variations from oscillations in the donor star's mass loss, suggesting a transient common envelope, donor star density discrepancies, and potential outflow evidence in later 2024 data.

Quantitative exploration of the similarity of gamma-ray pulsar light curves

Time: 2025-03-04

Authors: C. R. García, Diego F. Torres

Abstract: The study introduces a dynamic time warping (DTW)-based method to analyze gamma-ray light curves from the Third Fermi-LAT Pulsar Catalog, revealing striking morphological similarities between some millisecond and young pulsars despite differing physical properties, beyond traditional peak-based comparisons.

Signatures of Einstein-Maxwell dilaton-axion gravity from the observed quasi-periodic oscillations in black holes

Time: 2025-03-04

Authors: Anirban Dasgupta, Nishant Tiwari, Indrani Banerjee

Abstract: This study investigates the Einstein-Maxwell dilaton-axion (EMDA) gravity from heterotic string theory, utilizing the Kerr-Sen spacetime to model high-frequency quasi-periodic oscillations (HFQPOs) with eleven QPO models, and compares results with observations of five black holes (XTE J1550-564, GRS 1915+105, H 143+322, GRO J1655-40, Sgr A*), deriving spin constraints and showing EMDA remains observationally viable against general relativity while discussing limitations and implications.

Gravitational wave probes of particle dark matter: a review

Time: 2025-03-04

Authors: Andrew L. Miller

Abstract: This review outlines how various dark matter candidates—including axions, dark photons, and WIMPs—produce distinct gravitational-wave signatures through their interactions with compact objects, isolated black holes, neutron stars, or detectors, necessitating new data analysis methods beyond matched filtering to detect their prolonged signals.

Constraints on the X-ray-to-radio fluence ratio of FRB 20240114A

Time: 2025-03-04

Authors: F. Eppel, M. Krumpe, P. Limaye, N. Intrarat, J. Wongphechauxsorn, M. Cruces, W. Herrmann, F. Jankowski, P. Jaroenjittichai, L. G. Spitler, M. Kadler

Abstract: Simultaneous multiwavelength observations of the nearby, hyperactive repeating fast radio burst FRB 20240114A constrain its X-ray-to-radio fluence ratio to levels consistent with emission mechanisms akin to the Galactic magnetar SGR 1935+2154, supporting a similar origin and underscoring the potential of future campaigns to enhance these constraints.

Emergence of cHz Quasi-Periodic Oscillations from low angular momentum flow onto a supermassive black hole

Time: 2025-03-04

Authors: Indu K. Dihingia, Yosuke Mizuno

Abstract: This study demonstrates through simulations that low-angular momentum accretion flows around supermassive black holes (e.g., Sgr~A$^*$) can produce centiHz quasi-periodic oscillations (QPOs) with 2:1 harmonic ratios, offering a signature to identify such accretion processes.

Observational evidence for a correlation between the magnetic field of jets and star formation rate in host galaxies

Time: 2025-03-04

Authors: Yongyun Chen, Qiusheng Gu, Junhui Fan, Xiaotong Guo, Xiaoling Yu, Nan Ding, Dingrong Xiong

Abstract: The study of 96 galaxies hosting supermassive black holes reveals a correlation between jet magnetic fields and star formation rates, suggesting that jets from active galactic nuclei may power star formation in host galaxies, underscoring the critical role of magnetic fields in galaxy evolution.

Deflection angle in the strong deflection limit: a perspective from local geometrical invariants and matter distributions

Time: 2025-03-04

Authors: Takahisa Igata

Abstract: This paper introduces a universal analytical framework that explains the logarithmic divergence of the deflection angle in strong gravitational lensing through local energy density and tangential pressure at the photon sphere, revealing a coefficient \(\bar{a}\) which becomes universal (\(\bar{a}=1\)) when \(\rho + \Pi = 0\), applicable to diverse spacetimes and connecting lensing behavior to quasinormal modes, offering a theory-independent tool for testing gravitational theories and interpreting astrophysical data.

Time: 2025-03-04

Authors: Mohsen Fathi, Y. Sekhmani

Abstract: This study investigates photon orbits, shadow characteristics, and evaporation rates of rotating black holes in the Einstein-$\mathrm{SU(N)}$ nonlinear sigma model, using Event Horizon Telescope data from M87* and Sgr A* to constrain model parameters $K$ and $N$ while analyzing static and rotating black hole solutions.
