High Energy Emission from the Intrabinary Shocks in Redback Pulsars

Time: 2025-03-31

Authors: Andrew G. Sullivan, Roger W. Romani

Abstract: Joint soft and hard X-ray analyses of three redback spider pulsars reveal synchrotron cooling breaks, enabling estimates of intrabinary shock magnetic fields (40–100 G) and electron energy limits, which constrain pulsar wind magnetization and magnetospheric pair-production mechanisms.

EP240414a: Off-axis View of a Jet-Cocoon System from an Expanded Progenitor Star

Time: 2025-03-31

Authors: Jian-He Zheng, Jin-Ping Zhu, Wenbin Lu, Bing Zhang

Abstract: The study explains the multi-phase emissions of fast X-ray transient EP240414a through a slightly off-axis jet-cocoon system, attributing X-ray, optical, and radio signals to cocoon cooling and jet afterglow processes, constraining the progenitor to a ~3 solar radius helium star and predicting future detections of similar events by the Einstein Probe.

Non-linear saturation of gravito-inertial modes excited by tidal resonances in binary neutron stars

Time: 2025-03-31

Authors: Alexis Reboul-Salze, Aurélie Astoul, Hao-Jui Kuan, Arthur G. Suvorov

Abstract: This study investigates the nonlinear saturation of gravito-inertial modes in binary neutron-star mergers, developing a novel nonlinear model and conducting 3D simulations that demonstrate magnetic field amplification to ≥10¹⁴ G, parametric instabilities, and spin-up effects extending resonance windows.

Populations of evolved massive binary stars in the Small Magellanic Cloud II: Predictions from rapid binary evolution

Time: 2025-03-31

Authors: C. Schürmann, X. -T. Xu, N. Langer, D. Lennon, M. U. Kruckow, J. Antoniadis, F. Haberl, A. Herrero, M. Kramer, A. Schootemeijer, T. Shenar, T. M. Tauris, C. Wang

Abstract: The study uses the ComBinE population synthesis code to model massive binary star evolution in the Small Magellanic Cloud, finding that a variable mass-transfer efficiency (high for reproducing Be stars and low for Wolf-Rayet stars) is needed to align with observations, and proposes a mass-dependent efficiency model that explains observed binaries but predicts unobserved OB+BH systems.

Populations of evolved massive binary stars in the Small Magellanic Cloud I: Predictions from detailed evolution models

Time: 2025-03-31

Authors: X. -T. Xu, C. Schürmann, N. Langer, C. Wang, A. Schootemeijer, T. Shenar, A. Ercolino, F. Haberl, B. Hastings, H. Jin, M. Kramer, D. Lennon, P. Marchant, K. Sen, T. M. Tauris, S. E. de Mink

Abstract: The study employs MESA models to simulate massive binary star evolution at low metallicity in the SMC, revealing that 8% and 7% of OB stars are post-mass transfer systems and merger products, respectively, with spun-up mass gainers forming Oe/Be stars; while matching some observed features (e.g., binary WR stars), discrepancies like underpredicted Be/X-ray binaries and unobserved WR+OB binaries suggest model adjustments through lower merger rates or higher mass transfer efficiency.

Explodability criteria for the neutrino-driven supernova mechanism

Time: 2025-03-31

Authors: K. Maltsev, F. R. N. Schneider, I. Mandel, B. Müller, A. Heger, F. K. Röpke, E. Laplace

Abstract: The study presents criteria based on carbon-oxygen core mass and metallicity to predict whether massive stars undergoing core collapse form supernovae or black holes, enabling efficient population synthesis with >99% accuracy and addressing observational gaps like the missing red supergiant problem.

Multispacecraft Observations of the 2024 September 9 Backside Solar Eruption that Resulted in a Sustained Gamma Ray Emission Event

Time: 2025-03-31

Authors: Nat Gopalswamy, Pertti Mäkelä, Sachiko Akiyama, Hong Xie, Seiji Yashiro, Stuart D. Bale, Robert F. Wimmer-Schweingruber, Patrick Kuehl, Säm Krucker

Abstract: The September 9, 2024 sustained gamma-ray emission event observed by Fermi’s Large Area Telescope, linked to a backside solar eruption with a rapidly accelerated CME flux rope and energetic particles from an extended shock, supports shock-driven CMEs as the SGRE source, aligns with similar events, and reveals significant east-west source asymmetry.

Narrow-Line Seyfert 1 Galaxies Beyond the Local X-ray Universe: An X-ray spectral sample

Time: 2025-03-31

Authors: Jiachen Jiang, Dominic J. Walton, Luigi C. Gallo, Andrew C. Fabian, Dirk Grupe, Richard McMahon, Christopher S. Reynolds, Andrew Young, Zhibo Yu, Honghui Liu, Zuobin Zhang

Abstract: This study of 14 high-redshift (z≈1) NLS1 AGNs using SDSS and eROSITA data reveals X-ray emission consistent with AGN activity, high photon indices indicating soft excess emission, alignment of Eddington ratios with optical data through X-ray luminosity corrections (challenging prior accretion disk models), and underscores the need for deeper surveys to explore X-ray-weak NLS1s.
