Expectation for the MeV Gamma-Ray Emission from Pair-Instability Supernovae

Time: 2025-03-27

Authors: Ryo Sawada

Abstract: The study developed a 1D Monte Carlo radiation transport code to simulate gamma-ray and hard X-ray emissions from pair-instability supernovae (PISNe), finding that next-generation MeV telescopes could detect key 847 and 1238 keV gamma-ray lines from Co-56 decay in 130M⊙ models up to 300-400 Mpc (unlike the undetectable 100M⊙ case), with an estimated rare event rate of ~0.1 per year within 300 Mpc, highlighting their feasibility as future observational targets despite their scarcity.

Transitioning to Memory Burden: Detectable Small Primordial Black Holes as Dark Matter

Time: 2025-03-27

Authors: Gia Dvali, Michael Zantedeschi, Sebastian Zell

Abstract: The memory burden effect, which suppresses black hole decay by retaining information, strengthens small primordial black holes (PBHs) below \(10^{15}\,{\rm g}\) as dark matter candidates by altering observational constraints via a transition from semi-classical evaporation, with current astrophysical particle fluxes imposing stringent bounds and high-energy neutrinos offering detection prospects for transitioning PBHs.

A possible GeV-TeV connection in the direction of the Globular Cluster UKS 1

Time: 2025-03-27

Authors: Jiwon Shin, C. Y. Hui, Sangin Kim, Kwangmin Oh, Ellis R. Owen

Abstract: Using Fermi LAT and HAWC data, researchers detected significant GeV and displaced TeV emissions near globular cluster UKS 1, suggesting the TeV signal arises from inverse Compton scattering by relativistic particles moving away from the bow shock due to the cluster's supersonic motion.

3D MHD simulations of runaway pulsars in core collapse supernova remnants

Time: 2025-03-27

Authors: D. M. A. Meyer, D. F. Torres, Z. Meliani

Abstract: The study employs 3D magnetohydrodynamic simulations to show that the circumstellar medium and supernova remnant of a progenitor star critically shape pulsar wind nebulae through interactions with a runaway pulsar, bending its jets and altering radio synchrotron emission, underscoring the necessity of understanding stellar evolutionary history.

Polarized equatorial emission and hot spots around black holes with a dark matter halo

Time: 2025-03-27

Authors: Tsanimir Angelov, Rasim Bekir, Galin Gyulchev, Petya Nedkova, Stoytcho Yazadjiev

Abstract: This study investigates the impact of a dark matter halo, modeled via a Schwarzschild black hole combined with a Hernquist-type matter distribution, on the linear polarization of accretion disks, revealing minimal deviations (under 1% for direct images, up to 10% for indirect images) from isolated black holes, indicating significant challenges in detecting dark matter effects through polarized emissions in the near future.

Study of the origin of the azimuthal variation of synchrotron X-ray spectrum from SNR RX J0852.0-4622

Time: 2025-03-27

Authors: Dai Tateishi, Nobuaki Sasaki, Yukikatsu Terada, Satoru Katsuda, Shiu-Hang Lee, Hidetoshi Sano, Yasuo Fukui, Aya Bamba

Abstract: The azimuthal variations in X-ray flux and photon index of the supernova remnant RX J0852.0-4622, observed via Suzaku/XIS, are primarily attributed to cloud density differences (correlated at ~5σ in the north) and shock velocity (south), driven by interactions with dense clouds and faster shocks, rather than the non-aligned galactic magnetic field.

Gravitational-wave kicks impact spins of black holes from hierarchical mergers

Time: 2025-03-27

Authors: Angela Borchers, Claire S. Ye, Maya Fishbach

Abstract: The spin distributions of hierarchically formed black holes are skewed by gravitational-wave ejection effects, leading to bimodal or broad spin ranges in low escape velocity environments and higher generations, with aligned spins creating observable peaks, offering insights into their formation histories and environments through gravitational-wave signatures.

Are Neutron Stars Rich in H-dibaryons?

Time: 2025-03-27

Authors: Jesper Leong, Anthony W. Thomas, Pierre A. M. Guichon

Abstract: This study explores the potential impact of a hypothetical H-dibaryon near the $\Lambda-\Lambda$ threshold on neutron stars using the quark-meson coupling model and excluded volume formalism, finding that while the H-dibaryon becomes abundant in heavy stars, its presence only slightly reduces the maximum neutron star mass.

Heavy Neutron Star Phenomenology with an H-dibaryon

Time: 2025-03-27

Authors: Jesper Leong, Anthony W. Thomas, Pierre A. M. Guichon

Abstract: This study finds that incorporating a near-ΛΛ threshold mass H-dibaryon into β-equilibrated dense nuclear matter via the quark-meson coupling model slightly reduces neutron star maximum mass, leaves tidal deformability unchanged, and leads to abundant H-particles extending up to 6 km from the core in massive neutron stars.
