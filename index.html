The very high X-ray polarisation of accreting black hole IGRJ17091-3624 in the hard state

Time: 2025-03-28

Authors: Melissa Ewing, Maxime Parra, Guglielmo Mastroserio, Alexandra Veledina, Adam Ingram, Michal Dovčiak, Javier A. García, Thomas D. Russell, Maria C. Baglio, Juri Poutanen, Oluwashina Adegoke, Stefano Bianchi, Fiamma Capitanio, Riley Connors, Melania Del Santo, Barbara De Marco, María Díaz Trigo, Poshak Gandhi, Maitrayee Gupta, Chulsoo Kang, Elias Kammoun, Vladislav Loktev, Lorenzo Marra, Giorgio Matt, Edward Nathan, Pierre-Olivier Petrucci, Megumi Shidatsu, James F. Steiner, Francesco Tombesi

Abstract: The Imaging X-ray Polarimetry Explorer (IXPE) detected significant X-ray polarization (9.1% ±1.6%, angle 83°±5° at 5.2σ confidence) in the corona-dominated hard state of black hole binary IGR J17091-3624, suggesting a high-inclination geometry and possible alignment with a radio jet, akin to Cygnus X-1 and Swift J1727.8-1613, with the high polarization likely requiring a favorable corona configuration and potential outflow or scattering effects.

Diffraction around caustics in gravitational wave lensing

Time: 2025-03-28

Authors: Jose María Ezquiaga, Rico K. L. Lo, Luka Vujeva

Abstract: This study examines diffraction effects around gravitational lensing caustics (point, fold, cusp) on gravitational wave signals, demonstrating that while detectable lensing distortions—derived through analytical amplitudes and phases—could identify lensed events via Bayesian analysis, the stationary phase approximation remains reliable even near caustics despite waveform interference.

Shadow and gravitational lensing produced by the nonlinear accretion of a scalar field onto a black hole

Time: 2025-03-28

Authors: J. C. Acevedo-Muñoz, F. D. Lora-Clavijo, A. Cruz-Osorio

Abstract: A numerical study demonstrates that accretion of ultralight scalar field dark matter onto black holes induces significant growth in apparent horizons, photon rings, and Einstein rings through nonlinear dynamics and gravitational lensing, suggesting these effects could influence observed black hole shadows in Sgr A* and M87*.

Disc breaking through forced eccentricity growth

Time: 2025-03-28

Authors: Madeline Overton, Rebecca G. Martin, Stephen H. Lubow, Stephen Lepp

Abstract: Studying eccentric Be/X-ray binaries, simulations and theory reveal that retrograde discs exhibit greater radial extension, eccentricity oscillations, faster precession, and potential disc breaking into distinct eccentric rings, influencing observed lightcurves and X-ray outbursts.

Cosmological Origin of the KM3-230213A event and associated Gravitational Waves

Time: 2025-03-28

Authors: Ki-Young Choi, Erdenebulgan Lkhagvadorj, Satyabrata Mahapatra

Abstract: The proposed cosmological scenario explains the KM3-230213A ultra-high-energy neutrino event via decay of a super-heavy sterile neutrino produced by primordial black hole evaporation, while predicting distinct gravitational wave signatures from PBH evaporation and sterile neutrino decay, offering a multi-messenger approach to explore neutrino and gravitational wave cosmology.

A novel approach of optimizing the image cleaning performance of Imaging Atmospheric Cherenkov Telescopes: Application to a time-based cleaning for H.E.S.S

Time: 2025-03-28

Authors: Jelena Ćelić, Rodrigo Guedes Lang, Simon Steinmassl, Jim Hinton, Stefan Funk

Abstract: This study introduces a time-based cleaning method (TIME3D/TIME4D) and optimization workflow for H.E.S.S.'s CT5, demonstrating significant low-energy sensitivity improvements (15–200%) over traditional methods through enhanced noise suppression, event reconstruction, and gamma-hadron separation, enabling detection of faint astrophysical sources and applicable to other IACT systems like VERITAS and MAGIC.

Axial perturbations of black holes with primary scalar hair

Time: 2025-03-28

Authors: Christos Charmousis, Simon Iteanu, David Langlois, Karim Noui

Abstract: This study examines axial perturbations of static, scalar-charged black holes in degenerate higher-order scalar-tensor (DHOST) theories, which generalize Schwarzschild black holes via a scalar charge parameter, constructs new solutions through disformal transformations (yielding light-speed gravitational waves or Horndeski theories), and analyzes quasi-normal modes via an effective metric with distinct causal horizons using a WKB approach.

Auto- and cross-correlations for multiple images of corotating hotspots in accretion disks

Time: 2025-03-28

Authors: Qing-Hua Zhu

Abstract: This study explores spatiotemporal correlations in multiple images of a corotating hotspot near Sgr A*, revealing inclined band-like structures in the ΔΦ-Δt plane influenced by inclination angles and hotspot dynamics, with lower-order image correlations dependent on orbital configurations and hotspot shapes affecting light curves but not correlation bands, challenging black hole geometry inferences.

The persistent nature of the Be X-ray binary pulsar 4U 0728-25

Time: 2025-03-28

Authors: N. La Palombara, L. Sidoli, S. Mereghetti, P. Esposito, G. L. Israel

Abstract: The April 2023 XMM-Newton observation of the Galactic Be X-ray binary pulsar 4U 0728-25 revealed its lowest recorded luminosity (∼1.3×10³⁵ erg s⁻¹), a slightly increased spin period (103.301 s, 0.15% longer than 2016), four viable spectral models (including thermal and non-thermal components), and characteristics aligning it with persistent Be X-ray binaries.

Discern Misclassified flat-spectrum radio quasars from low-frequency peaked BL Lacertae objects

Time: 2025-03-28

Authors: S. Liang, W. G. Yang, Y. G. Zheng, S. J. Kang

Abstract: This study analyzes 312 low-frequency peaked BL Lacertae objects (LBLs) and 694 flat spectrum radio quasars (FSRQs) from Fermi-LAT's 4LAC-DR2 and 4FGL-DR2 catalogs, employing multi-wavelength spectral fitting and a support vector machine (SVM) in the $\log L_\gamma$-$\Gamma_\gamma$ parameter space to identify 25 FSRQ candidates from LBLs, with optical spectra confirming 8 out of 13 candidates as having emission lines ($EW > 5 \mathring{\mathrm{A}}$).
