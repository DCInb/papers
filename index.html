Variability study of classical supergiant X-ray binary 4U 1907+09 using NuSTAR

Time: 2025-04-03

Authors: Raj Kumara, Sayantan Bhattacharya, Sudip Bhattacharyya, Subir Bhattacharyyaa

Abstract: The 2024 NuSTAR observation of supergiant X-ray binary 4U 1907+09 revealed significant flux variability with dips and flares, detected persistent cyclotron resonance features at ~17.6 keV and ~38 keV, confirmed continued pulsar spin-down (443.99s period), and linked spectral parameter variations and a luminosity-consistent "pencil" beam pattern to accretion dynamics and magnetic field geometry.

Monte Carlo evaluations of gamma-ray and radio pulsar populations

Time: 2025-04-03

Authors: Shawaiz Tabassum, Duncan Lorimer

Abstract: This study evaluates seven γ-ray luminosity models using Fermi-LAT data and neutron star population syntheses, finding four models align with observed unidentified pulsar-like sources, while three over-predict them, suggesting revised beaming fractions or luminosity scaling, noting a discrepancy for canonical pulsars, and advocating future radio searches to resolve these issues.

Unveiling the reflection spectrum in the ultracompact LMXB 4U 1820-30

Time: 2025-04-03

Authors: A. Anitra, A. Gnarini, T. Di Salvo, R. Iaria, A. Sanna, L. Burderi, A. Marino, M. Del Santo, G. Matt, F. Ursini, S. Bianchi, F. Capitanio, S. Fabiani, A. Tarana, A. Di Marco

Abstract: Using simultaneous NICER, NuSTAR, and IXPE observations, this study of the ultracompact X-ray binary 4U 1820-30 detected a strong reflection component, measured a low system inclination (~31°), found subsolar iron abundances linked to its metal-poor globular cluster environment, and observed energy-dependent polarization rising to ~8% at 7–8 keV, challenging current models despite the low inclination.

GRB 170519A: Thermal Radiation in an X-ray Flare and Decaying Magnetic Fields for the Early-Time Afterglow

Time: 2025-04-03

Authors: Zi-Min Zhou, Liang-Jun Chen, Rui-Quan Li, Xiang-Gao Wang, Xing-Ling Li, En-Wei Liang, WeiKang Zheng, Alexei V. Filippenko

Abstract: GRB 170519A, observed by *Swift* and ground-based telescopes, exhibited a multiwavelength afterglow with X-ray flares, an optical onset, decay phases, and a potential jet break, where spectral analysis revealed a thermal component with decreasing temperature and Lorentz factor, adhering to specific luminosity relations, and was successfully modeled by an external shock with time-dependent magnetic field efficiency (εB).

Search for Fast Radio Bursts and radio pulsars from pulsing Ultraluminous X-ray Sources

Time: 2025-04-03

Authors: Juntao Bai, Na Wang, Rui Luo, Wei-Yang Wang, Shi Dai, Songbo Zhang, Shiqian Zhao, Shuangqiang Wang

Abstract: A radio survey of eight pulsing ultraluminous X-ray sources (PULXs) using FAST and Parkes telescopes detected no FRBs or pulsars, implying that accretion processes and dense stellar winds suppress coherent radio emission and constraining models linking FRBs to magnetized neutron stars in binary systems.

Investigating the Recursive Short X-ray Burst Behavior of Magnetars Through Crustal Interactions

Time: 2025-04-02

Authors: Ozge Keskin, Samuel K. Lander, Ersin Gogus

Abstract: This study employs a cellular automaton model of magnetar crustal dynamics to simulate observed clustering of X-ray bursts, successfully replicating active episode durations and energetics, providing insights into the mechanisms driving magnetar burst clusters and reactivations.

Transient gamma rays from the 2021 outburst of the recurrent nova RS Ophiuchi: the effect of gamma-ray absorption

Time: 2025-04-02

Authors: Vo Hong Minh Phan, Pierre Cristofari, Enrico Peretti, Vincent Tatischeff, Andrea Ciardi

Abstract: The observed two-day delay between the GeV and TeV gamma-ray peaks in the 2021 RS Ophiuchi nova is explained by gamma-ray absorption via interactions with optical photons emitted during the outburst, as shown through shock acceleration modeling and radiative transfer analysis, highlighting the necessity of multi-wavelength observations to understand particle acceleration in novae.

Probing axion-like particles with multimessenger observations of neutron star mergers

Time: 2025-04-02

Authors: Francesca Lecce, Alessandro Lella, Giuseppe Lucente, Vimal Vijayan, Andreas Bauswein, Maurizio Giannotti, Alessandro Mirizzi

Abstract: The study finds that MeV gamma-ray telescopes, combined with gravitational-wave detectors, could detect axion-like particles (ALPs) produced in binary neutron star mergers via their couplings to nucleons and photons, achieving sensitivities down to \(g_{a\gamma}\gtrsim \textrm{few} \times 10^{-13}\,\text{GeV}^{-1}\) for ultralight ALPs (\(m_a \lesssim 10^{-9}~\mathrm{eV}\)), rivaling the SN 1987A constraint.
