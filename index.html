A multiwavelength light curve analysis of the classical nova V392 Per: Optical contribution from an irradiated accretion disk during the nova wind phase

Time: 2025-03-17

Authors: Izumi Hachisu, Mariko Kato

Abstract: The classical nova V392 Per 2018, characterized by a fast optical decline, GeV gamma-ray detection, and nearly identical decay in B, V, and IC bands (unlike typical novae where emission lines cause divergence), is explained by a model where continuum radiation from an irradiated accretion disk around a 1.35–1.37 M☉ white dwarf dominates due to high mass-accretion rates, with a derived distance of ~3.45 kpc.

Extended mass and spheroidal deformation effects on epicyclic frequencies and periapsis shift in quasi-circular orbits

Time: 2025-03-17

Authors: Takahisa Igata

Abstract: This study explores how extended mass and spheroidal deformation influence the periapsis shift of quasi-circular orbits in Newtonian gravity, showing that uniform density (Maclaurin spheroid) yields a constant retrograde shift of −π, while density inhomogeneity and spheroidal deformation (oblate suppressing, prolate amplifying) cause radius-dependent shifts, with conditions for orbital stability and marginally stable orbits identified through parameter variations.

Sombrero Galaxy as an Accelerator of Ultrahigh Energy Cosmic Ray Nuclei

Time: 2025-03-17

Authors: Luis A. Anchordoqui, Karem Peñaló Castillo

Abstract: This study challenges the conventional belief by demonstrating that the supermassive black hole in the Sombrero galaxy can efficiently accelerate cosmic ray nuclei to the highest observed energies without spallation, suggesting such accelerators need not anti-correlate with electromagnetic source power.

Contribution to the Radio Light Curves of a Core-Collapse Supernova due to the Off-axis Gamma-Ray Burst Afterglow

Time: 2025-03-17

Authors: Yo Kusafuka, Tomoki Matsuoka, Ryo Sawada

Abstract: This study presents the first comprehensive theoretical model predicting double-peaked radio emission from Type Ic-BL supernovae linked to gamma-ray burst jets, showing such features emerge under low-energy (low-luminosity GRB) or off-axis viewing conditions, and proposes that radio follow-ups of nearby supernovae within a few years can uncover hidden off-axis GRBs, advancing understanding of their progenitor connections.

Starburst-Driven Galactic Outflows -- Unveiling the Suppressive Role of Cosmic Ray Halos

Time: 2025-03-17

Authors: Leonard E. C. Romano, Ellis R. Owen, Kentaro Nagamine

Abstract: Cosmic ray (CR) halos enable slow outflows in young galaxies with low star-formation rate (SFR) surface densities but suppress such outflows in older systems with accumulated CR halos, while fast outflows in high-SFR starburst galaxies remain driven by momentum injection and unaffected by CRs.

A multi-messenger mass determination method for LISA Neutron-Star White-Dwarf Binaries

Time: 2025-03-17

Authors: Kaye Jiale Li, Jane SiNan Long, Kinwah Wu, Albert K. H. Kong

Abstract: By combining gravitational wave observations from LISA and optical data, this study proposes a multi-messenger method to break the mass-inclination degeneracy in neutron-star white-dwarf binaries, enabling neutron star mass determination with ±0.2 solar mass accuracy for galactic ultra-compact systems.

Exploring a tentative link between FRBs and pulsars with broad energy distributions and applications for nearby FRB survey strategies

Time: 2025-03-17

Authors: S. B. Zhang, G. Hobbs, S. Johnston, S. Dai, Y. Li, J. S. Wang, X. Yang, X. F. Wu, L. Staveley-Smith

Abstract: This study investigates fast radio bursts (FRBs) and pulsars with broad energy distributions, revealing that shallow power-law indices (α ≳ -1) in certain repeating and nearby FRBs and pulsars imply bright pulses dominate their energy output, suggesting a connection between high- and low-luminosity radio bursts, and recommends combined FAST and Parkes cryoPAF observations to detect new FRBs in nearby globular clusters.

Gravitational Wave Effects on Radio Spectral Lines of Atomic Hydrogen: Hyperfine Splitting and Broadening Mechanisms

Time: 2025-03-17

Authors: Nontapat Wanwieng, Nithiwadee Thaicharoen, Narupon Chattrapiban, Apimook Watcharangkool

Abstract: This study investigates the effects of gravitational waves (GWs) on hydrogen's radio spectral lines, revealing that while energy shifts in the 21 cm hyperfine transition are undetectable with current instruments, spectral broadening in highly excited Rydberg states' radio recombination lines (RRLs) could probe ultra-high-frequency GWs, such as those emitted by planetary-mass primordial black hole binaries or the stochastic gravitational-wave background, with broadening effects potentially exceeding natural levels at high quantum numbers (n > 100).
