Does the 220 PeV Event at KM3NeT Point to New Physics?

Time: 2025-02-28

Authors: Vedran Brdar, Dibya S. Chattopadhyay

Abstract: The KM3NeT collaboration's observation of a high-energy neutrino undetected by IceCube, despite its larger effective area, suggests new physics via sterile neutrino oscillations—enabled by differing propagation distances through Earth—could resolve the tension, hinting at neutrino telescopes potentially detecting beyond-Standard Model phenomena.

Reconstruction of spider system's observables from orbital period modulations via the Applegate mechanism

Time: 2025-02-28

Authors: Vittorio De Falco, Amodio Carleo, Alessandro Ridolfi, Alessandro Corongiu

Abstract: This study develops a time-evolution model based on the Applegate mechanism, which explains orbital period variations in redback and black widow pulsars through magnetic-induced quadrupole changes in their companions, and applies it to analyze 47 Tuc W and 47 Tuc O, offering insights into magnetic field dynamics and system behavior via observational data and literature comparison.

Probing the interstellar medium toward GRB 221009A through X-ray dust scattering

Time: 2025-02-28

Authors: B. Vaia, Ž. Bošnjak, A. Bracco, S. Campana, P. Esposito, V. Jelić, A. Sacchi, A. Tiengo

Abstract: The observation of 21 X-ray dust-scattering rings from GRB 221009A enabled high-resolution mapping of the Galactic interstellar medium, revised the GRB's soft X-ray fluence downward by ~35% (still exceeding model predictions), and constrained the host galaxy's hydrogen column density to \(N_{\rm{H,z=0.151}} = (3.7\pm0.3) \times 10^{21}\,\rm{cm^{-2}}\), clarifying Galactic absorption effects and implications for host galaxy studies and afterglow modeling.

Pattern and Origin for the Extreme $γ$-ray Flares of 3C 454.3 and 3C 279: An Astrophysical Critical Damper?

Time: 2025-02-28

Authors: Haiyun Zhang, Dahai Yan, Jianeng Zhou, Li Zhang, Niansheng Tang

Abstract: A Gaussian process analysis of extreme γ-ray flares in 3C 454.3 and 3C 279, utilizing SHO, DRW, and Matérn-3/2 kernels, revealed a preference for SHO (over-damped) and Matérn-3/2 kernels, indicating critical damping (Q≈0.4) with characteristic timescales of 2-3 and 3-5 days, respectively, which constrain energy dissipation region sizes and suggest abrupt energy release following disturbance.

Discovery of 15 new pulsars at high Galactic Latitudes with FAST

Time: 2025-02-28

Authors: Xin Xu, Shi Dai, Qijun Zhi, Juntao Bai, Joanna Berteaud, Francesca Calore, Maïca Clavel, Weiwei Zhu, Di Li, Rushuang Zhao, Renxin Xu, Guojun Qiao

Abstract: Using the FAST telescope, a high Galactic latitude survey near the Galactic Center discovered 15 pulsars (11 normal, 4 millisecond pulsars with three in binaries), obtained timing solutions for three normal pulsars, observed mode-changing/subpulse drifting in four, and suggested their likely Galactic disk origin while highlighting the survey's potential to advance understanding of the Bulge's millisecond pulsar population.

Photon-ALP beam propagation from Mrk 501

Time: 2025-02-28

Authors: L. J. Dong, Y. G. Zheng, S. J. Kang, C. Y. Yang

Abstract: This study demonstrates that photon-ALP oscillations in astrophysical magnetic fields reduce extragalactic background light absorption in TeV gamma-ray spectra from sources like Mrk 501, with specific ALP parameters (mass ∼10⁻¹⁰ eV, coupling ∼0.417×10⁻¹¹ GeV⁻¹) under realistic magnetic field scenarios reproducing observed data.

Numerical studies of (in)stabilities of shocks in perturbed advective flows around black holes

Time: 2025-02-28

Authors: Junxing Zhou, Junxiang Huang, Xin Chang, Toru Okuda, Chandra B. Singh

Abstract: Two-dimensional hydrodynamic simulations of hot advective accretion flows around black holes demonstrate that non-axisymmetric perturbations induce quasi-periodic oscillations (QPOs) with frequencies spanning 0.44–146.67 Hz, aligning with observed low- to high-frequency QPOs in black hole X-ray binaries.

Resummed multi-line gamma-ray spectra for Cherenkov Telescopes from heavy spin-1 dark matter

Time: 2025-02-28

Authors: Motoko Fujiwara, Martin Vollmann

Abstract: This study presents a TeV-scale spin-1 electroweak dark matter model, predicting detectable gamma-ray line features and employing SCET-based NLL resummation to mitigate Sudakov corrections, revealing spin-independent spectral universality across DM spins and enabling precise analysis of annihilation channels (γγ, Zγ, Z') with reduced uncertainty for future observational tests.

On the Extremely X-ray Variable Active Galactic Nuclei in the XMM-LSS Field

Time: 2025-02-27

Authors: Zijian Zhang, Bin Luo, Linhua Jiang, W. N. Brandt, Jian Huang, Qingling Ni

Abstract: A study of eight extremely X-ray variable AGNs in the XMM-SERVS XMM-LSS region attributes their variability to changing accretion states (supported by multiwavelength data and confirmed in one changing-look AGN) or obscuration by clumpy accretion-disk winds, with timing and observational limitations explaining the absence of changing-look behavior in some cases.
